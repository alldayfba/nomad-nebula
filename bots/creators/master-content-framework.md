## The 8-Second Content Filter

Before publishing ANYTHING, check:

1. **Does it have a specific number?** (Buyers + Jonas + Nik Setting)
2. **Does it sell the OPPORTUNITY of AI Amazon, not coaching?** (Jonas + Ben Bader)
3. **Does it make one of Ben's 3 Sales?** (Vehicle / Authority / Belief in self)
4. **Would someone burned by courses watch this without feeling sold?** (Buyers + Max)
5. **Does it show the WHAT, not the HOW?** (Kabrin + Nik Setting)
6. **Is the topic compelling regardless of hook?** (Nik Setting)
7. **Does it stack proof throughout?** (Jeremy Haynes + Hormozi)
8. **Would I watch this if I didn't know who posted it?** (Max + Jonas)

5+ yes = post. Less than 5 = rework or kill.

## Hormozi Value Equation (Maximize in Every Piece)

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort)**

- Increase Dream Outcome: "$88K week" / "$10K first month"
- Increase Perceived Likelihood: Student case studies by age, background, situation
- Decrease Time Delay: "Xavier's first week" / "AI found it in 60 seconds"
- Decrease Effort: "The system does it for you" / "AI handles sourcing"

**Buyer Mirror:**
- [ ] Speaks to someone scared but motivated
- [ ] Includes a specific number (not a range)
- [ ] Features Sabbo's story in some form
- [ ] Addresses: finding products / ungating / not knowing where to start
- [ ] Anti-grifter safe — doesn't feel like a pitch

- [ ] Stacks proof (at least one real student result)
- [ ] Increases Dream Outcome or reduces perceived Effort (Hormozi)
- [ ] Hook leads with result, not method
- [ ] Genuinely useful standalone, not a teaser

**Positioning:**
- [ ] Sells AI Amazon as THE opportunity
- [ ] Shows the WHAT not the HOW
- [ ] Uses subconscious hooks (ICP language, no direct callout)
