## Part 1: Revenue Architecture

### The Revenue Split

After analyzing all 5 creator frameworks, the optimal split is **agency-heavy with coaching as an accelerant**. This follows Hormozi's top-down pricing principle and Ben Bader's Phase 4 model.

**Target Split: $130K Agency + $70K Coaching = $200K/month**

| Offer | Monthly Target | Pricing | Clients/Students Needed | Close Rate Needed | Calls Needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Growth Agency | $130,000 | $15K-$25K/mo retainer | 6-8 active clients | 30-40% [Hormozi] | 3-4 new/month |
| Amazon FBA Coaching | $70,000 | $7K enrollment avg | 10 new students/month | 25-30% | 35-40/month |
| **Combined** | **$200,000** | | | | |

**Why agency-heavy:**
- [Hormozi] "Start with HIGHEST TICKET first. Get 5-8 premium clients. Use profits to build coaching program infrastructure."
- [Hormozi] Tesla model: "Brand narrative works top-down, not bottom-up." Agency credibility makes coaching offer sell easier.
- [Ben Bader] "One banger changes everything." One $25K/mo agency client = 3.5 coaching students.
- [SooWei] "Price is the biggest lever because you can reinvest into the best talent, the best fulfillment systems, and also acquisition."

### Pricing Strategy

**Growth Agency Pricing:**

| Tier | Price | Target Client | Expected Volume |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core retainer | $15K/mo | 7-figure founder, needs full growth OS | 4-5 clients |
| Premium retainer | $25K/mo | 8-figure founder, needs operator-level embed | 2-3 clients |
| Setup fee (one-time) | $10K-$15K | All new clients | Per engagement |
| Revshare bonus | 10-15% of revenue above baseline | High-ceiling clients | 1-2 clients |

- [Hormozi] Close rate ladder: If closing above 50%, you are underpriced by 1.5-2x. Target 30-40% close rate.
- [Nik Setting] Reverse-based pricing option: 50% upfront + 50% when client hits revenue milestone. "Dramatically increases closing rate."
- [Ben Bader] Add revshare component: "Retainers cap income. Revshares compound." Start at 10-15% on incremental revenue generated.

**Amazon FBA Coaching Pricing:**

| Tier | Price | What They Get | Market to |
|---|---|---|---|
| DWY Coaching (core) | $7K | 90-day program, group calls, AI sourcing tools, accountability | Market THIS one |
| DIY Course | $3K | Self-paced course, community access, basic tool access | Offer on call if DWY not right |
| Premium 1-on-1 | $15K | Direct mentorship, custom sourcing plan, Sabbo's personal guidance | Upsell after DWY results |

- [Nik Setting] "Only market ONE offer. Never present all three options in your content. Market the DWY."
- [SooWei] "The moment we removed the 3K entirely, we started making more money. Marketing was simpler because we were just talking to one type of person."
- [Hormozi] 5-10x rule between tiers: $3K -> $7K -> $15K -> potential $50K+ for portfolio partnerships.

```

- Don't make content ONLY for your ICP. Make it for your "dream follower."

- Coaching dream follower: people interested in entrepreneurship, side income, financial freedom (broader than "people with $5-20K capital for Amazon")
- The ICP is always a subset of the dream follower audience. Broader TAM = more reach.

**AI as Sub-Mechanism, Not Brand [Nik Setting Anti-Pattern]:**

- Do NOT lead with "AI-powered coaching" in content or ads
- Lead with outcomes: "proprietary sourcing tools," "technology advantage," "systems others don't have"
- Reveal the AI tools AFTER they're in the program as a differentiator, not before

```
Foundational Campaigns:
  ├── Broad targeting (no interests -- let Meta optimize)
  ├── Interest stack (combined interests)

Testing Campaigns:
  └── New creatives tested here -> graduate winners to foundational

If-Then-That Scaling Logic:
  IF CPA < target     -> increase spend 20%
  IF CPA > 2x target for 3 days -> kill creative
  IF new creative > control      -> swap in

```

  - 7-8 figure founder posting content

| Element | How Sabbo Delivers |
|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | Repeatable growth engine generating qualified pipeline on autopilot |
| Perceived Likelihood | Case studies, operator model (not account manager), senior talent embedded |

### 3.3 Pricing Psychology

**The Pre-Price Frame [Hormozi]:**
> "Before I tell you the price, it's super expensive."

This creates perfect emotional accommodation:
- Rich people immediately think of what's expensive FOR THEM and anchor higher
- Everyone else braces and handles it better

**Close Rate Diagnostic [Hormozi]:**

| Current Close Rate | What It Means |
|---|---|
| 80%+ | Underpriced by 3-4x. Raise price immediately. |
| 60-80% | Underpriced by 2-3x. |
| 50-60% | Underpriced by 1.5-2x. |
| 40-50% | Underpriced by 1.25-1.5x. |
| 30-40% | Appropriately priced. |
| Below 30% | Fix selling or avatar, not price. |

**Action:** Track close rates for both offers. If above 40% on either, raise prices immediately.

**Three-Tier Presentation [Nik Setting]:**
- DIY ($3K) / DWY ($7K) / DFY ($15K)
- Gap between $3K and $7K is larger than $7K to $15K -- makes middle look like best value, DFY feels like a small upgrade
- Only market the DWY. Discover tier fit on the call.

**Upsell Structure [Hormozi]:**
- 5-10x price at each tier, expect 20% uptake
- $7K coaching -> $15K premium 1-on-1 (20% uptake) -> potential $50K+ portfolio partnership (4% uptake)
- Each tier roughly doubles total revenue

### 4.4 Close Rate Optimization

**Buyer Types [Jeremy Haynes]:**

| Type | Profile | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Type 1 | High-risk first mover. Already sold. | Court aggressively. Close fast. |
| Type 2 | Considered risk taker. Has questions but appetite. | Handle objections. Provide proof. |
| Type 3 | High-skepticism. Needs group-think, long cycle. | Back-end systems can convert. Be cautious. |
| Type 4 | Skeptic/cynic. Time vampire. | **Actively deter.** Do NOT waste time. |

> "Every dollar spent trying to win a Type 4 is a dollar not spent deepening conviction in a Type 2." [Jeremy Haynes]

**Handling Objections Through Content [SooWei]:**
> "Every objection on a sales call = a gap in your marketing. Create content that solves each common objection BEFORE the call."

| Common Objection | Content to Create |
|---|---|
| "I don't have enough capital" (coaching) | Case study: student who started with $5K |
| "What if products don't sell?" (coaching) | Show the AI sourcing pipeline and data-driven approach |
| "I've been burned by agencies before" (agency) | Video: "Why agencies fail (and what we do differently)" |
| "Can I do this myself?" (both) | Identify the ONE thing they can't solve alone |
| "It's too expensive" (both) | ROI case study showing 10x returns |

**The Consultative Close [Ben Bader]:**
> "Don't be Jordan Belfort with the script. Be casual, consultative. Just figure out what they want. Place it to them."

**Pre-Call Research [SooWei]:**
> "If they thought they were talking to you in the DMs, and you're clueless on the call, trust is broken."

Checklist before every call:
- [ ] Review their application answers
- [ ] Review their Instagram/social profile
- [ ] Review the DM conversation with setter
- [ ] Know their specific situation before they say a word

### 4.5 Sales Team Narrative Management

**Narrative Control [Jeremy Haynes]:**
> "If you tell them the leads are bad, they'll prove you right."

- Never tell sales team about lead quality tests
- Frame everything as opportunity
- If testing new messaging: tell them "these are premium leads"
- Self-fulfilling prophecy of "bad leads" destroys close rates

**Authority Transfer [Jeremy Haynes]:**
- Sabbo introduces closer by name in content
- Closer references Sabbo's specific teachings
- Group chat introductions (Sabbo introduces prospect to closer personally)

**Sales Team Culture [Ben Bader]:**
> "One offer went up 40% month-over-month with zero changes to funnel/ads -- just better team energy."

- Morning energy check-ins
- Cut underperformers fast
- Find A-players who take ownership

## Part 5: Fulfillment & Retention

### 5.1 Agency Delivery Model

**Apply Nik Setting's DWY Framework + Ben Bader's 8 Functions:**

```
Week 1-2: Installation Phase
  ├── Growth audit (offer, acquisition, sales, fulfillment chain analysis)
