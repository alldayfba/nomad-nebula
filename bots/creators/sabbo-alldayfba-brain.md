## 3. The Leverage Ladder (Sabbo's Core Framework)

The progression from beginner to advanced. Each level = more leverage, more scale, less time per dollar.

### Level 1: Retail Arbitrage (RA)
- Go to physical stores (Walmart, Target, CVS, Walgreens)
- Scan items with SellerAmp or Amazon Seller app
- Buy profitable items at clearance/discount prices
- Ship to Amazon FBA
- **Pro:** Lowest barrier to entry, immediate cash flow, learn the fundamentals
- **Con:** Most time-intensive, least scalable, physically limited
- **Who starts here:** Tier A beginners with <$5K capital

### Level 2: Online Arbitrage (OA)
- Same concept but from your laptop
- Browse retailer websites (Walmart.com, Target.com, Home Depot, etc.)
- Stack discounts: cashback + credit card rewards + coupons + gift cards
- Ship to prep center — never touch products
- **Pro:** More scalable than RA, can source from anywhere, automated tools available
- **Con:** Competition on popular deals, need tools (SellerAmp, Keepa), learning curve on data analysis
- **Sweet spot:** Where most students should aim to operate long-term
- **Key method:** Storefront Stalking (reverse-source from successful sellers)

### Level 3: Wholesale
- Find authorized distributors/wholesalers
- Buy branded products in bulk at 40-60% off retail
- Consistent supply, reorderable
- Example: Find Expo markers distributor in LA, buy in bulk, sell on Amazon
- **Pro:** Consistent inventory, better margins, reorderable, less competition than OA
- **Con:** Higher MOQs, need relationships, capital-intensive
- **How to find suppliers:** Trade shows, B2B directories, manufacturer websites, cold outreach

### Level 4: Brand Direct
- Contact brands directly: "Hey, we want to become direct brand partners"
- Best pricing, exclusive access
- Some brands aren't even on Amazon yet — opportunity to bring them on
- **Pro:** Best margins, exclusivity possible, long-term relationships
- **Con:** Hardest to get, requires business credibility, higher capital

### Level 5: Freelance Brand Scaling (SMMA Hybrid)
- Run Facebook/Meta ads for brands to sell their products on Amazon
- Get paid $4,000+ from brands to manage their advertising
- Brands with 680K Instagram followers but no Facebook ads = opportunity
- **Pro:** Recurring revenue, leverage brand's existing products, SMMA-like income
- **Con:** Client management, need ad skills, scope creep

### Level 6: Private Label
- Create your own brand
- Most risky but highest long-term value
- **Not Sabbo's primary model** — this is the endgame, not the starting point

### Key Insight
"99% of people don't even talk about this. The highest level of Amazon FBA has so many things." Most YouTube gurus only teach Level 1 or jump to Level 6. The middle levels (OA → Wholesale → Brand Direct) are where the real money is.

## 5. Product Sourcing Methodology

### Key Metrics (from OA_RA_Wholesale_Research.md)

**BSR (Best Sellers Rank):**
- Top 1%: Buy with confidence (very high turnover)
- Top 2%: Good buy, check competition
- Top 5%: Must be lowest price, few competitors
- Below top 10%: Generally avoid
- BSR under 30,000 stable for 90 days = strong indicator

**ROI Minimums:**
| Level | Minimum | Ideal |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner | 30% | 50%+ |
| Intermediate | 20-30% | 40%+ |
| Advanced | 15% | 30%+ |

**Profit Per Unit:** $3+ minimum (below this, one return wipes profit from 2-3 sales)

**Seller Count Sweet Spot:** 2-5 FBA sellers on the listing. 15+ = saturated. If Amazon is a seller = risky.

### Keepa Graph Reading
- Each BSR drop = at least 1 sale
- More drops = faster selling
- Look at 90-day BSR drops in Statistics tab
- Check if Buy Box price is stable or volatile
- Verify seller count trends (increasing = getting crowded)

### The 5 Core OA Strategies (2026)
1. **Multi-Pack Arbitrage** — Buy single units at retail, bundle into multi-packs that match Amazon listings. Brands: Crayola, Fujifilm, Convatec.
2. **Brand-Direct + Discount Code Stacking** — Buy from brand's own website + apply codes + cashback. Brands: Vitacost, Yankee Candle, Dr. Bronner's, Native.
3. **Specialty Retailer Moat** — Source from niche retailers competitors don't know about. Retailers: Lollicup (1883 syrups), Stylevana (rom&nd cosmetics), PetEdge (Andis clippers).
4. **FBM for Restricted/Fragrance/Hazmat** — Sell merchant-fulfilled for items that can't go FBA easily. Brands: Zara fragrances, MAC, Fenty, Jones Road Beauty.
5. **Seasonal Timing** — Q4 toys/candy, Back-to-School supplies, post-season clearance. Time buys to seasonal demand spikes.

### Brand Watchlist (Proven Winners)
- **S-tier:** Zara fragrances, 1883/Monin syrups, Convatec, rom&nd
- **A-tier:** Dr. Bronner's, MAC, Jellycat, Yankee Candle, Crayola

## 14. Additional Frameworks (From Video Deep-Dives)

### The Middleman Model (Core Mental Model)
"All you do with this business model is step in between supply and demand and make money in the middle." Amazon, Uber, Walmart, TikTok — all middlemen. You're doing the same thing at micro-scale. Amazon solves the traffic problem that killed Sabbo's Shopify stores.

### 4-Step Amazon System (Simplified Pitch)
1. Find profitable products (tools: Seller Central + SellerAmp + Keepa)
2. Buy products for low, resell for profit (OA, RA, Wholesale)
3. Fulfill products (FBA vs FBM vs Prep Center)
4. Rinse, repeat, scale to 6-7 figures

### Google Maps Wholesale Method
Search "[category] wholesaler" on Google Maps by city/state → find verified warehouses with real distribution centers → contact directly for wholesale accounts. Combined with SmartScout supplier discovery (filter by state → city → category → validate on Google Maps).

### Scan Unlimited Workflow (Wholesale Sheet Scanning)
Upload supplier UPC spreadsheet → auto-match to Amazon ASINs → filter by: sales rank < 200K, offers >= 3, min profit >= $2, ROI >= 25%, price >= $13. Out of 2,000 products, typically ~16 meet strict criteria. AI version (Claude Code) does this in 5-10 minutes vs. days manually.

### Growth Arbitrage Model (Advanced — Level 5+)
Instead of just reselling, partner with brands as their growth operator:
1. Find brands via Keepa Product Finder / SmartScout (validate total products + sales ranks)
2. Identify growth gaps: bad photos, no descriptions, garbage website, no ads
3. Contact directly — present strategic solutions (Zoom, email, phone)
4. Install growth engine: Facebook ads, funnels, email, SMS, website, Amazon optimization
- Revenue: Amazon sales + ad management retainer ($4K-$5K/mo) + rev share
- Ungating solved: direct brand partnerships = letters of authorization
- "If you do good business for one brand, you're going to get about four or five more knocking at your door"

### FBA vs FBM Decision Matrix
- Check buy box statistics per product to see FBM seller rotation
- Q4 (Oct-Dec): FBM preferred (Amazon warehouses packed, FBM = same-day sales)
- Rest of year: Product-dependent — FBA for most, FBM when FBM sellers are getting buy box share
- FBM useful for restricted categories (fragrance, hazmat) where FBA is blocked

### Variation Analysis Method
Use Keepa ratings percentage to determine which product variation actually sells. Example: If main bottle gets 67% of sales and gallon gets 1 review — don't sell the gallon. Always check which variation moves before buying.

### Product Stability Indicator
Look for products where offer count goes from 40 to 70 sellers and the price does NOT move. That = stability = winner. If price crashes when sellers increase = fragile listing, avoid.

### SKU Naming Convention
`ProductName_Source_Cost_SellingPrice_Date` (e.g., "Dryel_Walmart_10_25_103125") — keeps inventory organized and traceable.

### Revenue Math: $10K/Month Profit
To make $10K/month profit at 30% margins: need ~$31K/month in inventory spend (~$1K/day deployed). This frames the capital requirement clearly for students.

### SmartScout Supplier Discovery (Detailed)
SmartScout → Suppliers tab → Filter by state → Filter by city → Filter by category. Example: California → San Diego → 67 suppliers → Candy → 4 suppliers. Copy address → Google Maps → verify real warehouse. Check website for B2B login, wholesale accounts, contact forms.
