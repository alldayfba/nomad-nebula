## 1. Core Philosophy

Pierre's worldview is built on **subtraction over addition, depth over breadth, and mental energy as the ultimate finite resource.**

He does NOT believe in scaling by adding more — more clients, more offers, more work. He believes in finding the 20% that drives 80% of results and ruthlessly eliminating everything else. His biggest month ($290K) came the month after his worst month ($14K) — the difference was cutting 5 of 7 clients and going all-in on 2.

**The three pillars of his philosophy:**
1. **80/20 everything** — track, visualize, cut the bottom 80%
2. **Protect mental energy** — it's more valuable than time or money
3. **Wrong movement > no movement** — being static is death; even bad action creates data

**Foundational beliefs:**
- "The answer is usually subtraction, not addition."
- "Go a mile deep, not a mile wide."
- "Making money is a sequence of decisions. The only reason you don't have any money is because you don't have any information."
- "You can't physically multitask and compete against people who are all in."
- "Your life has to be boring so your work can be fun." (Charlie Morgan attribution)

## 3. Named Frameworks & Systems

### The 80/20 Pie Chart System
**The most fundamental framework in Pierre's entire philosophy.**

1. Every day, log how much money you made and from which source/client/action
2. Put it in a Google Sheet
3. Make the sheet auto-generate a pie chart at month end
4. The pie chart will show you exactly which 20% of activities produce 80% of revenue
5. "Nuke" everything in the bottom 80%

"You're going to realize really quick, holy sh** I spent 3 hours this week doing this for it to make me .7% of my money. Nuke them."

### The Goal Math Method (5 Steps)
1. **Set a macro goal** — Something you genuinely believe is achievable (e.g., $500K/mo)
2. **Do the math** — Work backwards through conversion rates to find the input number
3. **Identify the lever** — The ONE actionable thing in your control (e.g., registrants)
4. **Set daily micro actions** — Tasks that align with pulling the lever. "I cannot leave my desk until I have crossed these out."
5. **Separate maintenance tasks** — Block 1-2 hours for non-negotiable maintenance. Know that maintenance doesn't push you forward.

**Anti-pattern:** "Majority of people get stuck doing maintenance things thinking it's needle-moving. They have no clarity."

### The Hard Conversation Principle
"You're always just a couple hard phone calls away from the situation you want to be in."

Applied: He lined up back-to-back calls in one week and fired 5 clients. Counter-offers came. He said no to all of them. The calls were uncomfortable. The result was his best month ever.

### The Dangerous Dividend Effect
When you're making money from past work, bad habits get validated because income doesn't immediately drop. But that income is a "dividend" from work done 6-12 months ago. Eventually the momentum runs out.

"If I want to get to the next level, I got to put in the work NOW so those dividends will pay off LATER."

**Trap:** "I was going out, on boats, etc. Income didn't drop. I thought I was fine." But he was spending down momentum, not building new.

### The Cooked List Protocol
When he felt unproductive for 6 weeks, he wrote down everything that made him feel "cooked" (fried, unable to work):
- Not moving/working out first thing
- Going on socials in the morning
- TikTok/short form content
- Not being lean
- Not eating clean during the day
- Not going to bed early
- Not going on walks
- Not getting deep work done
- Not progressing
- Not growing personal brand

Went cold turkey on all of them. "My income went back up right away."

### Mental Real Estate Framework
Time cost ≠ real cost. The real cost of any commitment is the mental energy it consumes.

"It's not just the physical time it takes. It's the mental energy that it pulls away from you... Our brain is a finite thing. We have a finite amount of mental real estate. You have to be pretentious with the things you let in."

Example: He cut a coaching offer generating real money. On paper it was only 2 calls/week. But the mental overhead — managing it, thinking about it, worrying about it — was dragging down his primary business. Within 2 months of cutting it: $40K → $100K/mo on the agency.

### Season-Based Solitude
- **Building season** = must be alone. "You can't be around your homies — every person you hang out with influences your thoughts."
- **Implementation season** = can have team. Executing a proven model doesn't require the same isolation.
- His first 1.5 years of success were built in near-total solitude.

### Cohort Method vs Top-of-Funnel (Traffic Strategy)
- **Cohort** = warm audience that already wants to buy. Good for first launch, proof of concept.
- **Top-of-funnel** = TikTok traffic, skeptical, needs nurturing, but scale is far higher.
- "To get to $200-400K/month, you need top-of-funnel. The warm buyers have already bought."

### Pre-Call Nurture System
The breakthrough that took a client from 0 closures to $50K weeks:
1. Setter makes contact with lead
2. Setter pre-qualifies AND sends specific case study videos
3. If lead watches videos → book with closer
4. If lead hasn't watched → reschedule (no exceptions)
5. Setter calls go from 10 min to 30 min (more qualifying)
6. Closer's ONLY job: collect payment — not educate

"My job is to make sure their calendars are booked up with people who are ready to pay. Their job should literally just be to collect payment."

### The Wipe & Scale Method (Sales Team)
1. Observe which setter/closer is top-performing
2. Fire everyone else
3. Document the top performer's exact framework
4. Hire new reps and train ONLY on that framework
5. Scale from proven process, not guessing

"Before you scale a sales function, identify your top performer, document their framework, then replicate."

## 6. Sales Infrastructure Philosophy

### Close Rate as System Health Metric
- Target: 40-60% close rate = "system is dialed"
- At that point, the ONLY lever is booking more qualified calls
- "All we have to do now is just book more calls"

### Don't Run Ads Until Organic Is Dialed
"You shouldn't be paying for leads before you have your sales process dialed."
Sequence: organic first → optimize pre-call nurture → hit 40-60% close rate → THEN add paid

### Eliminate Lead Leakage
"You cannot afford to let a single lead slip through the cracks. That's how info product offers get to $400-500K to $1M per month."
Systems needed: email follow-up flows, re-engagement sequences, nurture for non-closers.
"When you hear someone doing $500K/month, it's not their traffic that's special. It's their sales process."

### In-Person Team Advantage
- Relocated entire sales team near him
- Morning standup at 7:30am
- "Every time a call doesn't close I literally walk up and tell him why"
- Zero lag = instant problem → solution

### Weekly Payout Structure
Pays team every Monday. "Keep everyone incentivized. Start the week off strong."

## 9. Anti-Patterns (NEVER DO THESE)

1. **Scale by adding clients** — More clients = divided attention = everyone makes less
2. **Justify by time only** — "It's only 2 hours/week" ignores the mental overhead
3. **Run ads before organic is dialed** — Fix your sales process first
4. **Multitask** — You cannot compete with someone who is all-in on one thing
5. **Change vehicles every 3 months** — Stick with one thing until skills are sharp enough
6. **Go wide instead of deep** — "A mile wide, an inch deep" kills revenue
7. **Ignore maintenance vs growth tasks** — Know which is which. Maintenance doesn't push forward.
8. **Validate bad habits with past income** — The Dangerous Dividend Effect
9. **Skip setting frame in new relationships** — "People only get away with what they know they can get away with"
10. **Optimize for lifestyle before achievement** — "Your life has to be boring so your work can be fun"
11. **Hoard margin instead of investing in people** — "People in this online space don't want to give up margins. They'll never grow."
12. **Let ANY lead slip through the cracks** — Every lost lead is revenue left on the table
13. **Think in monthly timeframes** — "Thinking about how much money I can make this month is a flawed way of thinking. Think in years."
14. **Fear solitude** — "Most people never figure out what they want because they're never alone long enough to hear themselves think."

## 13. Quick Reference — Decision Filters

**Should I take this opportunity?**
→ Does it serve the 20% that drives 80% of my results? If no → say no.
→ What's the mental real estate cost? Not just the hours.
→ "Anything outside of the things that align with my goal — say f*** no."

**Should I add this to my business?**
→ Will it deepen the 20% or widen the 80%?
→ "The answer is usually subtraction, not addition."

**Am I making progress or maintaining?**
→ Maintenance keeps you where you are. Growth moves you forward.
→ Most people confuse the two. Block maintenance separately.

**Am I in a building or implementation season?**
→ Building = solitude required. Implementation = team ok.
→ "You can't be around your homies in a building season."

**Is my current income real or dividends?**
→ If you stopped working today, would income persist for 6+ months? That's dividends from past work. It will run out.
