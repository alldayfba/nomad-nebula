## 2. Named Frameworks

### The PLAN Framework (Business Selection)
- **P** = Problem — Does it solve a real need someone pays cash for?
- **L** = Landscape — Is the market growing? Will it exist in 5-10 years?
- **A** = Alignment — Can you do this for 5-10 years without burning out?
- **N** = Natural Advantages — Do you have domain expertise, access, or knowledge others don't?

### Category of One (from Peter Thiel)
Niche down until you have zero competition:
- Marketing agency → too broad
- Marketing agency in Arizona → still broad
- Marketing agency in Phoenix for dental clinics → getting there
- TikTok ads agency in Phoenix for dental clinics doing $50K+/mo → **category of one**

### The 3S Offer Framework (via Spencer Van)
- **Specific Person** — exactly who you serve
- **Specific Problem** — exactly what pain they have
- **Specific Mechanism** — your unique method/system

Plus: Hard promise > Soft promise. Tie to money. Make deliverable tangible.

### The Competitor Landscape Report
Before launching, analyze top 3-5 competitors on: pricing, brand, target demo, product structure. Find holes. Pick differentiation lever: brand/design, marketing angle, product focus, or pricing.

### Dollar-Per-Hour Opportunity Cost
If Business A pays 2x but requires 4x the time, your $/hr is the same. Could that 4x time go into Business B for more than 2x return? If yes → cut Business A.

### The 9-Step Millionaire Blueprint
1. Live lean — eliminate golden handcuffs
2. Build a skill to top 10% (not top 1%)
3. Build V1 offer (copy proven, add your flavor)
4. Get testimonials (give away free, ask for testimonial as "payment")
5. Make first sale (network, FB groups, in-person)
6. Improve product + raise prices on repeat (20-30 iterations)
7. Hit plateau → pivot to scalable model (1-on-1 → group/online)
8. Master ONE marketing channel — paid OR organic, not both
9. Run lean team — under $1M/mo, focus on profitability

### Detachment Framework — 6 Hard Truths
1. Let go of the version of yourself that needs control
2. What you obsess over most slips away
3. Multiply effort through leverage, not personal effort
4. Letting go forces you to discover identity beyond the grind
5. Focus on inputs daily; outcomes take care of themselves
6. "My most profitable year was the year I focused on it the least — because I had the right people"

### The J-Curve Pivot
When transitioning business models, revenue drops temporarily. Make the J shallow by moving aggressively, not slowly. Have 6-12 months war chest.

## 3. Paid Ads Frameworks (from Spencer Van interview)

### Offer Sentence Template
One sentence with three components:
1. What do you do?
2. What results do you get?
3. What's the risk reversal?

### Ad Structure (First-Time Framework)
1. **Call out** — literally say who you're targeting
2. **Offer** — one sentence (the offer sentence)
3. **Proof/authority** — "after helping X people with Y problem..."
4. **CTA** — book a call / click below

### Five Stages of Market Awareness
Unaware → Problem Aware → Solution Aware → Product Aware → Most Aware
"All markets are all stages at once. You're choosing which to speak to."

### The Parade Market Analogy
Market = parade (constant flow), not lake (static). People constantly enter/leave. New creative = new billboard for new parade-goers.

### Creative Fatigue Thresholds
| Ad Spend | New Creative Needed |
|---|---|
| <$100K/mo | Don't worry about it |
| $100-300K/mo | 1-2 new ads/month |
| $300-600K/mo | 2-3 new variations/WEEK |
| $600K+/mo | Full testing protocols, cost-cap campaigns |

### Cost Cap Testing Protocol (Advanced)
Launch new creatives in CBO cost cap at 70-75% of current average CPL. Only ads that spend at reduced bid are likely winners. Pull out winners, launch normally. Spencer ran $43K/day with 1.5 media buyers using this.

### VSSL Call Funnel Is Permanent
Tested 18 different funnels over years. Always came back to VSL → call. "What's physically stopping it from going to $100M/year? Nothing. Your ability to operate it is the only constraint."

### Bisop Targeting Rule
- More passive the opportunity → higher up-market you can go
- Active income replacement (coaching) → blue collar / wage workers have more urgency
- Passive / asset-building → white collar / investors respond better
- DFY version of same offer = higher close rate + higher price + better clients

### Anti-Hook Pattern
Say something controversial but true. "I quit my business, got a job, and now I make more money." Completely backwards to everything else in the market → attention grab → same core message underneath.

## 4. Sales Team Management

### The Trifecta (from Cole Gordon's $120K program)
1. **Daily call coaching** — review every call
2. **Daily team meetings** — motivation + accountability + numbers
3. **Weekly one-on-ones** — mindset + technical + push deals across the line

### Scaling Stages
- $0-100K/mo: Founder closes everything
- $100-300K/mo: 1-2 closers, founder manages directly
- $300K+/mo: Founder goes FULL TIME sales manager (not on calls)
- Don't hire a sales manager until you can afford a great one

### Don't Let Closers Change Your Offer
Closers will sell you on changing your targeting because they don't want hard calls. Resist. If your product helps broke people become un-broke, you're going to talk to broke people. That's the business.

### AI Sales QC System
Fathom (call recording) → Zapier → Custom GPT trained on sales script → Slack message tagging closer → Closer responds with what they'll fix. Every call reviewed. Better than human QC.

### Post-Sale AI Feedback Loop
2-3x/week: pull sales call transcripts → Gemini/Claude → "How aligned are callers with our ideal avatar?" → adjust messaging. Result: average booked call went from $25K/mo revenue clients to $300K/mo revenue clients in 60 days.

## 5. Hiring Philosophy

### People Gap = #1 Business Plateau
"I counted six people in Cole's room who could each scale an 8-figure company. How many did I have? Zero except me."

### Talent Acquisition
- Do 20-30 interviews per key role (most founders do 3)
- Write ideal job description FIRST based on KPIs
- Don't hire the person who sells you — salespeople are salespeople
- Brand makes hiring easier exactly like it makes selling easier

### Copywriter Hiring
- "Almost all of them suck. Don't believe a word they say."
- Trial contract: 3 ads, pay regardless, provide research + winning controls + avatar
- If they get to 70-80%, you add founder sauce for the last 20-30%
- Email copywriting ≠ cold ad copywriting ≠ VSSL copywriting (all different skills)
