## 2. Core Philosophy

### Operators > Account Managers
The fundamental thesis: agencies fail because they hire account managers who manage. DTCMA hires operators who own outcomes. Operators have skin in the game — they treat client businesses like their own money is on the line. Performance-only compensation reinforces this.

### The Agency Model Is Dead
Direct quote: "Owning an agency and calling my company an agency is cringe." Big brands consistently churn agencies that over-promise and under-deliver. The future is AI OS + operator oversight + performance compensation.

### AI Execution + Human Strategy
"The revenue model behind RunAds.ai is not just having an AI agent autonomously run ads with employees acting as prompt managers." The model: AI handles execution velocity (24/7 monitoring, bid optimization, budget allocation). Humans handle strategy, creative direction, and edge cases.

## 3. Frameworks

### 3.1 — DTCMA 4-Step Methodology

**Step 1 — Application & Evaluation**
Selective intake. Decline clients who don't fit.

**Step 2 — Full Teardown**
Rip apart every marketing channel — ads, organic, email, attribution. No assumptions, no politics, just data and the truth about where money is going.

**Step 3 — Strategy & Forecasting**
Revenue-forecasted roadmap built using internal tools trained on $4.5B+ in real operational data. Not theoretical models.

**Step 4 — Execute, Scale, Dominate**
Deploy campaigns, iterate with continuous testing, scale winners.

### 3.2 — Google Ads: Performance Max Structure

**Critical rule: Brand terms MUST be excluded from Performance Max.**
- Known failure: clients have burned $250K/month by including brand terms in PMax
- Why: Brand terms have high ROAS → PMax optimizes toward them → non-brand budget wasted → new customer acquisition collapses
- When exclusions applied: +5% new customer acquisition, -7% acquisition cost, +31% ROAS improvement

**Campaign architecture:**
- Feed-only PMax campaign = primary shopping campaign (20-40% of total spend)
- Separate branded search campaigns (never mix with non-branded)
- Non-branded search campaigns for intent capture
- YouTube for awareness and retargeting

### 3.3 — Scaling Philosophy

- Scale immediately when new creatives outperform or match existing winners
- Maintain 8-12+ active variations per campaign
- Refresh 25-30% of creative library monthly
- 20-30% of creative production should test outside current winning formula
- New creative duplicates: assign 50-70% of original budget to avoid cannibalization

### 3.4 — Commercial Model

- Performance guarantee: no results = no payment
- No long-term contracts
- No excuses model: if they can't beat the client's current team, client doesn't pay
- Most clients see measurable improvement within 30-60 days
