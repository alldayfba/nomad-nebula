## 2. Named Frameworks & Mechanisms

### The Founder Workflow
The framework for operating as a CEO rather than an employee in your own business.

**Core principle:** Maximum output for minimum input (leverage).

**Two workflow modes:**
1. **Manager Workflow** (Mon/Wed/Fri): Time is currency. Stack calls back-to-back from 1pm-7pm. No gaps. Client check-ins, team calls, sales calls all compressed.
2. **Leader Workflow** (Tue/Thu/Sat): Zero calls. Deep work only. Content creation, fulfillment improvements, learning, strategy. This is the highest-ROI time.

**Daily structure:**
- 9am-12pm: Deep work on content (the #1 lever) -- blocked off every single day
- 12pm-1pm: Handle fires, reply to clients, admin
- 1pm-7pm: Calls stacked back-to-back (manager days) or deep work (leader days)
- Sundays: MANDATORY off day. Reset. Reflect on the week. Plan next week.

**Key rules:**
- Never scatter calls throughout the day
- Never have that "I have a call in 3 hours" feeling
- Protect your calendar like it's your most valuable asset
- "If you can't go 3 days without working in the business, you're working on the wrong shit."
- Detach your dopamine from hours worked -- attach it to output

### Ethos-Pathos-Logos Framework
The three pillars of a strong personal brand. You need ALL three to make $100K/mo.

1. **Ethos (Character/Credibility):** Why should anyone listen to you? Time in the game, investment in knowledge, financial results. "I've been in business since I was 16. I'm 20, so I've been doing this for 5 years."
2. **Logos (Proof):** Can you do it for OTHER people? Case studies, client results, associations. "I can do it. But can I do it for other people?"
3. **Pathos (Emotion/Connection):** Do they feel connected to you? Your story, your beliefs, your personality. The most important of the three.

**The Best Friend Analogy:** When you had your first breakup, you didn't ask a therapist (expert). You asked your best friend (connection). Connection > expertise. "Technically your best friend has no damn experience. He had one girlfriend. But you asked him because you felt like he understood you."

**Diagnosis:** If you're not making 100K/mo, you probably have 1 or 2 of 3 but not all 3. Figure out which one you're lacking and double down.

### The Five Stages of Awareness (Applied)
SooWei's application of Eugene Schwartz's awareness levels:
1. **Unaware** -- doesn't know they have a problem (top-of-funnel content)
2. **Problem Aware** -- knows they have a problem (top-of-funnel content)
3. **Solution Aware** -- knows solutions exist (middle-of-funnel content begins)
4. **Product Aware** -- knows your specific solution (middle-of-funnel content)
5. **Most Aware** -- ready to buy (CTAs, urgency, price increases)

**Top-of-funnel:** Move people from unaware to problem aware. Brand awareness, resonance, likability.
**Middle-of-funnel:** Move people from solution aware to most aware. Brand credibility, problem awareness, trust.

### ITF Tracker (Inability To Follow Through)
- Track every single time you say you'll do something but don't.
- "The universe counts every single time that you say you're going to do something but don't do it."
- Each broken promise erodes self-trust.
- Use a simple notepad on your phone. Note every instance.
- Build the muscle of following through on small commitments to enable following through on big ones.

### The Dream Follower Concept
- Don't make content for your ICP. Make content for your "dream follower."
- Dream follower = someone beyond your ICP that you want to connect with on a deeper personal level.
- The ICP is always a subset of the dream follower audience.
- Dream follower targeting gives you more Total Addressable Market (TAM) on social media.
- The ICP will naturally appear within the broader dream follower audience.
- "If you're only targeting coaches making 3-20K/mo, that TAM is not good enough to scale."

### The 90-Day Business Cycle
- "We're in a 90-day business. The things you do now won't lead to the results in a few days or a few weeks. It's in 90 days."
- Everything you do today compounds and shows results ~90 days later.
- This is why long-term thinking matters and why you can't judge a month by a single month's effort.

### Urgency-Pain-Scarcity Triangle (Appointment Setting)
The three things that must be present for a prospect to book a call:
1. **Urgency** -- they need to feel this can't wait
2. **Pain** -- they need to feel the cost of inaction
3. **Scarcity** -- they need to feel this opportunity is limited

"When you propose the call, these clients should feel like they are getting an opportunity of a lifetime. They cannot fuck this up and not book in."

### The Curiosity vs. Value Diagnostic
If your content isn't working, it's one of two problems:
1. **Curiosity Problem:** People don't care enough about you to listen. (Fix: top-of-funnel content showing your desired reality)
2. **Value Problem:** Your information isn't good enough or it's regurgitated. (Fix: expand your knowledge, say things only you can say)

"If you have 100 people watching a value video and you get zero calls from it, that means you're not saying anything profound."

### The Packaging Principle
"What's the difference between a Gucci bag and an H&M bag? The packaging."
- The value/content itself is largely the same across competitors.
- What separates you is HOW you package and position it.
- Example: Don't say "how to lose fat" -- say "my 3-hour/week fitness system for busy men."
- The packaging must be tailored to your ICP's language and desires.
- "You can only solve so many fitness problems. Someone 100 years ago solved the same problem. It's all about packaging."

### BAMF (Book A Meeting From A Meeting)
- After every client interaction, the next meeting should already be scheduled.
- Sales call -> onboarding call -> creative director call -> sales guy call -> group call.
- "There always has to be a meeting from a meeting."
- This builds conviction and ensures clients feel cared for continuously.

### The Competitive Advantage Flywheel
Content -> Premium Brand -> Higher Prices -> More Cash Flow -> Better Talent -> Better Fulfillment -> Better Results -> More Proof -> Better Content -> ...

"If you can charge 4x what your competitors are making, you can outspend them in every aspect. You can hire better closers, better setters, better editors, better fulfillment."

## 3. Strategies & SOPs

### Appointment Setting Process (Complete SOP)

**Phase 1: Setter Onboarding (First 48 hours)**
1. **Training:** Full appointment setting course (5+ hours, 14+ lessons), plus advanced DM setting module (1 hour), plus phone setting/triage module
2. **Alignment:** Setter must fully understand the offer, the ICP, and the business mission. Not just money -- they need a why.
3. **Support:** Loom breakdowns of all systems, direct access to head coach
4. **Testing:** Quiz (must score 90%+ in 2 attempts) + niche-specific mock call with head coach who approves/rejects

**Phase 2: KPI Alignment**
- Set KPIs that are mutually beneficial (setter hits his goals = business hits its goals)
- Key metric: New Conversation to Qualified Call ratio
  - Above 20% = need more lead flow (content/ads problem)
  - 5-15% = appointment setting problem (fix the conversations)
- Track in Looker Studio: new convos, qualified calls, follow-ups, warm outbound, booking rate
- Break monthly targets into weekly and daily goals
- Example reverse engineering: 100K/mo goal -> 10 closes -> 40% close rate = 25 calls -> 85% show rate = 29 booked calls -> 20% convo-to-qualified = 147 new conversations per week

**Phase 3: Ongoing Management**
- Daily KPI tracking and accountability
- Weekly team calls showing each department's performance
- Setter ascension path (to manager/closer as they grow)
- MVP voting form: anonymous monthly vote, $1K bonus + public recognition

**Key Principles:**
- Setters are usually beginners -- invest heavily in training
- Never use scripts -- build foundations so conversations are natural
- "Look at appointment setting as fun. I was texting my boys."
- Have notifications on throughout the day; respond quickly to hot leads
- Structure: initial outreach is dedicated time, but follow-ups happen organically throughout the day

### Outreach & DM Strategy
- Don't brute force 500+ DMs per day. Quality over quantity.
- Reach out to 10-20 super qualified prospects rather than 50 random ones.
- Personalized openers based on the prospect's content/profile
- For insecure niches (dating, fitness): compliment first, build rapport, then transition to business
  - Example for fitness coaches: "Yo, your physique is insane. What's your workout split?"
  - For Asian prospects: "Are you Asian? What type of Asian are you?" (SooWei's signature opener)
- Track reply rates religiously -- most people don't, and it's "terrible"
- The opener is the most important variable. Fix that before anything else.
- Instagram grabs attention -> funnel to YouTube for long-term trust -> re-engage in DMs to book calls

**The Ideal Organic Funnel:**
1. Instagram (top-of-funnel) -> grabs attention, builds curiosity

"The sales call should be the easiest part. If your marketing is good enough, you don't even need sales calls."

## 5. Buyer Psychology & Sales Insights

### Why People Buy
1. **Connection over expertise** -- they buy from people they feel understood by (the best friend analogy)
2. **Desired reality** -- they see you living the life they want and want the path
3. **Proof** -- undeniable results that they can see themselves achieving
4. **Urgency** -- a reason to act NOW rather than "next month"
5. **Uniqueness** -- something about you that no competitor has

### The Buying Journey
- Most prospects need multiple touchpoints before buying
- Some buy after one piece of content that hits hard (e.g., "called me a fat fuck and I liked it")
- Others need months of nurturing
- You must accommodate both types without defaulting to the "quick close" mentality
- "Increase the duration of the buyer's journey and actually provide more resources."

### Handling Objections Through Content
- Every objection on a sales call = a gap in your marketing
- Create content that solves each common objection BEFORE the sales call
- Goal: "Zero objections on sales calls because every limiting belief has been handled in the content."
- Example objections -> content pieces:
  - "I don't have time" -> client interview of a busy CEO who only had 2 hours/week
  - "I don't trust the fulfillment" -> show client DM response times, accountability tracking
  - "It's too expensive" -> case study of ROI, show 10x returns
  - "I can do this myself" -> identify the problem they CAN'T solve alone and center the offer around that

### The Awareness Gap
- "Make them problem aware AFTER they buy, not before."
- Don't lead with the boring technical solution (e.g., "appointment setting"). Lead with the sexy outcome (e.g., "sign 5 clients this month without running ads").
- "If I sold my main mechanism as appointment setting, people wouldn't buy. It's boring."
- Package it as "sales process" or "systems" -- then once they're in the program, reveal the real lever.

### Sales Call Insights
- 75% close rate at $7,668 AOV
- George (closer) has 90%+ close rate
- Key: Pre-call research. Review the prospect's account, content, DM conversation.
- "They thought they were talking to you on that DM. So you have to make sure you understand what your setter did."
- Show you care and came prepared -- "This guy came prepared" = instant trust
- Closer is part of fulfillment: they don't close people they can't help

### Social Proof Mechanics
- "People are sheep. Show that people are interested. Show that people are in your DMs. Show that people are paying you."
- When you show demand, prospects ask themselves: "Why are people paying this guy?" Then they find the reason and want in.
- Show packed calendar, client results, DM conversations (with permission)
- Price increase campaigns create artificial scarcity and urgency

### The "Moment of Greatest Satisfaction" Rule
- Greatest satisfaction = ask for a REFERRAL
- Greatest pain = ask for an UPSELL/RENEWAL
- Timing is everything in the client lifecycle
- Got ~5 referrals per month organically from satisfied clients

## 6. Content & Marketing Strategy

### Content Format Tier List (S to D)

**S Tier:**
- **Mastermind recordings:** Subconscious authority from speaking in front of crowds. "Humans subconsciously group together as crowds. When people see a crowd listening to you, they give you more respect." Led to tens of thousands in sales every time.
- **Storytelling reels:** Scripted, intentional documentation (e.g., surprising parents for Christmas = 600K views). "As authentic as it looks, I actually scripted out that entire reel."
- **Scripted talking head:** Direct-to-camera with a pre-planned script. Best for beginners. Forces you to master hooks and fundamentals.

**A Tier:**
- **Highlights (long-form clips):** Raw, authentic clips from long-form conversations. Unparalleled authenticity. Depends on editor quality and your ability to create interest peaks.
- **Client montages/interviews:** Lowest-performing in views, highest-converting for warm leads. "It's going to convert the most amount of leads possible."

**B Tier:**
- **Transformations (before/after):** Goes viral easily but attracts unqualified prospects. "You have to play the volume game and your setters will face a lot of unqualified leads."
- **Diagrams/charts:** Shows authority and composition. Great conversion but hard to produce.
- **Podcasts:** Professional, builds trust. Highly variable -- depends on host quality.

**C Tier:**
- **Green screen:** Doesn't look authoritative. Better to do split-screen (visual on top, you on bottom with good lighting).
- **Voice-overs:** Stimulating but hard to film consistently. Need lots of B-roll.

**D Tier:**
- **Trending audio + caption:** "Probably the stupidest format you could ever rip." No selling power, no brand building.
- **Street interviews:** Very B2C, brain rot. "Conversion is almost literally zero."

### Top-of-Funnel vs. Middle-of-Funnel Content

**Top-of-Funnel (TOF):**
- Purpose: Force people to listen to you. Build reach and curiosity.
- Show your DESIRED REALITY that prospects want.
- Target dream followers (broader than ICP).
- Examples: telling parents how much you make (1M+ views), lifestyle content, transformation arcs, day-in-the-life
- "You have to focus on your influence and reach before you do anything."
- "If you have a strong message but five people are looking at your content, it doesn't matter."

**Middle-of-Funnel (MOF):**
- Purpose: Build credibility, solve objections, create trust.
- Target ICP specifically.
- Examples: client breakdowns, system walkthroughs, specific problem solutions, one-on-one coaching recordings
- "Middle-of-funnel content doesn't have to be content that doesn't perform well. You just have to find the right packaging."
- Key insight: "Middle-of-funnel content still needs to start from market research."

### The "My Story" Video Strategy
- Massively effective for going viral + building pathos
- CRITICAL: Be intentional about which struggles you include
- "If you mention you were broke, you'll attract broke people."
- SooWei never mentioned being broke, lazy, or skinny in his story video
- Instead: "I was stuck at 10K/mo and was stagnant" -- a struggle his ICP relates to
- "The problems in your story must be applicable to problems your client is facing RIGHT NOW."
- Wrong: "I had nothing, I was desperate" -> attracts desperate people
- Right: "I was stuck at a level I knew I could surpass" -> attracts stuck but capable people

### Content Production Math
- Track: Qualified followers per day (not just new followers)
- Track: UTM links on every YouTube video to know which video drove which sale
- Track: Views-to-inbound-lead ratio per format

## 8. Anti-Patterns

### What NOT to Do

**Content Anti-Patterns:**
1. **Posting for the sake of posting.** "You're doing the input but you don't realize that input leads to nothing. You're better off doing nothing."
2. **Volume without intentionality.** "Fitness coaches always preach volume, but volume is nothing without intentionality."
3. **Recycling the same content.** "I'm just recycling the same shit" -- if you feel this, you need new angles from market research, not more of the same.
4. **Making content FOR your ICP only.** Target dream followers (broader). The ICP is a subset.
5. **Only posting value content.** Value without curiosity = nobody watches. Give them a REASON to listen first.
6. **Copying competitor formats blindly.** "Nothing stopped my competitors from copying my value. I had a value brand, not a personal brand."
7. **10+ Instagram stories per day.** Too many stories = easily skippable = algorithm buries you. Fewer, intentional stories.
8. **Sending one generic YouTube video to all DM leads.** Send specific videos for specific problems.
9. **Including "broke" struggles in your story video.** You'll attract broke people. Include struggles your ICP relates to.

**Business Anti-Patterns:**
1. **Being the hamster wheel.** If you disappear for a week, does the business collapse? If yes, you're a glorified employee.
2. **Tying dopamine to hours worked.** "Too many of you are complete slaves to your to-do list."
3. **Working on Sundays.** "That's exactly why you guys are not making the money you want to make." Sundays = reset + reflect.
4. **Scattering calls throughout the day.** Stack them. Back-to-back. No gaps.
5. **Doing one-to-one tasks as a founder.** Sales calls, appointment setting, editing -- these are one-to-one tasks. Content and fulfillment are one-to-infinity.
6. **Spending on lifestyle before reinvesting.** Cars, watches, penthouses before you've reinvested in talent, courses, mentorship, ads.
7. **Having two offers at different price points.** Remove the cheaper one. Marketing gets simpler, AOV goes up.
8. **Relying on Affirm/Klarna for cash collection.** It masks a marketing problem. "Subconsciously, you're ignoring that marketing problem because there's a vehicle that allows you to keep doing what you're doing."
9. **Not tracking metrics.** "You don't even know if you're getting enough qualified leads. You don't know if it's the conversation itself."
10. **Tolerating mediocrity on your team.** "If you tolerate mediocrity and you don't punish that, they're going to fall to mediocrity."

**Sales Anti-Patterns:**
1. **Trying to book calls as fast as possible.** "It's not about how do I book them in as fast as possible. It's how do I make them realize there's so much pain by the time I send a link, they have no choice but to book."
2. **Not doing pre-call research.** "If they thought they were talking to you in the DMs, and you're clueless on the call, trust is broken."
3. **Making bold claims/guarantees.** "I never make claims. I show results and let the client decide."
4. **Launching a consulting business at 25K/mo.** "We don't want to be like most guys who get to 25K and start consulting. We want 100K+ as leverage."

**Mindset Anti-Patterns:**
1. **Comparing yourself to others.** "Comparison will strip away every bit of fulfillment in your entire business."
2. **Complacency at 10-50K/mo.** "The 10 to 50K mark is the complete swamp. You're doing the most amount of work for the least return."
3. **Searching for the perfect plan.** "Consistent execution of a reasonable plan is so much more effective than constantly searching for a perfect one."
4. **Not wanting it bad enough.** "If you wanted it bad enough, you'd figure it out."
