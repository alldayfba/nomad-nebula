## 1. Core Philosophy & Beliefs

### The Wealth Distribution Insight
- Top 1% of Americans hold $32 out of every $100 of US household wealth ($163 trillion total)
- Top 10% hold $70 out of every $100
- Bottom 50% hold $2 out of every $100
- **Implication:** "Sell to the rich. They pay better" is not a pithy statement -- it's mathematical reality
- "If you sell to rich people long enough, they will make you one of them"

### Pareto Principle (80/20) Applied to Business
- 20% of customers = 80% of revenue
- 4% of customers = 64% of aggregate profit
- 1% of customers = 51% of profit
- This maps directly to wealth distribution -- the business gains profits in the same pattern wealth is distributed

### Passion Redefined
- Root of "passion" = Latin "pacio" = suffering/endurance
- First usage: "Passion of Christ" = crucifixion story
- **Hormozi's reframe:** "Find something worth suffering for" -- not "do what you love"
- "Your passion only exists in the vague, not in the specific"
- 95% of what you do daily if successful will NOT be your passion
- "Success and failure are on the same path. Failure is just an earlier exit."

### Suffering as Fixed Cost
- Suffering exists on ALL paths (entrepreneurship, employment, poverty, wealth)
- "Suffering is a fixed cost. The secret is to pick the path that pays better."
- "The goal is to reframe reality so that bad things are good. Not to try and only experience good things."
- Subjective well-being returns to baseline after major life changes (hedonic adaptation)

### Growth Requires Unreasonable Standards
- "Industry standards are a handicap that you let your competitors use"
- "Unless the laws of physics prevent it, consider it mental handicaps that your competition gets to live by"
- "Profit is unnatural" -- there is constant pressure of normalcy fighting against it
- Someone must "hold the line" -- the most important job in the company
- Jeff Bezos: "Differentiation is survival and the universe wants you to be typical"
- "You can tell how good someone will be as an entrepreneur by the standards they keep for themselves"

### Identity & Self-Improvement
- "The strongest force in human personality is the need to stay consistent with how we identify ourselves"
- Identity must expand, not stay fixed -- "Would you use a phone from 10 years ago? Why use the same old identity?"
- Tony Robbins distinction: "Analytical Alex" vs "Anabolic Alex" -- the intellectual vs the joyful, connected self
- Push motivation (duty, willpower) vs Pull motivation (calling, contribution) -- pull is sustainable
- "The science of achievement" vs "the art of fulfillment" -- different skills entirely

### On Trade-offs
- "You cannot have it all. There are literally structurally impossible combinations."
- "The worst trades are the ones we don't consciously make but still trade anyway"
- "The downside of your bets is not as bad as you think. The upside is also not as good as you think."
- "All the upside is on the other side of uncertainty and delay"
- "Humans underestimate the upside and overestimate the downside" (fundamental psychological bias)

### On Commitment
- "I never knew when I would succeed or if I would succeed, but I did know that I wouldn't stop"
- "If I didn't stop, I couldn't be called a failure"
- "A man must have a quest"

## 2. Named Frameworks & Mechanisms

### The Value Equation
- From $100M Offers book
- "We question all of our beliefs except for the ones we really believe in, and those we never think to question"
- Rich people evaluate: value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
- Rich people want 3 things: **Fast, Easy, Guaranteed**

### The Grand Slam Offer (Trim & Stack)
- From $100M Offers: List all resources/assets, then cut, combine, package for leanest and most valuable
- "Each bonus should be worth the price of the whole thing on its own"
- Jack Dorsey quote: "Make all the details perfect. Limit the number of details."
- People decide to buy from ONE thing -- "Oh, that's the one" -- then they're in

### The Money Formula (Pricing Tiers)
- **Rule of thumb for upsells:** 5-10x price at each tier, expect 20% uptake
- Each tier should roughly double total revenue
- Hormozi's own tiers: School ($100/mo hobby, $100/mo pro) -> L1 ($5,000) -> L2 ($35,000) -> L3 ($135,000) -> Portfolio company (equity)
- "The only thing worse than offering a $1,000 thing to someone with a $100 budget is offering a $100 thing to someone with a $1,000 budget. First you lose $100, second you lose $900."

### Close Rate vs Pricing Ladder
| Close Rate | You're Underpriced By |
|---|---|
| 80%+ | 3-4x |
| 60-80% | 2-3x |
| 50-60% | 1.5-2x |
| 40-50% | 1.25-1.5x |
| 30-40% | Appropriately priced |
| Below 30% | Fix selling or avatar, not price |

### LTV to CAC Ratios (by Operational Drag)
| Human Touch Points | Target LTV:CAC |
|---|---|
| 0 (full automation: ads -> checkout -> software delivery) | 3:1 |
| 1 human in loop (e.g., salesperson OR manual delivery) | 6:1 |
| 2 humans in loop (e.g., salesperson + service delivery) | 9:1 |
| 3 humans in loop (outbound + sales + service) | 12:1 |
- Gym Launch year 1: 100:1 LTV:CAC ($100K spend -> $10M revenue)
- "Every time I had 30:1+, the business exploded"

### The Four Business Shapes
1. **E-commerce** (stair-step growth): Fast revenue scaling, capital-intensive, supply chain constraints, brand is ultimate moat. 70/30 split: 70% brand/top-of-funnel, 30% direct response
2. **Service** (slow linear growth): 78% of US businesses. Easy to start, hard to scale. Talent is the bottleneck. Highest cash flow. Price = indicator of business maturity
3. **Education/Info** (fast spike then plateau): Fastest to make money, lowest barrier entry. Hard to scale past $1-3M. Retention is the core problem. "Big head, long tail" pricing
4. **SaaS** (slow start, exponential growth): Most scalable but most capital-intensive. Revenue retention is everything. 60%+ annual logo retention minimum, 100%+ revenue retention target

### The Rule of 100
- 100 primary actions per day for 100 days on your first/next acquisition channel
- 100 x 100 = 10,000 actions in one direction
- "I have never seen a business not grow when they implement the Rule of 100"
- Volatility/feast-famine = symptom of insufficient volume
- Companies doing 30x your sales are doing 30x your advertising

### The Gatorade Analogy (Coaching Dilution)
- Scenario 1: Pour your X-factor into shot glasses (small but undiluted)
- Scenario 2: Pour into big glasses and fill rest with water (diluted)
- People would rather have a small amount of the real thing than a diluted version
- "If a coaching business skill is easy to teach, it's not valuable. If it's valuable, it's hard to teach."

### The Bridge Analogy (Skill Acquisition)
- Skills are like bridge bricks -- you need ALL of them to cross
- Each course/training adds a brick -- no single one gets you across
- "The problem with an inexperienced teacher is they accept money no matter what"
- Skill gaps create different outcomes with the same education

### The Fusion Reactor (Self-Sustaining Marketing)
- Goal: normal business function creates the marketing for the business
- Customer advertises -> customer comes in -> great experience -> documented -> advertised -> next customer
- Only thing required to start: one big marketing push, then the snowball rolls on its own

### Transformation Vocabulary (from Tony Robbins interview)
- Words you attach to experience BECOME your experience
- "Furious" vs "annoyed" vs "peeved" vs "tinkled" -- same event, radically different emotional responses
- The language you use shapes your psychology and creates an "emotional home"
- Remove catastrophizing words from vocabulary entirely

### The Phone Test (Key Man Risk)
- Can you leave for 3 months and the business grows?
- If same = need growth systems
- If shrinks = still key man dependent
- If grows = you have a sellable business

## 3. Strategies & SOPs

### Pricing Strategy
- **Start at the top** (Tesla model): Expensive -> affordable. Brand narrative works top-down, not bottom-up
- "Honda making a better car is tough. Rolls-Royce making a Rolls-Royce Light is easier."
- **Stop selling from your own wallet** -- imagine everyone is rich. Top 10% of Americans = million-dollar+ net worth
- Before stating price: "Hey, before I tell you the price, it's super expensive" -- creates perfect emotional anchor for both rich and poor prospects
- Can also write price down and slide it to them if you choke on saying it

### Service Business Scaling Formula
1. Select narrow avatar
2. Limit deliverables to 1-3 consistent offerings
3. Systematize/productize delivery
4. Build training around systematized steps
5. Replace yourself in operations
6. Invest in recruiting + brand
- **Virtuous cycle:** Higher price -> higher margins -> better talent -> better service -> better reputation -> more demand -> higher price
- **Vicious cycle:** Lower price -> lower margins -> worse talent -> worse service -> worse reputation -> less demand -> lower price

### Cash Flow Acceleration
- **Target:** Recover CAC within 30 days (credit card float = free scaling capital)
- 30-day cash collected must be > COGS + CAC
- **Layaway model:** Pay before you receive. The faster they pay, the faster they get
- **Payment structures:** Ask for pay-in-full first (10% discount), then half now/half in 30, then thirds
- Align payment dates with customer's payday or peak cash flow
- **Third-party financing:** 35% increase in overall sales (A-firm data)
- **Prepay incentives:** Buy 10 get 2 free = 15-20% take rate. Moderate discount + bonus = 30-40% take rate

### Lead Response Times
- Call leads within 60 seconds. Non-negotiable.
- Waiting = 4x higher CAC + lower close rates + pricing wars with competitors
- "It's how you do one thing is how you do everything"

### Sales Team Management
- **70% calendar utilization** is the sweet spot (range: 60-85%)
- Below 60%: morale drops, commission breath
- Above 85%: conversion drops, pipeline neglected, show rates decline
- "I have yet to make less money by hiring more salespeople"
- Rather have salespeople just below their comfort nut so they're always striving

### Removing Key Man Risk (5 Steps)
1. **Self-inventory:** List everything you do, time study (15-min intervals)
2. **Red/Yellow/Green:** Green = delegate now, Yellow = build process, Red = hire for
3. **Decision trees:** If-this-then-that rules for common scenarios with approval thresholds
4. **Shadow -> Supervise -> Support:** Watch you do it, do it while you watch, then independent
5. **Remove yourself from marketing:** Install 7+ systems that generate ads without your face

### 7 Ways to Remove Founder from Marketing
1. Weekly screenshots of positive community/social mentions -> run as ads
2. Incentivized testimonials (credit, tier upgrades, trials)
3. Lifecycle ads: Compress sales call -> onboarding -> milestones -> testimonial into timeline ad
4. Key moment documentation (reveals, results, celebrations)
5. Customer support team paid per mini-testimonial captured
6. Mine Google/Yelp/Amazon reviews for ad content (including funny 1-stars)
7. Affiliate programs / TikTok Shop whitelisting

### Content-to-Ads Pipeline
- Post organically daily (minimum 1/day, ACQ does ~450/week)
- Organic = free testing ground for ads
- Take highest performing clips -> add 5-second CTA at end -> run as paid
- "For School last year, we spent tens of millions on ads and never changed the copy once. Once copy is right, just keep changing the creative."
- Run 100+ static images from camera roll as ads -- "You have no idea which will work"

### Lead Magnet Strategy
- Three types: (1) Reveal a problem, (2) Free trial/sample, (3) One step of many
- Four delivery methods: Software/tools, Information, Services, Physical products
- **Naming formula options:**
  - Number + Outcome + Timeframe: "3 emails that turn cold leads into clients in 24 hours"
  - How to [good thing] without [bad thing] even if [insecurity]
  - Adjective + type + good thing: "The lazy funnel template that converts like crazy"
  - X mistakes preventing [goal]: "5 ad copy mistakes killing your conversion rate"
- Split-test the NAME more than the content -- name determines engagement more than anything
- Hormozi split-tested 6 headlines for $100M Leads book
- "Bad lead magnets don't work. Lead magnets work."

### Event Strategy (In-Person Sales)
- Greet attendees by name with specific facts about their business
- Use colored lanyards to assign team members to specific attendee groups
- Day 1: Intro (blow them away) -> Meeting blocks -> Soft pitch -> Dinner for buyers
- Day 2: Short reframe of objections from Day 1 -> Teaching blocks -> Stack and close (15 min)
- "If you're going to pitch, pitch. If you're not, don't."
- Renewal at in-person events: "When are you coming back?" + fast action bonus (e.g., first-class tickets)

### Hiring / Talent
- **Interview volume:** Sam interviewed 600 developers to find School's CTO
- Measure employees by **rate of improvement**, not starting skill level
- "Intelligence = rate of learning"
- Prefer intelligent fast-learner over experienced slow-learner
- Look for people who describe their role in metrics -- quality and quantity of metrics = proxy for skill
- Group interviews for frontline roles save massive time
- "If you aren't learning from the person in the interview, especially for leadership roles, waste of time"

## 4. Offer Architecture

### Offer Design Principles
- Decommoditize: Make it so different people can't compare your thing to competitor's thing side by side
- The rich want: Fast, Easy, Guaranteed
- Pre-do work for them, pre-choose, drive to door, scout location
- "Simplicity is the ultimate in business. Simplicity makes for simpler marketing, simpler selling, simpler delivery."

### Tier Structure
| Tier | Price Range (Consumer) | Price Range (B2B) | Expected Uptake |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | $10-100/mo | $200-500/mo | Majority |
| Mid | $50-500/mo | $2,000-3,000/mo | ~20% of base |
| High | $500-10,000 one-time | $5,000-35,000 | ~20% of mid |
| Ultra | $3,000-10,000+ | $35,000-135,000+ | ~4% of original |

### Coaching Business Architecture (from Couple Preneurs case study)
- **Kill the low-ticket offer** if economics don't work
- **Combine into ONE premium offer** ($25K-30K/year)
- Deliverables: Deep dive onboarding (1x) + Quarterly 1-on-4 calls + 2x/year in-person events
- **Total delivery time:** ~10 hours per client per year
- Sunset Slack (40% time savings) -- redirect to community
- Group format better than 1-on-1 for most problems
- "One of the hardest parts of being a [niche] is it's lonely. Wouldn't it be nice to know others share the same struggles?"

### Pricing Psychology
- "Poor people think in terms of cost. Rich people think in terms of the ratio: cost vs value."
- Poor person hears $20K: "That's expensive." Rich person: "For what?"
- A share of Berkshire Hathaway for $20K = deal of the century
- "Some of you are so cheap that people don't believe you're good"
- Case study: Doctor's practice raised price 50%, close rates went UP

### The "Big Head, Long Tail" Model (Education)
- Charge high one-time price for education (inherently valuable, no recurring)
- Charge low recurring for consumable elements (community, continued education, buying group discounts)
- Day before learning = incredibly valuable. Day after learning = value of same course is approximately zero
- Align pricing to value delivery timeline

### Fast Action Bonuses
- $2,500 rebate on $25K program for sending date night video testimonial (dual purpose: discount + ad content)
- "For everyone who renews today, we'll buy you first-class tickets to the next event"
- Start now + deep dive immediately = fast action bonus
- "The reason someone buys isn't always necessarily the reason someone stays" (Planet Fitness pizza example)

## 5. Buyer Psychology & Sales Insights

### Core Sales Beliefs
- "You will always lose until you learn. Then you win. That's how winning works."
- "The sweet spot isn't the most yeses. It's the most money."
- Sales is about maximizing total revenue, not close rate
- Most sales people get choked up at the price moment -- "give yourself a breather"

### Hormozi's First High-Ticket Sale
- Was calling gyms to cancel. Said $6,000 expecting rejection. Guy said "done."
- Next call: $8,000. "Done." Next: $10,000. By end of day: $60,000 collected
- "That moment was where I elevated. That was the moment where my life really changed."
- The number you think will be rejected is often the number that unlocks everything

### Absolute vs Relative Profit
- 1 person paying $10,000 for something costing $2,000 = same as 400 people buying a $50 thing at $25 cost
- "Do not underestimate the power of large prices in small quantities"

### The Learning Budget
- Hormozi met an e-commerce entrepreneur doing $35M who committed a fixed % of income monthly to "learning budget"
- Spent it on experiments, testing, courses, events
- Hormozi took this, increased ad spend from $400/day to $1,400/day
- Gym Launch went from $300K/mo to $780K to $1M to $2M+ in consecutive months

### Skill Investment Economics
- $2,000 invested in learning sales -> $30K income becomes $90K income -> $35K/year investable surplus permanently
- $3,000/month invested from age 18 to 67 = $31 million (at 9% returns)
- "$1 saved at 18 is worth $13 at retirement" (adjusted for inflation)
- "People don't want to spend money on skills because the cost is certain and immediate, but the return is uncertain and delayed"

### Overcoming Price Objections
- "This is going to be expensive" -- say it BEFORE the price
- Rich people immediately think of what's expensive FOR THEM and anchor higher
- Poor people brace themselves and handle it better
- Either way, creates perfect emotional accommodation for the prospect's buying power

### Shame vs Guilt (Selling in Groups)
- Shame = breaking other people's rules that you respect
- Guilt = breaking your own rules
- Selling group coaching: "One of the big things about shame is it lives in darkness. It makes you feel alone."
- Reframe: "The only thing that's not normal is that people think it's not normal"

## 6. Content & Marketing Strategy

### Volume Philosophy
- ACQ makes ~450 pieces of content per week (~25,000-30,000/year)
- Typical $1M business: ~1/day (365/year)
- "Diminishing returns are still returns"
- "The amount of conversations I've had with small business owners who are all about optimization -- you can optimize OR you can maximize. You can't do both."

### Video vs Image Ads
- "Video has higher volatility than images. Your best ads will be video and your worst ads will be video."
- Images outperform videos if the videos are bad
- "Static images are better than bad videos because people's imaginations fill in something compelling"

### Ad Testing Philosophy
- Copy rarely needs to change -- once the message resonates, keep it
- Change creative (hooks, visuals) while keeping copy constant
- Test 100+ image variations from existing camera roll
- Organic content = free testing ground for paid ads

### Brand Building Principles
- Brand delivers: (1) Higher click-through rates, (2) Higher repurchase rates, (3) Higher price premiums
- = Lower CAC + Higher LTV + Higher gross margins
- Brand is the only moat against commodity competition
- "Pricing is a two-way communication. You tell them what you're about, they self-select as customers."
- Rich people shop high to low -- they look at the most expensive stuff first
- 70/30 rule for e-commerce: 70% brand/awareness spending, 30% direct response

### Challenge Funnel to Date Night Conversion
- From Couple Preneurs case study: 5-day challenge had 9% show rate, 50% drop-off day 1 to 2
- Hormozi's recommendation: Convert to 4-hour "date night" format
- Same amount of teaching time (strip intros/outros from 5 days = ~3 hours of real content)
- 2x the people at the pitch moment (no attrition across 5 days)
- Unique angle impossible to compete with
- Frame: "If you're anything like us, date night is not business or fun. It's both."

## 8. Anti-Patterns

### Things Hormozi Says NOT to Do

1. **Selling from your own wallet** -- projecting your own financial reality onto prospects
2. **Using industry averages as benchmarks** -- "The average business is average. Why measure yourself by that?"
3. **Tiny upsell jumps** ($1K core -> $1,500 upsell) -- should be 5-10x ($1K -> $5-10K)
4. **Unlimited Slack/text access** -- "You've reinforced them messaging you all day." Kills renewal rates
5. **Adding bonuses for volume** instead of impact -- each bonus should be worth the whole price alone
6. **Hiring coaches and training them in a weekend** -- if the skill is that easy to teach, it's not valuable
7. **Bottom-up pricing** (starting cheap, trying to go expensive later) -- always start at the top
8. **Scaling before nailing the model** -- "Nail it, then scale it"
9. **Accepting slow lead response** -- anything over 60 seconds = 4x CAC increase
10. **Confusing features with bugs** -- every business model has inherent hard problems; they're features, not flaws
11. **Over-expanding locations** before passing the "phone test" (3 months no involvement, business grows)
12. **Offer hopping** -- changing what you sell every year instead of fixing retention
13. **Running only direct-response ads** without brand investment (should be 70% brand, 30% DR for e-commerce)
14. **Measuring 30-day results** on a model that needs 90+ days -- wrong time horizon
15. **Gross margins below 80%** for service businesses -- "I will not get into a business with less than 80% gross margins"

### Belief Traps That Keep Entrepreneurs Stuck
- "No one can do it like I can do it" -- "I can outwork anyone in my business. You will not be able to outwork everyone."
- "I need to find my passion first" -- "Delaying your pursuit because you're waiting to find your passion is a fool's errand"
- "I tried Facebook ads and it didn't work" -- "It's not that it didn't work. It's that you weren't skilled enough to make it work."
- "This can't be done in this timeline" -- "What physical law prevents it?"
- "My offer is different because [industry]" -- "No, it's not. You get what you tolerate."
- "Free services attract freebie seekers" -- "Only do free work for qualified people. When I worked for free for a year, almost all of them happily paid when I asked."

## 9. Key Terminology

| Term | Hormozi Definition |
|---|---|
| **Grand Slam Offer** | An offer so good people feel stupid saying no. Decommoditized, high-value, stacked bonuses |
| **Lead Magnet** | Mini offer: complete solution to narrow problem that reveals need for core offer |
| **Big Head, Long Tail** | High one-time price for education + low recurring for consumables |
| **Layaway** | Customer pays first, receives after payment complete. Pulls cash forward, eliminates delivery risk |
| **Money Model** | A sequence of offers designed to maximize cash flow and lifetime value |
| **Payback Period** | Time to recover CAC. Target: 30 days |
| **LTV:CAC** | Lifetime value to cost of acquisition ratio. Minimum 6:1 for any business with humans |
| **Rule of 100** | 100 actions/day for 100 days on one acquisition channel |
| **Revenue Retention** | Of original cohort revenue, how much remains after 12 months. Target: 100%+ |
| **Logo Churn** | Percentage of customers who leave (not revenue). Different from revenue retention |
| **The Trim and Stack** | List all deliverables, cut the weak ones, stack the valuable ones |
| **Fusion Reactor** | Self-sustaining marketing where business operations create marketing assets |
| **Lifecycle Ads** | Ads built from sales call -> onboarding -> milestones -> testimonial footage |
| **Smash and Grab** | Pure media arbitrage business with no brand moat -- buy eyeballs at $10, sell product for $11 profit |
| **Overkill Bias** | People want the best person to teach them, even for basic skills (Tony Hawk teaching ollie) |
| **Anchor Offer** | Ultra-expensive top-tier offer. Even if no one buys it, makes everything else look like a deal |
| **Calendar Utilization** | Percentage of sales team calendar booked. Sweet spot: 70-75% |
| **Self-Annealing** | Errors -> fix -> test -> update. System gets stronger after each failure |
| **Watermark Investing** | Keep $X in bank, invest everything above that threshold |
