## 3. The Pre-Frame Psychology System

This is Johnny Mau's signature methodology. The core insight:

> **Don't handle objections at the end. Prevent them during discovery.**

### The Root Cause Philosophy
- All objections are rooted in **limiting beliefs**, not legitimate concerns
- When someone says "I need to think about it," they're actually expressing:
  - "I don't know if I can afford it"
  - "I don't have enough time"
  - "I'm not ready yet"
  - "What will [spouse/friends] think?"
  - "I can do this myself"
- These beliefs were **formed by past experiences**
- By reframing those experiences during discovery, you dissolve the objection before it forms

### The Discovery Question That Reveals Everything
Ask this 10-15 minutes into the call:

> *"What have you done in the past to reach your desired outcome?"*

This question reveals the EXACT objection they'll present at the end of the call.

### Two-Path Response Structure

**Path 1 — They say "Nothing":**
- Follow-up: *"What do you feel like prevented you from taking action?"*
- They'll give surface answers (money, time, priorities)
- Dig deeper: *"And what specifically about [their answer] held you back?"*
- Keep going until you uncover the actual limiting belief
- Once found, reframe it during the call so it's dissolved by close time

**Path 2 — They took action but stopped:**
- Explore: *"What stopped you from continuing?"*
- Connect their comfort/inaction to financial consequences
- Mirror the problem back without accusation
- Show how their pattern of stopping is THE problem, not external circumstances

### The Reframe Technique
- **Never attack the person.** Attack the thinking.
- Separate the person from their limiting belief:
  > *"That way of thinking is the issue"*
- This positions you as **teammates against the problem**, not adversaries
- You're both looking at the limiting belief as the enemy, together

### Pre-frame Outcome
When done correctly, by the time you reach the close:
- The prospect has already acknowledged their limiting beliefs
- They've agreed those beliefs are the problem
- They've expressed desire to change
- The investment becomes the obvious next step
- Traditional objection handling becomes largely unnecessary

## 4. Sales Call Structure & Framework

### Before the Call
- Research the prospect (if possible)
- Set your own state — conviction and energy must be high
- Review the offer so you can speak with certainty

### The 3 Points to Hit on Every Sales Call
1. **Uncover the real problem** (not the surface symptom)
2. **Connect the problem to consequences** (what it costs them to NOT fix it)
3. **Position the offer as the bridge** (from where they are to where they want to be)

### During the Call — Flow

**1. Opening / Rapport (2-3 min)**
- Build genuine connection
- Set the frame for the conversation
- Opening question: *"What was it that you either saw or heard that attracted your attention?"*

**2. Deep Discovery (15-20 min)**
- Get specific — reject vague answers like "boost my sales"
- Ask: *"Can you give me a concrete example of what that looks like?"*
- Deploy the Pre-Frame Psychology System (see Section 3)
- Uncover limiting beliefs and reframe them in real-time

**3. Vision / Future Pacing (5 min)**
- Paint the picture of their life after the transformation
- Make it vivid and emotional
- Connect it to what they told you matters most

**4. Transition to Offer (3-5 min)**
- Bridge from their problem to your solution
- Show how the offer addresses exactly what they described

**5. Close / Investment Discussion (5-10 min)**
- Present the investment with confidence
- If pre-framing was done well, minimal objections
- Handle any remaining objections with factual framework

### After the Call
- Follow up immediately if not closed
- Document learnings for pattern recognition
- Update scripts and approaches based on what worked

## 5. Objection Handling Framework

### Core Principle: Factual-Based Objection Handling
- Don't argue with feelings — present facts
- Isolate the **real** objection (it's usually fear underneath)
- Address the root, not the surface

### The Objection Categories Johnny Handles

**1. Money / "I can't afford it"**
- This is almost never actually about money
- It's about **value perception** — they don't see the ROI clearly enough
- Reframe: Connect the cost of inaction to a number greater than the investment
- If genuinely about cash flow: offer payment plans, downsell, or financing
- NLP technique: Reframe the investment as what they're already spending on the problem

**2. Spouse / Partner / "I need to talk to my wife"**
- Pre-frame early: *"Is there anyone else involved in this decision?"*
- If yes, get the partner on the call or schedule a joint call
- If they bring it up at the end (after not mentioning it earlier), it's a smokescreen
- Reframe: *"When you go to your [partner], what specifically are you going to tell them?"*
- This forces them to articulate the value, essentially selling themselves

**3. "I need to think about it"**
- This is the most common objection and the most misunderstood
- It's NOT a request for time — it's an expression of unresolved limiting beliefs
- If pre-framing was done correctly, this objection rarely surfaces
- When it does appear: *"Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think about?"*
- Then address whatever they surface as the actual concern

**4. Value / "It's too expensive"**
- Isolate: Is it genuinely too expensive, or do they not see enough value?
- Demonstrate clear ROI: *"If this generates [X result], would [investment] make sense?"*
- Reduce financial friction through payment plans or financing

**5. Timing / "Not the right time"**
- Reframe: *"When would be the right time? What would need to change?"*
- Connect their delay pattern to their current results
- Show that waiting IS the problem, not the solution

### The Only Rules for Objection Handling
1. Never get defensive
2. Always isolate the real objection
3. Use questions, not statements, to reframe
4. Agree first, then redirect
5. Make the prospect articulate the cost of inaction

## 6. Specific Word Tracks & Scripts

### Discovery Phase Word Tracks

**Opening:**
> *"What was it that you either saw or heard that attracted your attention?"*

**Getting Specific:**
> *"When you say [vague answer], can you give me a concrete example of what that looks like in your day-to-day?"*

**Past Action Discovery:**
> *"What have you done in the past to try to reach [their stated goal]?"*

**Digging Into Inaction:**
> *"What do you feel like prevented you from taking action?"*

**Going Deeper:**
> *"And what specifically about [their surface answer] held you back?"*

### Reframe Word Tracks

**Separating Person from Belief:**
> *"I don't think that's who you are — I think that way of thinking is the issue."*

**Permission to Share Perspective:**
> *"Can I share a perspective on that?"*
(Always ask permission before reframing — it opens them up to receive it)

**Connecting Comfort to Consequences:**
> *"It sounds like comfort has been the most expensive thing in your life. Would you agree?"*

**Identity Reframe:**
> *"My question isn't whether you want [result] — it's how committed are you to becoming the person who gets [result]?"*

### Closing Word Tracks

**Transition to Close:**
> *"Based on everything you've shared, it sounds like [summarize their problem, their goal, and the limiting belief they identified]. Does that sound right?"*

**Investment Frame:**
> *"The investment is [price]. And based on what you told me about [their specific cost of inaction], this pays for itself when [specific outcome]."*

**Urgency Without Pressure:**
> *"I'm not here to pressure you. But I want you to be honest with yourself — if not now, when? And what will be different then that isn't different today?"*

### Objection Response Word Tracks

**"I need to think about it":**
> *"Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think about?"*
> Then: *"Is it the money, the timing, or whether this is the right fit?"*

**"I need to talk to my spouse":**
> *"Of course. When you go to [name], what specifically are you going to tell them about this?"*
> Then: *"Would it make sense to get them on a quick call so I can answer their questions directly?"*

**"I can't afford it":**
> *"I hear you. Let me ask you this — what is it costing you right now to NOT have this handled? Per month, per year?"*
> Then: *"So the question isn't really whether you can afford the program — it's whether you can afford to keep doing what you're doing."*

## 7. NLP Techniques for Sales

### Techniques Johnny Teaches

**1. Presuppositions**
- Embed assumptions in questions that assume the sale
- *"When you start seeing results in the first 30 days, what's the first thing you'd change?"*
- The prospect answers as if they've already bought

**2. Mirroring & Matching**
- Match the prospect's energy, pace, and language
- Use their exact words back to them (not synonyms)
- This builds unconscious rapport

**3. Reframing (Core NLP Technique)**
- Change the meaning of an experience without changing the facts
- Example: "I've been too comfortable" becomes "comfort has been the most expensive thing in your life"
- Same fact, completely different emotional weight

**4. Future Pacing**
- Guide the prospect to vividly imagine their life after the transformation
- Make it sensory: what they see, feel, hear
- This creates emotional investment in the outcome

**5. Pattern Interrupts**
- Break the prospect out of scripted responses
- When they give a rehearsed objection, respond with something unexpected
- Forces genuine engagement instead of automatic resistance

## 10. Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

### Things Johnny Warns Against
1. **Reading scripts verbatim** — prospects can tell, it creates distance
2. **Pitching instead of discovering** — if you're talking more than the prospect, you're doing it wrong
3. **Handling objections only at the end** — by then it's too late, pre-frame during discovery
4. **Clocking in and out mentality** — treating sales as a job instead of a skill to master
5. **Random volume without strategy** — hoping enough calls will convert without improving technique
6. **Getting defensive when objected** — the prospect is not attacking you, they're expressing fear
7. **Using the same response for every objection** — isolate the real concern first, then respond
8. **Skipping the discovery** — rushing to pitch means you have no ammunition for the close
9. **Arguing with feelings** — you can't logic someone out of an emotion, reframe instead
10. **Selling without genuine conviction** — if you don't believe, the prospect won't either

## 11. Application for Sabbo's Business

### For Amazon FBA Coaching Sales Calls
- **Deploy Pre-Frame Psychology** during discovery: *"What have you done in the past to try to build an Amazon business or generate extra income?"*
- **Identity Reframe** for hesitant prospects: *"The question isn't whether Amazon works — it's whether you're committed to becoming the person who builds a real asset."*
- **Comfort Reframe** for "I need to think about it": *"It sounds like thinking about it is what you've been doing. How long have you been thinking about starting something? And where has that gotten you?"*
- **Spouse Objection Pre-Frame**: Ask early — *"Is there anyone else who'd need to be involved in this decision?"*
- **Money Objection Reframe**: *"You said you have $5-20K in capital for the Amazon business itself. The coaching investment ensures that capital actually produces returns instead of becoming expensive mistakes."*

### For Growth Agency Sales
- **Speed-to-Lead**: Contact prospects within minutes of inquiry
- **Factual-Based Objection Handling**: Use data/case studies to address "is this worth it?"
- **Team Specialization**: As the agency scales, implement setter/closer model
- **Conviction Transfer**: Sabbo's operator positioning (actively running what he teaches) is a natural conviction amplifier

### Key Takeaway
Johnny Mau's methodology is the **inverse of most sales training** — instead of getting better at handling objections, get better at **preventing them**. The Pre-Frame Psychology System is the core innovation. Everything else supports it.
