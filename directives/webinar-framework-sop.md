# Webinar Framework SOP — Fladlien "Genius Webinars" 4-Part Structure

> **Source:** Jason Fladlien & Joe Polish — "Genius Webinars" framework
> **Fladlien stats:** $100M+ in webinar sales, #1 affiliate for Amazing Selling Machine ($9.8M), $30M from a single Amazon FBA webinar over 4 years
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-24

---

## The 4-Part Structure

Every webinar follows this exact structure. No exceptions.

### Part 1: INTRODUCTION (5-15 min)

| Element | Purpose | How |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Authority** | Establish credibility | Results, positioning, testimonials. NOT credentials/logos. Show real numbers. |
| **Commitment** | Get buy-in early | "By the end of this, you'll know exactly how to [outcome]. Deal?" |
| **Objections** | Pre-handle resistance | Name the top 2-3 objections upfront: "You might be thinking..." |
| **Hope** | Paint the future | "Imagine waking up to [desired outcome]" |
| **Intrigue** | Create curiosity | "I'm going to show you something most [audience] have never seen..." |

### Part 2: CONTENT (45-60 min)

This is the VALUE section. Teach real, actionable content. Not teasers.

| Element | Purpose | How |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Defined Outcome** | Set expectations | "By the end, you'll be able to [specific action]" |
| **Step by Step** | Teach the process | "First do this, then that, then this..." with screen-shares and real examples |
| **Context** | Explain WHY each step matters | "Why what you're about to see is important" |
| **Vision** | Get them to take action in their mind | "Picture yourself doing this..." |
| **Strategy** | Give actionable criteria | Steps within steps, what to look for, decision frameworks |
| **Commitment** | Lock in belief | "Can you see how this could work for you?" |

**Key:** The Content section should include at least ONE screen-share moment — a live demo that creates a "wow, this is real" reaction. This is the most important moment in the webinar.

### Part 3: TRANSITION (3-5 min)

| Element | Purpose | How |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **60-Second Recap** | Summarize everything | "Here's everything I showed you: [rapid recap]" |
| **Yes Momentum** | Build agreement pattern | 6 yes questions in a row: "Do you want [benefit]? YES." |
| **Two Choices** | Frame the decision | "You can try to do this alone... OR you can let us do it with you." |

### Part 4: CLOSE (variable — see below)

**For direct-sell webinars (checkout on page):** 45+ min
**For call-booking funnels (our model):** 10-20 min

| Element | Purpose | How |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Offer** | Present what's included | Product name, tag line, benefit statement, what's included |
| **Price** | Reveal investment | For call-booking: "Discussed on your strategy call." For direct: anchor high, drop, reveal. |
| **Bonuses** | Increase perceived value | Headline, 3 benefits, proof, future pace. Time-limited. |
| **Guarantee** | Reverse risk | "If [condition], we [guarantee action]" |
| **Objections** | Handle remaining resistance | Validate → Reframe → Shatter → Re-engage |
| **Scarcity** | Create urgency to act NOW | Real scarcity: capacity limits, cohort size, time-limited bonuses |

## Call-Booking Funnel Adjustments

When the webinar drives to an APPLICATION (not checkout):
- **No specific pricing** on slides or landing pages
- Close section = 10-20 min (shorter)
- CTA = "Apply Now" / "Book a Strategy Call"
- Bonuses tied to application deadline (48hr window)
- The CLOSE transfers trust to the strategy call, not to a payment page

## Slide Design Principles (Fladlien Style)

1. **One idea per slide** — never overload
2. **Bold headline, minimal body** — dark background, white bold text
3. **Screen-share for demos** — show the actual tool/process, not slides about the tool
4. **Student proof = screenshots** — real Amazon dashboards, real Discord messages, real numbers
5. **Profit math slides** — show the actual calculation (Retail Price → Fees → COGS → Profit)
6. **Questions as slides** — "Is This Good?" / "What Would You Do?" creates engagement

## Pre-Webinar Nurture (Mason CopyMBA Pattern)

| Timing | Subject Style | Tone |
|--------|--------------|------|
| Registration | "I know you got an automated email, but..." — personal, address fears, stack proof | Casual, friend-to-friend |
| +1 day | "When people call my class a webinar, it makes me feel like a scammer" — anti-pitch positioning | Anti-grifter, testimonials |
| +2-3 days | "There's a surprise gift at the end" — create curiosity | Short, builds anticipation |
| +24 hours | "You excited for tomorrow?" — what you'll learn bullets | Direct, energetic |
| +8 hours | "Today could change your life" — urgency + social proof | FOMO, but genuine |
| +2 hours | "In about 2 hours, I'm going live" — special gift reminder | Brief |
| Live | "I'm about to go live" — link | One line + link |

**Key principle:** These emails are PERSONAL, not corporate. Written like a text from a friend. Real testimonials from real people who haven't even bought yet.

## Show Rate Optimization (Jeremy Haynes "Hammer Them")

1. **Confirmation page video** — 8 min urgency video after opt-in
2. **Value-dense emails** — full content in email body, not just links
3. **Targeted social ads** — run retargeting to registrants during the pre-webinar window
4. **Personalized outreach** — setter researches each registrant, sends contextual message
