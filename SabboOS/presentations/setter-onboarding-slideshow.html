Before Every Dial

60-Second Research (Mandatory)

Leads with context convert 3-4x higher. Do this for EVERY lead.

  • 1. Open their website — What do they sell? Who do they sell to? Is the site trash?
  • 2. Check LinkedIn or Instagram — How big? Running ads? Posting content?
  • 3. Spot obvious gaps — No email capture? Weak landing page? No funnel? Bad design?
  • 4. Find ONE thing to reference — "I saw you're running Facebook ads..." / "I noticed your site doesn't have..."

This is NOT optional. If you skip research, your connect-to-booking rate drops to near zero. 60 seconds of prep = 4x better results.

Phase 1 — Pick Your Style

6 Opener Styles

Pick the one that fits the lead. Master all 6 over time.

1. Customer Approach

"Hey, I was trying to look for a [listing/appointment] but couldn't find one on your site. I ended up going with someone else — but I think we could help you fix that so you don't lose future customers."

Best for: Bad/no website. You sound like a real customer.

2. Familiarity Play

"[Name]? What's up! This is [You]. We spoke a while back about getting you more clients — does that sound familiar?"

They say "who?" → "Yeah no worries — is getting more clients something you're still looking into?"

Best for: Any lead. Creates instant rapport.

3. Website Gap

"Hey, I was trying to see where I could schedule [service] with you but there was no website. I'm all good now, but do you still need a website? Because that's what we do."

Best for: No website at all. Direct and helpful.

4. Direct Cold Open

"Hey [Name], this is [You] — I know this is out of the blue, do you have 30 seconds?"

Best for: When you have strong research. Honest, no games.

5. Social Media Observer

"Hey [Name], I was scrolling through your Instagram — your content is really solid. But I noticed you don't have anywhere to send people when they want to work with you. Is that something you've been meaning to fix?"

Best for: Active on IG/FB but no website or funnel.

6. Referral Frame

"Hey [Name], your name came up when I was talking to someone in [industry] in [city]. Quick question: are you guys actively looking to grow right now, or are you at capacity?"

Best for: Any lead. Builds trust through implied referral.

Phase 2 — After They Engage

The Hook (Once They're Talking)

No matter which opener you used, transition into one of these hooks. Either/or questions get 4x the response rate.

Primary Hook

"I was looking at [Company] — looks like you've built something solid. Quick question: is your growth right now more systematic, or is it more like... you're figuring it out as you go?"

Situation-Specific Hooks
Bad/no website
"So right now, where are most of your customers actually finding you? Because online isn't working for you yet."
Running ads poorly
"Are you happy with what you're getting back from that ad spend, or does it feel like you're kind of guessing?"
Posting content
"I see you're putting out content — but are you seeing actual customers come from it, or is it more hope-and-pray?"
No online presence
"How are people finding you right now? Is it all referrals and word-of-mouth, or do you have something driving new leads?"

After you ask — SHUT UP. Let them talk. Listen 70%, talk 30%.

Phase 3

Dig Into the Pain

Listen 70%, talk 30%. Pick 2-3 of these based on conversation flow.

Questions to Ask

"What does your customer acquisition look like right now — where do most of your clients come from?"

"Do you have a marketing team or person handling this, or is it on you?"

"What have you tried that hasn't worked?"

"What would it look like if this was actually dialed in? What would change?"

"What's the cost of NOT fixing this? How much are you leaving on the table every month?"

Then Recap Their Pain

"So it sounds like the main issue is [X], and that's costing you roughly [Y] a month — is that right?"

Objections

When They Push Back

Handle and redirect. Don't argue. Don't debate. Acknowledge, redirect, move forward.

"I'm not interested."
"Totally respect that. Out of curiosity — is that because growth is already dialed in, or is it more of a timing thing?"
"Just send me an email."
"Happy to — what's the best email? And just so I send something relevant, what's the biggest marketing challenge you're dealing with right now?"
"We already have an agency."
"Nice — are you happy with the results? I only ask because a lot of founders I talk to have a team but still feel like growth is unpredictable."
"What do you guys do?"
"Short version — we install a full growth system for founder-led businesses. But honestly, the call is where we'd dig into whether it makes sense for you."
"I don't have time."
"Totally get it — you're busy, that's exactly why this might be worth it. The call is 30 min and it's specifically about getting your time back from marketing."
