6. Pain Points You'll Hear

When you talk to real estate agents and business owners, they'll describe their problems in their own words. Here's what to listen for:

Website Problems

  • "I don't have a website" — they know they need one
  • "My website is outdated" — built it 5 years ago, never updated
  • "I get no leads from my site" — it's a brochure, not a funnel
  • "I paid someone and it didn't work" — burned by a cheap freelancer

Marketing Problems

  • "I post on social but nothing happens" — content without strategy
  • "I tried ads and wasted money" — no targeting, no funnel, no follow-up
  • "All my business is referrals" — no predictable lead flow
  • "I don't have time for marketing" — too busy working IN the business

The Deeper Pain (What They Really Feel)

  • "I'm doing everything myself" — no team, no systems, burning out
  • "Growth is random — some months great, some dead" — no predictability
  • "I know I'm leaving money on the table" — they see competitors growing
  • "I've hired agencies before and got burned" — trust issues (our opportunity to be different)

  • "Founder-led businesses like yours"

"It really depends on what you need — that's exactly what the strategy call covers. We'll take a look at your situation, where the gaps are, and give you a straight answer. No pressure either way."

8. Common Objections & Responses

Handle and redirect. Don't argue. Don't debate. Acknowledge → redirect → move forward.

"I'm not interested."
"Totally respect that. Out of curiosity — is that because growth is already dialed in, or is it more of a timing thing?"

If timing → "When's better to revisit?" If dialed in → "Best of luck — keep me in mind."

"Just send me an email."
"Happy to — what's the best email? And just so I send something relevant, what's the biggest marketing challenge you're dealing with right now?"

If they answer the question → you're back in conversation.

"We already have an agency / someone handling this."
"Nice — are you happy with the results? I only ask because a lot of founders I talk to have a team but still feel like growth is unpredictable."

If unhappy → "What's not working?" If happy → "Not trying to fix what's not broken."

"I don't have time for a call."
"Totally get it — you're busy, that's exactly why this might be worth it. The call is 30 minutes and it's specifically about getting your time back from marketing."

Offer a specific time: "What about [Day] at [early/lunch/end of day]?"

"Is this a sales call?"
"It's a strategy session — we look at what you're doing now and give honest feedback on where the gaps are. If there's a fit, great. If not, you walk away with clarity. No pressure either way."
"How'd you get my number?"
"Your business came up when I was researching [industry] in [area]. I'm not trying to waste your time — genuinely had a question about how you're handling growth. 30 seconds?"
"I've been burned by agencies before."
"I hear that a lot, honestly. That's usually because the agency only did one piece — ran ads with no system behind it. What we do is different — we install the full engine. But the strategy call is where you'd see exactly how. No commitment, just a conversation."
"I can't afford it right now."
"Totally understand. Out of curiosity — what's the cost of NOT fixing this? Like, how many clients are you missing out on every month because your online presence isn't working for you?"
