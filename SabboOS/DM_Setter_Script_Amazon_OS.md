# SECTION 4: PRE-QUALIFICATION (Before You Message)

**60-Second Profile Scan:**
1. Check their bio — do they mention business, hustle, side income, Amazon?
2. Check their content — are they posting about entrepreneurship, e-commerce, or income goals?
3. Check follower count — not a vanity check, just context (micro-influencer? student? corporate?)
4. Check their story — are they consuming business content? Reposting motivational stuff?

**Qualify if ANY of these are true:**
- Bio mentions business, Amazon, e-commerce, FBA, side hustle, "building something"

- Clearly a bot or spam account
- No content, no bio, no signals

# SECTION 5: THE 8-STEP DM FLOW

## CRITICAL RULES
- **New followers → NEVER book immediately.** Send a YouTube video first. Nurture.
- **Old followers (2+ weeks) → Book directly** if they're warm.
- **Every single message follows:** Acknowledge → Answer → Follow-up question
- **NEVER answer without a follow-up question** — or they leave you on seen.

---

## STEP 1: CURIOSITY OPENER

**Goal:** Get a response. That's it. Not to build trust, not to sell — just get a reply.

### Option A: The "Yo" (60% response rate)
> Yo

That's it. One word. Works because it's pattern-breaking — they expect a pitch, they get a casual greeting. Disarming.

### Option B: Binary A/B Question (pre-qualified from profile)
> Hey! Quick question — are you looking to start selling on Amazon, or are you already selling and trying to scale?

### Option C: Assumption Opener (if profile shows signals)
> Your content is solid — looks like you're building something real. Is the main thing holding you back finding the right products, or getting everything set up?

### Option D: Engagement-Based (if they liked/commented on content)
> Saw you liked the [88K week / sourcing walkthrough / $14K in 48 hours] post — is Amazon something you're actively looking into, or just researching for now?

### Option E: Ad/Reel Trigger Word Response
> Hey! Saw you DMed "[trigger word]" — I'll send that over. Quick question first: are you completely new to Amazon, or have you sold before?

### RULES:
- Always use yes/no or either/or questions. NOT open-ended.
- Open-ended = they have to think = they don't reply
- Binary = they pick A or B = conversation started

---

## STEP 2: WHY DID YOU REACH OUT?

**Goal:** Put them in a buying state. Make them articulate their own motivation.

After they respond to your opener:

> Got it — what made you start looking into Amazon? Was there something specific that got you interested?

**Why this works:** When they explain WHY they want this, they're selling themselves. They're articulating their own pain and desire. You don't have to — they do it for you.

**If they came from a specific video, mirror it:**
> Makes sense. That [88K week / toilet cleaner origin / beginner guide] video resonates with a lot of people. What part of it hit home for you?

---

## STEP 3: QUALIFYING QUESTIONS

**Goal:** Confirm they're a fit (capital, motivation, timeline). Do NOT ask these like an interrogation — weave them in naturally.

Pick 2-3 based on conversation flow:

> Are you looking to do this full-time, or as a side thing while you keep your job?

> Have you invested in anything Amazon-related before — a course, software, anything?

> What's your timeline looking like — are you trying to get started ASAP, or is this more of a "sometime this year" thing?

> Roughly how much would you be comfortable investing to get set up — inventory, tools, the whole thing?

**Capital Qualification (CRITICAL — do not skip):**
The offer requires $5K-$20K+ for program + initial inventory. If they clearly can't invest, don't waste their time or yours.

> Totally understand. Just so I can point you in the right direction — most people starting out have around $5K-$15K set aside for inventory and getting set up. Does that sound like where you're at, or is it a different situation?

**If under $3K:** Be honest and helpful, not dismissive.
> Real talk — I'd say keep stacking capital for another month or two and come back when you're closer to $5K. That way when you start, you actually have room to move. In the meantime, [Sabbo]'s free YouTube course covers everything you'd need to know to hit the ground running.

**Goal:** Find their #1 pain so you can match the right asset.

> Got it. So what's been the biggest thing holding you back from actually pulling the trigger — is it finding products, knowing where to start, or something else?

## STEP 5: DURATION CHECK

**Goal:** Understand urgency. The longer the pain, the more urgent the need.

> Has this been something you've been thinking about for a while — like months — or is it more recent?

**If months/years:**
> So you've been sitting on this for [X months]. What would it mean for you if 90 days from now you actually had this running?

**If recent:**
> Nice — sounds like you're in action mode. That's the best time to start because the motivation is fresh.

---

## STEP 6: TRIGGER

**Goal:** Get them to confirm they need help. This is the psychological commitment point.

> Is that also why you followed — because you were looking for someone to actually help you get this going?

**Or:**
> So it sounds like the main thing you need is [mirror their pain back]. Is that why you reached out?

**Why this works:** When they say "yes," they've just told themselves they need help. You didn't say it — they did. Now they're primed for the asset and the call.

## STEP 8: BOOK THE CALL (or Close in DMs)

**Goal:** Get them on a strategy call — or close them right in the DM if they're ready.

### Path A: Book the Strategy Call

After they engage with the asset:

> What'd you think? Did that clear some things up?

*Wait for response. Then:*

> Here's the thing — a video can only do so much. The real value is when someone actually looks at YOUR situation and maps out a game plan. That's what the strategy call is.
>
> You free [day]? I can get you on the calendar. It's basically a business audit — [Sabbo] looks at where you are, what's blocking you, and maps out the steps to get to your first $10K month. No pressure, just clarity.

### Path B: DM Close (High-Intent Prospects)

If they're clearly ready — asking about pricing, asking "how do I start," mentioning capital they have ready:

> Sounds like you're ready to go. Here's what the program looks like: you work directly with [Sabbo] — he walks you through product research, sourcing, listing, launch, everything. It's not a course you watch alone. You build your business with someone who's actively selling every day.
>
> The investment is [range] with payment plans available. Want me to send over the details?

### Path C: Not Ready Yet (Nurture)

If they're interested but not ready:

> No rush at all. I'll send you some stuff over the next few days that I think you'll find valuable. When you're ready to have a real conversation about it, just DM me.

Then add them to the follow-up list (50/day).


**Examples:**

*(Dead end. No question. They don't respond.)*

*(Passive. Puts the ball in their court. They won't.)*

✅ Good: "That video covers the exact process. After you watch it — did the sourcing part make sense, or do you want me to send you the more detailed walkthrough?"

# SECTION 10: KEY PSYCHOLOGY PRINCIPLES

### 1. Emotion-First Reading
Before responding to any message, forget the words. Read the emotion:
- **Shame:** "I've been thinking about this for 2 years and never started" → respond to the shame, not the timeline
- **Fear:** "What if I lose money?" → respond to the fear, not the objection
- **Excitement:** "I just watched your video and I'm hyped" → match the energy, don't cool them down
- **Confusion:** "There's so much information I don't know where to start" → simplify, don't add more info

### 2. Mirror Their Language
If they say "side hustle" → you say "side hustle." Don't upgrade it to "business" or "enterprise."
If they say "scared" → you say "scared." Don't soften it to "concerned."
People trust people who talk like them.

### 3. The Anti-Pitch IS the Pitch
Real buyers said Sabbo converted them because he WASN'T pitching:
- "I didn't want to be mentored by someone who just solely focused on content creation about their program"
- "The delivery seemed genuine"
- The more you sound like a salesperson, the more you trigger their "guru detector"
- Let the content and results speak. Your job is to route them to the right content.

### 4. Specific Numbers Beat Vague Claims
Every video that was named by real buyers had a specific dollar figure:
- 88K week, $2K/day, $14K in 48 hours, $10,900 first week
- "You can make money on Amazon" → no one buys
- "$3,400 profit from one Walmart run" → people DM asking how

### 5. Youth IS the Proof
112 buyers ranked Sabbo's age as the #1 reason they bought:
- "You're young like me"
- "If you can do it why can't I"
- Never hide the age. Never over-professionalize. The relatability converts.

# SECTION 11: SHOW-UP RATE PROTECTION

**Target: 70-82% show rate. Below 70% = setter is booking too fast or not nurturing enough.**

**Pre-call sequence:**
1. **Booking confirmation** (immediate): "You're locked in for [day/time]. Looking forward to it."
2. **24 hours before**: Send pre-call asset (Section 9)
3. **1 hour before**: "Hey — just a reminder you've got your call in an hour. [Calendly link]"
4. **No-show**: Wait 10 minutes, then: "Hey — missed you on the call. Everything good? Want to reschedule?"

**If show rate drops below 70%:**
- Check: Are you booking too early in the conversation? (Should be Step 8, not Step 3)
- Check: Are you sending a pre-call asset? (Section 9)
- Check: Are you qualifying hard enough? (Unqualified = no-show)
