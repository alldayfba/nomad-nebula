## Role Definitions

### CEO (Sabbo)

**What you do:**
- Set vision, offer, pricing, positioning
- Record content (VSL, YouTube, IG) -- this is your traffic engine
- Review daily numbers via EOD scorecard from Sales Manager
- Weekly 30-min strategy call with Sales Manager
- Handle enterprise deals ($25K+) personally
- Final say on hires and fires

**What you do NOT do:**
- Sit on the dial floor daily (that is the Manager's job)
- Coach individual reps (that is the Manager's and Team Lead's job)
- Build dial lists (that is the CRM Admin's job)
- Handle client delivery post-close (that is the CSM's job)

**Your time split:**
| Activity | Hours/Week |
|---|---|
| Content creation | 10 |
| Strategy + numbers review | 5 |
| Enterprise sales | 3-5 |
| Product/offer development | 5 |
| Team leadership (weekly calls) | 2 |

---

### Sales Manager (1 Person)

**The single most important hire. This person runs the entire revenue engine.**

**Daily responsibilities:**
- Lead 11 AM morning standup (voice channel)
- Run Training Block 1 (11:15 AM, objection drill or talk track review)
- Be present in voice chat during BOTH dial blocks (12-2:30, 4-6)
- Run Training Block 2 (3 PM, pod-level role-plays)
- Lead 6 PM EOD review
- Review every EOD report, flag gaps, follow up
- Coach 2-3 reps individually each day (rotating schedule)
- Post objection-of-the-day in Discord each morning

**Weekly responsibilities:**
- 1-on-1 with each Team Lead (15 min each, Monday or Tuesday)
- Weekly performance review: who's hitting, who's slipping, who's getting cut
- Update rep scorecards and send summary to CEO
- Review 10+ call recordings, post feedback in #game-film
- Run weekly team meeting (Friday afternoon, 30 min)

**Owns these metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Team daily dials | 750+ (15 setters x 50) |
| Booked calls/day | 25-30 |
| Show rate | 75%+ |
| Close rate (closers) | 30%+ |
| Ramp time (new setter to productive) | 5 days |
| Weekly attrition | <10% |

**Comp structure:**
- Base: $8,000-$12,000/mo
- Team override: 3-5% of total team revenue
- Target bonus: $2,000-$5,000/mo for hitting team revenue targets
- Example: Team does $200K/mo, manager earns $12K base + $8K override + $3K bonus = $23K/mo

**Who to hire:**
- Has managed 10+ reps before
- Can run a dial floor, not just "manage" from Slack
- Lives in voice chat, not in spreadsheets
- Former top closer who moved into management
- Comfortable firing underperformers quickly

---

### Team Lead (1 Per Pod of 5-7 Setters)

**Promoted from:** Top-performing setter after 2-3 weeks of consistent KPI performance.

**Daily responsibilities:**
- Manage their pod: energy, accountability, micro-coaching
- Run pod-level role-plays during Training Block 2
- Report pod numbers to Sales Manager at EOD
- Be first escalation point for setter questions
- Still dial (reduced target: 30/day instead of 50)

**Weekly responsibilities:**
- 1-on-1 with Sales Manager (15 min)
- Identify struggling setters in pod, flag to Manager
- Onboard new setters assigned to their pod

**Promotion criteria (Setter to Team Lead):**
- 2+ weeks on floor
- Consistently hitting 50/50/50
- 4+ booked shows/week
- Positive attitude, coachable, team-oriented
- Can articulate WHY the script works (not just read it)

**Comp structure:**
- Setter base pay (same as before)
- +$500/mo Team Lead bonus
- +1% override on pod's booked shows that close
- Example: Pod books 40 shows/mo, 12 close at $10K avg = $120K. Lead earns setter base + $500 + $1,200 override

---

### Closer (2-3 People)

**What they do:**
- Take strategy calls booked by setters
- 4-6 calls/day, back-to-back blocks
- Follow 9-stage NEPQ closer script
- Send proposals and handle post-call follow-up (24-48hr window)
- Weekly 1-on-1 with Sales Manager
- Review own call recordings, identify patterns

**Daily flow:**
| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 11:00 AM | Review today's calendar, prep for calls |
| 11:30 AM | First call block (3 calls) |
| 1:00 PM | Break + follow-up messages |
| 2:00 PM | Second call block (3 calls) |
| 4:00 PM | Follow-ups, proposals, pipeline management |
| 5:30 PM | Update CRM, log results |
| 6:00 PM | Post EOD report |

**Owns these metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Calls/day | 4-6 |
| Close rate | 30-40% |
| Average deal size | $7K-$15K |
| Follow-up response rate | 80%+ within 24hr |
| No-show recovery | 50%+ rebooked |

**Comp structure:**
- Base: $3,000-$5,000/mo
- Commission: 8-12% of cash collected on their closes
- Bonus: Extra 2% on all deals if monthly close rate exceeds 40%
- Example: Closer does $80K/mo in closes at 10% = $8K commission + $4K base = $12K/mo

**When to hire Closer #3:**
- When Closer 1 and 2 calendars are 85%+ full
- When setters are booking 25+ shows/day and closers can't keep up
- The bottleneck shifts from "not enough calls" to "not enough closers" fast

---

### Senior Setter (Promoted from Setter)

**Promotion criteria:**
- 2+ weeks on the dial floor
- Consistently hitting or exceeding 50/50/50
- 4+ qualified bookings/week
- 75%+ show rate on their bookings
- Positive team presence

**Additional responsibilities vs. Setter:**
- Mentor 1-2 new setters in their pod
- Can pin messages in TRAINING channels
- Eligible for Team Lead promotion
- Expected to help with onboarding role-plays

**Comp structure:**
- Same base as Setter ($2K-$3K/mo)
- +$250/mo seniority bonus
- Same per-show and per-close bonuses
- Fast track to Team Lead (next opening)

---

### Setter (The Bulk of the Team)

**This is the engine. Everything else exists to support setters making dials.**

**Daily responsibilities:**
- 50 cold dials (new prospects from CRM lists)
- 50 warm follow-ups (existing pipeline, callbacks)
- 50 re-engagement touches (old leads, no-shows, lost deals)
- Join voice chat for ALL dial blocks (12-2:30, 4-6)
- Post EOD report daily by 6:30 PM, no exceptions
- Attend morning standup at 11 AM

**Weekly responsibilities:**
- Attend all training sessions
- Listen to 5+ call recordings (own or team's)
- Update CRM notes on all active prospects
- Hit minimum KPIs or face performance conversation

**Minimum KPIs to stay on team:**
| Metric | Minimum | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Total daily touches | 100 | 150 |
| Booked calls/week | 3 | 5+ |
| Show rate | 65% | 75%+ |
| EOD reports/week | 5/5 | 5/5 |
| Voice chat attendance | 100% | 100% |

**Comp structure:**
- Base: $2,000-$3,000/mo (depending on experience/market)
- Per qualified show: $25-$50
- Per close from their booking: $100-$200
- Example: Setter books 20 shows/mo (x$35 = $700), 6 close (x$150 = $900) + $2,500 base = $4,100/mo

**Performance management:**
- Week 1: Grace period, coaching-heavy, no cuts for low numbers
- Week 2: Must hit 80% of minimums
- Week 3+: Must hit minimums or enter PIP (performance improvement plan)
- PIP: 5 business days to hit minimums, daily 1-on-1 with Team Lead
- Fail PIP: Terminated

---

### CRM Admin / VA (1 Person)

**What they do:**
- Build and maintain dial lists (500+ new leads/week minimum)
- Clean CRM data: deduplicate, update statuses, merge records
- Pull daily/weekly reports for Sales Manager
- Manage Calendly links, Zoom rooms, meeting infrastructure
- Handle lead list segmentation (cold, warm, re-engage, DNC)
- Import scraped leads from nomad-nebula into GHL

**Tools they own:**
- GHL (GoHighLevel) CRM
- Google Sheets (reporting)
- Calendly (scheduling)
- nomad-nebula lead scraper output

**Comp:** $1,500-$2,500/mo (can be offshore VA, Philippines or LatAm)

**When to hire:** Day 1. You need lists ready before setters start dialing.

---

### CSM -- Client Success Manager (1 Person)

**Hire when:** You have 5+ active clients and delivery is starting to slip.

**What they do:**
- Own client onboarding (first 30 days)
- Weekly check-in calls with each client
- Track client KPIs and flag churn risk
- Handle client requests and route to delivery team
- Collect testimonials and case studies at 30/60/90 days

**Comp:** $3,000-$5,000/mo + retention bonus (% of retained revenue)

## How to Onboard 20-30 Reps in One Week

### Day 1 (Monday): Mass Hiring Blitz

**Where to post:**
- Your IG story (multiple times throughout the day)
- LinkedIn (personal post + job listing)
- Twitter/X
- Skool communities (sales, business, side hustle)
- Closers.io
- Indeed
- Your existing network (DM 20 people: "know anyone who can sell?")

**Application form (Google Form, 4 questions):**
1. Tell me about your sales experience in 2-3 sentences.
2. What's the most you've earned in a single month from sales?
3. Why do you want this role specifically? (Not "I need money" -- why THIS role?)
4. Record a 60-second Loom: pitch me on why I should hire you.

**Goal:** 100+ applications by end of day.

**Filtering (cut 80% immediately):**
- No Loom submitted = cut
- Loom is boring, monotone, or reads from a script = cut
- No sales experience and no energy to compensate = cut
- "I need money" as only motivation = cut
- Keep anyone with energy, confidence, and some proof of hustle

### Day 2 (Tuesday): Phone Screens

**Schedule:** 15-min phone screens, back-to-back, 30-40 calls.

**What you're testing:**
- Energy level in the first 10 seconds
- "Sell me on why I should hire you. You have 60 seconds. Go."
- Can they think on their feet?
- Do they ask questions about the role (shows curiosity)?
- Are they coachable (ask: "if I told you your pitch was wrong, what would you do?")?

**Cut signals:**
- Low energy, monotone, sounds like they just woke up
- Can't articulate why they want THIS (generic answers)
- Defensive when challenged
- No availability for full-time schedule

**Goal:** 20-25 pass phone screen.

### Day 3 (Wednesday): Group Onboarding Call

**Format:** 90-min Zoom, all 20-25 candidates.

**Agenda:**
| Time | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0-10 min | Welcome, who you are, company vision |
| 10-25 min | The offer: what we sell, who we sell to, why it works |
| 25-40 min | ICP training: who is the customer, what are their pains |
| 40-55 min | Daily schedule, expectations, KPIs, comp structure |
| 55-70 min | The script: walk through opener + hook + booking flow |
| 70-80 min | Black Marker Method explained: memorize, don't read |
| 80-90 min | Q&A, assignment: watch all Loom videos + read script 5x by Thursday |

**After the call:**
- Give them Discord access (@Onboarding role)
- Share links to all training materials
- Assignment: memorize script opener + hook by Thursday

### Day 4 (Thursday): Script Test + Role Play

**Format:** Individual 10-min role-play with Sales Manager or Team Lead.

**What you're testing:**
- Can they deliver the opener naturally (not reading)?
- Do they hit the hook without stumbling?
- Can they handle one basic objection ("not interested")?
- Energy, pace, tone -- do they sound like someone you'd stay on the phone with?

**Scoring:**
| Score | Result |
|---|---|
| 8-10 | Pass. Ready for Friday. |
| 6-7 | Conditional pass. One more practice session Thursday evening. |
| Below 6 | Cut. Didn't do the work. |

**Goal:** 15-20 pass role-play.

### Day 5 (Friday): First Dial Day

**The moment of truth.**

- Surviving reps join dial-block-1 voice channel at 12 PM sharp
- 25 practice dials each (half of normal volume)
- Sales Manager coaching live in voice chat between calls
- Real prospects, real conversations, real pressure
- Post EOD report at 6:30 PM

**Cut criteria:**
- Didn't show up at 12 PM = cut
- Didn't post EOD = cut
- Zero conversations from 25 dials AND bad attitude = cut
- Made 25 dials, struggled but stayed positive and coachable = keep

**Goal:** 12-18 survive first dial day.

### Day 6-7 (Weekend): Self-Study

**Assignment for surviving reps:**
- Continue Black Marker Method on full script (not just opener)
- Listen to 10 recorded calls posted in #game-film
- Study objection battle cards (top 15 objections)
- Come Monday ready for full 50/50/50 cadence

### Week 2: Full Volume

**Monday 11 AM:** Team standup with surviving 15-20 reps.

**The week:**
- Full 50/50/50 cadence begins
- Daily coaching in voice chat
- Daily numbers posted, daily accountability
- Manager doing 2-3 live coaching sessions per day
- Team Leads identified and promoted by Friday

**By Friday Week 2:**
- Identify top 3-4 performers: promote to Senior Setter or Team Lead
- Cut bottom 20% who aren't hitting minimums despite coaching
- Finalize pod assignments (5-7 setters per Team Lead)

## Expected Attrition

This is normal. Plan for it. Do not be surprised or discouraged.

| Stage | Headcount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Applications | 100+ | Cast a wide net |
| Phone screen pass | 20-25 | Cut 75-80% on application |
| After group call | 20-25 | Some self-select out |
| After script test (Day 4) | 15-20 | Cut those who didn't prepare |
| After first dial day (Day 5) | 12-18 | Cut no-shows and no-reports |
| After Week 2 | 10-15 | Cut bottom performers |
| Steady state (Week 3+) | 10-15 | Solid producers |

**Rule of thumb:** Hire 2-3x what you need. The cream rises. Continuous hiring pipeline should always be running -- never stop recruiting.

## The Math at Scale

### 15 Setters Producing

```
15 setters x 50 dials/day = 750 dials/day
750 dials x 20% connect rate = 150 conversations
150 convos x 30% qualified = 45 qualified prospects/day
45 qualified x 66% booking rate = 30 booked calls/day
30 booked x 80% show rate = 24 shows/day
24 shows x 35% close rate = 8.4 closes/day
8.4 closes x $10K avg deal = $84,000/day
```

### Revenue Projections (14-Day Sprint)

| Scenario | Daily Revenue | 14-Day Total |
|---|---|---|
| Full efficiency | $84,000 | $1,176,000 |
| Half efficiency (realistic ramp) | $42,000 | $588,000 |
| Quarter efficiency (conservative) | $21,000 | $294,000 |

**At 15 setters producing, $200K in 14 days is CONSERVATIVE.**

### The Bottleneck Shift

At this volume, the bottleneck shifts from "not enough calls" to "not enough closers."

```
24 shows/day / 3 closers = 8 calls each/day
```

Three closers at 8 calls each can handle the volume. If shows exceed 24/day consistently, hire Closer #4.

### Monthly Projection at Steady State

```
15 setters, 3 closers, full cadence:
- 8.4 closes/day x 20 working days = 168 closes/month
- 168 x $10K = $1.68M/month gross revenue
- Even at half: $840K/month
- Even at quarter: $420K/month
```

### Payroll vs. Revenue

| Item | Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| 15 Setters ($2.5K avg + bonuses) | $45,000-$60,000 |
| 3 Closers ($4K base + 10% commission) | $36,000-$50,000 |
| 4 Team Leads ($3.5K avg) | $14,000 |
| 1 Sales Manager ($12K + override) | $15,000-$25,000 |
| 1 CRM Admin | $2,000 |
| **Total payroll** | **$112,000-$151,000** |
| **Revenue at half efficiency** | **$840,000** |
| **Payroll as % of revenue** | **13-18%** |

Payroll should be 15-20% of revenue at scale. If it creeps above 25%, you have a performance problem, not a cost problem.

## Firing Framework

Not everyone makes it. Move fast on cuts.

| Signal | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Didn't show up, no communication | Immediate cut | Same day |
| Missed 2+ EOD reports in a week | Warning conversation | 24 hours |
| Below 80% of minimums after Week 1 | PIP (5 business days) | 5 days |
| Failed PIP | Terminated | Same day |
| Bad attitude, drama, toxicity | One warning, then cut | 48 hours max |
| Dishonest numbers (inflating dials) | Immediate termination | Same day |

**Manager owns the cut conversation.** CEO only gets involved for Team Leads or contested situations. Be fast, be direct, be respectful. "This isn't working out. No hard feelings. Here's your final payment."
