## 6. AD STRATEGY

### Platform Priority
1. **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** — Primary cold traffic driver
2. **YouTube** — Pre-roll for long-form VSL amplification
3. **LinkedIn** — If targeting B2B / enterprise clients
4. **Google Search** — Brand protection + intent capture

### Creative Framework

**3 Core Angles:**

**Angle 1 — The Problem (Pain)**
Hook: "Your marketing isn't broken. Your marketing system is."
Format: Talking head or text-on-screen, 30–60s
CTA: "See how we fix it → [link]"

**Angle 2 — The Outcome (Aspiration)**
Hook: "What $50K/mo in new revenue looks like when your acquisition machine actually works."
Format: Case study walkthrough, 60–90s
CTA: "Apply to work with us"

**Angle 3 — The Mechanism (Differentiation)**
Hook: "Most agencies run ads. We build the system behind the ads."
Format: Founder or operator walking through the model
CTA: "Watch the breakdown"

### Budget Structure
- Test phase: $50–100/day per angle (3 angles = $150–300/day)
- Scale: Double spend on winning angle. Kill losers after 3–5 days.
- Retargeting: 15–20% of total budget

### KPIs
- CPL (Cost per Lead/Application): Target < $150
- CPQL (Cost per Qualified Lead): Target < $300
- ROAS on CAC: Target 3x minimum (LTV-based)

## 7. LANDING PAGE STRUCTURE

### Above the Fold
- Headline: Bold outcome-driven promise
- Subheadline: Mechanism explanation (why it works)
- VSL embed (autoplay with sound off + captions)
- CTA: "Apply to Work With Us" (button anchors to form)

### Section 2 — The Problem
- Agitate the exact pain of your ICP
- 3–5 bullet points of what they're experiencing right now
- "If this sounds familiar, keep reading."

### Section 3 — The Mechanism
- Name and explain your proprietary system
- How it's different from what they've tried
- Visual diagram of the operating system / process

### Section 4 — Social Proof
- 2–3 featured case studies (before/after format)
- Revenue metrics, time to result, client industry
- Video testimonials if available

### Section 5 — What's Included
- Breakdown of deliverables in plain English
- Scope, timeline, and what they don't have to do

### Section 6 — Who This Is For (and Who It's Not)
- Creates qualification in the prospect's mind
- Repels bad fits, attracts ideal clients

### Section 7 — Application CTA
- Application form (5–7 questions)
- Emphasize limited spots, exclusivity
- Redirect to booking page after submission

### Footer
- FAQ (3–5 common objections handled)
- Privacy policy / legal

## 8. VSL OUTLINE

**Total Runtime:** 12–18 minutes

### Hook (0:00–1:00)
- Lead with the #1 pain point or counterintuitive claim
- "If you're spending money on ads and still can't predict revenue, it's not your ads. It's what you don't have behind them."

### Problem Amplification (1:00–3:00)
- Walk through the common broken playbook (hire agency, run ads, get mediocre results, repeat)
- Make them feel seen and understood

### Credibility Bridge (3:00–5:00)
- Who you are, what you've built, what you've operated
- Not your bio — your operator proof
- Reference specific results, companies, verticals

### Mechanism Introduction (5:00–8:00)
- Introduce your Growth Operating System by name
- Walk through each layer (paid, conversion, retention, data)
- Explain WHY each layer matters and what breaks without it

### Proof / Case Studies (8:00–12:00)
- 2–3 client stories with specific metrics
- Format: "Here's where they were → what we built → what happened"
- Use real numbers whenever possible

### The Offer (12:00–14:00)
- What working with you looks like
- Timeline, deliverables, what they keep
- Investment range (optional — can gate to application)

### CTA + Urgency (14:00–End)
- Direct call to apply
- Explain what happens after they apply (call, strategy, no obligation)
- Scarcity: limited client intake, why
- Final restate of core promise

## 9. EMAIL FLOWS

### Flow 1: Lead Intake (Post-Application, Pre-Call) — 5 Emails

| # | Trigger | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Immediate | "Your application is in — here's what's next" | Confirm, set expectations, build excitement |
| 2 | +1 day | "The real reason your marketing isn't compounding" | Educate, build authority |
| 3 | +2 days | "How [Client Name] went from $80K to $280K/mo in 4 months" | Proof, social proof |
| 4 | +3 days | "Before our call — watch this" | Link to VSL or case study, re-engage |
| 5 | +1 day before call | "See you tomorrow — prep this" | Pre-call primer, increase show rate |

### Flow 2: No-Show Re-Engagement — 3 Emails

| # | Trigger | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | No-show +1hr | "Missed you — want to reschedule?" | Low pressure rebook |
| 2 | No-show +2 days | "Still thinking about it?" | Insight + soft pitch |
| 3 | No-show +5 days | "Last attempt — closing your file" | Final urgency push |

### Flow 3: Long-Term Nurture (Non-Converted) — Weekly

- Format: Short insight email (300–500 words)
- Topics: Acquisition breakdowns, operator lessons, case studies, frameworks
- CTA: Soft — "If you're ready to talk, reply to this email"
- Cadence: 1x per week

### Flow 4: Win-Back — 3 Emails at 30/60/90 Days

| # | Timing | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 30 days | New result or case study drop |
| 2 | 60 days | "A lot has changed — here's what we built" |
| 3 | 90 days | Direct: "Are you still open to a conversation?" |

## 10. RETARGETING PLAN

### Audience Segments

| Segment | Definition | Creative Type | Offer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hot | Visited LP + watched 50%+ of VSL | Direct CTA — apply now | Application |
| Warm | Visited LP, bounced before form | Problem/pain creative | Watch VSL |
| Cold Retarget | Engaged with ad (click/view 25%) | Case study or social proof | LP visit |
| Email List | Leads who haven't booked | Testimonial or result | Book call |
| Lookalike | 1% LAL of converted clients | Angle 1 or 2 creative | LP |

### Retargeting Stack
- Meta Custom Audiences (LP visitors, VSL viewers, form starters)
- Google Display (pixel-based, LP visitor retarget)
- YouTube (skippable pre-roll for VSL viewers)
- Email sequence (parallel to ad retargeting)

### Frequency Cap
- Retargeting: 2–3 impressions/day max per person
- Rotate creative every 7–10 days to prevent fatigue

## 11. FULFILLMENT WORKFLOW

### Phase 1: Onboarding (Week 1)
- [ ] Kick-off call (90 min) — goals, ICP, positioning, assets audit
- [ ] Access: ad accounts, CRM, analytics, website
- [ ] Baseline audit: current funnel, CAC, LTV, conversion rates
- [ ] Set 90-day OKRs and KPI scorecard

### Phase 2: Infrastructure Build (Weeks 2–4)
- [ ] Funnel architecture finalized
- [ ] LP built or rebuilt
- [ ] CRM/email flows installed
- [ ] Creative briefs written and approved
- [ ] Ad accounts structured (campaigns, ad sets, creative)
- [ ] Tracking verified (pixel, GA4, UTMs)

### Phase 3: Launch & Test (Days 30–60)
- [ ] Campaigns live
- [ ] Daily monitoring: spend, CTR, CPL, conversions
- [ ] Weekly review call with client (30 min)
- [ ] Weekly performance report (templated)
- [ ] Creative iteration cycle starts (new hooks every 10 days)

### Phase 4: Optimize & Scale (Days 60–90+)
- [ ] Winning angle identified → scale budget
- [ ] Losing angles killed
- [ ] Funnel CRO based on data
- [ ] A/B test LP headline and CTA
- [ ] Monthly deep-dive reporting call

### Recurring Cadences
- **Daily:** Ad spend and conversion monitoring (internal)
- **Weekly:** Performance report + client call
- **Monthly:** Full growth review, next 30-day plan
- **Quarterly:** OKR reset, offer/positioning review

### Delivery Team Structure (Per Client)
- Growth Lead (strategy + client relationship) — 1 person
- Media Buyer (paid ads execution) — 1 person
- Email/CRM Operator — 1 person (can be shared across 2–3 clients)
- Creative Strategist — 1 person (can be shared across 3–5 clients)
