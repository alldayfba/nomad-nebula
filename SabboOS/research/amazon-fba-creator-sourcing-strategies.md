## 1. Clips for Miles (Flips4Miles) — Miles Longstreth

### Background
- Amazon seller since 2019, moved into online arbitrage in early 2021
- Over $4 million in sales in his first 2 years of OA; $2.1M+ in sales in the last 12 months
- Started with LEGO reselling, scaled into multiple categories
- Co-owner of SellerAmp SAS (product analysis tool)
- 113K Instagram followers; offers free 8-hour FBA 101 course
- Co-creator (with Warner Fields) of "The FBA Roadmap" course

### Core Sourcing Philosophy
- **100% online arbitrage** — does not do wholesale, purely OA
- "Don't overcomplicate it — follow the process and use the right tools consistently"
- Skill-based sourcing, not luck-based — learn the systems and repeat them
- Find products you can buy **over and over** (replenishable leads) to generate $10K+ profit from a single lead
- Focus on the process, not the individual product

### Tools Recommended
- **SellerAmp SAS** (co-owner) — primary product analysis and sourcing extension
- **Keepa** (paid version, ~$21/month) — price history and sales rank tracking
- **Tactical Arbitrage** — automated product scanning across 1,400+ retail websites
- **Boxem** (co-owner) — inventory shipping optimizer
- **IP Alert** — brand legal risk detection
- **BQool / SellerSnap** — dynamic repricing

### Product Criteria (from course content and SellerAmp methodology)
- **BSR:** Top 1% of category (otherwise move on); "Very good" BSR is 1–50,000
- **ROI:** Minimum 20% ROI threshold when using Tactical Arbitrage scans
- **Seller count:** 2–15 FBA sellers ideal on a listing
- **Profit per unit:** Aim for at least $3.50 per unit
- **Amazon as seller:** Avoid listings where Amazon sells directly (they control Buy Box 90%+ of the time)
- Look at 90-day average BSR, not a single snapshot

### Sourcing Methods
- **Storefront stalking** — examine successful sellers' storefronts to find what they're selling
- **Reverse sourcing** — start with high-demand Amazon products, then find cheaper sources
- **Rabbit trailing** — following product leads from one find to related products
- **Tactical Arbitrage scans** — automated cross-retailer price comparison
- **Clearance/deal hunting** — online retailer clearance sections

### Scaling & Automation
- Delegates menial tasks to Virtual Assistants
- Uses prep centers for FBA shipment preparation
- Course covers VA hiring and delegation strategies
- Goal: $0 to $10K/month, then scale from there

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Overcomplicating the process as a beginner
- Not using tools consistently
- Ignoring price history (relying on current price only)
- Not getting ungated early enough in restricted categories

## 3. Jazz Hustles (JasHustles) — Jasmine Green

### Background
- Started Amazon FBA at age 19 (January 2023)
- Six-figure annual revenue by age 21
- Primarily retail arbitrage (RA) focused
- Runs "Exclusive Hustlers" community on Whop ($55/month)
- Runs "Hustler's Academy" mentorship ($997) on Teachable
- Active on TikTok (@jashustles) and Instagram
- Got started through TikTok content discovery

### Core Sourcing Philosophy
- **"Just start scanning everything"** — quantity of scans beats overthinking
- Break sourcing into manageable sections: "Go into the beauty section one day, the grocery aisles another"
- Persistence and consistency beat strategy in the early stages
- Embrace trial-and-error learning — losing money early is normal and expected
- Focus on brand-name, established products (not generics)
- Treat it like "finding a treasure chest" — persistence across multiple locations

### Tools Recommended
- **SellerAmp** — primary scanning tool for in-store barcode scanning and product analysis
- Uses SellerAmp to check: product competitiveness, sales history, profitability, legal risk

### Product Criteria
- **ROI:** Maintains "100% return on investment" target
- Invests ~$10,000/month in inventory
- Focuses on brand-name products with established demand
- Clearance/discount items from retail stores
- Products that "sell higher on Amazon" than retail purchase price

### Sourcing Methods
1. **In-store clearance scanning** — Ross, TJ Maxx, retail clearance sections
2. **Grocery arbitrage** — reselling American grocery items (e.g., cereal) on Amazon to international buyers
3. **Walmart sourcing** — cost-effective, high-volume product sourcing
4. **Department-by-department scanning** — systematic approach to scanning stores section by section
5. **Discount/off-price retailers** — brand-name goods at significant markdowns

### Key Milestones & Metrics
- Lost $3,000 on initial failed products (considers this normal)
- 5 months in: $10,000/month in sales
- October 2023: $25,000 single-month revenue
- Invests $10,000/month in inventory
- 2–4 hours daily sourcing (early phase)

### Scaling & Automation
- Initially 2–4 hours daily sourcing (before/after day job)
- As winning products identified, time commitment decreased significantly
- Replenishment model — once you find winners, keep buying them
- Community support through Discord (300+ active members)

### Approach to Gated Categories
- Spent 2–3 weeks getting ungated for restricted categories early on
- Emphasizes ungating as "one of the smartest investments of time"
- Teaches ungating for grocery, health, beauty categories
- Has TikTok content specifically on ungating grocery category via Target receipts

### Common Mistakes Warned Against
- Not scanning enough products (shortcuts don't work)
- Giving up after initial losses
- Not getting ungated early
- Switching to FBM when FBA doesn't immediately work (she did this, regretted it)
- Not staying updated with market trends — "Ecommerce is constantly evolving, and adaptability is the main key"

## 5. Dan Buford / A Thousand ASINs — Daniel Boufford (@1000asins)

### Background
- Veteran Amazon seller based in Massachusetts
- Left demanding real estate career in New York for e-commerce
- Started with retail arbitrage, pivoted to wholesale during COVID pandemic
- Breakthrough product: 1-pound bags of yeast during pandemic panic-buying
- 8-figure annual seller ($20M in a single year, $50M+ over 6 years)
- Manages 1,000+ replenishable ASINs
- 400+ wholesale accounts, 75%+ brand-direct
- Founded ECG Wholesale Education
- Served 500+ students/clients
- Team spans US, Philippines, and Serbia
- In-house prep operations

### Core Sourcing Philosophy
- **Wholesale over arbitrage for scale** — "you can buy more and sell more"
- **Brand-direct relationships are king** — 75%+ of his accounts are direct with brands
- **Replenishable ASINs** — build a catalog of 1,000+ products you can reorder consistently
- **Provide value to brands** — don't just buy from them; help them with SEO, content, advertising on Amazon
- **Consistency over chaos** — wholesale provides predictable, repeatable revenue vs. arbitrage's hunt-and-find model
- **Outreach is a numbers game** — expect heavy rejection, keep contacting brands

### Tools & Systems
- In-house prep operations (not outsourced)
- International team (US operations, Philippines/Serbia for support)
- Professional website for brand outreach credibility
- Focus on Amazon listing optimization as a value-add to brands

### Product Criteria (Wholesale Focus)
- **Replenishability:** Can you reorder this product consistently?
- **Brand relationship quality:** Is the brand willing to work directly?
- **Margin sustainability:** Long-term profitability, not one-time flips
- Focus on products where you can add value to the brand's Amazon presence
- Manages 1,000+ ASINs simultaneously — breadth of catalog matters

### Sourcing Methods
1. **Brand-direct outreach** — contact brands directly for wholesale accounts
2. **Distributor relationships** — established supply chain partnerships
3. **Value-add approach** — offer brands SEO, content creation, and advertising support in exchange for direct accounts and better terms
4. **Exclusive partnerships** — deeper collaboration leads to exclusive invitations to supplier events

### Scaling Strategies
- **Team building across multiple countries** — US (operations), Philippines and Serbia (support)
- **In-house prep** — maintains own prep center rather than outsourcing
- **Systems and SOPs** — consistent, scalable operations
- **Brand Direct Blueprint** — structured system for securing brand accounts
- Build a professional website as foundation for brand outreach

### Common Mistakes Warned Against
- Expecting immediate acceptance from brands ("You're going to get rejected a lot")
- Not building a professional website before outreach
- Not learning how Amazon listings work (you need to help your suppliers)
- Staying in arbitrage too long when wholesale offers better scale
- Not treating outreach as a numbers game
- Underestimating account suspension risk — it's a real challenge even at scale
- Ignoring rising Amazon fees and changing FBA rules

### Approach to Account Health & IP
- Account suspensions are a reality he has dealt with
- In-house prep helps navigate changing FBA regulations
- Brand-direct relationships reduce IP complaint risk (you have authorization)
- Emphasizes proper documentation and invoicing

### Key Lessons (from "8 Things I Learnt Reselling on Amazon")
1. Wholesale is not all sunshine and rainbows — expect heavy rejection
2. Build a website to present yourself professionally
3. Learn how Amazon's listings work to help the suppliers
4. Outreach is a numbers game
5. Provide value beyond just buying products
6. Scale requires systems, not just more products
7. Team building is essential for growth
8. Brand-direct > distributor relationships for long-term success

## CONSOLIDATED BEST PRACTICES

### Product Selection Criteria (Consensus Across Creators)

| Metric | Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **ROI** | 20–30% minimum (some target 100%+) | Jazz targets 100%; Fields/Miles use 20% as minimum filter |
| **BSR** | Top 1% of category or < 50,000 | Use 90-day average, not snapshot |
| **Profit per unit** | $3.50+ minimum | After all Amazon fees |
| **Seller count** | 2–15 FBA sellers on listing | More = more competition, less Buy Box share |
| **Sales velocity** | 30+ units/month | Ensures product actually moves |
| **Price range** | $15–$70 sweet spot | Lower = thin margins; higher = slow turns |
| **Amazon as seller** | AVOID | They control Buy Box 90%+ of the time |

### Tool Stack (Ranked by Frequency of Recommendation)

| Tool | Purpose | Recommended By |
|---|---|---|
| **SellerAmp SAS** | Product analysis, barcode scanning, profitability calc | Miles, Warner, Jazz (all three) |
| **Keepa (paid)** | Price history, BSR tracking, sales graphs | Miles, Warner, industry standard |
| **Tactical Arbitrage** | Automated OA scanning (1,400+ retail sites) | Miles, Warner |
| **IP Alert** | Brand legal risk detection | Miles, Warner |
| **Boxem** | Shipping/inventory optimization | Miles, Warner (co-own) |
| **BuyBotPro** | Deal analysis (alternative to SellerAmp) | Industry standard for RA |
| **Getida** | Amazon reimbursement auditing | Warner |
| **BQool / SellerSnap** | Dynamic repricing | Miles, Warner |
| **ScoutIQ** | Book/media scanning for RA | Industry standard |
| **Amazon Seller App** | Basic in-store scanning | All beginners |

### Sourcing Methods (From Most to Least Scalable)

1. **Brand-Direct Wholesale** (Dan Boufford) — Most scalable, highest barrier to entry
   - Contact brands directly for wholesale accounts
   - Provide value: SEO, content, advertising support
   - Build professional website first
   - Expect heavy rejection; it's a numbers game
   - Results in replenishable, consistent revenue

2. **Automated Online Arbitrage** (Miles, Warner) — Highly scalable, moderate barrier
   - Tactical Arbitrage scans across 1,400+ sites
   - Filter: 20%+ ROI, BSR < 50,000
   - Reverse sourcing: start with Amazon demand, find cheap sources
   - Storefront stalking: spy on successful sellers
   - Can be delegated to VAs

3. **Manual Online Arbitrage** (Miles, Warner) — Moderately scalable
   - Clearance sections of online retailers
   - Cashback/coupon stacking
   - Credit card reward optimization
   - Seasonal/discontinued product hunting

4. **Retail Arbitrage** (Jazz) — Least scalable, lowest barrier to entry
   - In-store scanning with SellerAmp
   - Focus on clearance sections: Ross, TJ Maxx, Walmart
   - Department-by-department systematic scanning
   - Grocery arbitrage for international buyers
   - 2–4 hours daily in early phase

### The Arbitrage-to-Wholesale Pipeline

Based on these creators' journeys, there is a clear progression path:

```
Stage 1: Retail Arbitrage (RA)
  - Lowest capital requirement ($500–$1,500)
  - Learn product analysis fundamentals
  - Build scanning discipline
  - Get ungated in categories
  ↓
Stage 2: Online Arbitrage (OA)
  - Scale beyond geographic limits
  - Use Tactical Arbitrage automation
  - Build replenishable product list
  - Start delegating to VAs
  ↓
Stage 3: Wholesale
  - Highest capital requirement ($5K–$20K+)
  - Brand-direct relationships
  - 1,000+ replenishable ASINs
  - Predictable, consistent revenue
  - Team across multiple countries
```

### Critical First Steps (Consensus)

1. **Get ungated FIRST** — All creators emphasize this as priority #1 before aggressive sourcing
2. **Learn the tools** — SellerAmp + Keepa (paid) are non-negotiable
3. **Start scanning volume** — More scans = more data = better judgment
4. **Accept early losses** — Jazz lost $3K; this is normal and expected
5. **Focus on replenishable products** — One good lead can generate $10K+ over time

### Common Mistakes (Consensus Across All Creators)

1. **Not using price history** — Relying on current price spread without checking Keepa history
2. **Entering overcrowded listings** — Too many sellers = race to the bottom
3. **Not getting ungated early** — Missing entire categories of profitable products
4. **Overcomplicating the process** — Analysis paralysis instead of scanning and learning
5. **Ignoring Amazon as a competitor** — If Amazon sells it, move on
6. **No professional presence for wholesale** — Brand outreach without a website = rejection
7. **Not providing value to brands** — Wholesale is about relationships, not just buying
8. **Staying in one model too long** — RA has a ceiling; scale requires OA or wholesale
9. **Not tracking total landed cost** — Ignoring fees, shipping, storage, and placement costs
10. **Selling products without proper authorization** — IP complaints can destroy your account

### Gated Categories & IP Protection Strategy

- **Priority ungating categories:** Grocery, Health & Beauty, Topicals, Major Brands
- **Use IP Alert** to check brand risk before purchasing
- **Brand-direct accounts** eliminate most IP complaint risk (you have authorization)
- **Keep invoices** — wholesale invoices from authorized distributors protect you
- **Retail receipts are NOT valid documentation** for IP complaints
- **If you get an IP complaint:** Contact brand owner immediately, request retraction letter
- **Check product restrictions** before sourcing using SellerAmp's eligibility feature

### Financial Frameworks

**Starting Capital Tiers:**
- **$500–$1,500:** Retail arbitrage (Jazz, Warner/Miles recommend)
- **$5,000–$10,000:** Online arbitrage at scale
- **$10,000–$20,000+:** Wholesale (Dan Boufford's model)

**Revenue Milestones:**
- Month 1–2: Learn tools, get ungated, first shipments (may not break even)
- Month 3–5: $5K–$10K/month in sales
- Month 6–12: $10K–$25K/month if consistent
- Year 1+: $10K+/month profit achievable (Warner/Miles target)
- At scale: $20M+/year possible (Dan Boufford at wholesale)

**Margin Targets:**
- Retail Arbitrage: 30–45% profit margin (Warner/Miles target)
- Online Arbitrage: 20–30% ROI minimum
- Wholesale: Lower margins but higher volume and consistency

### Automation & Delegation Hierarchy

1. **Tactical Arbitrage** — Automate product discovery (OA)
2. **SellerAmp / BuyBotPro** — Automate deal analysis
3. **Keepa** — Automate price/rank monitoring
4. **Dynamic repricing (BQool/SellerSnap)** — Automate pricing
5. **Prep centers** — Outsource shipment preparation
6. **Virtual Assistants** — Delegate sourcing, listing, customer service
7. **International team** — Full operations delegation (Dan's model)
