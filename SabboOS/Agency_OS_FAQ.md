## HOW IT WORKS

---

### 1. What exactly do you do? What is a "Growth Operating System"?

We build and operate the full customer acquisition infrastructure for founder-led businesses. That means paid ads, landing pages, email sequences, CRM automation, attribution, and conversion optimization — all connected into a single system with clear scorecards and KPIs. Think of it as replacing the patchwork of freelancers, tools, and agencies you have now with one integrated growth engine and one operator who owns the outcome. We install the system in 30 days, then run it for you month over month so you can focus on product and team instead of managing marketing.

Most businesses have the individual pieces — an ad account, a website, a CRM, maybe an email tool. The problem is none of it is connected. Leads fall through the cracks. Attribution is a guessing game. Nobody owns the full picture. A Growth Operating System connects every layer — acquisition, conversion, retention, and data — into one machine with one operator accountable for the outcome.

**Closer note:** If the prospect asks this on a call, keep the answer to 60 seconds. Use the line: "We build the system behind the ads — then run it for you." If they want more detail, walk them through the four layers (acquisition, conversion, retention, data) in 30 seconds each.

---

### 2. How is this different from a normal agency?

Traditional agencies assign you an account manager who juggles 15 clients, runs your ads, and sends you a monthly PDF report. We embed a senior growth operator directly into your business. They own your full funnel — not just the ad account. They build documented SOPs, track real KPIs (not vanity metrics), and are accountable for revenue outcomes, not campaign deliverables. The agency model optimizes for client volume. The operator model optimizes for client results. That is the core difference.

Here is a practical way to see it: an agency sends you a report showing CPM and CTR. We send you a scorecard showing cost per qualified lead, pipeline value generated, close rate on leads we produced, and revenue attributed to the system. We care about what happens after the click, not just the click itself.

**Closer note:** This is the single most important differentiator. If a prospect has been burned by agencies, lean into the operator vs. account manager distinction. Use the line: "Your operator has 3-5 clients, not 15. They know your ICP, your product, your numbers."

---

### 3. What does the 30-day install look like?

Week one is a full audit — your funnel, ad accounts, CRM, email flows, attribution, and sales handoff process. We identify every leak and set 90-day OKRs. Weeks two and three are the build — new or rebuilt landing pages, restructured ad account, email sequences (intake, nurture, no-show, win-back), CRM automation, tracking and attribution setup, and creative production. Week four is launch — campaigns go live across tested angles, data starts flowing, and the system begins producing leads. By day 30, you have a fully operational growth engine with a dedicated operator running it.

From there, it is ongoing optimization: daily ad monitoring, weekly client calls, creative iteration every 10 days, monthly deep-dive reviews, and quarterly OKR resets. The system compounds — it gets better every month as data accumulates and we optimize each layer.

**Closer note:** Walk through the timeline visually if possible. Prospects need to see that 30 days is fast but structured, not rushed.

---

### 4. Do you replace our current marketing team?

Not necessarily. If you have an in-house team, we work alongside them and fill the gaps. Most founder-led businesses we work with either have no dedicated growth team (the founder is doing it) or have a team that handles execution but lacks the strategic infrastructure to make everything work together. We bring the system, the playbooks, and the operator. If you already have a media buyer, we give them a better framework. If you already have a CRM person, we connect their work to the full funnel. We complement what you have, or we handle it entirely if you do not have it.

In many cases, once the system is built and running, clients use the documented SOPs and scorecards to eventually bring execution in-house with confidence. The system we build is yours to keep — we do not hold infrastructure hostage.

**Closer note:** If the prospect has a marketing team, frame us as the "strategic layer" and the "connective tissue" their team is missing. Do not position as a replacement — that creates internal resistance.

---

### 5. What industries do you work with?

We work with founder-led businesses across B2B services, SaaS, e-commerce, and professional services. The common thread is not industry — it is stage. Our best-fit clients have product-market fit, are doing at least $1M in annual revenue, and have validated demand for their product or service. They are past the "does this work?" phase and are stuck in the "how do I scale this predictably?" phase. If that describes you, the model fits regardless of industry.

Who we do NOT work with: early-stage pre-revenue startups that have not validated their offer, businesses with no existing sales process, or companies looking for someone to "run some ads and see what happens." We build growth systems for businesses that already work — they just need the infrastructure to scale predictably.

**Closer note:** Use the "who this is NOT for" framing on calls. It builds trust and self-selects the right prospects. If someone is pre-PMF, refer them to a consultant or coach instead — do not force the fit.

## INVESTMENT

---

### 6. How much does it cost?

Our engagements range from $5K to $25K per month depending on scope, complexity, and which layers of the growth system need to be built. A business that needs a full rebuild from scratch (funnel, ads, email, CRM, attribution) is a larger engagement than one that has solid infrastructure but needs an operator to run and optimize it. The exact scope and pricing is determined on the strategy call after we understand your situation, goals, and current infrastructure. Ad spend is separate and paid directly to the platform — we do not markup ad spend or take a percentage of it.

**Closer note:** Give the range confidently. Do not apologize for the price. If they balk, ask: "What are you currently spending on marketing tools, freelancers, and agency fees combined?" The answer is usually more than our retainer — we just consolidate it into one system with one operator and better results.

---

### 7. What is the minimum commitment?

We ask for a 90-day minimum commitment. It takes 30 days to build the infrastructure and another 30-60 days to generate enough data to optimize and scale. Agencies that let you go month-to-month are incentivized to show quick vanity wins rather than build real systems. We are incentivized to build something that compounds over time. Most of our clients stay for 12+ months because the system keeps producing results, but we do not lock you into long-term contracts. After 90 days, it is month-to-month.

The 90-day structure protects both sides. It gives us enough runway to build, launch, and optimize. It gives you enough data to make an informed decision about whether to continue. We have never had a client leave at 90 days because the system is producing by then — but the option is always there.

**Closer note:** If a prospect pushes back on 90 days, ask: "How long did your last agency take to show real results?" The answer is usually "they never did" or "6+ months." Ninety days with a system is faster than 12 months of campaign guessing.

---

### 8. What if it does not work? Do you have a guarantee?

We guarantee the system gets built and launched within 30 days. If we fail to deliver the infrastructure on time, you do not pay for that month. We do not guarantee specific revenue outcomes because results depend on factors we do not control — your product, your market, your sales team, your pricing. What we do guarantee is that you will have a functioning, measurable growth system that did not exist before. If after 90 days the system is not producing qualified leads at a reasonable cost, we will diagnose why and either fix it or tell you honestly that the problem is upstream of marketing.

We are also honest about what we cannot fix. If the core offer does not convert, no growth system will save it. If the sales team cannot close qualified leads, more leads will not help. Part of our value is telling you the truth about where the real bottleneck is — even when the answer is not "spend more on ads."

**Closer note:** Never promise specific revenue numbers. The infrastructure guarantee is strong enough. If the prospect pushes hard for a revenue guarantee, it is usually a red flag — they may not have confidence in their own offer or sales process. Qualify deeper before closing.

---

### 9. Can we start with a smaller scope and scale up?

Yes. Many clients start with one or two layers — usually paid acquisition and conversion infrastructure — and expand into email, retention, and organic once the core system is producing. We would rather start focused and expand based on results than try to do everything at once. The strategy call is where we determine what the highest-leverage starting point is for your specific business.

In practice, most clients start between $5K and $10K/mo for core acquisition infrastructure and scale to $15K-$25K/mo as we add layers and the ROI justifies expansion. The system is modular by design — each layer works independently but performs better when connected to the others.

**Closer note:** This is a strong answer for price-sensitive prospects. Frame it as "start where it makes sense, prove the model, then expand." Removes the all-or-nothing pressure.

## RESULTS

---

### 10. How quickly will we see results?

The system goes live on day 30. You will see lead flow within the first week of launch. Meaningful data on cost per lead, conversion rates, and pipeline quality typically comes in during weeks 5-8. Optimized, scalable performance — where you know your numbers and can predictably increase or decrease spend — usually takes 60-90 days. If you are expecting results in week one, this is not the right model for you. If you are willing to invest 90 days to build something that compounds, this is exactly the right model.

Here is why the timeline matters: we do not spend a dollar on ads until the infrastructure behind the ads is built. That means your landing page converts before we send traffic. Your email sequences nurture before we generate leads. Your CRM tracks before we fill it. This front-loaded build phase is what separates a growth system from a campaign.

**Closer note:** Set expectations clearly on this one. Underpromise on timeline, overdeliver on results. If a prospect needs leads tomorrow, they need a lead broker, not a growth system. Be direct about that.

---

### 11. What kind of ROI can we expect?

Our target is a minimum 3x return on total investment (our fee plus your ad spend) within the first 90 days, measured on a lifetime value basis. Some clients see that in 60 days. Some take the full 90. It depends on your average deal size, your sales cycle, and how quickly your team closes the leads we generate. A $10K/mo engagement producing $30K+ in new revenue within 90 days is the baseline we aim for. Many clients significantly exceed that once the system is optimized and scaling.

We measure ROI on LTV, not first-purchase revenue, because the system is designed to reduce CAC and increase retention over time. A client acquired through a well-built funnel with proper nurture sequences stays longer and refers more than a client acquired through a cold ad with no follow-up infrastructure.

**Closer note:** Use specific math when possible. If their average deal is $5K and they close 25% of calls, and we produce 20 qualified calls a month, that is $25K in new revenue. Make the prospect do the math themselves — it is more convincing than you telling them a number.

---

### 12. Do you have case studies?

Yes. We share relevant case studies during the strategy call based on your industry, business model, and growth stage. We do not publish generic case studies on the website because every engagement is different and we want to show you examples that are directly relevant to your situation. If you want to see proof before the call, ask your DM contact or reply to any of our emails and we will send you the most relevant one.

Each case study follows a consistent format: where the client was before (revenue, CAC, pain points), what we built (specific infrastructure), and what happened after (metrics, timeline, outcome). We use real numbers with client permission.

**Closer note:** Always have 2-3 case studies ready that match the prospect's industry or business model. Send the most relevant one before the call if they ask. If no exact industry match exists, use one that matches their growth stage and pain point — stage matters more than industry.

## PROCESS

---

### 13. What happens after we apply?

You fill out a short application (5-7 questions about your business, revenue, and growth goals). If you look like a fit based on your answers, we book a 30-minute strategy call with a growth strategist on our team. On that call, we dig into your current situation, identify the biggest gaps, and give you honest feedback on where the leverage is. If there is a mutual fit, we present a scope and proposal. If there is not, you walk away with clarity on what to fix. No pressure, no 90-minute webinar pitch, no bait and switch.

The application is a filter, not a hoop. We ask about revenue, team size, current marketing setup, and growth goals so we can come to the call prepared with relevant examples and honest feedback. If you are not a fit, we tell you before the call — we do not waste your time or ours.

**Closer note:** Emphasize the "no pressure" angle. Prospects who have been through high-pressure agency sales processes will appreciate the honesty. Use the line from the cold call script: "It is a strategy session — if there is a fit, great. If not, you walk away with clarity."

---

### 14. What do we need to provide on our end?

Access to your ad accounts, CRM, analytics (GA4), email platform, and website backend. A 90-minute kickoff call where we align on your ICP, positioning, offer, and goals. And a point of contact on your team (usually the founder or head of sales) who is available for a 30-minute weekly check-in. That is it. We handle the build, the launch, and the ongoing operations. You do not need to write copy, design creative, or manage campaigns. We bring the team and the system.

The weekly check-in is important because growth systems work best when the operator understands the business deeply. The 30-minute call is not a status update — it is a strategy alignment. We discuss what is working, what changed in the market, and what we are testing next. Your input on sales feedback and customer behavior makes the system smarter every week.

**Closer note:** Prospects often worry about the time commitment on their end. Reassure them: the kickoff is the biggest time investment (90 minutes). After that, it is 30 minutes a week. Compare that to the hours they currently spend managing marketing themselves.

---

### 15. How do we communicate? What is the reporting cadence?

You get a dedicated Slack channel (or your preferred communication tool) with direct access to your growth operator. No support tickets, no account manager gatekeeping. Daily monitoring happens internally — your operator watches the numbers every day and makes adjustments in real time. You receive a weekly scorecard with clear KPIs: spend, leads, qualified leads, cost per lead, conversion rates, and pipeline value. We have a 30-minute weekly call to review performance and align on next steps. Monthly, we do a deeper strategy review where we assess what is working, what to scale, and what to kill. Quarterly, we reset OKRs and revisit positioning if needed.

Transparency is non-negotiable. You will always know exactly how much is being spent, where it is going, what results it is producing, and what we are doing about it. If something is underperforming, you will know within 24 hours — not at the end of the month in a PDF. Our operators flag issues proactively and present solutions, not excuses.

**Closer note:** This is a major selling point for founders who have felt "in the dark" with previous agencies. Lean into the direct Slack access and real-time scorecard visibility. The line "no support tickets, no gatekeeping" resonates strongly with founders who have been stuck behind account managers.

## CLOSER NOTES

When using this FAQ on sales calls, remember:

- Answer the question directly, then bridge to booking or next steps. Do not let FAQ answers turn into 10-minute monologues.
- If the prospect asks about price before the call, give the range ($5K-$25K/mo) and bridge: "The exact scope depends on what you need. That is what the call is for."
- If they push on guarantees, lean into the infrastructure guarantee and the 90-day commitment structure. Do not promise revenue numbers.
- The "not an agency" distinction is the most powerful differentiator. Use it in every answer where it fits.
- If they ask a question not on this list, answer honestly and log the question so we can add it.
- Mirror the prospect's language. If they say "leads," say "leads." If they say "pipeline," say "pipeline." Do not correct their terminology.
- When a prospect asks multiple questions in a row, it means they are interested but need reassurance. That is a buying signal, not resistance.

## LANDING PAGE FAQ FORMATTING

When using these on the landing page, follow these rules:

- Use the question exactly as written (these are phrased the way prospects actually ask them)
- Use accordion/expandable format — all collapsed by default
- Show only the first paragraph of each answer on the landing page (omit closer notes and extra context)
- Group headers (How It Works, Investment, Results, Process) should be visible as section labels
- Place the FAQ section above the footer but below the final CTA
- The FAQ section itself should include a soft CTA at the bottom: "Still have questions? Apply and ask on the call — no pressure."
- Do not include all 15 on the landing page. Pick the top 8-10 most commonly asked. Use the full 15 in the closer reference doc.

These cover the four main objection categories: understanding, price, risk, and process.
