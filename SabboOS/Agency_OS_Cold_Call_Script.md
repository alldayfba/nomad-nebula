# AGENCY OS — SETTER COLD CALL PLAYBOOK

**Your Role:** Qualify founders. Book strategy calls. Protect closer time.
**Last Updated:** March 16, 2026

---
---

# SECTION 1: YOUR MISSION

```
You are NOT selling. You are qualifying and booking.

Your ONLY job ──► Get qualified founders on a strategy call with a closer.

You don't pitch the offer.
You don't talk pricing.
You don't try to close.
You find pain, confirm fit, and book the call.
```

**Who You're Calling**
- Founders doing $1M+/year
- Have product-market fit (customers love what they sell)
- Do NOT have a repeatable growth system
- Burning money on ads, freelancers, or doing marketing themselves
- They KNOW it's broken — they just haven't fixed it yet

**What You're Booking Them Into**
- 30-45 minute strategy call with a closer
- Closer diagnoses their growth bottleneck + presents the Growth OS
- NOT a "sales call" — it's a strategy session

# SECTION 2: THE CALL FLOW

```
Total call time: 2-3 minutes. That's it.

    PHASE 1 ──► Pattern Interrupt .............. 10 seconds
    PHASE 2 ──► The Hook ....................... 15-20 seconds
    PHASE 3 ──► Dig Into Pain .................. 60-90 seconds
    PHASE 4 ──► The Bridge ..................... 15-20 seconds
    PHASE 5 ──► Book the Call .................. 30-45 seconds
    PHASE 6 ──► Protect the Show ............... After hang-up
```

---

## PHASE 1: PATTERN INTERRUPT
**Goal:** Get 30 seconds of their time. That's all.
**Time:** 10 seconds
**Tone:** Calm. Direct. Slightly curious. NOT hyped up.

### Primary Opener

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] — I know this is out of the blue, do you have 30 seconds?"

### If They Push Back

| They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| **"Who is this?"** / **"What's this about?"** | "Totally fair — I work with growth systems for founder-led businesses. I was looking at [their company] and had a quick question. Literally 30 seconds." |
| **"I'm busy"** | "Totally get it. When's a better 2 minutes? I'll call you back then." |
| **"How'd you get my number?"** | "Your business came up when I was researching [their industry] in [their area]. I'm not trying to waste your time — genuinely had a question about how you're handling growth. 30 seconds?" |

### Rules
- Do NOT say your company name — it means nothing to them yet
- Do NOT pitch in the opener — you're asking for time, not selling
- Match their energy — relaxed person = relaxed tone, direct person = direct tone

---

## PHASE 2: THE HOOK
**Goal:** Make them curious enough to keep talking.
**Time:** 15-20 seconds
**Key Principle:** Always use yes/no or either/or questions — NOT open-ended. Either/or gets 4x the response rate.

### Primary Hook

> "I was looking at [Company] — looks like you've built something solid. Quick question: is your growth right now more systematic, or is it more like... you're figuring it out as you go?"

Almost every founder will say some version of "figuring it out." That's your in.

### Alternative Hooks (choose based on your 60-second research)

| Situation | Hook |
|---|---|
| **They're running ads** | "I noticed you're running ads — are you happy with what you're getting back from that spend, or does it feel like you're kind of guessing?" |
| **Big following, no clear funnel** | "You've got a solid following — are you actually converting that into revenue systematically, or is it more word-of-mouth right now?" |
| **Recently hired/fired an agency** | "I saw you were working with [agency/freelancer]. Are you still with them, or are you looking for something different?" |

### After You Ask — SHUT UP. Let them talk.

---

## PHASE 3: DIG INTO THE PAIN
**Goal:** Understand their situation. Confirm they're qualified. Identify their #1 pain.
**Time:** 60-90 seconds
**Key Principle:** Listen 70%, talk 30%. You are diagnosing, not pitching.

### Pick 2-3 of These Questions (based on conversation flow)

```
"What does your customer acquisition look like right now —
 where do most of your clients come from?"

"Do you have a marketing team or person handling this, or is it on you?"

"What have you tried that hasn't worked?"

"What would it look like if this was actually dialed in?
 What would change for you?"

"What's the cost of NOT fixing this?
 Like, how much are you leaving on the table every month?"
```

### Qualification Checklist (mental — don't read this to them)

```
    QUALIFIED ✓                              DISQUALIFY ✗
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Revenue $1M+/year                        Under $1M or pre-revenue
    Has product-market fit                   No clear product or market
    Spending money on marketing              "We don't really do marketing"
    Frustrated, wants to fix it              Looking for "cheap help"
    Founder is decision maker                Needs board approval for a call
    Willing to invest in a solution          Wants free advice
```

### After They Talk — Recap Their Pain Back to Them

> "So it sounds like the main issue is [X], and that's costing you roughly [Y] a month — is that right?"

This does two things: (1) shows you were listening, (2) makes the pain feel more real when they hear it reflected back.

### If They're NOT Qualified — Be Honest and Move On

> "Appreciate the time — honestly based on what you're telling me, I don't think this would be the right fit right now. But if things change, I'd love to reconnect."

Do NOT book bad leads. It destroys closer time and YOUR show rate.

---

## PHASE 4: THE BRIDGE
**Goal:** Connect their pain to the strategy call. NOT to the offer.
**Time:** 15-20 seconds. Two to three sentences. That's it.

### Primary Bridge

> "Look, this is exactly the kind of thing we help with. We work with founder-led businesses in the [their revenue range] and build out the entire growth engine — not just run ads, but install the full system so it's predictable. I think it'd be worth a conversation. Not a pitch — just a strategy session to see if there's a fit."

### Alternative Bridges

> "We've helped a few businesses in [their space] go from exactly what you're describing to a predictable pipeline. It might make sense to jump on a quick call with [Closer Name] to see if we can do the same."

> "Honestly, what you're describing is a systems problem, not a marketing problem. That's what we solve. Would it make sense to get on a 30-minute call to dig into this properly?"

### Rules
- Do NOT explain the offer in detail — that's the closer's job
- Say "strategy session" or "conversation" — NEVER "sales call" or "pitch"
- If you have a case study: "We helped [similar business] go from random leads to $X/month in 90 days"

---

## PHASE 5: BOOK THE CALL
**Goal:** Lock in the time. Get tie-downs. Protect the show.
**Time:** 30-45 seconds
**Key Principle:** Assume the booking. Don't ask "would you be interested?" — give a choice of times.

### The Booking

> "I've got time on [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] — which works better for you?"

### Once They Pick a Time

> "Perfect. I'll send you a calendar link right now. A couple things — the call is with [Closer Name], it's about 30 minutes. Before the call, I'll send you a short video that'll give you context on how we work so you get the most out of it. And [Name] — just make sure you're somewhere quiet with no distractions. Cool?"

### Tie-Downs (NON-NEGOTIABLE — Do These Before You Hang Up)

**1. Decision-Maker Tie-Down**

> "Quick question — if you liked what you heard on the call, is there anyone else who'd need to be involved in the decision? A business partner, COO, anyone?"

```
    If YES ──► "Can they be on the call too?"
               If they can't ──► Reschedule to when both can attend.

    If NO  ──► "So just to confirm — if you wanted to move forward,
               you wouldn't need anyone else's sign-off?"
```

**2. Financial Tie-Down (optional — use when rapport is strong)**

> "Just so the call is valuable for everyone — our clients typically invest $5K-$25K a month depending on scope. Is that realistic for you if the fit is right?"

```
    If YES ──► Great. Move on.

    If NO / hesitation ──► "No pressure at all — I just want to make sure
                           the call is worth your time. If budget is tight,
                           maybe we reconnect in a couple months?"
```

---

## PHASE 6: PROTECT THE SHOW
**Goal:** Get them to actually show up. This is where most setters lose.
**Key Principle:** Booking is step 1. Getting them to SHOW UP is the actual job.

### The 6-Touchpoint Show Rate System

```
WITHIN 5 MINUTES OF BOOKING
├── 1. Send calendar invite with Zoom link
└── 2. Text: "Hey [Name], [Your Name] here — just sent over the
         calendar link for [Day] at [Time]. Looking forward to it."

WITHIN 1 HOUR
└── 3. Send pre-call video MATCHED TO THEIR PAIN POINT:
         "Hey [Name], before your call with [Closer], this video
          is super relevant to what you mentioned about [their
          specific bottleneck]. Worth a watch. [link]"

24 HOURS BEFORE
└── 4. Text: "Hey [Name]! Quick reminder — you've got your call
         with [Closer] tomorrow at [Time]. Still good?"
         └── No reply? ──► Call them.

MORNING OF
└── 5. Text: "[Name]!"
         ...then 30 seconds later:
         "It's [Your Name] from [Company]. We still good
          for [Time] today?"

IF NO-SHOW AT CALL TIME
├── 6a. Call twice.
├── 6b. Voicemail: "Hey [Name], [Your Name] here — we had you
│        down for [Time] today. No worries if something came up.
│        Shoot me a text and we'll get you rescheduled."
└── 6c. Email, subject: "^ bumping this" — short, no guilt trip.
```

**Show rate target: 80%+**

If you're below that, it's one of two problems:
1. You're booking unqualified leads (fix qualification)
2. You're not following up enough (fix follow-up discipline)

# SECTION 3: OBJECTION HANDLING

Handle and redirect. Don't argue. Don't get stuck debating. Acknowledge, redirect, move forward.

---

| Objection | Response | Then... |
|---|---|---|
| **"I'm not interested."** | "Totally respect that. Out of curiosity — is that because growth is already dialed in, or is it more of a timing thing?" | If timing → "When's better to revisit?" If dialed → "Best of luck — keep me in mind." |
| **"Just send me an email."** | "Happy to — what's the best email? And just so I send something relevant, what's the biggest marketing challenge you're dealing with right now?" | If they answer the question, you're back in conversation. |
| **"We already have an agency."** | "Nice — are you happy with the results? I only ask because a lot of founders I talk to have a team but still feel like growth is unpredictable." | If unhappy → "What's not working?" If happy → "Not trying to fix what's not broken." |
| **"What do you guys do exactly?"** | "Short version — we install a full growth operating system into founder-led businesses. Acquisition, conversion, retention — and we operate it for you. But honestly, the call is where we'd dig into whether it makes sense for you." | Bridge back to booking. Do NOT get pulled into a 10-min explanation. |
| **"I don't have time for a call."** | "Totally get it — you're busy, that's exactly why this might be worth it. The call is 30 min and it's specifically about getting your time back from marketing." | Offer a specific time: "What if we did [Day] at [early AM / lunch / end of day]?" |
| **"Is this a sales call?"** | "It's a strategy session — [Closer] looks at what you're doing now and gives honest feedback on where the gaps are. If there's a fit, great. If not, you walk away with clarity. No pressure either way." | Bridge to booking. |

# SECTION 5: WHO TO BOOK vs. WHO TO DROP

```
    BOOK ✓                                  DROP ✗
    ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Revenue $1M+/year                       Under $1M or pre-revenue
    Has PMF — customers love the product    No clear product or market
    Spending on marketing (even poorly)     "We don't do marketing"
    Frustrated + wants to fix it            Wants "cheap help" / discount
    Founder is the decision maker           Needs a board vote to talk
    Can invest $5K-$25K/mo if fit is right  No budget, startup with no funding
    Willing to get on a strategy call       "Just send info" and won't engage
```

**It's better to book 3 qualified calls than 8 unqualified ones.**

Close rate on bad leads: 0%.
Close rate on good leads: 40-60%.

Every bad booking costs the closer an hour of momentum. Protect their calendar like it's your own commission.

# SECTION 7: BEFORE YOU DIAL (60-SECOND RESEARCH)

Do this for every lead. Not optional. Leads with context convert 3-4x higher.

```
    1. Open their website
       └── What do they sell? Who do they sell to?

    2. Check LinkedIn or Instagram
       └── How big? Running ads? Posting content?

    3. Spot obvious gaps
       └── No email capture? Weak landing page?
           No retargeting? No funnel? No content?

    4. Find ONE specific thing to reference on the call
       └── "I saw you're running Facebook ads..."
           "I noticed your site doesn't have..."
           "Looks like you're doing a lot on Instagram..."
```

# SECTION 8: MINDSET

```
1.  You are a filter, not a pitcher.
    Your job is to find the right people and get them in the room.
    The closer does the rest.

2.  Tonality > words.
    Sound like a confident, calm human — not a script reader.
    "Don't be Jordan Belfort. Be consultative." — Ben Bader

3.  Rejection is data.
    Every "no" sharpens your targeting.
    Don't take it personally. Don't argue. Move on. Next dial.

4.  Show rate is YOUR number.
    If they don't show, that's on you — not the closer,
    not the prospect. Follow up harder.

5.  Bad bookings are worse than no bookings.
    A closer who wastes 3 calls on unqualified leads
    loses momentum for the rest of the day.
    Protect their calendar like it's your own commission.

6.  The math is simple.
    50 dials → 10 conversations → 3 qualified → 2 booked → 1.5 show
    Do this every day and the numbers take care of themselves.

7.  Energy is everything.
    If you sound bored, they hang up.
    If you sound desperate, they hang up.
    Sound like someone who has something valuable
    and is checking if they're the right fit.
```
