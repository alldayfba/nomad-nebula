# Agency OS — DM Conversation Playbooks
> Version 1.0 | Created: 2026-03-21
> Reference: Hormozi DM Setting Breakdown, Nik Setting Story Funnel + Opener Philosophy, Cold Call Script objection table

---

## Operating Principles

1. The purpose of the first message is ONLY to get a response. Not to sell. Not to explain. Get a reply.
2. Use yes/no or either/or questions, never open-ended ("tell me about your business"). Either/or questions get 3-5x higher response rates than open-ended.
3. Qualify on three things: commitment, urgency, and resources. If any of the three is missing, do not book.
4. Every conversation gets logged in GHL immediately. No exceptions.
5. Never send the booking link until all three qualifiers are confirmed.
6. Match the prospect's energy and diction. If they write casually, write casually. If they write formally, adjust.
7. Voice memos beat text for building trust. Use them strategically, not as openers.

## PART 1: LINKEDIN DM SEQUENCE

LinkedIn is the professional channel. Prospects here are business owners who expect business conversations. The tone is direct, respectful, and value-forward. No hype, no emojis, no slang.

---

### Connection Request Notes (< 300 characters)

Pick the one that fits the prospect's profile best. Always personalize the bracketed sections.

**Variation A — Mutual Interest:**
Hey [First Name] — saw you run [their company]. We work with founder-led businesses on growth infrastructure. Would love to connect and share some notes if relevant.

**Variation B — Content Reference:**
[First Name] — your post on [specific topic] was sharp. We help founders in [their industry] build predictable acquisition systems. Happy to connect.

**Variation C — Direct:**
[First Name], I help 7-8 figure founders install growth systems so they stop managing marketing. Thought it might be relevant to [their company]. Either way, happy to connect.

---

### Opening DM After Acceptance (send within 24 hours)

Do NOT pitch. Ask an either/or question that identifies their situation. The goal is to start a conversation, not deliver a monologue.

**Variation A — Bottleneck Identifier:**
Thanks for connecting, [First Name]. Quick question — when it comes to growth at [their company], is the bigger challenge generating enough leads, or converting the ones you already have?

**Variation B — System Check:**
Appreciate the connect. Curious — do you guys have a documented acquisition system at [their company], or is growth still mostly founder-driven right now?

**Variation C — Agency Experience:**
Good to connect. Out of curiosity — are you handling marketing in-house at [their company], or are you working with an agency right now?

---

### Qualification Flow (4-5 messages deep)

This is where you qualify commitment, urgency, and resources. Follow the branching logic based on their responses.

**After they respond to the opener:**

Message 2 — Dig into the pain:
"Got it. And is that something you have been actively trying to solve, or more of a backburner thing right now?"

- If backburner → "Totally fair. When it does become a priority, I have some frameworks I can share. Mind if I send you something useful in the meantime?" (Move to nurture)
- If actively solving → Continue to Message 3

Message 3 — Establish urgency:
"Makes sense. What is driving the urgency on it — is there a revenue target you are trying to hit this quarter, or is it more about getting the infrastructure right before scaling?"

- If no urgency → Park in nurture. Send a case study. Revisit in 14 days.
- If clear urgency → Continue to Message 4

Message 4 — Qualify resources:
"Appreciate you sharing that. And just to make sure I am not wasting your time — do you currently have budget allocated for growth infrastructure, or is that something you would need to figure out first?"

- If no budget → "No worries at all. Here is a case study that shows the model — [link]. When budget opens up, reach out anytime." (Disqualify gracefully)
- If budget exists → Continue to Message 5

Message 5 — Bridge to booking:
"Sounds like there might be a real fit here. The best next step would be a 30-minute strategy call with [Closer Name] — they will look at what you are doing now, identify the gaps, and give you honest feedback on where the biggest leverage is. No pitch, no pressure. If there is a fit we will tell you. If not, you walk away with clarity. Want me to send you a link?"

---

### Booking Transition

Once they agree to a call:

"Perfect. Here is the link — [Calendly URL]. Pick a time that works and make sure you are in a quiet spot with 30 minutes free. No need to prep anything — [Closer Name] will walk you through everything. Just drop me a quick confirmation with the time you booked and I will make sure the team is prepped on your end."

After they confirm:

"Locked in. [Closer Name] will reach out before the call with a quick note. Looking forward to hearing how it goes."

---

### Follow-Up Cadence

**Day 1 (no response to opener):**
"Hey [First Name] — just bumping this up. Was genuinely curious about [reference the either/or question]. No pressure either way."

**Day 3 (still no response):**
"[First Name] — totally understand if timing is off. Dropping a quick case study here in case it is useful down the line — [link]. Happy to chat whenever."

**Day 7 (still no response):**
Send a short, relevant insight. Not a follow-up — a value touch.
"Saw something this week that reminded me of [their industry]. [1-2 sentence insight about a growth lever relevant to their business]. Thought you might find it interesting."

**Day 14 (final touch):**
"Hey [First Name] — last note from me. If growth infrastructure ever becomes a priority at [their company], my door is open. Wishing you a strong Q[X]."

---

### Disqualification Script (Graceful Exit)

Use when the prospect clearly does not fit: pre-revenue, no budget, no urgency, or wrong business model.

"Honestly, based on what you have shared, I do not think this would be the right fit right now. We work best with businesses that already have product-market fit and are looking to build predictable acquisition — it sounds like you are still in the [earlier stage / different phase]. That said, here is a [resource/case study] that might help. If things change down the road, reach out anytime."

---

### "Not Now" Parking Script

For qualified prospects where timing is wrong. Add to nurture sequence in GHL with a 30-day revisit tag.

"Totally get it — timing matters. I will make a note to circle back in [30/60/90 days]. In the meantime, I will send you a case study and a couple of frameworks that might be useful. No follow-up spam, I promise. When the timing is right, just reply to this thread."

## PART 2: INSTAGRAM DM SEQUENCE

Instagram is the casual channel. Prospects here found you through stories, reels, or ads. The tone is conversational, human, and low-pressure. Write like you are texting a friend who also happens to run a business.

---

### Story Reply Openers (Most Natural Entry Point)

Story replies are the highest-converting DM opener because the prospect initiated the interaction. They saw your story and reacted. Meet them where they are.

**Variation A — Acknowledge + Pivot:**
When they reply to a story with a reaction or short comment:
"Appreciate that. You run [their business] right? Curious — is the growth side of things dialed in or still a work in progress?"

**Variation B — Go Deeper on the Story Topic:**
When they reply with a question or comment about the content:
"Yeah exactly. That is one of the biggest things we see with founders at your stage. Quick question — is the bottleneck on the lead gen side or the conversion side for you guys?"

**Variation C — Reciprocate Interest:**
When they react to a case study or results story:
"Thanks. That one was a fun build. Are you guys dealing with anything similar right now, or is growth pretty locked in?"

---

### Cold DM Openers (Profile-Based Personalization)

For outbound DMs to prospects who have not engaged yet. Always check their profile first — do they post content? Do they have a business? Are they in the right revenue range? Do not message unqualified profiles.

**Variation A — Content Compliment:**
"Hey [First Name] — been seeing your posts about [specific topic they post about]. Solid stuff. Quick question — when it comes to scaling [their business], is the main challenge right now getting more leads or converting the ones you have?"

**Variation B — Mutual Connection:**
"Hey — noticed we are both connected with [mutual follow]. I work with founders in [their space] on building growth systems. Is that something you are actively working on or pretty set on that front?"

**Variation C — Direct + Value:**
"Hey [First Name]. I help founder-led businesses install growth operating systems — basically turn marketing from guesswork into a machine. Saw you run [their company]. Is growth something you are actively trying to figure out right now, or is it dialed in?"

---

### Qualification Flow

Same three qualifiers as LinkedIn — commitment, urgency, resources — but the tone is looser.

**After they respond:**

Message 2 — Pain or priority:
"Got it. So is that something you are actively trying to fix right now, or more of a 'yeah I know I need to deal with it eventually' kind of thing?"

- If eventually → "No worries. I will send you something useful — check this out [case study link]. Hit me up when it moves up the priority list."
- If actively → Continue

Message 3 — Urgency check:
"What is making it urgent now? Revenue target? Trying to scale before [season/event]? Or just tired of the inconsistency?"

- If vague → One more qualifying question: "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your growth right now, what would it be?"
- If clear urgency → Continue

Message 4 — Resource check:
"Makes sense. And real talk — do you have budget set aside for this, or would that need to be figured out first? Just want to respect your time."

- If no budget → Nurture. Send value. Revisit in 30 days.
- If budget confirmed → Continue

Message 5 — Booking:
"Cool. Best next step would be a quick call with [Closer Name] — 30 min, no BS. They will look at what you have now and tell you exactly where the gaps are. If it is a fit, great. If not, you walk away with free intel. Want me to send the link?"

---

### Booking Transition

"Here you go — [Calendly URL]. Grab a time that works. Just make sure you have 30 min and a quiet spot. Drop me the time when you book and I will make sure [Closer Name] is prepped."

After booking confirmation:

"Locked in. You are going to like [Closer Name] — they are sharp. Talk soon."

---

### Follow-Up Cadence

**Day 1 (no response):**
"Hey — just making sure this did not get buried. [Reference original either/or question]."

**Day 3 (still no response):**
Send a voice memo (see guidelines below). 15-20 seconds max.

**Day 7 (still no response):**
Share a relevant reel, story, or case study. No ask attached.
"Thought this might be relevant to what you guys are building — [link or share post]."

**Day 14 (final touch):**
"No worries if timing is off. If growth infrastructure ever becomes a focus, hit me up. Rooting for [their company]."

---

### Voice Memo Guidelines

Voice memos build trust faster than text because they prove you are a real person who took time to record a personal message. Use them strategically — not as openers, but as follow-ups or booking confirmations.

**When to send:**
- Day 3 follow-up (if no response to text)
- After they express interest but before booking (to build rapport)
- After they book (to increase show-up rate)

**30-Second Script Template:**
"Hey [First Name], it is [Your Name]. Just wanted to reach out real quick — I saw you [reference something specific about their business or their response]. I think there might be a real fit here. The call with [Closer Name] is going to be super valuable either way — they will break down exactly where the gaps are and give you a clear picture of what is possible. Just wanted to put a voice to the name. Talk soon."

**Rules:**
- Keep it under 30 seconds
- Reference something specific (their business, their response, their content)
- Sound natural, not scripted
- Do NOT pitch the offer in the voice memo
- End with a soft close, not a hard CTA

## PART 3: UNIVERSAL RULES (BOTH CHANNELS)

---

### Disqualification Criteria (When to Walk Away)

Walk away immediately if any of these are true:
- Pre-revenue / pre-product-market-fit (they need to build, not scale)
- Monthly revenue under $50K (not enough volume to justify a growth system)
- No decision-making authority (talking to a marketing manager, not the founder)
- Combative or disrespectful tone (not worth the energy)
- Asking for free strategy before any commitment (tire kickers)
- In an active agency contract they are happy with (revisit when contract ends)

Walk away gently if:
- Budget is under $5K/mo (offer a resource, add to nurture, revisit in 90 days)
- Timing is genuinely bad (add to nurture with specific revisit date)

---

### "Not Now" vs "Not Ever" Distinction

**"Not Now" signals:**
- "Timing is not right" / "Maybe next quarter"
- "I need to talk to my partner"
- "We are in the middle of [something]"
- "I do not have budget right now"

**Action:** Tag in GHL as "nurture-[date]". Send one value piece. Set a follow-up reminder for the date they mentioned. They are a future prospect.

**"Not Ever" signals:**
- "I am not interested"
- "We handle everything in-house and it is working"
- "I do not believe in agencies/outsourcing"
- No response after full 14-day cadence

**Action:** Tag in GHL as "closed-lost". Do not follow up again unless they re-engage organically.

---

### When to Send a Case Study vs When to Book Directly

**Send case study first when:**
- They are cold (no prior engagement with your content)
- They are skeptical or asking "what do you actually do?"
- They asked for more information before committing to a call
- Their objection is "I have been burned by agencies before"

**Book directly when:**
- They came from a story reply or reel comment (already warm)
- They explicitly say they need help with growth
- They have already seen your content and are familiar with the model
- Urgency is high ("I need this fixed this quarter")

---

### CRM Logging Rules

Every single DM conversation gets logged. No exceptions.

**What to log in GHL:**
- Contact name, handle, platform (LinkedIn or Instagram)
- Date of first message
- Which opener variation was used
- Qualification status (qualified / nurture / disqualified)
- If qualified: commitment level, urgency, budget range
- If booked: call date/time, closer assigned
- If not booked: reason, follow-up date
- Tags: platform, stage, revisit date, lead source (story reply, cold DM, ad, etc.)

**Tagging convention:**
- `dm-linkedin` or `dm-instagram`
- `stage-opener` / `stage-qualifying` / `stage-booked` / `stage-nurture` / `stage-closed-lost`
- `source-story-reply` / `source-cold-outbound` / `source-ad-response` / `source-inbound`
- `revisit-YYYY-MM-DD` (for "not now" prospects)

### Objection Handling in DMs

These mirror the cold call objection table for consistency across all channels.

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "What do you guys do exactly?" | "Short version — we install a full growth system into founder-led businesses. Acquisition, conversion, retention — and we operate it for you. Honestly the call is where we would dig into whether it makes sense for your situation specifically." |
| "I already have an agency." | "Nice — are you happy with the results? Not trying to fix what is not broken. A lot of founders I talk to have a team but growth still feels unpredictable." |
| "Is this a sales call?" | "It is a strategy session. [Closer Name] looks at what you are doing now and gives honest feedback on where the gaps are. If there is a fit, great. If not, you walk away with clarity." |
| "Just send me some info." | "Happy to. What would be most useful — a case study from [their industry], or a breakdown of how the growth OS model works? And what is the best email?" |
| "I do not have time for a call." | "Totally get it. The call is 30 min and it is specifically about getting your time back from marketing. What if we did [specific day] at [early AM or end of day]?" |
| "How much does it cost?" | "It depends on scope — most clients are in the $5K-$25K/mo range. But honestly, the call is where [Closer Name] would scope it based on what you actually need. No pressure either way." |
| "I have been burned by agencies before." | "Heard that a lot. That is actually why we built this differently — we are not an agency in the traditional sense. We embed operators into your business. Let me send you a case study that shows the difference. [Send link]" |

---

### Sending Volume Guidelines

**Per setter, per day:**
- 50 new outreach messages (cold DMs to qualified profiles)
- 50 warm outreach messages (engage existing followers, story repliers)
- 50 follow-ups from prior conversations

**Response time targets:**
- Inbound DMs (story replies, ad responses): respond within 15 minutes during business hours
- Active qualification conversations: respond within 1 hour
- Follow-ups: batch send at 9AM and 2PM daily
