## 1. PRODUCT SOURCING CRITERIA

### 1.1 Best Sellers Rank (BSR) by Category

BSR represents a product's sales performance relative to other products in the same category. **Lower number = more sales.** BSR is category-specific, meaning a rank of 50,000 in a category with 700,000 products is actually strong.

**BSR Performance Tiers (General Rule):**

| Tier | Position | Sales Velocity | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top 1% | Fastest sellers | Very high turnover | Buy with confidence (if margins work) |
| Top 2% | Fast sellers | High turnover | Good buy, check competition |
| Top 5% | Occasional sellers | Moderate turnover | Must be lowest price, few competitors |
| Top 10% | Slow sellers | Low turnover | Avoid unless ROI is exceptional (100%+) |
| Below top 10% | Very slow | Risky | Generally avoid for OA/RA |

**Category-Specific BSR Thresholds (US Marketplace):**

| Category | "Good" BSR Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Toys & Games | Under 50,000 | High-demand category, moves fast |
| Grocery & Gourmet | Under 50,000 | Consumables = repeat purchases |
| Health & Personal Care | Under 75,000 | Large category, strong demand |
| Beauty | Under 75,000 | High volume, watch for restrictions |
| Home & Kitchen | Under 100,000 | Very large category |
| Sports & Outdoors | Under 75,000 | Seasonal swings |
| Books | Under 100,000 | Massive category, low margins |
| Electronics | Under 30,000 | Competitive, fast price drops |
| Office Products | Under 50,000 | Steady demand |
| Pet Supplies | Under 50,000 | Growing category |
| Baby | Under 50,000 | Gated but profitable |
| Clothing | Under 100,000 | High return rates |

**Key Rule:** BSR under 30,000 that stays relatively stable for at least 90 days is considered a strong indicator. Always verify with Keepa historical data -- a single low BSR reading can be misleading (one sale can temporarily drop BSR).

**BSR to Daily Sales Estimates (Approximate):**

| BSR Range | Estimated Daily Sales |
|---|---|
| Under 1,000 | 50+ units/day (top 1% dominance) |
| 1,000 - 5,000 | 10-50 units/day |
| 5,000 - 10,000 | 5-15 units/day |
| 10,000 - 50,000 | 1-5 units/day |
| 50,000 - 100,000 | Few per week |
| 100,000+ | Slow -- could be weeks between sales |

These vary significantly by category. Use sales estimator tools (Jungle Scout, Helium 10, SellerAmp) for category-adjusted estimates.

### 1.2 ROI Thresholds

ROI = (Profit / Cost) x 100

**Minimum ROI Targets by Seller Experience:**

| Seller Level | Minimum ROI | Ideal ROI | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | 30% | 50%+ | Need buffer for mistakes and price drops |
| Intermediate | 20-30% | 40%+ | Better at managing risk |
| Advanced/Scaled | 15% | 30%+ | Volume compensates for lower per-unit ROI |

**ROI by Inventory Velocity:**

| Turnover Speed | Minimum ROI Target | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Fast (sells in <30 days) | 15-20% | Capital rotates quickly, lower risk |
| Standard (30-60 days) | 30-40% | Typical OA sweet spot |
| Slow (60-90 days) | 50%+ | Need compensation for capital tie-up |
| Very slow (90+ days) | 100%+ | Or skip -- long-term storage fees kill margin |

**Common Rule of Thumb:** Many experienced sellers won't touch products with less than 30% ROI. This provides a cushion against unexpected fee increases, price drops during shipping/processing time, and competitor price wars.

### 1.4 Seller Count on Listings

**Ideal FBA Seller Count:**

| FBA Seller Count | Assessment | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1-2 sellers | Low competition, check if private label | Potential gold mine if demand exists |
| 2-5 sellers | Sweet spot | Buy with confidence if other metrics check out |
| 5-10 sellers | Moderate competition | Acceptable if BSR is strong and demand is high |
| 10-15 sellers | Getting crowded | Only buy if BSR is top 1-2% in category |
| 15+ sellers | Saturated | Generally avoid -- Buy Box share will be minimal |
| Amazon is a seller | Risky | Amazon dominates Buy Box; avoid unless they're out of stock |

**Key Considerations:**
- Look at FBA sellers specifically (not FBM -- they rarely win Buy Box)
- If Amazon.com is selling the item, they typically own 80%+ of the Buy Box
- Check Keepa for seller count TRENDS -- is it increasing or decreasing?
- A listing with 3 FBA sellers and strong BSR is better than one with 1 seller and weak BSR

### 1.5 Buy Box Rotation & Competition

**How Buy Box Rotation Works:**
- Amazon rotates the Buy Box among eligible sellers rather than giving it to one seller 100% of the time
- Rotation is NOT equal -- sellers with better metrics get more turns
- Your offer price (including shipping) should be within 5% of the lowest offer to stay eligible

**Factors That Determine Buy Box Share:**

| Factor | Weight | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Price | High | Must be competitive (within 5% of lowest) |
| Fulfillment method | High | FBA and Seller-Fulfilled Prime have strong advantages |
| Seller metrics | Medium | Order Defect Rate, Late Shipment Rate, Cancellation Rate |
| Inventory levels | Medium | Low stock = reduced Buy Box eligibility |
| Shipping speed | Medium | Faster = better |
| Account age | Low-Medium | Older accounts have slight edge |

**2025-2026 Update:** Amazon's eligibility checks are now more automated. Even a brief dip in performance metrics can result in immediate Buy Box suppression. Real-time monitoring is essential.

**Practical Buy Box Analysis for Sourcing:**
1. Check if Amazon.com is on the listing (if yes, be cautious)
2. Count FBA sellers at or near the Buy Box price
3. Use Keepa to see if the Buy Box price is stable or volatile
4. Estimate your Buy Box share: if 5 FBA sellers at similar price, expect ~20% share
5. Calculate: (estimated daily sales x your Buy Box share %) = your estimated daily sales

## 2. READING KEEPA GRAPHS

### 2.1 Chart Layout

- **Left axis:** Price levels (for price lines)
- **Right axis:** Sales Rank figures (for BSR line)
- **Bottom axis:** Dates/timeline
- **Time range options:** Day, Week, Month, 3 Months, Year, All

### 2.2 Color-Coded Lines

| Line Color | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| Orange | Amazon's price (when Amazon is selling) |
| Blue | New 3rd party FBA price |
| Black | New 3rd party FBM price |
| Green | Sales Rank (BSR) -- plotted against right axis |
| Pink/Red | Buy Box price |
| Dark Green | Used price |
| Purple | New offer count (number of sellers) |

### 2.3 How to Read BSR (Green Line)

- **Drops (downward spikes):** Product made at least one sale
- **Frequent drops:** High sales velocity -- product sells regularly
- **Flat line at high rank:** Product barely sells
- **Steady low rank:** Consistent, strong seller
- **Seasonal patterns:** Look for BSR drops that correlate with holidays, seasons

**Red Flags on Keepa:**
- BSR that's been climbing steadily (demand is dying)
- Price that's been falling over months (race to the bottom)
- Seller count that's been increasing while BSR stays flat (more sellers, same demand)
- Amazon entering/leaving the listing periodically (unpredictable competition)
- Huge BSR spikes followed by long flat periods (one-off sales, not consistent)

### 2.4 What to Look For (Decision Framework)

**Green Light (Buy):**
- BSR drops frequently (multiple times per week)
- Price has been stable or increasing over 90+ days
- Seller count is stable (not rapidly increasing)
- Amazon is NOT currently selling
- Buy Box price is consistent

**Yellow Light (Proceed with Caution):**
- BSR drops occasionally (few times per month)
- Price fluctuates but has a stable average
- Seller count is slowly increasing
- Amazon was selling recently but is currently out of stock

**Red Light (Avoid):**
- BSR rarely drops (once a month or less)
- Price trending downward
- Seller count increasing rapidly
- Amazon is actively selling at a competitive price
- Large price swings (volatile market)

### 2.5 Time Horizons for Analysis

| Period | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Current snapshot, recent price changes |
| 90 days | Standard evaluation window for OA decisions |
| 180 days | Seasonal patterns, trend confirmation |
| 1 year | Full seasonal cycle, long-term viability |
| All time | Product lifecycle stage (growing, mature, declining) |

**Always look at 90+ days minimum.** Short-term data (7-30 days) can be misleading.

## 3. GATING, IP COMPLAINTS & RESTRICTIONS

### 3.1 Amazon Gated Categories

Categories that require approval before you can list products:

**Commonly Gated Categories (2025-2026):**

| Category | Approval Difficulty | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Grocery & Gourmet Food | Medium | Need invoices from authorized distributors |
| Jewelry | Medium-Hard | Quality/authenticity concerns |
| Watches | Hard | Anti-counterfeit measures |
| Dietary Supplements | Hard | FDA compliance required |
| Topicals (beauty) | Medium | Some sub-categories only |
| Automotive | Medium | Safety concerns |
| Collectible Coins | Hard | Authentication issues |
| Fine Art | Hard | Authentication + quality |
| Music & DVD | Medium | Mostly condition gating |
| Sports Collectibles | Hard | Authentication required |
| Video & DVD | Medium | Condition requirements |

**Approval Requirements:**
- Order Defect Rate < 1%
- Late Shipment Rate < 4%
- Pre-Fulfillment Cancellation Rate < 2.5%
- Invoices from authorized wholesalers (typically within last 365 days)
- Minimum purchase quantities on invoices (usually 10+ units)
- Professional seller account in good standing

**Five Types of Restrictions on Amazon:**
1. **Category gating** -- entire category locked
2. **Sub-category gating** -- specific sub-categories within open categories
3. **Brand gating** -- specific brands require authorization
4. **ASIN-level restrictions** -- individual products locked
5. **Condition restrictions** -- e.g., can sell "New" but not "Used"

**Pro Tip:** Always check if you can list an item BEFORE buying it. Use the "Add a Product" feature in Seller Central or tools like SellerAmp/IP Alert to verify.

### 3.3 IP (Intellectual Property) Complaints

**Types of IP Complaints:**

| Type | What It Means | Common Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Trademark | Using brand name/logo without authorization | Brand name in title, listing copy |
| Copyright | Using protected images, text, or content | Copying listing photos or descriptions |
| Patent | Selling a patented product or design | Knock-offs or unauthorized reproductions |
| Counterfeit | Selling fake branded products | Unauthorized sourcing channels |
| Unauthorized Distribution | Selling authentic product without authorization | Retail/online arbitrage of protected brands |

**How IP Complaints Affect You:**
- Single complaint: Listing suspended, inventory stranded
- Multiple complaints: Account Health score drops
- Unresolved complaints: Account suspension or termination
- TRO (Temporary Restraining Order): Frozen seller funds + legal proceedings

**How to Avoid IP Complaints:**
1. **Check IP Alert databases** before sourcing (SellerAmp, BuyBotPro Suspension Safeguard)
2. **Keep all receipts/invoices** -- you need proof of authentic sourcing
3. **Never use brand images** you don't have rights to
4. **Avoid brands known to file** against resellers (check community lists)
5. **Don't list on private label listings** -- single-seller listings are usually PL
6. **Research the brand** before buying: Google "[brand name] Amazon IP complaint"
7. **Use only your own product photos** or licensed images

**If You Get an IP Complaint:**
1. Don't panic -- check if the claim is valid
2. Look up the actual trademark/copyright/patent cited
3. If you have legitimate sourcing proof, file an appeal
4. Contact the rights holder directly to attempt retraction
5. Consider consulting an Amazon seller lawyer (e.g., Amazon Sellers Lawyer)

### 3.4 Hazmat / Dangerous Goods

**What Qualifies as Hazmat:**
Any substance or material posing a risk to health, safety, property, or environment during storage, handling, or transportation.

**Common Surprise Hazmat Items:**
- Perfumes and fragrances
- Phone chargers and electronics with lithium batteries
- Household cleaners
- Aerosol products (hairspray, spray paint)
- Nail polish and remover
- Laptops, cameras, cell phones
- Tools with batteries
- Speakers and headphones (magnets)
- Glue, dyes, and adhesives
- Sunscreen

**How to Identify Hazmat:**
1. Check ASIN classification in Seller Central
2. Look for Safety Data Sheet (SDS) from manufacturer
3. Check if product contains: lithium batteries, aerosol propellant, flammable liquids, corrosive materials
4. Use Amazon's Dangerous Goods identification guide
5. Tools like BuyBotPro flag hazmat items automatically

**FBA Hazmat Program:**
- Separate program with a waitlist
- Limited storage quantities
- Higher fees
- Cannot use Amazon Partnered Carriers
- Must ship to designated fulfillment centers only
- Cannot mix hazmat and non-hazmat in same shipment
- Requires SDS documentation

**Consequence of Non-Compliance:** Account suspension, inventory disposal, potential legal action.

## 6. MULTI-PACK & BUNDLE CONSIDERATIONS

### 6.1 Quantity Mismatch Pitfalls

This is one of the most common and costly mistakes in OA/RA:

**The Problem:**
- You find a single bottle of shampoo at Walmart for $5
- You scan the barcode and match it to an Amazon ASIN showing $18
- You think: "$13 profit!"
- Reality: The Amazon listing is for a 3-pack. You need to send 3 bottles = $15 cost + fees = loss

**How Mismatches Occur:**
- Same UPC assigned to single AND multi-pack versions
- Amazon's system sometimes maps UPCs to wrong ASIN
- Variation listings (size, count) share parent ASIN but have different child ASINs
- Retail packaging doesn't always indicate Amazon's listing format

### 6.2 How to Verify Correct Matching

**Before Every Purchase:**
1. Scan the barcode with the Amazon Seller App
2. Verify the ASIN matches the EXACT product you're holding
3. Check "Item Package Quantity" on the Amazon listing
4. Read the listing title carefully (look for "Pack of 2", "3-Count", etc.)
5. Compare product images to what you have in hand
6. Check the product dimensions/weight vs what's listed

### 6.3 UPC vs ASIN Rules

- Each unique product configuration needs its own UPC
- A single item and a 3-pack of that item should have DIFFERENT UPCs
- Amazon requires separate UPCs for each multi-pack quantity
- When they share UPCs, Amazon may map to either version unpredictably

### 6.4 Variation Listings

**What They Are:** Parent-child ASIN relationships where one listing page shows multiple options (size, color, count, flavor).

**Sourcing Complications:**
- The parent ASIN BSR covers ALL variations combined
- Individual variation sales may be much lower than the combined BSR suggests
- Some variations may be the "hero" (80% of sales) while others barely move
- Price can vary dramatically between variations
- You need to analyze the SPECIFIC child ASIN you'd be selling, not just the parent

**How to Handle:**
- Use Keepa to check the SPECIFIC child ASIN's BSR and price history
- Use RevSeller's Variation Viewer to see all variations and their data
- Never assume the parent listing's BSR applies to your specific variation

### 6.5 Bundling Rules (Amazon Policy)

- You cannot create your own multi-packs of branded products
- Multi-pack listings should only be manufacturer-created packs with unique UPCs
- Custom bundles of different complementary products CAN be created (with limitations)
- Bundles need their own unique ASIN and UPC
- Bundles cannot include products from different brands in most categories

## 7. SOURCING WORKFLOWS

### 7.1 The Standard OA Workflow

```
STEP 1: SOURCE           Find clearance/deals on retail websites
    |                    OR run Tactical Arbitrage scans
    v
STEP 2: MATCH            Match retail product to Amazon ASIN
    |                    (verify UPC, quantity, exact product)
    v
STEP 3: ANALYZE           Check profitability:
    |                    - FBA fees (use calculator)
    |                    - ROI (target 30%+ minimum)
    |                    - Profit per unit ($3+ minimum)
    v
STEP 4: VALIDATE          Check viability:
    |                    - BSR history (Keepa -- 90 day minimum)
    |                    - Seller count trends
    |                    - Buy Box stability
    |                    - Restrictions/gating (can you actually list it?)
    |                    - IP complaint risk
    |                    - Hazmat check
    v
STEP 5: PURCHASE          Buy through cashback portal
    |                    Apply coupon codes + gift cards
    |                    Use rewards credit card
    v
STEP 6: RECEIVE & PREP    Receive inventory at home or prep center
    |                    Label (FNSKU), poly bag, bundle as needed
    |                    Create FBA shipment in Seller Central
    v
STEP 7: SHIP TO FBA       Ship to Amazon fulfillment centers
    |                    Use Amazon Partnered Carrier for discounts
    v
STEP 8: LIST & PRICE      Listing goes live when Amazon receives inventory
    |                    Set competitive price for Buy Box
    v
STEP 9: MONITOR           Track sales, adjust pricing
                         Replenish winners, learn from losers
```

### 7.2 Daily Sourcing Routine (Recommended)

**Time Block: 2-4 hours/day for active sourcing**

| Time | Activity | Tools Used |
|---|---|---|
| First 30 min | Review previous day's purchases, check for restrictions/flags | Seller Central, email |
| 30-60 min | Run/review Tactical Arbitrage scans from overnight | Tactical Arbitrage |
| 60-90 min | Manual sourcing: browse retailer clearance pages, deal sites | BuyBotPro/SellerAmp, Keepa |
| 30 min | Validate top finds: deep Keepa analysis, IP check | Keepa, IP Alert tools |
| 30 min | Purchase approved deals, log in spreadsheet | Cashback portals, credit cards |
| 15 min | Set up next TA scan for overnight run | Tactical Arbitrage |

**Retailers to Check Daily (OA):**
- Walmart.com (clearance + rollbacks)
- Target.com (clearance + Circle deals)
- Kohls.com (clearance + Kohl's Cash)
- HomeDepot.com (special buys)
- BestBuy.com (open-box + clearance)
- Walgreens.com / CVS.com (BOGO deals)
- Amazon.com itself (clearance, warehouse deals)
- Office Depot / Staples
- Bed Bath & Beyond successors
- Seasonal: Nordstrom Rack, TJ Maxx, etc.

### 7.3 Hit Rate Benchmarks

| Method | Products Evaluated/Hour | Profitable Finds/Hour | Hit Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manual sourcing (beginner) | 15-25 | 0-1 | 0-4% |
| Manual sourcing (experienced) | 40-60 | 2-3 | 4-6% |
| Tactical Arbitrage scans | 1,000+ | 5-15 (requires validation) | 0.5-1.5% (pre-validation) |
| Deal sheets (pre-sourced) | N/A | 5-10 per sheet | Higher (already filtered) |

**What This Means:**
- Manual sourcing: expect to evaluate 50+ products to find 2-3 winners
- With automation (TA): evaluate 1,000+ overnight, manually validate top 50 in the morning
- Speed comes from tool proficiency and pattern recognition, not rushing analysis
- Quality > quantity: one great deal beats ten marginal ones

### 7.4 Retail Arbitrage (RA) Specific Workflow

```
1. Go to retail store (Walmart, Target, etc.)
2. Head to clearance sections first
3. Scan barcodes with Amazon Seller App
4. Check: Can I sell this? Is it restricted?
5. Check: BSR, seller count, profit estimate
6. If profitable: add to cart
7. Check out, keep receipt (proof of sourcing)
8. At home: prep, label, create FBA shipment
9. Ship to Amazon
```

**RA-Specific Tips:**
- Focus on clearance endcaps, markdown aisles, seasonal clearance
- Walmart yellow stickers, Target clearance (red stickers with extra 50-70% off)
- Visit stores on markdown schedule days (varies by retailer)
- Build relationships with store managers for early clearance info
- Use store-specific apps (Walmart app for in-store prices, Target Circle for deals)

### 7.5 Wholesale Sourcing Workflow

```
1. Research brands/products selling well on Amazon (use Keepa, Jungle Scout)
2. Find the manufacturer or authorized distributor
3. Contact via email/phone with wholesale inquiry
4. Request price list, MOQs, terms
5. Analyze product list against Amazon prices/BSR
6. Place initial test order (minimum quantity)
7. Verify product quality and listing accuracy
8. Create FBA shipment
9. Reorder winners at larger quantities for better pricing
```

**Finding Wholesale Suppliers:**
- Trade shows (ASD Market Week, NY NOW, National Hardware Show)
- B2B directories: SaleHoo, Faire, Worldwide Brands, Wholesale Central, ThomasNet
- Google: "[product type] wholesale distributor USA"
- Manufacturer websites: "Become a Retailer" or "Wholesale Inquiries" pages
- Competitor analysis: see what brands sell well, contact those manufacturers

**Wholesale Margins:**
- Target: 20-30% net profit after all costs
- Typical: 10-20% net profit
- Rule of thumb: buy at 50% or less of Amazon selling price (before fees)
- MOQ varies: $100-$5,000+ depending on supplier
- Better pricing with larger orders (volume discounts)

**Advantages Over OA/RA:**
- Replenishable inventory (buy the same product repeatedly)
- Invoices satisfy Amazon's ungating/authenticity requirements
- More predictable margins
- Scalable with systems

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher upfront capital required
- Need to manage supplier relationships
- Slower to start (relationship building takes time)
- Competition from other wholesale sellers on same listings

## 8. SCALING & AUTOMATION

### 8.1 Scaling Stages

| Stage | Revenue | Team | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solo Starter | $0-$5K/mo | Just you | Learn the process, find winning categories |
| Growing Solo | $5K-$15K/mo | You + maybe 1 VA | Systemize sourcing, increase volume |
| Small Team | $15K-$50K/mo | You + 2-3 VAs | Delegate sourcing, focus on strategy |
| Scaled Operation | $50K-$200K/mo | You + team of 5-10 | Systems-driven, management role |
| Enterprise | $200K+/mo | Full team + management | CEO role, multiple sourcing channels |

### 8.2 What Can Be Automated vs What Needs Human Judgment

**Automate (No Human Required):**
- Price tracking alerts (Keepa)
- Automated scans of retail sites (Tactical Arbitrage)
- Repricing on Amazon (automated repricers)
- FBA shipment creation (templates + software)
- Cashback portal routing
- Inventory alerts (low stock, price changes)
- Order tracking and receipt organization

**Semi-Automate (VA Can Handle with SOPs):**
- Deal validation (following your criteria checklist)
- Product listing creation
- Inventory prep and labeling
- Supplier communication (wholesale)
- Customer service responses
- Return processing
- Bookkeeping and receipt logging

**Requires Your Judgment:**
- Final buy/don't-buy decisions on edge cases
- New brand/category risk assessment
- IP complaint evaluation and response
- Supplier relationship decisions (wholesale)
- Strategic pricing decisions
- Account health management
- Cash flow and capital allocation

### 8.3 Virtual Assistant (VA) Sourcing

**How VA Sourcing Works:**
1. You create detailed SOPs for your sourcing criteria
2. Hire VA(s) -- typically from Philippines, Pakistan, or other low-cost regions
3. Train them on your tools (SellerAmp, Keepa, TA)
4. VA sources products and fills a spreadsheet with potential buys
5. You review and approve/reject their finds
6. Over time, give more autonomy to proven VAs

**VA Sourcing SOP Should Include:**
- Minimum ROI threshold (e.g., 30%+)
- Minimum profit per unit (e.g., $3+)
- Maximum BSR by category
- Maximum seller count (e.g., no more than 10 FBA sellers)
- Brands/categories to avoid (IP risk list)
- How to check restrictions
- How to read Keepa (what patterns to look for)
- Screenshot/documentation requirements for each find

**VA Costs:**
- Philippines VA: $4-8/hour (experienced Amazon VA)
- Pakistan VA: $3-6/hour
- Full-time VA (40 hrs/week): $640-1,280/month
- Agencies (pre-trained Amazon VAs): $800-2,000/month

**VA Management Best Practices:**
- Start with 1 VA, perfect the process, then scale
- Create video SOPs (Loom) -- not just written docs
- Review their work daily for the first 2-4 weeks
- Implement a scoring system for their finds (accuracy rate)
- As trust builds, reduce review frequency
- Create a lead VA role to supervise sourcing VAs
- Use project management tools (Asana, Trello, Notion)

### 8.4 Deal Sheets & Pre-Sourced Leads

**What They Are:** Subscription services that send you a list of pre-analyzed profitable products daily.

**How They Work:**
1. Service employs sourcers who find deals
2. Deals are analyzed for ROI, BSR, restrictions
3. Subscribers receive a daily list (usually 10-30+ products)
4. You verify and purchase the ones you like

**Popular Deal Sheet Services:**
- OABeans
- OA Leads / FBA Leads List
- BuyBotPro deal sheets
- Various private communities

**Pros:**
- Time savings (someone else does the sourcing)
- Good for learning what profitable products look like
- Can supplement your own sourcing

**Cons:**
- Shared with many subscribers (competition increases)
- Price can drop by the time you buy
- Cost: $50-200+/month for quality lists
- Dependency (you don't build the skill yourself)

### 8.5 Scheduled/Automated Deal Scanning

**Tactical Arbitrage Scheduling:**
- Set up searches to run overnight or during off-hours
- Configure filters: min ROI, min profit, max BSR, max sellers
- Review results each morning -- your "daily leads" ready to validate
- Can scan 900+ retail sites automatically

**Keepa Alerts:**
- Set price drop alerts on specific ASINs or categories
- Get notified when prices drop below your target buy price
- Track competitor pricing changes
- Monitor BSR changes on watched products

**Automated Workflows (Advanced):**
- Zapier/Make.com integrations between tools
- Google Sheets automations for tracking inventory
- Automated cashback tracking
- Price monitoring bots for specific retailer clearance pages
