# Agency OS — VSL Script (Full Production Draft)

> **Format:** Talking head, teleprompter, clean professional background
> **Framework:** Jeremy Haynes 6-Step + Step 0 (Summary Section)
> **Audience:** Affluent — 7-8 figure founders with product-market fit
> **Tone:** Direct, operator-to-operator, zero fluff

So why me? Why should you listen to anything I am about to say?

I will tell you what I am not going to do. I am not going to show you a Forbes logo and tell you I was "featured." I am not going to read you a list of certifications. Here is what I will tell you.

I have operated growth systems across multiple verticals — real estate, professional services, e-commerce, coaching, SaaS. Not as a consultant sitting on the sidelines drawing on a whiteboard. As an operator inside the business, with my hands on the ad accounts, the CRM, the email sequences, the landing pages, the sales data — all of it.

I have personally managed hundreds of thousands of dollars in ad spend and tracked every dollar from first click to closed deal. Not in theory. In spreadsheets with real numbers attached to real bank deposits.

Our client retention rate is above ninety percent at the three-month mark. That means the vast majority of people who start working with us stay — because the system produces results they can see in their revenue.

And before you think: "Well, of course he is going to say his stuff works" — I get it. You have been pitched by a hundred agencies. Every single one of them told you they were different. Every single one of them had a fancy deck and impressive-sounding case studies.

I know that because I have cleaned up after those agencies. I have inherited ad accounts where the previous team was optimizing for link clicks instead of qualified leads. I have taken over CRMs where half the pipeline was dead leads that nobody had bothered to remove. I have seen the mess.

So I am not going to ask you to trust me based on what I say. I am going to ask you to evaluate what I am about to show you and decide for yourself whether it makes sense.

That is the only pitch I am making.

## [5:30-10:00] STEP 3: BUYING MOTIVES (Majority Hook Cascade)

Let me walk you through the real reasons people come to us. These are not marketing talking points. These are the actual things founders tell me on our first call, almost word for word.

---

### MAJORITY HOOK (60%+): "I need predictable revenue that does not depend on me."

This is the number one thing I hear. Far and away the most common.

You have built something real. You have product-market fit. Customers love what you sell. But the revenue is inconsistent and it runs through you.

When you are pushing hard — calling people, running campaigns yourself, showing up at events — revenue goes up. When you step back for two weeks — maybe you take a vacation, maybe you get sick, maybe you just get buried in operations — revenue drops.

That is not a business. That is a job you created for yourself. And it is a job with no benefits, no PTO, and no ceiling.

What you actually need is a machine that generates qualified opportunities whether you are in the room or not. A system where you wake up on Monday morning and there are booked calls on the calendar, warmed-up leads in the pipeline, and campaigns running that you did not have to touch.

That is what we build. Not a campaign. Not a funnel. An entire acquisition engine with every piece connected — paid traffic, landing pages, email sequences, CRM workflows, lead scoring, sales enablement — all running as one coordinated system.

The cost of not having this is real. Every month you wait, you are leaving fifty, sixty, a hundred thousand dollars on the table. Not because your product is bad. Because your distribution is broken.

---

### SECONDARY HOOK (20%): "I am tired of managing agencies and freelancers."

This one comes up constantly from founders who have been in the game for a while.

You have a media buyer. You have a designer. You have someone doing email. You have someone doing SEO. Maybe you have an agency that handles one channel and a freelancer that handles another.

And somehow, you have become the project manager for all of them. You are on five different Slack channels. You are reviewing reports from three different dashboards. You are trying to figure out whether the Facebook ads are actually driving the revenue or whether it was the email sequence or whether it was just seasonal demand.

Nobody owns the full picture. Everyone owns their silo. And when results are bad, everyone points at someone else.

"The ads are performing well — it must be a sales problem."
"The sales team says the leads are bad."
"The email person says nobody is opening because the subject lines from the ads team are not congruent."

You are refereeing a blame game between contractors who do not talk to each other.

With us, one team owns everything. Paid. Organic. Email. Conversion. Data. One team, one strategy, one set of numbers. When something is not working, we know exactly where the bottleneck is — because we see the entire chain, not just one link.

You stop being the project manager. You start getting a weekly report from one person who can tell you exactly what happened, why, and what we are doing about it.

---

### TERTIARY HOOK (10%): "I have been through the agency carousel and I am done."

You know the cycle. You have lived it.

You hire an agency. First three weeks, everyone is excited. Onboarding calls, strategy sessions, big promises. Month two, things are "still ramping up." Month three, results are mediocre but they tell you to "give it time." Month four, you are frustrated. Month five, you fire them. Month six, you start looking for a new one.

Six months gone. Tens of thousands of dollars spent. And you are back at square one with nothing to show for it except a Google Drive folder full of someone else's unfinished work.

This happens because most agencies sell campaigns, not systems. When you leave, the campaign dies. There is no infrastructure left behind. No documented process. No playbook your team can run without them.

We build something that survives us. Every workflow is documented. Every SOP is written down. Every automation is built inside your accounts, not ours. If you ever decide to bring this in-house or move on, you keep everything. The system is yours. We are operators, not hostage-takers.

---

### MINORITY HOOK (5%): "I do not actually know what is working."

This one is less common but when it hits, it hits hard.

You are spending money. Revenue is coming in. But you cannot draw a straight line from dollar spent to dollar earned. You do not know which channel is actually producing customers. You do not know your real cost to acquire a client. You do not know whether your sales team is closing at twenty percent or forty percent because nobody is tracking it cleanly.

You are making decisions based on gut feel and rough estimates. And at seven or eight figures, gut feel is not good enough. A five-percent improvement in close rate might be worth twenty thousand dollars a month. A ten-percent improvement in show rate might be worth the same. But you will never capture those gains if you cannot see them.

Part of what we install is a full attribution and reporting layer. You will know your cost per lead, cost per qualified lead, cost per booked call, show rate, close rate, average deal size, and lifetime value — broken down by channel, by campaign, by month. Not in a pretty dashboard that nobody looks at. In a weekly review with your dedicated operator who walks you through what the numbers mean and what we are doing about each one.

## [13:00-16:00] STEP 5: OBJECTION HANDLING

Let me address the things you are probably thinking right now. I know these because I hear them on almost every first call.

---

**"I have tried agencies before. It did not work."**

I hear this one more than anything else. And I do not blame you for being skeptical.

Here is what makes this different: most agencies run campaigns. They set up ads, send you a report, and call it a day. When the campaign stops working, they either blame the market or suggest you spend more money.

We do not run campaigns. We install operating systems. A campaign is one piece of a machine. An operating system is the entire machine — traffic, conversion, nurture, sales enablement, attribution, and optimization — all connected, all measured, all owned by one team.

The difference is not marketing speak. The difference is that when you stop working with a campaign agency, the campaigns die. When you stop working with us, the system keeps running — because it was built inside your business, not inside ours.

---

**"That is a lot of money."**

Is it? Compared to what?

A full-time head of growth costs a hundred and fifty to two hundred and fifty thousand dollars a year — that is twelve to twenty thousand a month before benefits. And that is one person. You still need a media buyer, a designer, a copywriter, and an email strategist. Now you are looking at thirty to fifty thousand a month in payroll.

Or you can hire an agency for five to ten thousand a month that brings the full team, the full system, and the full accountability — with no long-term contract.

The question is not "can I afford this?" The question is "can I afford to keep doing what I am doing?" Because what you are doing right now has a cost too. Every month of unpredictable revenue, every month of managing freelancers, every month of guessing which channel is working — that has a price tag. You are just not writing a check for it, so it does not feel real.

---

**"I am not sure you know my industry."**

We probably do not. And that is actually why this works.

We are not industry experts. We are growth system experts. We do not need to understand the nuances of your specific niche to build a paid acquisition funnel, install a CRM, write a follow-up sequence, and track attribution. The playbook works because the mechanics of growth are the same across industries.

Traffic comes in. Some of it converts. Some of it does not. The people who convert need to be nurtured. The people who buy need to be retained. The leaks need to be found and fixed.

That framework does not change whether you sell consulting, SaaS, real estate, professional services, or physical products. What changes is the messaging — and we build the messaging with you, because you are the one who knows your customer.

We bring the system. You bring the expertise. That is the partnership.

---

**"I can just do this myself."**

You absolutely can.

The question is: should you?

You are the founder. Your time is worth more per hour than almost anyone in your company. Every hour you spend setting up email sequences, reviewing ad reports, tweaking landing pages, and managing a media buyer — that is an hour you are not spending on product, team, strategy, or sales.

You did not start this business to become a marketing manager. You started it to build something. Let us handle the engine so you can focus on the thing only you can do.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Filming Setup
- **Format:** Talking head, direct to camera
- **Background:** Clean, professional office or neutral environment. No clutter. No flashy cars or watches visible. Affluent audiences read "less showy" as "more credible" (Jeremy Haynes: "The more showy you are, the less rich you are")
- **Lighting:** Three-point setup, natural tones
- **Camera:** 4K, eye level, slight shallow depth of field
- **Audio:** Lavalier mic or Rode shotgun. Audio quality matters more than video quality.
- **Wardrobe:** Simple, high-quality. Solid color shirt or polo. No logos, no suits, no ties. Clean and confident.

### Pacing & Delivery
- **Target pace:** 130-150 words per minute (natural conversational speed)
- **Teleprompter:** Yes. This is a scripted delivery, not improvised. Practice 3-5 times before final take.
- **Energy:** Calm authority. Not hyped, not monotone. Like you are explaining something important to a peer over coffee.
- **Eye contact:** Direct to lens the entire time. No looking off-camera.
- **Pauses:** Deliberate pauses after key statements (price reveals, promise restatement, qualification). Let it breathe.

### B-Roll Suggestions
| Section | B-Roll |
|---|---|
| Step 0 (Summary) | None — pure talking head for maximum directness |
| Step 1 (Hook) | Quick cuts of messy dashboards, cluttered Slack channels, confusing analytics screens |
| Step 2 (Credibility) | Screen recordings of real ad dashboards (with numbers visible), CRM pipelines, client results (with permission) |
| Step 3 (Buying Motives) | Split-screen of founder at desk vs. revenue chart going up/down; calendar with booked calls; single dashboard replacing five tools |
| Step 4 (Offer) | Clean tier breakdown graphic (white text on dark background, minimal), side-by-side cost comparison graphic |
| Step 5 (Objection Handling) | None — keep this pure talking head for trust and directness |
| Step 6 (CTA) | Screen recording scrolling down to the application form; phone notification of new application submitted |

### Thumbnail & Title Options
| Option | Title | Thumbnail Concept |
|---|---|---|
| A | "How We Add $50K-$100K/mo to 7-Figure Businesses" | Sabbo at desk, revenue dashboard visible behind him, calm expression |
| B | "Why Your Marketing Agency Is Not Working" | Sabbo looking directly at camera, no text overlay, serious expression |
| C | "The Growth System Your Business Is Missing" | Split frame: left side = chaos (tabs, Slack, reports), right side = clean single dashboard |

### Hosting
- **Platform:** Wistia (primary) or Vidalytics (secondary). Never YouTube or Vimeo for VSL hosting.
- **Thumbnail:** Video thumbnail showing a key moment (not a static image, not a fake play button)
- **Chapters:** Add chapter markers at each step transition for navigation
- **Embed:** On VSL landing page with headline above and embedded application below. Nothing else on the page.

### Page Setup (Jeremy Haynes Rules)
- **Above the fold:** Headline + VSL only
- **Below the fold:** Embedded application form (Typeform + Calendly integration)
- **No additional sections:** No testimonials wall, no FAQ accordion, no extra copy
- **Conditional logic:** Qualified applicants see confirmation page (pixeled). Disqualified applicants see drop-sell page (NOT pixeled).

### Post-VSL Systems to Deploy
- [ ] Confirmation page with main video + 4-5 breakout videos (addressing specific objections)
- [ ] Hammer them campaign (30-50 short-form + 15-30 long-form content pieces targeting booked leads)
- [ ] Value-dense email sequence (5 emails post-application, not just reminders)
- [ ] Setter selfie video protocol (iPhone, personalized reference to application answers)
- [ ] Disposition tracking system for sales team feedback
