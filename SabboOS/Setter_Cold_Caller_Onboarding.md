## 1. WELCOME & YOUR MISSION

Congratulations on joining the team. You've been chosen to set for a multi-seven-figure growth agency — that's no easy feat. Now it's time to get to work.

### What You Are

```
You are NOT selling. You are qualifying and booking.

Your ONLY job ──► Get qualified founders on a strategy call with a closer.

You don't pitch the offer.
You don't talk pricing.
You don't try to close.
You find pain, confirm fit, and book the call.
```

### What You're Booking Them Into

- 30-45 minute strategy call with a closer
- Closer diagnoses their growth bottleneck + presents the Growth OS
- We call it a "strategy session" — NEVER a "sales call" or "pitch"

### Why This Role Matters

The setter is the front door of the entire revenue engine. Without qualified leads showing up to calls, nothing else matters.

- **A great setter** books 2-4 qualified calls per day, gets 80%+ show rate, and feeds the closer a pipeline of pre-warmed founders ready to buy.
- **A bad setter** wastes closer time on unqualified leads, kills team momentum, and burns through a list that took hundreds of dollars and hours to build.

**It's better to book 3 qualified calls than 8 unqualified ones.** Close rate on bad leads: 0%. Close rate on good leads: 40-60%. Every bad booking costs the closer an hour of momentum. Protect their calendar like it's your own commission.

## 5. THE COLD CALL SCRIPT (6 PHASES)

```
Total call time: 2-3 minutes. That's it.

    PHASE 1 ──► Pattern Interrupt .............. 10 seconds
    PHASE 2 ──► The Hook ....................... 15-20 seconds
    PHASE 3 ──► Dig Into Pain .................. 60-90 seconds
    PHASE 4 ──► The Bridge ..................... 15-20 seconds
    PHASE 5 ──► Book the Call .................. 30-45 seconds
    PHASE 6 ──► Protect the Show ............... After hang-up
```

---

### PHASE 1: PATTERN INTERRUPT (10 seconds)

**Goal:** Get 30 seconds of their time. That's all.
**Tone:** Calm. Direct. Slightly curious. NOT hyped up.

**PRIMARY OPENER:**

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] — I know this is out of the blue, do you have 30 seconds?"

**If they push back:**

| They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| "Who is this?" / "What's this about?" | "Totally fair — I work with growth systems for founder-led businesses. I was looking at [their company] and had a quick question. Literally 30 seconds." |
| "I'm busy" | "Totally get it. When's a better 2 minutes? I'll call you back then." |
| "How'd you get my number?" | "Your business came up when I was researching [their industry] in [their area]. I'm not trying to waste your time — genuinely had a question about how you're handling growth. 30 seconds?" |

**Rules:**
- Do NOT say your company name — it means nothing to them yet
- Do NOT pitch in the opener — you're asking for time, not selling
- Match their energy — relaxed person = relaxed tone, direct person = direct tone

---

### PHASE 2: THE HOOK (15-20 seconds)

**Goal:** Make them curious enough to keep talking.
**Key Principle:** Always use yes/no or either/or questions — NOT open-ended. Either/or gets 4x the response rate.

**PRIMARY HOOK:**

> "I was looking at [Company] — looks like you've built something solid. Quick question: is your growth right now more systematic, or is it more like... you're figuring it out as you go?"

Almost every founder will say some version of "figuring it out." That's your in.

**ALTERNATIVE HOOKS (choose based on your 60-second research):**

| Situation | Hook |
|---|---|
| They're running ads | "I noticed you're running ads — are you happy with what you're getting back from that spend, or does it feel like you're kind of guessing?" |
| Big following, no clear funnel | "You've got a solid following — are you actually converting that into revenue systematically, or is it more word-of-mouth right now?" |
| Recently hired/fired an agency | "I saw you were working with [agency/freelancer]. Are you still with them, or are you looking for something different?" |

**After you ask — SHUT UP. Let them talk.**

---

### PHASE 3: DIG INTO THE PAIN (60-90 seconds)

**Goal:** Understand their situation. Confirm they're qualified. Identify their #1 pain.
**Key Principle:** Listen 70%, talk 30%. You are diagnosing, not pitching.

**PICK 2-3 OF THESE QUESTIONS (based on conversation flow):**

> "What does your customer acquisition look like right now — where do most of your clients come from?"

> "Do you have a marketing team or person handling this, or is it on you?"

> "What have you tried that hasn't worked?"

> "What would it look like if this was actually dialed in? What would change for you?"

> "What's the cost of NOT fixing this? Like, how much are you leaving on the table every month?"

**QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST (mental — don't read this to them):**

Run through this silently while they talk. If they hit 4+ on the qualified side, book them.

**AFTER THEY TALK — RECAP THEIR PAIN BACK TO THEM:**

> "So it sounds like the main issue is [X], and that's costing you roughly [Y] a month — is that right?"

This does two things:
1. Shows you were listening
2. Makes the pain feel more real when they hear it reflected back

**IF THEY'RE NOT QUALIFIED — BE HONEST AND MOVE ON:**

> "Appreciate the time — honestly based on what you're telling me, I don't think this would be the right fit right now. But if things change, I'd love to reconnect."

Do NOT book bad leads. It destroys closer time and YOUR show rate.

---

### PHASE 4: THE BRIDGE (15-20 seconds)

**Goal:** Connect their pain to the strategy call. NOT to the offer.
**Two to three sentences. That's it.**

**PRIMARY BRIDGE:**

> "Look, this is exactly the kind of thing we help with. We work with founder-led businesses in the [their revenue range] and build out the entire growth engine — not just run ads, but install the full system so it's predictable. I think it'd be worth a conversation. Not a pitch — just a strategy session to see if there's a fit."

**ALTERNATIVE BRIDGES:**

> "We've helped a few businesses in [their space] go from exactly what you're describing to a predictable pipeline. It might make sense to jump on a quick call with [Closer Name] to see if we can do the same."

> "Honestly, what you're describing is a systems problem, not a marketing problem. That's what we solve. Would it make sense to get on a 30-minute call to dig into this properly?"

**Rules:**
- Do NOT explain the offer in detail — that's the closer's job
- Say "strategy session" or "conversation" — NEVER "sales call" or "pitch"
- If you have a case study: "We helped [similar business] go from random leads to $X/month in 90 days"

---

### PHASE 5: BOOK THE CALL (30-45 seconds)

**Goal:** Lock in the time. Get tie-downs. Protect the show.
**Key Principle:** Assume the booking. Don't ask "would you be interested?" — give a choice of times.

**THE BOOKING:**

> "I've got time on [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] — which works better for you?"

**ONCE THEY PICK A TIME:**

> "Perfect. I'll send you a calendar link right now. A couple things — the call is with [Closer Name], it's about 30 minutes. Before the call, I'll send you a short video that'll give you context on how we work so you get the most out of it. And [Name] — just make sure you're somewhere quiet with no distractions. Cool?"

**TIE-DOWNS (NON-NEGOTIABLE — Do These Before You Hang Up):**

**1. Decision-Maker Tie-Down**

> "Quick question — if you liked what you heard on the call, is there anyone else who'd need to be involved in the decision? A business partner, COO, anyone?"

```
If YES ──► "Can they be on the call too?"
           If they can't ──► Reschedule to when both can attend.

If NO  ──► "So just to confirm — if you wanted to move forward,
           you wouldn't need anyone else's sign-off?"
```

**2. Financial Tie-Down (use when rapport is strong)**

> "Just so the call is valuable for everyone — our clients typically invest $5K-$25K a month depending on scope. Is that realistic for you if the fit is right?"

```
If YES ──► Great. Move on.

If NO / hesitation ──► "No pressure at all — I just want to make sure
                       the call is worth your time. If budget is tight,
                       maybe we reconnect in a couple months?"
```

---

### PHASE 6: PROTECT THE SHOW (After Hang-Up)

**Goal:** Get them to actually show up. This is where most setters lose.
**Key Principle:** Booking is step 1. Getting them to SHOW UP is the actual job.

See Section 8 for the complete 6-Touchpoint Show Rate System.

Cold calling is your primary channel. DMs are your secondary channel for leads who engage on social.

### Instagram DM Opener (Agency OS)

> "Hey [Name] — noticed you're running [their business type]. Quick question: is your main growth bottleneck right now on the lead gen side or the conversion side?"

### LinkedIn DM Opener (Agency OS)

> "Hey [Name] — saw [something specific about their business]. Quick question about how you're handling growth — is it more systematized or more ad hoc right now?"

### DM Qualification Questions

| Question | Purpose |
|---|---|
| "What's your current monthly revenue?" | Revenue qualification ($1M+ annual) |
| "What's your current monthly marketing budget?" | Budget qualification ($5K-$25K/mo range) |
| "Is scaling something you're looking to solve this quarter?" | Timeline / urgency |
| "Are you looking for a partner to operate this or just advice?" | Fit — we're operators, not consultants |

### DM Setting Flow

```
1. Opener (personalized — reference something specific)
      ↓
2. Either/or question (creates conversation)
      ↓
3. 1-2 qualification questions (confirm fit)
      ↓
4. Bridge to call: "Sounds like this could be a fit —
   would it make sense to jump on a quick strategy call?"
      ↓
5. Send Calendly link + tie-downs via DM
      ↓
6. Enter them in CRM immediately
      ↓
7. Follow the 6-touchpoint show rate system
```

### DM Rules

- NEVER copy-paste the same opener to everyone — personalize every time
- Always reference something specific about their business or content
- Don't pitch in DMs. Qualify and book.
- If they go cold after showing interest, follow up 3 times over 7 days, then move to re-engagement list
- Log every DM conversation in CRM

## 7. OBJECTION HANDLING

**Core principle:** Handle and redirect. Don't argue. Don't get stuck debating. Acknowledge, redirect, move forward.

### Cold Call Objections

| Objection | Response | Then... |
|---|---|---|
| **"I'm not interested."** | "Totally respect that. Out of curiosity — is that because growth is already dialed in, or is it more of a timing thing?" | If timing → "When's better to revisit?" If dialed in → "Best of luck — keep me in mind." |
| **"Just send me an email."** | "Happy to — what's the best email? And just so I send something relevant, what's the biggest marketing challenge you're dealing with right now?" | If they answer the question, you're back in conversation. |
| **"We already have an agency."** | "Nice — are you happy with the results? I only ask because a lot of founders I talk to have a team but still feel like growth is unpredictable." | If unhappy → "What's not working?" If happy → "Not trying to fix what's not broken." |
| **"What do you guys do exactly?"** | "Short version — we install a full growth operating system into founder-led businesses. Acquisition, conversion, retention — and we operate it for you. But honestly, the call is where we'd dig into whether it makes sense for you." | Bridge back to booking. Do NOT do a 10-min explanation. |
| **"I don't have time for a call."** | "Totally get it — you're busy, that's exactly why this might be worth it. The call is 30 min and it's specifically about getting your time back from marketing." | Offer a specific time: "What if we did [Day] at [early AM / lunch / end of day]?" |
| **"Is this a sales call?"** | "It's a strategy session — [Closer] looks at what you're doing now and gives honest feedback on where the gaps are. If there's a fit, great. If not, you walk away with clarity. No pressure either way." | Bridge to booking. |
| **"How much does it cost?"** | "It depends on scope — that's actually what the strategy call covers. We work with businesses in the $5K-$25K/mo range. The call is to see if there's even a fit first." | Don't avoid price, but don't sell on it. Bridge to booking. |

### DM Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| **"Not interested right now"** | "No worries at all. If I check back in a couple months would that be better timing?" |
| **"I already have a marketing team"** | "Nice — is it working the way you want? Just curious because a lot of founders I talk to have a team but still feel like growth is unpredictable." |
| **"Send me more info"** | "Sure — here's a quick video that explains how we work: [link]. After you watch, let me know if it makes sense to hop on a quick call." |
| **Ghosting / no reply** | Follow up Day 2, Day 5, Day 10. Then move to re-engagement list. |

### Johnny Mau's Pre-Frame Psychology (Advanced)

Once you've internalized the basics, layer in these NLP techniques:

1. **Mirror their exact words.** If they say "we're struggling with consistency," use "consistency" — not "reliability" or "predictability." Their words are their world.

2. **The Revealing Question:** "What have you done in the past to fix this?" → Their answer reveals their limiting beliefs → You reframe in real-time.

3. **Presuppositions:** Assume the call is happening. "When we chat on [Day]..." not "If you want to chat..."

4. **Future Pacing:** "Imagine 90 days from now, your growth is predictable and you're not touching the marketing..." — let them visualize.

## 8. SHOW RATE SYSTEM (6 TOUCHPOINTS)

**Show rate target: 80%+**

If you're below that, it's one of two problems:
1. You're booking unqualified leads (fix qualification)
2. You're not following up enough (fix follow-up discipline)

### The 6-Touchpoint System

```
WITHIN 5 MINUTES OF BOOKING
├── 1. Send calendar invite with Zoom link
└── 2. Text: "Hey [Name], [Your Name] here — just sent over the
         calendar link for [Day] at [Time]. Looking forward to it."

WITHIN 1 HOUR
└── 3. Send pre-call video MATCHED TO THEIR PAIN POINT:
         "Hey [Name], before your call with [Closer], this video
          is super relevant to what you mentioned about [their
          specific bottleneck]. Worth a watch. [link]"

24 HOURS BEFORE
└── 4. Text: "Hey [Name]! Quick reminder — you've got your call
         with [Closer] tomorrow at [Time]. Still good?"
         └── No reply? ──► Call them.

MORNING OF
└── 5. Text: "[Name]!"
         ...then 30 seconds later:
         "It's [Your Name] from [Company]. We still good
          for [Time] today?"

IF NO-SHOW AT CALL TIME
├── 6a. Call twice.
├── 6b. Voicemail: "Hey [Name], [Your Name] here — we had you
│        down for [Time] today. No worries if something came up.
│        Shoot me a text and we'll get you rescheduled."
└── 6c. Email, subject: "^ bumping this" — short, no guilt trip.
```

### Why This Works

- **Touchpoint 1-2:** Confirms the booking is real in their mind
- **Touchpoint 3:** The pre-call video does the selling for you. They arrive pre-educated and pre-sold. (Jeremy Haynes: "80% of conversion happens in marketing, 20% on the call")
- **Touchpoint 4-5:** Creates social accountability — they'd feel bad ghosting someone who's been in touch
- **Touchpoint 6:** No-show recovery. ~20% of no-shows will rebook if you follow up immediately

### Pre-Call Video Selection

Match the video to their pain point from the call:

| Their Pain | Video to Send |
|---|---|
| "Our ads aren't working" | Case study: client who went from burning ad spend to predictable ROI |
| "We've hired agencies that failed" | Video: why operators vs. agencies (how we're different) |
| "Growth is unpredictable" | Case study: client who went from random leads to a system |
| "I'm doing all the marketing myself" | Video: what a growth operating system looks like |

## 10. WEEK 1 TRAINING PLAN (BLACK MARKER METHOD)

### What Is the Black Marker Method?

This is Alex Hormozi's signature script memorization technique. It works.

1. Print **2 copies** of this cold call script — one clean reference, one working copy
2. Read the working copy out loud, start to finish
3. Take a black marker and black out **one word**
4. Read the script again from memory. If stuck, glance at the clean copy.
5. Black out another word
6. Repeat until **every word is blacked out**

A 400-word script = ~400 repetitions. By the end, you don't think about the words. You OWN them.

**Why it works:** Reps who "know" the script but haven't internalized it sound robotic. Reps who've done the Black Marker Method sound natural because the script is in their subconscious. The boredom of repetition forces you to find natural inflection and personality within the words.

### Week 1 Schedule

| Day | Morning (2 hrs) | Afternoon (2 hrs) |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Company overview. Offer deep-dive. Why this product changes lives. Watch 5 recorded sales calls (take notes). | **Black Marker Method begins:** Print 2 copies of the cold call script. Read aloud. Start blacking out words. |
| **Tuesday** | ICP training. Who are our buyers? What are they afraid of? What do they want? Watch 5 more recorded calls. | Black Marker continues. Start role-playing the **Pattern Interrupt + Hook** with your manager. |
| **Wednesday** | Objection training: Learn all 7 objections + responses from Section 7. Listen to calls where objections were handled well. | Black Marker continues. Role-play the **Dig Into Pain** section. Practice asking discovery questions. |
| **Thursday** | Product knowledge deep-dive: Walk through the Growth OS, the 30-day install, the delivery model. Understand what the closer will present. | Black Marker continues. Role-play the **Bridge + Booking** section. Practice tie-downs. |
| **Friday** | Full mock cold call with manager. Manager plays the prospect (hard mode). Get feedback. | Black Marker continues. Role-play **objection handling** scenarios. |
| **Saturday** | (Optional) Listen to 10 more recorded calls. Take notes on patterns you hear. | Rest. |

### Milestone 1: Script Memorization (End of Week 1)

You must be able to:
- Recite the full 6-phase script cold — no notes
- Manager will test by interrupting you mid-script with random objections
- **If you can't pass this in 2-3 days, we'll reassess fit** (Hormozi: "Can't pass milestone 1 in 2-3 days = out")

## 12. CRM & REPORTING

### CRM Hygiene Rules

1. **Every conversation gets logged.** If it happened, it goes in CRM. No exceptions.
2. **Update lead status immediately after every call.** Don't batch it at end of day.
3. **Add notes for the closer.** When you hand off a lead, the closer should know: their name, business, revenue, main pain point, what they've tried, and any objections they raised.
4. **Lead response time: under 60 seconds** during business hours (Hormozi: non-negotiable). When a new inbound lead comes in, you drop everything and respond.

### Setter-to-Closer Handoff Process

```
1. Book the call in Calendly (round-robin or specific closer link)
2. Update CRM: Lead status → "Call Booked" with notes
3. Notify closer in Slack: "Handoff sent" with summary:
   - Name, business, revenue range
   - Their #1 pain point
   - Any objections raised
   - Pre-call video sent? Y/N
4. Closer replies "done" in thread when they've received it
5. YOU own the show rate — follow the 6-touchpoint system
6. After the call: ask closer for disposition → update CRM
```

## 14. MINDSET & PRINCIPLES

These aren't suggestions. These are the operating principles that separate top setters from everyone else.

### The 8 Commandments

```
1.  You are a filter, not a pitcher.
    Your job is to find the right people and get them in the room.
    The closer does the rest.

2.  Tonality > Words.
    Sound like a confident, calm human — not a script reader.
    "Don't be Jordan Belfort. Be consultative." — Ben Bader

3.  Rejection is data.
    Every "no" sharpens your targeting.
    Don't take it personally. Don't argue. Move on. Next dial.

4.  Show rate is YOUR number.
    If they don't show, that's on you — not the closer,
    not the prospect. Follow up harder.

5.  Bad bookings are worse than no bookings.
    A closer who wastes 3 calls on unqualified leads
    loses momentum for the rest of the day.
    Protect their calendar like it's your own commission.

6.  The math is simple.
    50 dials → 10 conversations → 3 qualified → 2 booked → 1.5 show.
    Do this every day and the numbers take care of themselves.

7.  Energy is everything.
    If you sound bored, they hang up.
    If you sound desperate, they hang up.
    Sound like someone who has something valuable
    and is checking if they're the right fit.

8.  Speed wins.
    Respond to new leads in under 60 seconds.
    Follow up within 5 minutes of booking.
    Text morning-of every call.
    Slow follow-up = lost revenue.
```

### Jeremy Haynes' Responsibility Doctrine

> "It is always your fault."

If leads don't answer — your timing or list quality needs work.
If they don't book — your hook or qualification needs work.
If they don't show — your follow-up system needs work.
If they don't close — your qualification was off.

Everything upstream is your responsibility. Own it.

### Hormozi's Rule of 100

> Do 100 primary actions per day for 100 days. That's 10,000 actions in one direction.

For you, that's 50 cold dials + 50 warm follow-ups = 100 actions. Every day. For 100 days. The people who do this don't fail.
