# Agency OS — Landing Page Copy Deck

> **Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-21
> **Purpose:** Section-by-section copy ready for designer implementation
> **Tone:** Premium, clean, minimal. Operator-to-operator. Not hypey. Not corporate.
> **Framework:** Jeremy Haynes VSL page rules (headline + VSL + application, nothing else competing above fold)
> **Hosted VSL:** Agency_OS_VSL_Script.md (~16 min, talking head)

## SECTION 1: ABOVE THE FOLD

**Layout:** Full-width hero. Dark background (#0a0a0a or deep charcoal). Centered content. Maximum white space. Nothing competes with the VSL.

**Design direction:** The page should feel like a premium SaaS product page or a high-end consulting firm. No stock photos. No gradients. No decorative elements. Clean typography (Inter, Geist, or similar sans-serif). The only color accent is the CTA button.

---

### Headline

```
We Install Growth Engines Into 7-Figure Businesses — Then Run Them For You.
```

**Typography:** 40-48px, font-weight 600, white on dark. Single line on desktop, max 2 lines on mobile. No period at the end.

---

### Subheadline

```
A full-stack acquisition system built inside your business in 30 days and operated month over month — so you stop managing marketing and start scaling revenue.
```

**Typography:** 18-20px, font-weight 400, muted white (rgba 255,255,255,0.7). Max 2 lines on desktop. Sits directly below headline with 12px gap.

---

### VSL Embed

**Placeholder:** 16:9 ratio container, centered, max-width 800px. Dark thumbnail with a subtle play button overlay. Autoplay muted with visible play button to unmute. Hosted on Wistia (never YouTube/Vimeo for VSL hosting).

**Play bar:** Visible. No chapter markers on the public-facing player.

**Spacing:** 40px above the video from subheadline. 40px below the video to CTA button.

---

### Primary CTA Button

```
Apply to Work With Us
```

**Design:** Solid white button with dark text (#0a0a0a). Rounded corners (8px). 16px font, font-weight 600. Padding 16px 40px. Anchors to the application form section below.

**No subtext under the button.** No "limited spots" language here — that comes later. The button is the only interactive element on the screen.

---

### Above-the-Fold Rules (Jeremy Haynes)

- **Nothing else on this screen.** No navigation bar (hide it or make it minimal/transparent). No testimonials. No logos. No secondary CTAs. No chat widget.
- The viewer's only two options: watch the video or scroll down.
- Mobile: headline, subheadline, VSL, and CTA should all be visible without scrolling or within one thumb-scroll.

### Pain Bullets

**Bullet 1:**

> **You're burning ad spend with no system behind it.**
> You have a media buyer running Facebook ads and a designer making creatives, but there is no connected infrastructure underneath — no follow-up sequences, no attribution, no way to know what is actually driving revenue. Money goes out. Sometimes money comes back. You cannot explain why.

**Bullet 2:**

> **You've been through three agencies in two years.**
> Each one promised they were different. Each one had an impressive deck and a confident pitch. Each one delivered mediocre results, blamed the market, and disappeared — leaving you with a Google Drive folder full of unfinished work and nothing you can actually use.

**Bullet 3:**

> **You — the founder — are still the growth engine.**
> When you push hard, revenue goes up. When you step back for two weeks, it drops. You did not build a company. You built a job with no PTO, no ceiling, and no off switch.

**Bullet 4:**

> **Nobody owns the full picture.**
> You have a freelancer for ads, someone else for email, maybe an agency for one channel. They do not talk to each other. When results are bad, everyone points at someone else. You have become the project manager for people who should be solving problems, not creating them.

**Bullet 5:**

> **Your revenue looks like a heart monitor.**
> Good month, bad month, good month, terrible month. You cannot tell your team what next quarter looks like because you genuinely do not know. Every board meeting or planning session starts with a guess.

### Q1

**Q:** How is this different from hiring a marketing agency?

**A:** Most agencies run campaigns on one or two channels and send you a monthly report. We build and operate a full acquisition system — paid traffic, landing pages, email sequences, CRM workflows, attribution, and optimization — all connected, all measured, all owned by one team. When you stop working with a campaign agency, the campaigns die. When you stop working with us, the system keeps running because it was built inside your business.

---

### Q2

**Q:** What does the first 30 days look like?

**A:** Week 1 is a deep-dive kickoff — goals, ICP, positioning, asset audit, and account access. Weeks 2-4 we build your funnel infrastructure, install CRM flows, structure ad accounts, create initial creative, and verify all tracking. By day 30, your system is live and generating leads.

---

### Q3

**Q:** How long before I see results?

**A:** Most clients see qualified leads within the first 30 days of campaigns going live. Meaningful revenue impact — the kind you can see in your bank account — typically shows between days 60-90 as the system optimizes and your pipeline fills. We share weekly performance data from day one so you always know where things stand.

---

### Q4

**Q:** What if I've been burned by agencies before?

**A:** You probably have, and that is exactly why we built this differently. Three things separate us: (1) one team owns everything end-to-end instead of siloed vendors, (2) every system is built inside your accounts so you keep everything if you leave, and (3) there are no long-term contracts — 30 days notice, and you walk away with a fully documented, operational growth system.

---

### Q5

**Q:** Do you work with my specific industry?

**A:** We are growth system experts, not industry specialists. The mechanics of acquisition, conversion, and retention work the same whether you sell consulting, SaaS, real estate, professional services, or physical products. We bring the system. You bring the industry expertise. The messaging is built collaboratively because you know your customer better than we ever will.

---

### Q6

**Q:** What is the minimum commitment?

**A:** There is no long-term contract. We operate on a month-to-month basis with 30 days notice to cancel. Most clients stay because the system produces results — our retention rate at the three-month mark is above 90%. You are never locked in.

---

### Q7

**Q:** Can I just hire someone in-house instead?

**A:** You can. A head of growth costs $150K-$250K/year — that is $12K-$20K/month before benefits. And that is one person. You still need a media buyer, designer, copywriter, and email strategist. In-house you are looking at $30K-$50K/month in payroll. With us, you get the full team, full system, and full accountability for $5K-$10K/month — with no long-term commitment.

---

### Q8

**Q:** What do I need to provide on my end?

**A:** Access to your ad accounts, CRM, analytics, and website. Time for a 90-minute kickoff call and a 30-minute weekly check-in. Domain expertise about your customer and product. That is it. We handle everything else — strategy, execution, creative, reporting, and optimization.

---

### Q9

**Q:** What happens after I submit the application?

**A:** If you qualify based on your answers, you will immediately be able to book a 15-minute call with our team. That call is not a sales pitch — it is a mutual fit conversation. If we both see a fit, we schedule a longer strategy session where we map out your specific growth system, timeline, and investment. If we are not a fit, we will tell you directly. No pressure, no follow-up spam.

---

### Q10

**Q:** What if it doesn't work?

**A:** If we do not produce measurable results within 90 days, we continue working at no additional cost until we do. We are selective about who we take on specifically so we can make this guarantee. We do not accept clients we do not believe we can help — it is bad for our retention rate and bad for your business.

## GLOBAL DESIGN NOTES

### Typography
- **Heading font:** Inter, Geist Sans, or SF Pro Display — clean, modern, neutral
- **Body font:** Same family at lighter weights
- **Scale:** H1 40-48px, H2 28-32px, Body 16-18px, Small 14px, Legal 12px

### Colors
- **Background:** Near-black gradient (#050505 to #0a0a0a to #111111) — subtle section separation through shade variation, not lines or dividers
- **Text primary:** White (#ffffff)
- **Text secondary:** Muted white (rgba 255,255,255,0.7)
- **Text tertiary:** Dim white (rgba 255,255,255,0.4)
- **Accent:** None — the CTA button IS the accent by being the only white solid element on the page
- **No brand colors on the page.** The restraint is the brand.

### Spacing
- **Section padding:** 80-120px vertical
- **Content max-width:** 680-800px depending on section
- **Line height:** 1.6 for body copy, 1.2 for headlines

### Mobile
- All sections stack vertically
- Headline drops to 28-32px
- VSL maintains 16:9 ratio, full-width
- Form fields go full-width
- FAQ accordion works well on mobile by default
- Tier cards stack vertically with clear separation

### Performance
- No hero images or background videos
- Minimal JavaScript — form validation, smooth scroll, FAQ accordion
- Total page weight should be under 500KB excluding the VSL embed
- Load time under 2 seconds on 4G

### What Is NOT On This Page (Jeremy Haynes Rules)
- No navigation bar (or minimal transparent bar with logo only)
- No testimonial carousel or wall above the fold
- No partner logos or "as seen in" bar
- No chatbot or live chat widget
- No exit-intent popups
- No countdown timers
- No multiple CTAs competing for attention
- No "value stack" pricing table with crossed-out numbers
- No stock photography
