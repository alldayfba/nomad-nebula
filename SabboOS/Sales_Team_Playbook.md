## PHASE 0: BEFORE YOU HIRE ANYONE

**You must dial in the sales process yourself first.** Every creator agrees on this.

### Pre-Hire Checklist

- [ ] **Close 20-30 deals yourself.** You need to understand every objection, every emotional trigger, every price-point reaction before handing it off. (Hormozi, Tim Luong, Pierre)
- [ ] **Document your winning script.** Not a rough outline — the actual words that close deals. (Hormozi)
- [ ] **Achieve 40-60% close rate on warm leads.** If you can't close at this rate, the problem is your offer, not your need for a team. (Pierre: "Don't run ads until organic is dialed")
- [ ] **Build a pre-call nurture system.** Prospects should arrive on calls 80% sold. (Jeremy: "80% of conversion happens in marketing, 20% on the call")
- [ ] **Set up CRM + tracking.** You cannot manage what you cannot measure. At minimum: leads in, calls booked, show rate, close rate, cash collected, disposition codes. (Nik, SooWei: "Track everything")
- [ ] **Record 20+ of your own sales calls.** These become training material.

### Revenue Thresholds for Hiring (Tim Luong's Scaling Stages)

| Revenue | Who Closes | Who Manages |
|---|---|---|
| $0-$100K/mo | Founder closes everything | Founder manages |
| $100-$300K/mo | 1-2 closers, founder manages directly | Founder = full-time sales manager |
| $300K+/mo | 3+ closers | Dedicated sales manager (not founder) |

**Critical rule:** Don't hire a sales manager until you can afford a GREAT one. A mediocre sales manager is worse than the founder managing directly.

### SETTER

**Mission:** Turn inbound/outbound leads into booked, qualified calls with closers. The setter's job is NOT to sell — it's to qualify and create urgency.

**Offer-specific focus:**
- **Amazon OS setter:** DMs on Instagram, responds to YouTube/ad leads, qualifies for capital ($5K-$20K available), timeline, and motivation level
- **Agency OS setter:** LinkedIn + Instagram DMs, responds to VSL/application leads, qualifies for revenue ($1M+ annual), marketing maturity, and budget

**Daily responsibilities:**
1. **Morning block (9:00-10:00):** Review new inbound leads from overnight. Respond to every lead within 60 seconds during business hours (Hormozi: non-negotiable)
2. **Outbound block (10:00-12:00):**
   - 50 messages to new followers/leads (Nik: "50 new, 50 warm, 50 follow-ups")
   - Personalized openers — NOT copy-paste
   - Check prospect profile before reaching out (SooWei: "10-20 super qualified > 50 random")
3. **Follow-up block (12:00-1:00):** Follow up with all open conversations from prior days
4. **Afternoon nurture (1:00-3:00):** Send targeted content to warm leads (Pierre: "Send case study videos BEFORE booking")
5. **Booking block (3:00-5:00):** Push qualified leads to book. Send calendar links.
6. **End of day (5:00-5:30):** Update CRM, log all conversations, report daily numbers

**Setter scripts:**

**Instagram DM Opener (Amazon OS):**
> "Hey [Name]! Saw you followed — are you currently selling on Amazon or looking to start?"

If yes/interested:
> "Nice. Quick question — is the main bottleneck for you right now finding the right product, or getting the PPC and ranking dialed in?"

This is a yes/no choice question (Nik: "Always use yes/no questions, not open-ended"). Their answer tells you which case study video to send.

**Instagram DM Opener (Agency OS):**
> "Hey [Name] — noticed you're running [their business type]. Quick question: is your main growth bottleneck right now on the lead gen side or the conversion side?"

**Qualification questions (both offers):**

| Question | Amazon OS | Agency OS |
|---|---|---|
| Budget/Capital | "How much capital do you have to invest in inventory?" | "What's your current monthly marketing budget?" |
| Timeline | "When are you looking to launch your first product?" | "Is scaling something you're looking to solve this quarter?" |
| Commitment | "Are you looking to build this as a full business or a side project?" | "Are you looking for a partner to operate this or just advice?" |
| Revenue | "What's your current monthly revenue on Amazon?" | "What's your current monthly revenue?" |

**Disqualification criteria (do NOT book):**
- Amazon OS: Under $3K capital, pure "passive income" seeker, not willing to invest time
- Agency OS: Under $1M annual revenue, no product-market fit, looking for "cheap help"

**Setter KPIs (Nik's 50/50/50 framework):**

| Metric | Daily Target | Weekly Target |
|---|---|---|
| New outreach messages | 50 | 250 |
| Warm follow-up messages | 50 | 250 |
| Old lead re-engagement | 50 | 250 |
| Qualified conversations | 5-10 | 25-50 |
| Calls booked | 2-4 | 10-20 |
| Show rate (of their bookings) | 80%+ | 80%+ |

**Compensation:**
- Base: $2,000-$3,000/month
- Per qualified booked call that shows: $25-$50
- Per close (from their booking): $100-$200 or 3-5% of deal
- Target total comp: $4,000-$7,000/month

### CLOSER

**Mission:** Convert qualified, pre-sold prospects into paying clients. The closer's job is to collect payment — NOT to educate. (Pierre: "Their job should literally just be to collect payment.")

**Offer-specific focus:**
- **Amazon OS closer:** 45-60 min calls. Discovery → match their situation to the right tier → present offer → handle objections → close. Prospects should already understand the program from content/setter nurture.
- **Agency OS closer:** 30-45 min strategy calls. Diagnose their growth bottleneck → show how the system solves it → present retainer structure → close. More consultative, less "pitch."

**Daily responsibilities:**
1. **Pre-call prep (30 min before first call):** Review every prospect's application, DM conversation with setter, any content they've consumed. (SooWei: "Know their situation before they say a word")
2. **Calls (9:00 AM - 1:00 PM):** Back-to-back, no gaps (SooWei/Nik calendar structure). Maximum 6 calls/day.
3. **Post-call admin (after each call):** Log disposition, update CRM, send follow-up if needed (payment link, FAQ doc, case study)
4. **Follow-up block (2:00-3:00 PM):** Call/text all pending closes, handle remaining objections via text/voice memo
5. **Daily training (3:00-3:30 PM):** See training section below

**Closer call framework:**

**Phase 1 — Rapport & Context (5 min)**
- "Hey [Name], good to connect. I saw you [reference something specific from their application/DM]. Before we dive in, what's your situation right now and what are you hoping to accomplish?"
- Listen. Take notes. Label their pain. (Hormozi: "Recap/labeling prospect pain into problem buckets")

**Phase 2 — Discovery (10-15 min)**
- Ask about their current situation, what they've tried, what's working, what's not
- For Amazon OS: "What products have you looked at? What's your timeline? What would $10K/month in Amazon revenue change for you?"
- For Agency OS: "Walk me through your current acquisition process. Where do leads come from? What happens after they come in? Where are you losing people?"
- The goal: make them articulate the pain OUT LOUD. (Jeremy: "Take them to the pit")

**Phase 3 — Bridge (5 min)**
- "Based on what you're telling me, here's what I'm seeing..." — diagnose their #1 bottleneck
- Connect their specific pain to the specific mechanism in your offer
- This is NOT a pitch yet — it's a prescription

**Phase 4 — Present (5-10 min)**
- "Here's how we solve this..." — walk through the program/service structure
- For Amazon OS: the 90-day system, modules, coaching cadence, what they'll have built by the end
- For Agency OS: the 30-day install, what gets built, the ongoing operating rhythm, the metrics they'll see
- Reference a specific case study that matches their situation

**Phase 5 — Price & Close (5-10 min)**
- "Before I tell you the price, it's super expensive." (Hormozi: exact words — creates perfect anchor for both rich and not-rich)
- State the price directly. No dancing.
- Amazon OS: "$[X] for the full 90-day program. Most of our students see their first profitable product within 60 days."
- Agency OS: "$[X] per month, 12-month commitment. We install the full system in 30 days and operate it month over month."
- Then SHUT UP. Let them respond.

**Phase 6 — Objection Handling (as needed)**
- Common objections and responses:

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I need to think about it" | "Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Let's work through it right now so you have full clarity either way." |
| "It's too expensive" | "I hear you. Let me ask — if this program gets you to $10K/mo on Amazon in 90 days, what's that worth over the next 12 months? That's $120K in revenue from a $[X]K investment." |
| "I need to talk to my spouse/partner" | "Makes sense. What questions do you think they'll have? Let's make sure you can answer them." |
| "I've been burned before" | "That's fair. What happened? ... Sounds like [diagnose the problem with their previous experience]. Here's specifically how this is different: [mechanism + proof]." |
| "Can I get a discount?" | "I appreciate you asking. The price reflects the value of the system and the results we deliver. Would a payment plan work better for your cash flow?" |

**Closer KPIs:**

| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|
| Close rate | 30-40% (cold), 55-75% (warm/pre-sold) | Below 20% |
| Average deal size | Amazon: $5-8K / Agency: $10-25K/mo | Below $3K |
| Cash collected per call | Amazon: $1,500-$3,000 / Agency: $3,000-$8,000 | Below $1,000 |
| Calls per day | 4-6 | Below 3 or above 8 |
| Calendar utilization | 70-75% | Below 60% or above 85% |
| Follow-up close rate | 15-25% of non-closes | Below 10% |

**Compensation:**
- Base: $3,000-$5,000/month
- Commission: 8-12% of cash collected
- Bonus: Extra 2% on deals over $[threshold]
- Target total comp: $8,000-$15,000/month
- Top performer can make $20K+/month

### CSM (Client Success Manager)

**Mission:** Own the client experience from payment to renewal. Generate referrals, testimonials, upsells, and retention.

**Daily responsibilities:**
1. **Morning check-in (9:00-10:00):** Review all active client dashboards. Flag anyone who hasn't engaged in 48+ hours.
2. **Client communications (10:00-12:00):** Respond to all client messages. Answer questions. Send encouragement on milestones.
3. **Onboarding calls (as scheduled):** Run new client welcome calls, set expectations, assign first action items
4. **Milestone tracking (2:00-3:00):** Update client progress trackers. Identify who's ready for upsell. Identify who's at risk.
5. **Testimonial/referral capture (3:00-4:00):** Reach out to clients at peak satisfaction moments (SooWei: "Moment of greatest satisfaction = ask for referral")
6. **End of day (4:00-5:00):** Report: clients at risk, testimonials captured, upsells identified, referrals in pipeline

**CSM KPIs:**

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Client response time | Under 4 hours |
| 30-day retention | 95%+ |
| 90-day retention | 85%+ |
| Testimonials captured per month | 4+ |
| Referrals generated per month | 3+ |
| Upsell/renewal rate | 30%+ |
| Client NPS | 8.5+ |

**Compensation (Nik's CSM Incentive Structure):**
- Base: $3,000/month
- Referral commission: 10% per referred client who pays
- Payment plan renewal bonus: 5% per client who renews
- Upsell commission: 5-10% of upsold amount
- Testimonial bonus: $250 per testimonial captured
- Client win bonus: $100 per documented client win
- Target total comp: $5,000-$10,000/month

### SALES MANAGER (Hire at $300K+/mo)

**Mission:** Own the entire sales org. Hit revenue targets. Develop the team. Report to founder.

**This is the role Hormozi says is the #1 bottleneck for most businesses.**

**Daily responsibilities:**
1. **7:30 AM Standup:** (Pierre: morning standup) Quick team check-in. Energy. Yesterday's wins. Today's targets.
2. **8:00-8:30 AM Training block:** Run daily training session (see Phase 4 below)
3. **9:00 AM-1:00 PM:** Listen to live calls. Shadow closers. Give real-time coaching between calls.
4. **1:00-2:00 PM:** Review morning performance. Pull dashboards.
5. **2:00-4:00 PM:** 1-on-1s with underperformers. Call coaching. Script refinement.
6. **4:00-5:00 PM:** Pipeline review. Forecast. Report to founder.
7. **5:00-5:30 PM:** End-of-day debrief with team. Celebrate wins. Set tomorrow's focus.

**Sales Manager KPIs:**

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Team close rate | 35%+ blended |
| Revenue per month | Hitting monthly target |
| Team calendar utilization | 70-75% |
| Show rate | 80%+ |
| Training completion | 100% daily attendance |
| Ramp time for new hires | Under 14 days to full calendar |
| Attrition | Under 15% annual |

**Compensation:**
- Base: $8,000-$12,000/month
- Override: 3-5% of total team revenue
- Bonus: $2,000-$5,000 for hitting monthly team targets
- Target total comp: $15,000-$25,000/month

## PHASE 2: HIRING

### How to Find Salespeople

**Volume is key.** Tim Luong: "Do 20-30 interviews per key role. Most founders do 3."

**Sources:**
1. **Your own audience** — Post about the role on your socials. People who already follow you are pre-sold on the mission.
2. **Sales communities** — Closers.io (Cole Gordon), HighLevel communities, School communities
3. **Referrals** — Ask current team, ask network. Best hires come from referrals.
4. **Job boards** — Indeed, LinkedIn. Volume play. Expect 90% to be bad fits.
5. **Poach from competitors** — Ethically. If you see a good setter/closer in someone else's funnel, reach out.

### Interview Process

**Round 1: Application (filter 80%)**
- Written application with questions:
  - "What's the most you've ever earned in a month from sales? How?"
  - "Describe a time you lost a deal you should have won. What happened?"
  - "Why do you want to sell [Amazon coaching / growth services] specifically?"
  - "What's your 12-month income goal?"
- Red flags: vague answers, no specific numbers, "I'm a people person" without substance

**Round 2: Phone Screen (15 min)**
- Energy check. Are they on? Are they sharp? Do they listen or talk over you?
- Hormozi: "If you aren't learning from the person in the interview, especially for leadership roles, waste of time"
- Ask: "Sell me on why I should hire you in 60 seconds." — This IS a sales role.

**Round 3: Role Play (30 min)**
- Give them a mock scenario:
  - Amazon OS: "I'm interested in starting on Amazon but worried about competition. I have $15K to invest. Sell me on the coaching program."
  - Agency OS: "We're doing $2M/year but our marketing is a mess. We've hired 3 agencies that all failed. Why would you be different?"
- Score on: discovery quality, listening vs. talking ratio, objection handling, close attempt, energy/tonality
- Ben Bader: "Don't be Jordan Belfort with the script. Be casual, consultative."

**Round 4: Trial Period (2 weeks paid)**
- Put them on half-calendar (Hormozi's 2-week on-ramp)
- Real calls, real prospects
- Daily check-ins with you or sales manager
- Clear pass/fail criteria before they start

### Hiring Red Flags (Do NOT Hire)

- Talks more than listens on the interview call
- Can't articulate specific numbers from their past performance
- "I can sell anything" — but can't demonstrate it in role play
- Wants to change your offer/targeting on day 1 (Tim Luong: "Don't let closers change your offer")
- More excited about base pay than commission (wrong hunger level)
- Uses iPhone? YES — this matters for rich B2B prospects (Jeremy: "Green texts = ignored")

## PHASE 3: ONBOARDING & TRAINING

### Hormozi's 2-Week On-Ramp (The Gold Standard)

**Week 1: Two-a-Days + Script Mastery**

| Day | Morning Session (2 hrs) | Afternoon Session (2 hrs) |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Company overview. Offer deep-dive. Why this product changes lives. Watch 5 recorded sales calls. | **Black Marker Method** begins: Print 2 copies of closing script. Read aloud. Black out one word. Repeat. |
| Tue | ICP training. Who are our buyers? What are they afraid of? What do they want? Watch 5 more calls. | Black Marker Method continues. Start role-playing intro sequence with manager. |
| Wed | Objection training: Top 10 objections + responses. Listen to calls where objections were handled well. | Black Marker Method continues. Role-play discovery section. |
| Thu | Product knowledge deep-dive. For Amazon OS: walk through the curriculum, sourcing process, PPC. For Agency OS: walk through the Growth OS, 30-day install, delivery. | Black Marker Method continues. Role-play pitch + price section. |
| Fri | Full mock call with manager. Manager plays the prospect (hard mode). Feedback session. | Black Marker Method continues. Role-play objection handling. |
| Sat | (Optional) Listen to 10 more recorded calls. Take notes on patterns. | Rest. |

**Milestone 1 (End of Week 1): Script Memorization**
- Must recite the full script cold — no notes
- Manager tests by asking random questions mid-script
- If can't pass in 2-3 days → consider cutting (Hormozi: "Can't pass milestone 1 in 2-3 days = out")

**Week 2: Ride-Alongs + Live Calls**

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Mon | Shadow manager on 3-4 live calls. Take notes. Debrief after each. |
| Tue | Shadow + handle one section of the call (intro or discovery). Manager takes over for close. |
| Wed | Handle full call with manager listening silently. Manager gives feedback after. |
| Thu | Solo calls begin (half calendar — 3 calls max). Manager reviews recordings same day. |
| Fri | Solo calls continue (half calendar). End-of-week review with manager. |

**Milestone 2 (Mid-Week 2): Recap/Labeling**
- Can accurately recap a prospect's situation and label their pain into the correct "problem buckets"
- Manager tests by having them recap a practice call from memory

**Milestone 3 (End of Week 2): Objection Handling**
- Can handle the top 10 objections naturally without reading from a sheet
- Pass all 3 milestones → half calendar continues
- Hit KPI 5 days straight on half calendar → graduate to full calendar
- Miss KPI → remedial training → one more shot → out

### The Black Marker Method (Script Memorization)

This is Hormozi's signature training technique. It works.

1. Print **2 copies** of the sales script — one clean reference, one working copy
2. Read the working copy out loud, start to finish
3. Take a black marker and black out **one word**
4. Read the script again from memory. If stuck, glance at the clean copy.
5. Black out another word
6. Repeat until **every word is blacked out**

A 400-word script = ~400 repetitions. By the end, the rep doesn't think about the words. They OWN them.

**Why it works:** Reps who "know" the script but haven't internalized it sound robotic. Reps who've done the Black Marker Method sound natural because the script is in their subconscious. The boredom of repetition forces them to find natural inflection and personality within the words.

### Setter-Specific Training

**48-Hour Setter Onboarding (SooWei's Framework):**
1. **Hour 0-5:** Full appointment setting course. Teach: what a setter does, why it matters, the psychology of DM conversations, how to qualify, how to create urgency
2. **Hour 5-6:** Advanced DM setting module. Teach: opener testing, follow-up cadence, lead scoring
3. **Hour 6-7:** Phone setting/triage module (for leads who need a call before closer)
4. **Hour 7-8:** Quiz test — must score 90%+ in 2 attempts
5. **Hour 8-9:** Niche-specific mock conversation with manager
6. **Hour 9-10:** First live conversations under supervision

**Setter Training Content:**
- How to read a prospect's profile in 30 seconds (what to look for)
- The opener library (5-10 tested openers for each offer)
- How to qualify without interrogating (conversational flow)
- How to create urgency (SooWei: Urgency-Pain-Scarcity Triangle)
- When to send a case study video vs. when to book directly
- How to handle "not interested" / "not right now" / ghosting
- When to escalate to the closer vs. when to nurture more

## PHASE 4: DAILY OPERATING RHYTHM

### The Non-Negotiable Daily Schedule

**Every single day. No exceptions. This is the heartbeat of the sales org.**

```
7:30 AM   — Morning Standup (15 min)
8:00 AM   — Daily Training (30 min)
8:30 AM   — Pre-call prep
9:00 AM   — Calls begin (closers) / Outreach begins (setters)
1:00 PM   — Lunch + pipeline review
2:00 PM   — Afternoon calls / follow-ups
4:00 PM   — End of calls
4:00 PM   — Admin: CRM updates, dispositions, follow-up scheduling
4:30 PM   — End-of-day numbers check
5:00 PM   — Day ends
```

### 7:30 AM — Morning Standup (Pierre's Model)

**Duration:** 15 minutes. Not 30. Not 45. Fifteen.

**Format:**
1. **Energy check** (2 min): "Good morning. How's everyone feeling? Scale of 1-10." (Ben Bader: "Energy is everything. Good morning in all caps, everyone's hyped.")
2. **Yesterday's wins** (3 min): Each rep shares their best moment from yesterday. A close, a great conversation, a lesson.
3. **Today's focus** (5 min): Manager sets the theme. "Today we're focusing on follow-up closes" or "Today's objection focus: 'I need to think about it'"
4. **Numbers** (5 min): Quick scoreboard — yesterday's booked calls, show rate, closes, cash collected. Celebrate. Don't dwell on misses (save that for 1-on-1s).

### 8:00 AM — Daily Training (Hormozi's Exact Framework)

**Duration:** 30 minutes. Non-negotiable. Even if revenue is up. Especially if revenue is up.

**Weekly Rotation:**

| Day | Topic | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Intro / Opening | Manager models the ideal intro. Then 1-on-1 role play. Then pairs practice. |
| Tuesday | Discovery | Manager models discovery. Pairs role play different prospect scenarios. |
| Wednesday | Pitch / Presentation | Manager models the pitch. Pairs practice presenting the offer. |
| Thursday | Close / Objection Handling | Manager models handling the week's top objection. Pairs practice. |
| Friday | Hot Topic / Game Film | Review a real call recording from the week. Discuss as a team. OR: address the week's trickiest objection/scenario. |

**Training rules:**
1. Manager ALWAYS models first. Don't just tell them — show them. (Hormozi: "Manager models first, then 1-on-1, then pairs")
2. Every rep participates. No spectators.
3. Training is practice, not theory. 80% doing, 20% explaining.
4. Rotate partners. Don't let reps only practice with their buddy.
5. Friday game film: pick ONE call (good or bad), play it for the team, discuss what to replicate or fix. (Hormozi: "Game footage review in front of team")

## PHASE 5: WEEKLY OPERATING RHYTHM

### Weekly 1-on-1 with Each Rep (Hormozi's 3-Part Structure)

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes per rep. Schedule every week. Never skip.

**Part 1 — Personal (10 min):**
- "How are you doing? What's going on in your life?"
- This is NOT small talk. This is how you catch problems before they tank performance.
- Are they sleeping? Eating? Stressed about something outside work? Having relationship problems?
- Hormozi: "Personal first — short-term second — long-term third"

**Part 2 — Short-Term Performance (20 min):**
- Rep submits 3 call recordings before the meeting (Hormozi: "3 submitted call recordings, specific coaching")
- Review together. Listen to the hardest moments.
- Identify ONE specific thing to improve this week. Not five. ONE.
- Example: "This week, I want you to pause for 3 full seconds after stating the price. You're rushing past it."

**Part 3 — Long-Term Development (10 min):**
- "Where do you want to be in 6 months? 12 months?"
- Career pathing conversation (see Phase 9)
- Perks for advancement: mastermind access, higher commission tier, better leads, title change
- Hormozi: "Give new hires a promotion within 30 days — something to call mom about"

### Weekly Team Meeting

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes. Same day/time every week.

**Agenda:**
1. **Scoreboard review** (10 min): Weekly numbers for the team. Celebrate top performer.
2. **Game film** (20 min): Play one call (ideally from a rep who volunteers). Discuss as a team. This is the #1 learning moment of the week.
3. **Seasonal/trending objection** (15 min): What's the hardest objection this week? Workshop it together. (Hormozi: "Focus on seasonal/trending objections")
4. **Announcements** (5 min): Any offer changes, new case studies, upcoming events.
5. **Energy close** (5 min): End on a high. Recognition. Pump-up.

### Weekly Sales Manager → Founder Report

Every Monday, the sales manager delivers:
1. Last week's numbers vs. target
2. Pipeline forecast for this week
3. Top risk: what could go wrong this week
4. Top opportunity: what could break out this week
5. Team health: any personnel issues, morale concerns
6. One recommendation: a specific change to improve performance

## PHASE 6: MONTHLY OPERATING RHYTHM

### Monthly Deep Dive

**Duration:** 2-3 hours. Founder + Sales Manager.

**Agenda:**
1. **Month review:** Revenue, close rate, show rate, CAC, cash collected, refund rate

### KPI Pairing Rule (Hormozi)

**Every metric MUST be paired: volume + quality.**
- Setter: Calls booked (volume) + Show rate (quality)

- CSM: Clients managed (volume) + NPS/retention (quality)

"I don't want you to burn through my leads." A closer who does 50 calls and closes 5 is worse than one who does 25 calls and closes 8.

### Disposition System (Jeremy Haynes)

**After every call, the closer must log a specific disposition — NOT "good lead" or "bad lead."**

| Disposition Code | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| CLOSED-PIF | Closed, paid in full | Welcome sequence triggered |
| CLOSED-PP | Closed, payment plan | Welcome sequence + payment tracking |
| FOLLOW-UP | Interested but needs time | Schedule follow-up call within 48 hrs |
| NOT-QUALIFIED-REVENUE | Revenue too low | Nurture sequence, revisit in 90 days |
| NOT-QUALIFIED-CAPITAL | Capital too low (Amazon) | Nurture sequence, revisit in 90 days |
| NOT-QUALIFIED-FIT | Wrong business model/stage | Archive |
| TIMING | Good fit but wrong timing | 30/60/90 day follow-up sequence |
| COMPETITOR | Already working with someone | Re-engage in 6 months |
| NO-SHOW-1 | First no-show | Setter reschedules within 24 hrs |
| NO-SHOW-2 | Second no-show | One more attempt, then archive |
| NO-SHOW-3 | Third no-show | Archive. Do not re-book. |
| GHOST | Booked, didn't answer, no response | 3-touch sequence, then archive |

This gives marketing actionable data. If 40% of dispositions are "NOT-QUALIFIED-REVENUE," the targeting is off. If 30% are "TIMING," we need better urgency creation.

## PHASE 9: CAREER PROGRESSION & RETENTION

### Bimodal Career Path (Hormozi)

Two tracks. People can advance on EITHER. Advancement is based on **GROSS OUTPUT** (deals closed, revenue generated), NOT tenure.

**Track A: Individual Contributor**
```
Junior Setter → Setter → Senior Setter → Junior Closer → Closer → Senior Closer
```

**Track B: Leadership**
```
Senior Closer → Team Lead → Sales Manager → VP Sales / Advisor / Partner
```

### Advancement Criteria

| Level | Title | Requirements | Perks |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Junior Setter | Hired. In training. | Base pay. Learning. |
| 2 | Setter | Passed 48-hr onboarding + quiz. Live conversations. | Full comp structure. "Something to call mom about." |
| 3 | Senior Setter | 3+ months, 80%+ show rate, 25+ books/month consistently | Higher per-book pay. Best lead assignments. No weekend shifts. |
| 4 | Junior Closer | Proven setter wants to close. Passes closer training + mock calls. | Half calendar. Closer commission on their deals. |
| 5 | Closer | Passed 2-week on-ramp. 30%+ close rate on full calendar for 30+ days. | Full calendar. Full comp. Best parking spot (Hormozi). |
| 6 | Senior Closer | 40%+ close rate sustained 3+ months. Consistently top 20% of team. | Higher commission rate. Mastermind access. First pick on premium leads. |
| 7 | Team Lead | Willing to mentor + develop 2-3 reps while maintaining own book. | Override on mentees' deals. Title. Leadership training. |
| 8 | Sales Manager | Full-time management. No longer taking calls (or minimal). Owns the team's number. | Manager comp package. Equity/profit share conversation. |

### Retention Mechanics

1. **Promote within 30 days.** Even if it's just Junior Setter → Setter. Give them a win early. (Hormozi: "Give new hires a promotion within 30 days — something to call mom about")
2. **Weekly pay** when possible. (Pierre: "Pay every Monday. Start the week strong.")
3. **Public recognition.** Call out top performers in the standup. Slack channel. Team group chat.
4. **Perks ladder:**
   - Setter → Senior Setter: No weekend shifts
   - Closer → Senior Closer: Mastermind/event access, best lead routing
   - Senior Closer → Team Lead: Override income, leadership training
5. **Growth investment.** Send top performers to events. Buy them courses. (Hormozi: "Access to masterminds as a perk")
6. **Career conversations.** Monthly. "Where do you want to be?" Show them the path. Make it real.

### The Nail Salon Principle (Hormozi)

From the video: A nail salon owner created a skill-based progression for technicians. Taught the most basic skill first → proved they'd stick → taught next skill → their billable rate went up. Took girls without high school diplomas to $75K/year (2x industry average). Kept them for years.

**Apply this:** Every level unlocks new skills (closing techniques, offer knowledge, leadership). Each skill makes them more valuable AND more compensated. They stay because they're growing.

## PHASE 10: SCALING THE TEAM

### When to Hire the Next Rep

**Hormozi's rule:** "I have yet to make less money by hiring more salespeople."

**Trigger points:**
- Calendar utilization consistently above 80% for 2+ weeks → hire another closer
- Setter booking 20+ calls/week and wants more → hire another setter
- Show rate dropping below 75% → could indicate setter is rushing bookings OR closer calendar is too full. Diagnose first.
- Close rate above 40% sustained → you're possibly leaving money on the table by not having more at-bats. Hire.

### How to Scale Without Breaking

1. **Never scale before standardizing.** (Pierre: "Observe top performer → document their framework → replicate")
2. **New hire shadows the best rep, not the manager.** Real calls, real energy, real workflow.
3. **Stagger hires.** Don't hire 3 closers on the same day. Hire one, stabilize for 2-3 weeks, then hire the next.
4. **The "training tax" is real.** Every new hire temporarily reduces the manager's bandwidth. Account for it.
5. **Quality of lead flow must scale WITH the team.** More closers with the same lead flow = underutilized calendars = demoralization. (Hormozi: "Below 60% utilization: morale drops, commission breath")

### Scaling by Revenue Band

| Revenue | Team Size | Key Move |
|---|---|---|
| $0-$50K/mo | Founder + 1 setter | Founder closes everything |
| $50-$100K/mo | Founder + 1 setter + 1 closer | Founder manages + takes overflow calls |
| $100-$200K/mo | 2 setters + 2 closers + 1 CSM | Founder goes 80% management, 20% calls |
| $200-$500K/mo | 3 setters + 3 closers + 1-2 CSMs + Sales Manager | Founder steps out of calls entirely |
| $500K+/mo | Full org (see Phase 1 chart) | Founder focuses on offer, content, strategy |

## PHASE 11: AI & AUTOMATION LAYER

### AI Sales QC System (Tim Luong)

**Setup:**
1. All calls recorded in Fathom (or Gong, Otter, etc.)
2. Transcripts auto-sent via Zapier to a custom GPT/Claude project
3. GPT trained on: your sales script, objection handling framework, disposition criteria, KPI benchmarks
4. AI reviews every call and generates:
   - Score (1-10) on: discovery quality, objection handling, close attempt, tonality, compliance
   - Specific feedback: "Closer skipped the price anchor — jumped straight to the number"
   - Suggested improvement for next call
5. Feedback auto-posted to Slack, tagging the specific closer
6. Closer responds with what they'll fix

**This replaces 60+ hours/month of manual call review.** (Ben Bader: Manis AI for monthly call analysis)

### AI Post-Sale Feedback Loop (Tim Luong)

2-3x per week:
1. Pull sales call transcripts from the last 48 hours
2. Feed to Claude/Gemini with prompt: "How aligned are these callers with our ideal avatar? What patterns do you see in objections? Are leads getting more or less qualified?"
3. Use insights to adjust messaging, targeting, setter scripts

**Tim's result:** Average booked call went from $25K/mo revenue clients to $300K/mo revenue clients in 60 days by tuning the messaging based on this loop.

### DM Automation (Nik's Machi-style)

- Auto-tag new followers by source (ad, organic, referral)
- Auto-send personalized opener based on source
- A/B test openers automatically and track reply rates
- Route qualified conversations to the right setter
- Auto-remind setter of follow-ups due today
- Weekly report: total new leads, qualified leads, calls booked, setter performance

### CRM Automation

- Lead comes in → auto-assigned to setter → setter gets Slack notification
- Call booked → pre-call nurture email sequence fires automatically
- No-show → auto-reschedule email + setter notification
- Closed → welcome email + CSM notification + onboarding sequence
- Disposition logged → feeds back to marketing dashboards automatically

### If you're at $0-$50K/mo (Do This First):

2. **Write your script.** Use the frameworks in Phase 7.
3. **Hire 1 setter.** Train using SooWei's 48-hour model.

9. **Implement weekly 1-on-1s.** Hormozi's 3-part structure.
10. **Set up AI QC.** Fathom → Zapier → Claude/GPT → Slack.

12. **Implement the full daily/weekly/monthly rhythm.** All of Phase 4-6.

16. **Hire the sales manager.** This is the hardest and most important hire.
17. **Remove yourself from all sales calls.**
18. **Implement Pierre's Wipe & Scale** if any underperformers remain.
19. **Scale the team using Phase 10 triggers.**
20. **Your job is now: content, offer, and strategy.** Nothing else.
