## 2. THE 6 SYSTEMIC PROBLEMS

### Problem 1: No Qualification Gate — Money Discovered Way Too Late

**What's happening:** You're running 30-45 minute pitches before learning whether the prospect can afford the program. The existing closing script has capital qualification at minute 3-18. You're doing it at minute 44+.

**Evidence:**
- **Brian (@44:17):** "I set aside like 1K" — minute 44. You'd already run the full 3-pillar pitch before learning he couldn't afford it.
- **Josh (@48:19):** "I didn't even know the mentorship was a thing. So I didn't even know it was like five grand... I said a thousand" — minute 48. He came for an info call and you ran the full pitch blind.
- **Deshawn (@1:01:25):** "Probably like 2000" — minute 61. You pitched for a full hour before learning he had $2K total and no personal credit card.
- **Carlos (@36:12):** Carlos asks YOU "how much do you think we need to invest?" You never asked him what he had available. You assumed.
- **Nkoye:** Never explicitly asked. He volunteered "I have the 5K" at minute 100 — after you'd been pitching for over an hour.

**Why it kills the deal:** When you don't know their capital early, you can't tailor the pitch. You pitched a $5K program to a kid with $1K for 44 minutes. You pitched the same program to a guy with $50K+ for 95 minutes. Neither pitch was calibrated because you didn't have the data.

**The fix:** The closing script already has this. In discovery (minute 3-10), ask:

> "The coaching program is a separate investment from your product inventory. You're going to need at minimum $5,000 set aside for inventory on top of the coaching fee. How much do you have available right now — for both?"

If they have less than $5K total (program + inventory), the call is 15 minutes. Diagnose, disqualify warmly, route to free content. Don't run a 90-minute pitch on someone who can't buy.

### Problem 3: Can't Identify Real Objections

**What's happening:** Every objection gets treated as a "mindset" or "commitment" problem. Money objections get commitment reframes. Trust wounds get "winners vs losers" framing. Comparison shopping gets urgency pressure. You're using one tool for every problem.

**Evidence:**
- **Carlos's real objection was trust** — he got scammed by Miami mentors who took his money and ghosted (@8:14-9:48). His objection was never "I don't believe in coaching." It was "the last people I paid didn't deliver." You never directly addressed this. You never said "here's specifically how we're different from them."
- **Deshawn's real objection was money** — he had $2K total, no credit card, shared credit with his sister (@59:19-1:01:25). You treated this as a commitment problem and pushed for 50+ more minutes.
- **Nkoye's real objection was comparison** — he told you he had a call Monday with another program (@1:10:20). He was shopping. You never asked what they offered, what their price was, or how to differentiate.
- **Josh's real situation was info-only** — he didn't know about the program before the call (@48:19-49:27). He came to learn about Amazon, not to buy coaching. You ran the full 81-minute pitch anyway.

**Why it kills the deal:** When you misdiagnose the objection, your response makes things worse. Telling a scam victim to "trust the process" reinforces their fear. Telling someone with $2K to "commit" when they literally can't afford it is tone-deaf. Pressuring a comparison shopper to decide NOW makes you look like the programs he's trying to avoid.

**The fix:** Before responding to any objection, classify it:

| Objection Type | Signal | Response Framework |
|---|---|---|
| **Money (real)** | Under $5K total, no credit card, shared finances | Warm disqualify or genuine payment plan. Do NOT pressure. |
| **Trust wound** | "I got scammed before," "how do I know you're different" | Acknowledge → Ask what happened → Differentiate specifically |
| **Comparison shopping** | "I'm talking to other programs," "I need to think about it" | Ask what they offer → Differentiate on 3 points → Book callback |
| **Authority** | "I need to talk to my partner/brother/wife" | Ask if they're bought in → Get the partner on the call |
| **Info-only** | "I didn't know about the program," "just learning" | Switch to Track B (20 min max) → Route to VSL/callback |
| **Fear/comfort** | "I need to be at peace," "biggest risk of my life" | Validate → Reduce the risk → Guarantee → Specific next step |

Hard cap: **55 minutes total**. If you haven't closed by minute 45, you're in wrap-up mode. Book a callback or release.

## 3. ADDITIONAL PATTERN FIXES

## 6. OBJECTION PLAYBOOK — TOP 5

### Objection 1: "I don't have the money"

**What you currently do:** Push payment plans, credit card strategies, "duffel bag" hypotheticals, and keep pressing for 30-50 more minutes.

**The real question:** Do they actually not have it, or are they hiding behind money?

**Step 1 — Clarify:** "I hear you. Is it that you don't have the capital at all right now, or is it that you're not sure this is the right place to put it?"

**Step 2 — If they genuinely don't have it (Deshawn):** "I respect that. I don't want you going into debt or stressing about payments. Here's what I'd recommend — save up, keep following Sabbo's content, and reach out when you've got the capital set aside. We'll be here." **End the call. 15 minutes.**

**Step 3 — If they have it but are unsure (Terrencio):** That's not a money objection. It's a belief or trust objection. Handle THAT instead.

### Objection 2: "I need to think about it" / "Let me pray on it"

**What you currently do:** Push for 30-50 more minutes with "10K version," "losers/winners," commitment scale, and faith manipulation.

**The real question:** What specifically do they need to think about?

**Step 1:** "I respect that. Can I ask — what specifically are you weighing? Is it the money, the timing, or whether this is the right program?"

**Step 2 — If they invoke faith/prayer:** "I respect that completely. Let's set a specific time to reconnect — not 'sometime,' but a real date. Does Thursday at 2pm work?" **Stop. Do not push. Do not ask what God would want. Do not compare them to Michael Jordan.**

**Step 3 — If "think about it" is a smokescreen:** The real objection usually came up earlier in the call. Reference the Pre-Frame (Stage 4): "Earlier you mentioned [X] held you back. Is that what's coming up right now?"

### Objection 3: "I've been scammed before" (Carlos-type trust wound)

**What you currently do:** Acknowledge it briefly, then run the standard pitch. Never address it head-on. Never differentiate specifically from the scammers.

**The fix — address it directly:**

**Step 1 — Acknowledge:** "That experience would make anyone cautious. Tell me specifically what happened — what did they promise, and what did they actually deliver?"

**Step 2 — Listen.** Let them tell the full story. Carlos needed to vent about the Miami guys. You rushed past it.

**Step 3 — Differentiate specifically:** "Here's how we're different from what you described:
- Sabbo sells on Amazon every single day. He's not just teaching from 2020 experience.
- You get a private Discord channel with direct access. If Sabbo doesn't respond in 30 minutes, something is wrong — that's not how this works.
- Every call is recorded. Every product lead is documented. There's no 'pay and disappear.'
- You can join the free Discord and see the community in action before you pay a dime."

**Step 4 — Reduce risk:** "I'll tell you what — join the free community first. Sit in for a week. Watch the group calls. See how Sabbo interacts with students. Then make your decision. If you still want to join after seeing it from the inside, we'll be here."

**Carlos had $50K+ and said "I got the money." This was a closeable deal if you'd addressed the trust wound instead of running the standard pitch.**

### Objection 4: "I'm comparing programs" (Nkoye-type)

**What you currently do:** Ignore it or pressure them to decide now.

**The fix:**

**Step 1 — Ask directly:** "What programs are you looking at? What are they offering, and at what price?"

**Step 2 — Differentiate on 3 points:**
1. **Operator-coach model:** "Sabbo sells on Amazon today — he's not someone who sold in 2020 and now just teaches. He's actively finding products, running the business, and showing students what's working right now."
2. **Daily product leads + AI sourcing:** "We send 3-5 vetted products to you every day. Our AI scrapes 300+ retailers. Name another program that does that."
3. **Lifetime access at $5K flat:** "Most programs are $200-500/month recurring. At $5K lifetime, you break even in 10-25 months vs monthly programs — and you keep access forever."

**Step 3 — Don't force a same-day close:** "Take the time to compare. I'd rather you make the right decision than a fast one. But let's set a specific date to reconnect — when are you planning to decide?"

### Objection 5: "I'm just here for info" / "I didn't know about the program" (Josh-type)

**What you currently do:** Run the full 81-minute pitch anyway, then push payment plans on someone who had no buying intent.

**The fix:** Route to Track B immediately.

> "Totally understand — let me give you the quick overview so you know exactly what we offer, and then if it makes sense, we can talk about timing."

20 minutes max. Overview the 3 pillars in 5 minutes (not 20). End with:

> "Based on where you're at, here's what I'd recommend: [specific next step — VSL, free Discord, callback when you have capital]. No pressure."
