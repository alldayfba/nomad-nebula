## 1. WELCOME & YOUR MISSION

Congratulations on joining the team. You've been chosen to close for a multi-seven-figure growth agency. That is not a small thing. Now it's time to get to work.

### What You Are

```
You are NOT pitching. You are diagnosing and collecting payment.

Your ONLY job ──► Convert qualified, pre-sold prospects into paying clients.

The setter's job is to get them in the room.
Your job is to collect payment.

You don't chase unqualified leads.
You don't beg for the sale.
You diagnose, prescribe, and close.
```

Pierre Khoury: *"Their job should literally just be to collect payment."*

### What You're Running

- 30-45 minute strategy calls via Zoom
- Prospects arrive pre-qualified by the setter: $1M+ revenue, product-market fit, no repeatable growth system
- You diagnose their growth bottleneck, present the Growth OS, and close the deal
- We call it a "strategy session" — NEVER a "sales call" or "pitch"

### Why This Role Matters

The closer is the revenue engine. Without you converting qualified calls into paying clients, every upstream effort — content, ads, setters — produces zero revenue.

- **A great closer** closes 35%+ of qualified calls, averages $3K+ cash collected per call, and builds a pipeline of follow-ups that close at 15%+ within 14 days.
- **A bad closer** blames the leads, skips pre-call prep, lets objections derail the call, and leaves money on the table every single day.

**It's better to close 2 deals at full price than 5 discounted ones.** Every discount trains the market to negotiate. Every full-price close validates the offer. Protect the price like it's your own equity.

## 4. WHO YOU'RE CLOSING (ICP)

### Ideal Client Profile

| Criteria | Qualified | Disqualified |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $1M+/year (ideally $2M-$10M) | Under $1M or pre-revenue |
| **Product-Market Fit** | Customers love the product, proven offer | No clear product, no repeat customers |
| **Growth Problem** | Has PMF but no repeatable acquisition engine | Needs help building the product itself |
| **Marketing Spend** | Currently spending on marketing (even poorly) | "We don't believe in marketing" |
| **Past Attempts** | Tried agencies, freelancers, or internal hires that didn't work | Has never invested in marketing |
| **Decision Maker** | Founder/CEO is on the call AND is the decision maker | Needs board approval, can't commit |
| **Budget** | Can invest $5K-$25K/mo if fit is right | Unfunded startup, no marketing budget |
| **Urgency** | Wants to solve this now, not "next quarter" | Exploring, no timeline, tire-kicker |

### What Makes a Great Prospect (Pre-Call Signals)

Before the call, look for these signals in the setter notes and application:

- They mentioned a specific revenue number (not "we're doing well")
- They described a growth bottleneck they've been trying to solve
- They have burned money on ads or agencies before
- They are the founder or CEO, not a marketing coordinator
- They consumed content before booking (watched VSL, read case study)

### Red Flags (Manage Expectations or Disqualify on the Call)

- They want you to guarantee specific revenue numbers
- They are comparing you purely on price against cheaper agencies
- They are not the decision maker and can't get the decision maker on the call
- Their business is pre-revenue or pre-PMF
- They want a "trial month" before committing
- They don't have an existing customer base (no proof of PMF)

## 5. THE STRATEGY CALL SCRIPT (9 STAGES)

The full script is documented in `Agency_OS_Closer_Script.md`. Study it. Memorize it. Own it.

Here is the structural overview:

```
Total call time: 30-45 minutes

    STAGE 1 ──► Connecting ........................ 2-3 min
    STAGE 2 ──► Situation Questions ............... 5-7 min
    STAGE 3 ──► Problem Awareness ................. 5-7 min
    STAGE 4 ──► Pre-Frame Psychology .............. 3-5 min
    STAGE 5 ──► Solution Awareness + Future Pacing  5-7 min
    STAGE 6 ──► Transition to Pitch ............... 1-2 min
    STAGE 7 ──► The Pitch (3 Pillars) ............. 5-10 min
    STAGE 8 ──► Price + Close ..................... 5-10 min
    STAGE 9 ──► Objection Handling ................ as needed
```

### Stage-by-Stage Summary

**Stage 1 — Connecting:** Set the frame. This is a strategy session, not a sales pitch. Get a micro-commitment that they'll give you 30-40 minutes of focused attention. If they only have 15 minutes, reschedule.

**Stage 2 — Situation Questions:** Collect data. Revenue, team, channels, CAC/LTV, lead flow, marketing budget. Sound like a doctor doing intake — gathering data, not reacting or suggesting yet.

**Stage 3 — Problem Awareness:** Diagnose. Make them articulate the pain out loud. What's holding them back? What have they tried? How did that go? What is inaction costing them every month? Get them to put a dollar number on the opportunity cost.

**Stage 4 — Pre-Frame Psychology:** The most important 3-5 minutes of the call. Ask: "What's actually prevented you from solving this problem until now?" Their answer IS the objection they will present at the close. Neutralize it here, before you ever pitch.

**Stage 5 — Solution Awareness + Future Pacing:** Paint two pictures. First: what changes if they have a full acquisition engine in 30 days? Let them describe it. Second: what happens if nothing changes for 12 more months? Make the cost of inaction concrete.

**Stage 6 — Transition to Pitch:** Get explicit permission to present. "I think there's a real fit here. Can I walk you through what working with us looks like?"

**Stage 7 — The Pitch:** Three pillars. (1) Infrastructure Install — what gets built in 30 days. (2) Full-Stack Operation — what happens month over month. (3) Data + Attribution — full visibility into what's working and why.

**Stage 8 — Price + Close:** Pre-frame that it's not cheap. State the range ($5K-$25K/mo). Give a specific estimate based on their scope. State it once. Stop talking. Let them react. Tie to ROI using THEIR opportunity cost number from Stage 3.

**Stage 9 — Objection Handling:** If Stage 4 was done correctly, most objections are pre-dissolved. When one surfaces: Acknowledge > Clarify > Suppose > Callback to Stage 4 > Reframe > Check > Re-commit.

### Key Transitions Between Stages

| From | To | What You Say |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 2 | Stage 3 | "So based on what you're telling me... what's the biggest thing holding you back from 2x-ing the business?" |
| Stage 3 | Stage 4 | "What's actually prevented you from solving this problem until now?" |
| Stage 4 | Stage 5 | "If you had a full acquisition engine in 30 days, what does that change?" |
| Stage 5 | Stage 6 | "I think there's a real fit here. Can I show you what it looks like?" |
| Stage 7 | Stage 8 | "Before I tell you the investment, I want to be upfront — this isn't cheap." |

### Tonality Reference

| Tone | When | How It Sounds |
|---|---|---|
| **Curious** | Stages 2-3, all discovery | Genuine interest, "I actually want to understand" |
| **Concerned** | When they share pain, past failures | Lean in, lower voice, slow down |
| **Challenging** | Consequence questions, calling out patterns | Direct, steady, not aggressive |
| **Confident** | Pitch, pricing, close | Even pace, no vocal fry, no uptalk |
| **Playful** | Opening rapport, breaking tension | Smile in your voice, casual |

## 6. OBJECTION HANDLING

The full objection battle card system is documented in `Agency_OS_Objection_Battle_Cards.md`. Study it. Internalize it. Know every card cold.

### The 5 Categories

| Category | Objections Covered |
|---|---|
| **1. Trust / Past Experience** | "We already have an agency," "We've been burned before," "How do I know you won't disappear?", "Can I talk to current clients?" |
| **2. Money / Budget** | "Too expensive," "We're bootstrapped," "Need to see results first," "Can we start with a trial?" |
| **3. Scope / Fit** | "Not sure you understand our industry," "We need to keep some in-house," "What if it doesn't work?" |
| **4. Timing / Decision** | "I need to think about it," "I need to talk to my partner/board," "We're too busy to onboard right now" |
| **5. Comparison** | "We can hire someone in-house for less," "What makes you different from [competitor]?" |

### The 7-Step Objection Handling Framework

Every objection follows the same sequence:

```
1. ACKNOWLEDGE ──► "I hear you. That's a fair concern."
2. CLARIFY     ──► "What specifically about [X] is the concern?"
3. SUPPOSE     ──► "If we could resolve that, anything else holding you back?"
4. CALLBACK    ──► "Remember in discovery when you said [Stage 4 answer]?"
5. REFRAME     ──► Use the appropriate battle card response
6. CHECK       ──► "Does that make sense?"
7. RE-COMMIT   ──► "So what would you like to do?"
```

### The 3 Principles (Every Objection)

1. **Reframe** the thinking, not the person
2. **Consequence** the inaction — what it costs them to stay
3. **Identity shift** — who do they need to become to get the outcome they described?

### Smokescreens vs Real Objections

If the objection appears suddenly at the close but was never mentioned during discovery — it is likely a smokescreen. Surface the real concern:

> "I totally hear you. Can I ask — is that the main thing, or is there something else underneath that's giving you pause?"

### Top 5 Objections (Quick Reference)

| Objection | Core Reframe |
|---|---|
| **"Burned by agencies"** | Operator model vs. account manager model. They failed because of structure, not because agencies don't work. |
| **"Need to think about it"** | What specifically? Surface the real concern. Callback to Stage 4. |
| **"Too expensive"** | Internal team = $350K-$500K/yr. ROI math: their opportunity cost > retainer. |
| **"Talk to partner"** | Arm them to sell internally. Send exec summary. Book specific follow-up. |
| **"Build internally"** | 6-12 months to hire and ramp. We build while they recruit. Knowledge transfer when ready. |

### 6 Closing Questions (Use After Handling Any Objection)

1. **Identity:** "The version of your business you described — what decision does that version of you make right now?"
2. **Cost of inaction:** "Every month this stays unsolved costs you [their number]. At what point does that become more expensive than fixing it?"
3. **Suppose:** "If you knew this would produce the pipeline you described, would you move forward today?"
4. **Control:** "You told me you're the one responsible for growth. So who's going to make this decision?"
5. **Binary:** "Two paths. You move forward and we start building. Or things stay exactly the way they are. Which one?"
6. **Future self:** "The version of you 6 months from now with a working growth engine — what does that person wish you'd done today?"

## 7. PITCH DECK WALKTHROUGH

The full pitch deck is documented in `Agency_OS_Pitch_Deck.md`. It has 14 slides with presenter notes for every transition.

### When to Use the Deck

1. **Screen share during closing calls** — walk through slides as you present the offer (Stage 7 of the script)
2. **Post-call leave-behind** — send as PDF after "I need to think about it" objections
3. **Application follow-up** — send to qualified applicants before their call to warm them up

### How to Use It During Calls

You do NOT go through all 14 slides on every call. You pull up specific slides to support what you're saying in the moment.

| Call Stage | Slides to Pull Up | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 3 (Problem)** | Slide 2: "Why Growth Stalls at 7 Figures" | When they're describing their growth problems — show them they're not alone |
| **Stage 5 (Future Pace)** | Slide 3: "The Shift" | When contrasting old way vs. new way |
| **Stage 7 (Pitch)** | Slides 4-7: System + 3 Pillars | Walk through each pillar with the slide on screen |
| **Stage 7 (Proof)** | Slides 8-9: Case Studies | After the pitch, before pricing — show relevant case study |
| **Stage 8 (Price)** | Slide 11: Investment Tiers | When stating the price — they see the three tiers on screen |
| **Stage 8 (Guarantee)** | Slide 12: Performance Guarantee | After stating price, before close — this dissolves risk |
| **Stage 8 (Next Steps)** | Slide 13: What Happens After Yes | When they're leaning in — show how fast and easy onboarding is |

### Deck Rules

- **Never read from the slides.** The slides support your conversation — they are not a script.
- **Don't pull up the deck until Stage 5 at the earliest.** The first 15-20 minutes are pure conversation. Screen share too early and you become a presenter, not a diagnostician.
- **Match the case study to their situation.** Slide 8 is B2B services, Slide 9 is e-commerce/DTC. Use whichever is closer to their business.
- **Leave the investment slide up during silence.** After stating price, keep Slide 11 on screen. Let them see the tiers while they process.
- **Always have the deck open and ready before the call starts.** Fumbling to find it mid-call kills momentum.

## 8. PRE-CALL PREP PROTOCOL

Spend 5-10 minutes prepping every single call. Not optional. Closers who skip prep close 20% lower than closers who don't. The prospect can tell when you haven't done your homework.

### Pre-Call Checklist (Complete Before Every Call)

```
5-10 MINUTES BEFORE EACH CALL
├── 1. APPLICATION DATA
│   └── Revenue, team size, industry, marketing channels,
│       stated challenge. Know their numbers before they say them.
│
├── 2. SETTER NOTES (in CRM)
│   └── What pain did the setter uncover?
│       What objections did they raise?
│       What pre-call video was sent?
│       Lead source? VSL watch time?
│
├── 3. DM CONVERSATION (if applicable)
│   └── Read the full thread. Note their exact words —
│       you'll mirror them back on the call.
│
├── 4. WEBSITE AUDIT (2 minutes)
│   └── What do they sell? Who's the buyer?
│       Any obvious funnel gaps? (no email capture, weak LP, no retargeting)
│       Check Meta Ad Library — are they running ads?
│
├── 5. LINKEDIN (1 minute)
│   └── Founder profile. Company page. Employee count. Recent posts.
│       This gives you context for rapport in Stage 1.
│
├── 6. COMPETITIVE SCAN (1 minute)
│   └── Who are their obvious competitors?
│       Are competitors running ads? What angles?
│
├── 7. TAILORED QUESTIONS (write 3)
│   └── Based on THEIR application, not generic discovery.
│       E.g. "I saw you're spending on Meta but your landing page
│       doesn't have an email capture — is that intentional?"
│
├── 8. RELEVANT CASE STUDY
│   └── Pick the one closest to their industry, revenue, or challenge.
│       Have it ready to reference during Stage 7.
│
├── 9. PRICING ESTIMATE
│   └── Based on scope clues from the application.
│       Know whether you're recommending $5K, $10K, or $25K
│       before the call starts.
│
└── 10. ENVIRONMENT CHECK
    └── Camera on. Professional background. Good lighting.
        Quiet room. No interruptions for 45 minutes.
        Deck open and ready. CRM open.
```

### Mental Frame Before Dialing

You are a growth operator evaluating whether this business is a fit for your team. You are NOT selling. You are diagnosing. If the fit is right, you will offer to help. If not, you will tell them honestly.

Jeremy Miner: "The person with the most certainty wins." You must be more certain about the solution than they are uncertain about the investment.

## 9. POST-CALL ADMIN

Complete within 15 minutes of every call. No batching. No "I'll do it later." If you hang up and don't do this immediately, details get lost and follow-ups get missed.

### Post-Call Checklist

```
WITHIN 15 MINUTES OF HANGING UP
├── 1. CRM DISPOSITION
│   └── Update pipeline stage:
│       • Closed → Won (include deal value, payment plan if applicable)
│       • Follow-Up → Scheduled (specific date/time)
│       • Lost → Reason code (price, timing, not qualified, no-show, competitor)
│       • No-Show → Rebook or move to re-engagement
│
├── 2. CRM NOTES
│   └── What pain did they describe?
│       What objections came up?
│       What was their reaction to pricing?
│       What case study resonated?
│       What is their timeline?
│
├── 3. FOLLOW-UP TRIGGERS (if not closed)
│   ├── "Think about it" → Book specific callback (2 days max)
│   │   + Send 1-page summary + relevant case study
│   ├── "Talk to partner" → Send exec summary
│   │   + Offer to join partner call + book specific follow-up
│   ├── "Too expensive" → Send ROI breakdown
│   │   + Reference their specific opportunity cost number
│   └── Any follow-up → Calendar invite WITH Zoom link
│
├── 4. PROPOSAL SEND (if applicable)
│   └── Scope document + contract sent within 2 hours.
│       Not tomorrow. Not end of day. Within 2 hours.
│
├── 5. SETTER FEEDBACK
│   └── Reply in the Slack handoff thread:
│       • Was the lead qualified? (Y/N + why)
│       • Quality of setter notes? (Helpful / Missing info)
│       • Any gaps in pre-call intel?
│       • Show rate was achieved? (Y/N)
│
├── 6. DEBRIEF NOTES (for yourself)
│   └── What worked on this call?
│       What could have been better?
│       Which objections came up?
│       What will you do differently next time?
│
└── 7. SLACK UPDATE
    └── Post in #sales-team:
        "[NAME] — [RESULT] — [REVENUE] company — [NEXT STEP]"
        Example: "John Smith — Follow-Up Booked Thu 2pm — $3M/yr SaaS — Needs partner approval"
        Example: "Sarah Jones — CLOSED $10K/mo — $1.5M/yr agency — Scoping call Friday"
```

### Follow-Up Cadence (If Not Closed on First Call)

```
Day 0 (same day)  ──► Summary email + case study + contract (within 2 hours)
Day 1             ──► Text: "Had a chance to review everything?"
Day 2             ──► Callback (already booked from close attempt)
Day 3             ──► If no response: text + voicemail
Day 5             ──► Value-add email (relevant article, new case study, or data point)
Day 7             ──► Final reach-out: "Want to close the loop — still interested or should I free up the slot?"
Day 14            ──► Move to long-term nurture (monthly check-in)
```

**Rule:** Never let a follow-up exist without a specific date and time. "I'll follow up next week" is not a follow-up. "I'll call you Tuesday at 2pm" is.

### The Math


## 11. WEEK 1 TRAINING (BLACK MARKER METHOD)

### What Is the Black Marker Method?

This is Alex Hormozi's signature script memorization technique. It works.

1. Print **2 copies** of the closer script (`Agency_OS_Closer_Script.md`) — one clean reference, one working copy
2. Read the working copy out loud, start to finish
3. Take a black marker and black out **one word**
4. Read the script again from memory. If stuck, glance at the clean copy.
5. Black out another word
6. Repeat until **every word is blacked out**

The closer script is longer than a setter's cold call script. Focus on one stage at a time. Start with Stages 1-4 (the first 20 minutes of the call), then move to Stages 5-8 (pitch and close), then Stage 9 (objections).

**Why it works:** Reps who "know" the script but haven't internalized it sound robotic. Reps who've done the Black Marker Method sound natural because the script is in their subconscious. The boredom of repetition forces you to find natural inflection and personality within the words.

### Week 1 Schedule

| Day | Morning (2 hrs) | Afternoon (2 hrs) |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Company overview. Offer deep-dive. Why the Growth OS changes businesses. Watch 5 recorded closing calls (take notes on structure, tonality, objection handling). | **Black Marker Method begins:** Print 2 copies of the closer script. Focus on Stages 1-4. Read aloud. Start blacking out words. |
| **Tuesday** | ICP training. Who are our buyers? What are they afraid of? What's their day like? What have they tried before? Watch 5 more recorded calls. | Black Marker continues (Stages 1-4). Start role-playing the **Connecting + Situation Questions** stages with your manager. |
| **Wednesday** | Objection training. Study all 5 categories from `Agency_OS_Objection_Battle_Cards.md`. Listen to calls where objections were handled well. | Black Marker continues (Stages 5-7). Role-play the **Problem Awareness + Pre-Frame** stages. Practice the Revealing Question. |
| **Thursday** | Product knowledge deep-dive. Walk through the Growth OS: the 30-day install, the operating rhythm, the data layer. Walk through the pitch deck. Understand every slide. | Black Marker continues (Stages 7-8). Role-play the **Pitch + Price** stages. Practice stating the price and sitting in silence. |
| **Friday** | Full mock strategy call with manager. Manager plays the prospect (hard mode — skeptical $3M founder who's been burned by 3 agencies). Get feedback. | Black Marker continues (Stage 9). Role-play **objection handling** scenarios. Manager throws 3 objections in a row. |
| **Saturday** | (Optional) Listen to 10 more recorded calls. Take notes on patterns you hear — what do the best closers do differently? | Rest. |

## 13. CRM & REPORTING

### CRM Hygiene Rules

1. **Every call gets dispositioned within 15 minutes.** If it happened, it goes in CRM. No exceptions.
2. **Notes are for the NEXT person who touches this lead.** Write them as if someone else will read them tomorrow — because they will (you, the manager, or a follow-up closer).
3. **Pipeline stages must be accurate.** If they said "I'm in," move them to Won. If they said "let me think," move them to Follow-Up with a specific date. Don't leave leads sitting in limbo.
4. **Commission tracking sheet is YOUR responsibility.** Track your own closes, deal values, and payment collections. Verify monthly against CRM.

### Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Definition | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Call Booked** | Setter booked the call. Lead is assigned to you. | Pre-call prep. Reply to handoff in Slack. |
| **Call Taken** | Call happened. Prospect engaged. | Disposition: move to Won, Follow-Up, or Lost. |
| **Follow-Up** | Not closed on first call. Specific next step scheduled. | Execute follow-up cadence (Section 9). |
| **Proposal Sent** | Scope doc and contract sent. Waiting for response. | Follow up per cadence. |
| **Won** | Deal closed. Payment collected or payment plan started. | Update deal value. Notify team. Begin handoff to ops. |
| **Lost** | Not moving forward. | Log reason code. Move to long-term nurture. |
| **No-Show** | Didn't show up. | Call twice. Text. Email. Attempt rebook or move to re-engagement. |

### Setter-to-Closer Handoff Process

```
1. Setter books the call in Calendly (round-robin or your specific link)
2. Setter updates CRM: Lead status → "Call Booked" with notes
3. Setter notifies you in Slack #handoffs: "Handoff sent" with summary:
   - Name, business, revenue range
   - Their #1 pain point
   - Any objections raised
   - Pre-call video sent? Y/N
4. YOU reply "done" in the thread when you've received and reviewed it
5. YOU own the show rate from this point:
   - Text within 5 min of handoff
   - Text 24 hours before
   - Text morning of
   - If no-show: call twice, voicemail, email
6. After the call: disposition in CRM, feedback to setter in Slack
```

## 14. COMPENSATION

### Pay Structure

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Base salary** | $3,000 - $5,000/month |
| **Commission** | 8-12% of cash collected |
| **Bonus** | Extra 2% on deals over monthly threshold |
| **Target total comp** | $8,000 - $15,000/month |
| **Top performer ceiling** | $20,000+/month |

### How Comp Works

- **Base** protects your floor so you can focus on quality, not desperation. Don't confuse having a base with not needing to perform.
- **Commission** is percentage of cash actually collected — not revenue sold, not pipeline value. If the client pays $10K and you're on 10% commission, you get $1,000.
- **Bonus tier** kicks in after you hit a monthly revenue threshold (set by sales manager). Everything above that threshold earns an extra 2%. This rewards consistent high performance.
- **Payment plans:** Commission is paid on cash collected, not total contract value. If a client is on a payment plan, you earn commission as each payment comes in.

### Payment Schedule

## 15. MINDSET & PRINCIPLES

These aren't suggestions. These are the operating principles that separate closers who make $8K/month from closers who make $30K/month.

### The 8 Commandments

```
1.  You are a doctor, not a salesperson.
    Doctors diagnose. Doctors prescribe. Doctors don't beg patients
    to take the medicine. You have the same authority when you've
    done the diagnostic work properly.

2.  Tonality > Words.
    The exact same sentence sounds like a pitch or a diagnosis
    depending on how you say it. Match the tone to the stage.
    "Don't be Jordan Belfort. Be consultative." — Ben Bader

3.  Silence is your most powerful tool.
    After asking a question — shut up. After stating the price — shut up.
    The person who fills the silence loses the frame.
    Hormozi: "Most closers talk themselves out of the deal."

4.  Pre-call prep is non-negotiable.
    Know their revenue, their pain, their past attempts, and
    their industry before they say a word. They can tell when
    you haven't done the work. "Know their situation before they
    say a word." — SooWei Goh

5.  Stage 4 wins or loses the deal.
    "What's prevented you from fixing this until now?" — their answer
    IS the objection they will present at the close. Neutralize it
    in discovery and the close becomes a formality.

6.  Never justify unprompted.
    If they don't object, don't create objections.
    State the price. Shut up. Let them react.
    Don't say "I know it's a lot, but..." — that's YOUR insecurity talking.

7.  Follow up like a professional, not a pest.
    Every follow-up adds value: a case study, a data point, a relevant
    article. Never "just checking in." Never "bumping this." Give them
    a reason to respond.

8.  Own the number.
    Close rate is YOUR number. Not the lead quality. Not the setter.
    Not the market. If you're below 35%, the problem is on the call.
    Review your recordings. Find the leak. Fix it.
```

### Hormozi on Coaching Calls

> "Coaching calls are just another sales call with a different frame."

Every client interaction is an opportunity to reinforce the value of the engagement. Every progress update is a chance to sell the next month. Every result is a proof point for the next prospect.

### Pierre Khoury on the Closer's Real Job

> "Their job should literally just be to collect payment. If the prospect isn't 80% sold before the call, the problem is upstream — marketing, content, or the setter."

If you're having to educate prospects on what we do during the call, the upstream system needs fixing. Flag it. But still close.

### Jeremy Haynes' 80/20 Rule

> "80% of conversion happens in marketing. 20% happens on the call."

This means the prospect should arrive pre-sold by content, the VSL, and the setter. Your 20% is diagnosis, prescription, and collecting payment. If you're doing all the selling, something is broken upstream.

### The Operator Mindset

You are not a salesperson. You are an operator who happens to be the last step in the revenue engine. You diagnose real problems, prescribe real solutions, and help businesses install the infrastructure they need to grow.

When you believe that — truly believe it — the close becomes a conversation, not a transaction. And that's when the numbers get big.
