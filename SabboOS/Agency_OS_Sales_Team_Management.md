## SECTION 1: TEAM STRUCTURE & ORG CHART

The org chart is not aspirational. It is tied directly to revenue. You hire when the numbers demand it, not when you feel like it. Every role added must be justified by a trigger metric that has been sustained for at least two consecutive weeks.

### Current Sprint Structure ($0-$50K/mo)

```
FOUNDER (Sabbo)
├── Setter 1 (DMs, qualifying, booking)
└── Closer 1 (takes calls, closes deals)

Total: 3 people
```

At this stage, the founder is the sales manager. You run the morning standup. You review calls. You coach. You set the pace. You are in the voice chat during dial blocks. There is no delegation of management at this level because no one else understands the product, the ICP, or the objections well enough to coach on them.

Tim Luong: "$0-100K/mo — founder closes everything." At the lower end of this band, the founder may still be taking overflow calls alongside the closer. That is fine. The goal is to get the closer to the point where the founder can stop taking calls entirely.

Pierre Khoury: "The day is won or lost the second you wake up." At this stage, the founder's energy IS the team's energy. If you show up flat, the whole day is flat.

### $50K/mo Structure

```
FOUNDER (Sabbo) — still managing
├── Setter 1 (Amazon OS leads)
├── Setter 2 (Agency OS leads OR overflow)
└── Closer 1 (all calls)

Total: 4 people
```

The second setter is hired when the first setter is consistently booking 15+ qualified calls per week and the closer's calendar is at or above 85% utilization. The trigger is not "we need more leads" — it is "we have proven demand and the closer literally cannot take more calls."

At $50K/mo, the founder should be spending 70% of their time on sales management (call review, coaching, standup, 1-on-1s) and only 30% on their own calls. If the founder is still taking more than 2-3 calls per day, the closer is either underperforming or the calendar is not full enough. Diagnose before hiring.

### $100K/mo Structure

```
FOUNDER (Sabbo) — transitioning out of calls
├── Setter 1 (Amazon OS)
├── Setter 2 (Agency OS)
├── Closer 1 (primary)
├── Closer 2 (hired when Closer 1 calendar >85% for 2 weeks)
└── CSM 1 (hired after 5+ active clients)

Total: 6 people
Founder promotes top closer or external hire to Sales Manager role
```

This is the inflection point. The founder must go from being the best closer on the team to being the best manager. These are fundamentally different skills. Tim Luong: "$100-300K/mo — 1-2 closers, founder manages directly. Founder = full-time sales manager."

The CSM is critical here. Without a CSM, the founder or the closers are handling retention, onboarding, and referrals. That is a distraction from selling. The CSM frees the closers to focus exclusively on new revenue.

### $200K/mo Structure

```
SALES MANAGER (dedicated hire — NOT the founder)
├── Setter Team
│   ├── Setter 1 (Amazon OS)
│   ├── Setter 2 (Agency OS)
│   └── Setter 3 (overflow / follow-up specialist)
├── Closer Team
│   ├── Closer 1 (Amazon OS — 45-60 min calls)
│   ├── Closer 2 (Agency OS — strategy calls)
│   └── Closer 3 (overflow / both offers)
└── CSM 1 (retention, onboarding, referrals)

FOUNDER (Sabbo) — off calls entirely
├── Content + offer development
├── Weekly report from Sales Manager
└── Monthly deep dive

Total: 8-9 people
```

At $200K/mo, the founder steps out of daily sales management. The sales manager owns the number. The founder's job is now content, offer, and strategy. If the founder is still in the daily standup, something is wrong.

Tim Luong: "$300K+ — founder goes FULL TIME on growth and offer. Don't hire a sales manager until you can afford a great one." A mediocre sales manager is worse than the founder managing directly. Wait until you can pay $15K-$25K/mo total comp.

### $300K+/mo Structure

```
FOUNDER (Sabbo)
├── VP of Sales / Sales Director (optional — hired when 10+ reps)
│   └── Sales Manager
│       ├── Setter Team Lead
│       │   ├── Setter 1
│       │   ├── Setter 2
│       │   └── Setter 3
│       ├── Closer Team Lead
│       │   ├── Closer 1
│       │   ├── Closer 2
│       │   └── Closer 3
│       └── CSM Team
│           ├── CSM 1 (Amazon OS students)
│           └── CSM 2 (Agency OS clients)
└── Operations
    ├── CRM Admin / VA
    └── AI QC System (automated call review)

Total: 10-14 people
```

At this stage, the org chart has layers. The sales manager cannot effectively manage more than 6-8 reps directly. When the team exceeds 8 reps, you either promote a Senior Closer to Team Lead (who manages 3-4 reps while maintaining a small personal book) or you hire a second manager.

### Hiring Trigger Metrics

Do NOT hire based on gut feel. Hire based on data that has been sustained for at least 2 weeks.

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Closer calendar >85% utilization for 2+ weeks | Not enough capacity to handle demand | Hire another closer |
| Show rate dropping while bookings increase | Setter is rushing qualification to hit volume | Either: coach the setter, OR hire a second setter to reduce pressure |
| Lead response time >60 seconds consistently | Setter is overwhelmed with volume | Hire another setter |
| Closer doing admin instead of selling | No CSM to handle post-sale | Hire a CSM |
| Manager spending >50% on coaching new hires | Too many reps for one manager | Promote a Team Lead or hire second manager |
| Close rate dropping while call volume stays same | Lead quality issue OR closer fatigue | Diagnose first. If fatigue, redistribute or hire. |

### Max Reps Per Manager: 6-8

This is a hard ceiling. Beyond 8 reps, the manager cannot do daily call coaching, daily meetings, and weekly 1-on-1s for everyone. The Trifecta (Tim Luong via Cole Gordon) requires time and attention. If you skip any element to "manage" more people, the whole system degrades.

Hormozi: "Coaching calls are just another sales call with a different frame. You must show up 110% every time." You cannot show up at 110% for 12 different people.

## SECTION 2: HIRING PIPELINE

### Where to Find Salespeople

Volume is the strategy. Tim Luong: "Do 20-30 interviews per key role. Most founders do 3." Three interviews gives you whoever applied first. Thirty interviews gives you the person who will actually perform.

**Source 1: Your Own Audience**
Post about the role on your socials. People who follow you already understand your offer, your ICP, your energy. They arrive pre-sold on the mission. This is the highest-conversion hiring channel.

**Source 2: Closers.io / Sales Communities**
Cole Gordon's Closers.io, HighLevel communities, Skool groups for sales professionals. These are people who have self-selected into high-ticket remote sales. The quality varies widely but the volume is there.

**Source 3: Referrals**
Ask your current team. Ask your network. Ask clients if they know good salespeople. Best hires come from referrals because someone is putting their reputation on the line. Offer a referral bonus ($500-$1,000 if the hire passes the 2-week trial).

**Source 4: LinkedIn + Indeed**
Volume play. Expect 90% to be unqualified. But the remaining 10% includes people with real B2B sales experience who are looking for better comp or more flexibility. Worth running a consistent job post.

**Source 5: Poach from Competitors**
Ethically. If you see a setter or closer in someone else's funnel who is sharp, reaches out well, and handles objections cleanly — reach out. "I noticed you're setting for [competitor]. We're building something similar and I think you'd be a great fit. Interested in chatting?"

**Source 6: Skool / Discord Communities**
Your own student/client community. If you have someone who is sharp, hungry, and already understands your product — they skip half the onboarding curve.

### Volume Target: 20-30 Interviews Per Role

This is not optional. The math is simple: if you interview 3 people, you hire the best of 3. If you interview 30, you hire the best of 30. The difference in performance between the 1st and 10th percentile of salespeople is enormous. A top closer does 3-5x the revenue of an average closer on the same calendar.

### 4-Round Interview Process

#### Round 1: Written Application (Filters 80%)

Post the application on your hiring channels. Four questions, written responses. No phone call. This is a filter, not a conversation.

**Question 1:** "What's the most you've ever earned in a month from sales? How did you do it?"
- What you want: Specific numbers. "$14,200 in March — I was selling solar, ran 47 appointments, closed 12 at $1,183 average." Numbers show they track performance.
- Red flag: Vague answers. "I've made really good money." "I was top rep on my team." No numbers = no awareness of their own performance.

**Question 2:** "Describe a time you lost a deal you should have won. What happened?"
- What you want: Self-awareness. They can identify what they did wrong. "I pitched too early before understanding her budget constraints. Should have asked about funding first."
- Red flag: Blaming the prospect. "They just couldn't afford it." "They ghosted me." No ownership = no coachability.

**Question 3:** "Why do you want to sell growth services [or Amazon coaching] specifically?"
- What you want: Genuine interest in the niche. "I've been following the coaching space for 2 years and I believe in the transformation." Or: "I've worked with agency clients before and I understand the pain of broken marketing."
- Red flag: "I can sell anything." Generic answers that would work for any job posting. If they are not interested in the product, they will not last.

**Question 4:** "What's your 12-month income goal?"
- What you want: Ambition that matches your comp structure. If your top closer can make $15K/mo and they say $20K, that is fine — they are hungry. If they say $5K, they are not motivated by commission.
- Red flag: Unrealistic ($100K/mo with no track record) or unmotivated ($3K/mo — they want base pay, not performance).

**Scoring:** If a candidate gives specific numbers, takes ownership of a loss, shows genuine interest, and has aligned income goals — advance to Round 2. Everyone else gets a polite rejection email.

#### Round 2: Phone Screen (15 Minutes)

This is an energy check. Not a deep interview. You are screening for fit, not evaluating skill.

**The Call:**
1. "Thanks for applying. Tell me a bit about yourself and your sales background." (Let them talk for 2-3 minutes. Are they organized? Clear? Engaging?)
2. "Sell me on why I should hire you. You have 60 seconds." (This IS a sales role. If they cannot sell themselves, they cannot sell your product.)
3. "What questions do you have for me?" (Good candidates ask about the product, the team, the growth path. Bad candidates ask only about pay and schedule.)

**What you are evaluating:**
- Energy and tonality. Do they sound like someone you would want to talk to for 45 minutes on a sales call?
- Clarity of thought. Can they organize their ideas and communicate them clearly?
- Listening ratio. Did they listen to your questions or talk over you?
- Genuine curiosity about the role.

**Hormozi:** "If you aren't learning from the person in the interview, especially for leadership roles, waste of time."

**Hormozi:** "If it's not a hell yes, it's generally a no. Candidates are abundant. Never hire someone with a bad attitude or lack of clarity that you don't want to put time into regardless of their background."

#### Round 3: Live Role Play (30 Minutes)

This is where most founders skip ahead and make a hire. Do not skip this. The role play separates people who sound good from people who ARE good.

**The Scenario (Agency OS):**
"I run a marketing agency doing $2M/year. Our marketing is a mess. We've hired 3 agencies and they all failed — burned through $200K with nothing to show for it. I'm skeptical. Why would you be different?"

**The Scenario (Amazon OS):**
"I have $15K to invest and I want to start selling on Amazon. But I've watched a bunch of YouTube videos and honestly it seems oversaturated. My brother-in-law tried it and lost $8K. Why should I trust your program?"

**Scoring (1-5 on each):**

| Criteria | 1 (Fail) | 3 (Acceptable) | 5 (Excellent) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery quality | Jumped straight to pitch | Asked a few questions | Spent 60%+ of time asking and listening |
| Listening ratio | Talked 80%+ of the time | 50/50 | Let prospect talk 60-70%, intervened strategically |
| Objection handling | Got flustered, gave up | Addressed surface level | Peeled back the objection, got to the real concern |
| Close attempt | Never asked for the sale | Soft close only | Clear, confident close attempt with next steps |
| Energy and tonality | Monotone, low energy | Professional | Warm, confident, varied tonality, matched prospect energy |

**Minimum passing score: 15/25.** Below 15, they are not ready. Between 15-20, they are trainable. Above 20, hire immediately.

Ben Bader: "Don't be Jordan Belfort with the script. Be casual, consultative."

#### Round 4: 2-Week Paid Trial

This is the final filter. Real calls, real prospects, real pressure.

**Setup:**
- Half calendar (3 calls/day max for closers, reduced outreach volume for setters)
- Real leads, not practice scenarios
- Daily check-in with manager (15 minutes, same time every day)
- Clear pass/fail criteria communicated BEFORE they start

**Pass/fail criteria (closer):**
- Must close at least 1 deal in the first 10 calls
- Must be at or near KPI by call 20-30
- Must AT KPI by call 50 or they are out
- Must submit EOD reports every day without being asked
- Must attend every training session and meeting
- Must demonstrate coachability (implements feedback, does not argue)

**Pass/fail criteria (setter):**
- Must book at least 5 qualified calls in the first week
- 80%+ show rate on their bookings
- Must respond to leads within 60 seconds during business hours
- Must submit EOD reports every day without being asked

Hormozi: "At ANY point if we are NOT seeing coachability, hunger, culture fit — we may let you go."

### Interview Red Flags (Instant Disqualification)

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| No camera on video call | If they hide during the interview, they will hide on sales calls |
| Late to the interview | Time management is non-negotiable in a role with scheduled calls |
| Conducting interview while walking, driving, or commuting | They do not take this seriously |
| Talks more than they listen | Sales is about listening. If they cannot listen in an interview, they will not listen on calls |
| Inflates experience without specifics | Dishonesty in the interview predicts dishonesty with pipeline numbers |
| Wants to change the offer on day 1 | Tim Luong: "Don't let closers change your offer." Closers sell you on targeting changes because they do not want hard calls. Resist. |
| More excited about base than commission | Wrong hunger level. You want someone who sees the base as a safety net, not the paycheck |
| No investment in self-development | Hormozi: "How much money have you personally invested into sales-related training and education?" Zero = no growth mindset |
| Cannot articulate a specific lost deal | Lack of self-awareness. If they have never reflected on a loss, they will repeat mistakes |

## SECTION 3: REP ONBOARDING TIMELINE

This is Hormozi's 50-call ramping framework adapted for our two offers. The timeline is 30 days. KPI must be hit by call 50. No exceptions.

### Day 1-7: Training + Shadowing

The first week is drinking from the firehose. The new hire does NOT take calls during this week. They learn.

**Daily schedule during Week 1:**

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 10:00 AM | Morning meeting with the team (they attend but do not present) |
| 10:30 AM | Training Block 1: company overview, offer deep-dive, ICP training |
| 11:30 AM | Shadow live calls (sit in on 3-5 calls per day, take notes) |
| 1:00 PM | Lunch |
| 2:00 PM | Training Block 2: Black Marker Method (script memorization) |
| 3:30 PM | Listen to 5-10 recorded call recordings |
| 5:00 PM | Debrief with manager (15 min): questions, observations, takeaways |

**Week 1 checklist:**
- [ ] Complete all training materials FIRST — do not skip ahead to calls
- [ ] Read through at least 2 months of testimonials (they WILL want to reference these on calls)
- [ ] Shadow current closers AS MUCH AS POSSIBLE
- [ ] Go through 5-10 call recordings per day
- [ ] Begin Black Marker Method on Day 1
- [ ] Attend all meetings — be alert, ask questions
- [ ] Watch all sales team meeting recordings from the last month
- [ ] Complete offer breakdown document review

**Milestone 1: Script Memorization (Must Pass by Day 3-5)**

The new hire must recite the full sales script cold — no notes. The manager tests by asking random questions mid-script to confirm they actually know it, not just the sequence.

Hormozi: "Can't pass Milestone 1 in 2-3 days = consider cutting." Script memorization is the easiest test of commitment. If they cannot memorize a script in 3-5 days with the Black Marker Method, they lack either the work ethic or the cognitive ability for the role.

**The Black Marker Method:**
1. Print 2 copies of the sales script — one clean reference, one working copy
2. Read the working copy out loud, start to finish
3. Take a black marker and black out ONE word
4. Read the script again from memory. If stuck, glance at the clean copy.
5. Black out another word
6. Repeat until EVERY word is blacked out
7. A 400-word script = ~400 repetitions

### Day 7-14: Limited Call Volume

The second week transitions from observation to participation. The new hire begins taking real calls but on a limited calendar.

**Daily schedule during Week 2:**

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 10:00 AM | Morning meeting (they now share a win/learning daily) |
| 10:30 AM | Training reinforcement: drill the section they are weakest on |
| 11:00 AM | Take 2-3 live calls (half calendar) with manager reviewing recordings same day |
| 1:00 PM | Lunch |
| 2:00 PM | Manager 1-on-1: review morning calls, give specific feedback |
| 3:00 PM | Continue training materials (repeat as needed) |
| 4:00 PM | Set their own appointments (show hunger — the best reps do both inbound and outbound) |
| 5:00 PM | Debrief + submit call recordings for review |

**Milestone 2: Recap/Labeling (Mid-Week 2)**
The new hire can accurately recap a prospect's situation and label their pain into the correct "problem buckets." Manager tests by having them recap a practice call from memory.

This matters because labeling is the bridge between discovery and presentation. If the rep cannot organize what the prospect told them into a clear diagnosis, the presentation will feel generic. The prospect needs to hear their specific pain reflected back before they trust the solution.

**Milestone 3: Objection Handling (End of Week 2)**
The new hire can handle the top 10 objections naturally without reading from a sheet. Manager runs rapid-fire objection drills: throws objections one after another, the rep responds in real time.

The top 10 objections (both offers):
1. "I need to think about it"
2. "It's too expensive"
3. "I need to talk to my spouse/partner"
4. "I've been burned before"
5. "Can I get a discount?"
6. "Is this a scam?"
7. "I don't have the time"
8. "Why can't I just do this myself?"
9. "What if it doesn't work?"
10. "I'm already working with someone"

### Day 15-30: Full Volume

After passing all three milestones, the rep moves to a full calendar.

**The benchmarks are non-negotiable:**
- Within the first 10 calls: at least 1-2 deals closed
- Within the first 20-30 calls: close to or AT KPI
- Within the first 50 calls: AT KPI or out

**AT KPI by call 50 or they are terminated.** This is not cruel — it is merciful. A rep who cannot hit KPI in 50 calls will not hit it in 100. Keeping them longer wastes their time and yours. The 50-call ramp gives them every opportunity: training, coaching, real calls, feedback, support. If they cannot perform with all of that, this is not the right fit.

Hormozi: "At ANY point if we're not seeing coachability, hunger, culture fit — we may let you go." The qualitative assessment runs in parallel with the quantitative. A rep who closes deals but is toxic to the team culture gets cut just as fast as one who cannot close.

**What "at KPI" means:**
- Closer: 30%+ close rate, hitting cash-per-call target
- Setter: 10+ qualified bookings per week, 80%+ show rate

## SECTION 4: KPI SYSTEM (EVERY ROLE)

Every metric is paired: volume + quality. Hormozi: "I don't want you to burn through my leads." A closer who does 50 calls and closes 5 is worse than one who does 25 calls and closes 8. Volume without quality is waste. Quality without volume is unrealized potential.

### Setter KPIs

| Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dials per day | 50+ | 30-49 | Below 30 |
| Conversations per day | 5-10 | 3-4 | Below 3 |
| Calls booked per day | 2-4 | 1 | 0 |
| Show rate (their bookings) | 80%+ | 70-79% | Below 70% |
| Booking-to-close ratio | 25%+ | 15-24% | Below 15% |
| Lead response time | Under 60 sec | 60-120 sec | Over 2 min |
| CRM notes quality | Detailed, actionable | Present but thin | Missing or "spoke with lead" |

**Interpretation:**
- Green across the board = top performer. Consider promotion or increased responsibility.
- Yellow on any metric = coaching conversation. Identify the specific gap and assign a drill.
- Red on any metric = intervention within 24 hours. If red persists 3+ days, see Performance Management.

### Closer KPIs

| Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Calls scheduled per day | 5-6 | 3-4 | Below 3 |
| Show rate | 80%+ | 70-79% | Below 70% |
| Close rate (warm/pre-sold) | 35%+ | 25-34% | Below 25% |
| Cash collected per call | $1,500+ (Amazon) / $3,000+ (Agency) | $800-$1,499 / $1,500-$2,999 | Below $800 / Below $1,500 |
| Calendar utilization | 70-80% | 60-69% | Below 60% or above 85% |
| Follow-up close rate | 20%+ | 10-19% | Below 10% |
| EOD report submitted | By 6 PM, complete | Late but complete | Missing |
| Average call duration | 35-55 min | 20-34 or 56-75 min | Under 20 or over 75 min |

**Calendar utilization note:** Below 60% means the closer does not have enough calls. This is a lead flow problem, not a closer problem. Above 85% means the closer is overloaded and quality will drop. Both extremes require action — the first from marketing/setting, the second from hiring.

Hormozi: "Below 60% utilization — morale drops, commission breath." When a closer's calendar is empty, they start pressuring prospects. They smell like commission. The prospect senses it and pulls back. Keep utilization between 70-80%.

### CSM KPIs

| Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client response time | Under 2 hours | 2-4 hours | Over 4 hours |
| 30-day retention | 95%+ | 90-94% | Below 90% |
| 90-day retention | 85%+ | 75-84% | Below 75% |
| Testimonials captured per month | 4+ | 2-3 | 0-1 |
| Referrals generated per month | 3+ | 1-2 | 0 |
| Upsell/renewal rate | 30%+ | 15-29% | Below 15% |
| NPS score | 8.5+ | 7.0-8.4 | Below 7.0 |
| Onboarding completion (first 7 days) | 100% of new clients | 80-99% | Below 80% |

### Sales Manager KPIs

| Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Team close rate (blended) | 35%+ | 25-34% | Below 25% |
| Revenue vs monthly target | 100%+ | 85-99% | Below 85% |
| Team show rate | 80%+ | 70-79% | Below 70% |
| Training completion (daily attendance) | 100% | 90-99% | Below 90% |
| New hire ramp time | Under 14 days to full calendar | 14-21 days | Over 21 days |
| Team attrition (annual) | Under 15% | 15-25% | Over 25% |
| Call coaching hours per week | 10+ | 5-9 | Below 5 |
| 1-on-1 completion rate | 100% weekly | Missed 1 | Missed 2+ |

## SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

Performance management is not punitive. It is diagnostic. When a rep is underperforming, the first question is always: "Is this a skill problem, a will problem, or a system problem?" The intervention depends on the answer.

### The Escalation Ladder

**3 Days Red on Any KPI: 1-on-1 Diagnosis**

Pull the rep aside. Not in front of the team. Not on Slack. A private call or meeting.

Format:
1. "I noticed [specific metric] has been below target for 3 days. What's going on?"
2. Listen. Do not assume you know the answer.
3. Diagnose together: is it a skill gap (they do not know how to handle a specific objection), a will gap (they are distracted, burned out, or checked out), or a system gap (lead quality dropped, calendar is wrong, CRM issues)?
4. If skill: assign a specific drill. "For the next 3 days, I want you to role-play objection #3 with [partner] for 15 minutes before calls start."
5. If will: have the personal conversation. "What's happening outside of work? Is there something I can help with?"
6. If system: fix the system. Do not blame the rep for a broken process.
7. Set a clear expectation: "I need to see [metric] at [target] by [date]. I believe you can do this. What support do you need?"

**5 Days Red: Written PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)**

If the 1-on-1 did not resolve the issue, formalize it.

PIP components:
- Specific metrics that are below target (with data)
- Root cause diagnosis from the 1-on-1
- Specific actions the rep must take (drills, call volume, training)
- Specific support the manager will provide (additional coaching, ride-alongs, adjusted calendar)
- Clear targets for the next 7 days
- Consequences if targets are not met: "If these targets are not reached by [date], we will part ways."

Both the rep and the manager sign. This is not adversarial — it is clarity. The rep knows exactly what is expected and exactly what happens if they miss.

**Failed PIP: Termination or Role Change**

If the PIP targets are not met, there are two outcomes:
1. **Termination** — if the gap is will or fundamental skill mismatch
2. **Role change** — if a closer is underperforming but was an excellent setter, move them back. This is not a demotion — it is finding the right fit.

### How to Fire a Rep

Do it the same day you make the decision. Do not wait until Friday. Do not "give it one more week." Delay breeds resentment on both sides.

**The conversation (5 minutes max):**
1. "I need to let you know that today is your last day with us."
2. State the clear reason: "Your close rate has been below 20% for 3 consecutive weeks, and the PIP targets were not met."
3. No ambiguity. No hedging. No "we're going in a different direction" — be direct.
4. "Your final compensation will be processed within 48 hours, including any commissions owed."
5. "I appreciate the effort you put in. I wish you well."

Do NOT:
- Negotiate. The decision is made.
- Apologize. You gave them training, coaching, a PIP, and support. The outcome is the outcome.
- Trash-talk them to the team. Simply say "X is no longer with us. We wish them well."
- Leave outstanding comp. Pay everything owed within 48 hours. Clean break.

### Monthly Performance Review

Every rep gets a 30-minute monthly review. This is separate from weekly 1-on-1s.

**Format:**
1. **Scorecard review (10 min):** Walk through the full month's metrics. Trends, not just averages. Are they improving, plateauing, or declining?
2. **Strengths acknowledgment (5 min):** What did they do well this month? Be specific. "Your discovery depth improved significantly — that 3-question-deep technique is showing up in your close rate."
3. **Growth areas (10 min):** One or two areas to focus on next month. Specific, actionable, measurable.
4. **Career conversation (5 min):** Where are they relative to the next promotion? What needs to happen?

### How to Promote a Rep

Promotion criteria are based on GROSS OUTPUT, not tenure. Time in seat means nothing. Results mean everything.

Hormozi: "Give new hires a promotion within 30 days — something to call mom about." The first promotion can be small (Junior Setter to Setter) but it must happen early. People stay at companies where they feel progress.

**Promotion timeline:**
- Setter to Senior Setter: 3+ months of consistent KPI performance (80%+ show rate, 25+ bookings/month)
- Setter to Closer: 6+ months, high booking-to-close ratio, passes closer training + mock call evaluation
- Closer to Senior Closer: 3+ months of 40%+ close rate, consistently top 20% of team
- Senior Closer to Sales Manager: demonstrated leadership, willing to stop taking calls, can coach and develop others

## SECTION 6: COMPENSATION ARCHITECTURE

### Complete Compensation Structure

| Role | Base (Monthly) | Variable | Bonus Triggers | Target Total Comp |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Setter | $2,000-$3,000 | $25-$50 per qualified show | 20+ shows in a month = $500 bonus | $4,000-$7,000/mo |
| Closer | $3,000-$5,000 | 8-12% commission on cash collected | 40%+ close rate = +2% commission rate | $8,000-$15,000/mo |
| CSM | $3,000 | 10% referral commission, 5% renewal, 5-10% upsell, $250/testimonial | 5+ referrals in a month = $1,000 bonus | $5,000-$10,000/mo |
| Sales Manager | $8,000-$12,000 | 3-5% override on total team revenue | Hit monthly team target = $2,000-$5,000 bonus | $15,000-$25,000/mo |

### Pay Frequency: Weekly

Pierre Khoury: "Pay team every Monday. Keep everyone incentivized. Start the week off strong."

Weekly pay creates a tighter feedback loop between performance and reward. When a closer closes a $10K deal on Wednesday and sees the commission in their account on Monday, the behavior is reinforced immediately. Monthly pay creates a 2-4 week delay between action and reward — the psychological connection weakens.

**Weekly pay implementation:**
- Base: Split into weekly installments (monthly base / 4.33)
- Commission: Paid the Monday after the week the deal closed
- Bonuses: Paid monthly on the first Monday of the following month

### Clawback Policy

If a client refunds within 30 days of purchase, the commission paid to the closer (and the setter bonus) is clawed back from the next pay cycle. This protects the business from high-pressure closes that create buyer's remorse.

Clawback rules:
- Refund within 30 days: 100% clawback
- Refund between 31-60 days: 50% clawback
- Refund after 60 days: no clawback
- If the clawback exceeds the next pay cycle's commission, the balance carries forward (never goes negative on base pay)

### Deal Threshold Bonuses

For high-value deals, the closer earns an additional bonus on top of standard commission:

| Deal Size | Additional Bonus |
|---|---|
| $10,000-$14,999 | +1% commission |
| $15,000-$24,999 | +2% commission |
| $25,000+ | +3% commission |

This incentivizes closers to sell higher-tier packages and longer commitments rather than defaulting to the lowest-price option.

### Overlapping Credit

Both the setter and the closer get paid on the same deal. The setter's booking bonus is NOT deducted from the closer's commission. This creates alignment rather than competition between the roles.

Example: Setter books a qualified call. Closer closes a $10K deal.
- Setter earns: $50 booking bonus
- Closer earns: 10% commission = $1,000
- If 40%+ close rate bonus is active: 12% commission = $1,200

## SECTION 7: TEAM COMMUNICATION STANDARDS

Communication is the nervous system of the sales org. When it breaks down, everything breaks down. These standards are non-negotiable.

### Response Time Standards

| Channel | Expected Response Time | Consequence for Violation |
|---|---|---|
| Slack (during business hours) | Under 30 minutes | First offense: verbal warning. Second: documented. Third: PIP conversation. |
| Lead DM (during business hours) | Under 60 seconds | Tracked via CRM timestamps. Consistently over 60 sec = coaching. |
| CRM updates | Immediately after every call | If CRM is not updated within 15 min of call ending, manager flags it same day. |
| EOD report | Submitted by 6 PM EOD meeting | Missing EOD = cannot present wins at next morning meeting. Hormozi: "Spot check new reps daily so they build the habit." |
| Handoff acknowledgment | Under 15 minutes | Setter posts handoff, closer replies "done" in thread. |

### CRM Update Standard

Every call gets a CRM update. Not at the end of the day. Not in a batch. Immediately after the call ends. The update includes:
- Disposition code (from the disposition system — see Sales_Team_Playbook.md Phase 8)
- 2-3 sentence summary of the call (pain points, objections raised, next step)
- Follow-up scheduled (date + method: call, text, email)
- Deal value updated if relevant

If a manager pulls up a lead in the CRM and the last note says "spoke with lead" from 3 days ago, that is a failure. Notes should be specific enough that any team member could pick up the conversation without asking the original rep what happened.

### EOD Report Deadline

EOD reports are submitted BEFORE the 6 PM EOD meeting. Not during. Not after. Before. The manager uses the EOD data to run the meeting. If a rep has not submitted, they sit silent during the meeting while everyone else shares wins.

Hormozi: "If you fail to submit your EODs on time and properly every day that you are working, it will result in suspension or termination." This is not about paperwork. It is about discipline and accountability.

### Setter-to-Closer Handoff Protocol

1. Setter qualifies the lead and books the call
2. Setter posts in #handoffs: "Handoff: [Lead Name] booked with [Closer Name] on [Date] at [Time]. Capital: $[X]. Main pain: [one line]. Notes in CRM."
3. Closer replies in thread: "Got it" within 15 minutes
4. Closer reviews CRM notes and application before the call
5. Closer sends confirmation text to the lead: "Hey [Name], looking forward to our call on [date]. Here's the Zoom link: [link]. Talk soon."
6. After the call, closer updates disposition in CRM and posts outcome in #handoffs thread: "Closed $8K PIF" or "Follow-up scheduled Thursday"

The handoff thread is a complete record. The manager can scroll through #handoffs and see every lead's journey from booking to close without opening the CRM.

### Escalation Protocol

When a closer encounters an objection they cannot handle or a prospect raises a concern outside their knowledge:

1. Do NOT make something up. Ever.
2. Say: "That's a great question. I want to make sure I give you the most accurate answer. Let me check with my team and I'll call you back within 2 hours."
3. Immediately DM the manager or founder with the specific question
4. Get the answer, call the prospect back, handle it
5. Document the objection and response in the shared objection library so the team has it for next time

### Slack Channel Structure

| Channel | Purpose | Who Posts |
|---|---|---|
| #sales-team | General team communication, quick questions, share resources | Everyone |
| #wins | Closes, milestones, celebrations ONLY | Everyone (manager makes a big deal here) |
| #eod-setters | Setter EOD reports | Setters post, manager reviews |
| #eod-closers | Closer EOD reports | Closers post, manager reviews |
| #handoffs | Setter-to-closer lead handoff thread | Setters initiate, closers respond |
| #announcements | Offer changes, new case studies, company updates | Manager and founder only |
| #training | Training resources, call recordings, drill assignments | Manager posts, everyone references |
| #objection-library | New objections + approved responses | Manager curates, everyone contributes |

### Communication Anti-Patterns

Do NOT:
- Discuss individual performance problems in public channels. Always private.
- Use #wins for anything other than wins. No complaints, no questions, no admin.
- Send unqualified leads through the handoff process. If the setter books a bad lead, it wastes the closer's time and erodes trust.
- Leave Slack messages on read. If you cannot answer now, react with an emoji and respond within 30 minutes.
- Use email for internal communication. Email is for prospects and clients. Slack is for the team.

## SECTION 8: CAREER PROGRESSION PATHS

Career progression is not a nice-to-have. It is a retention mechanism. People leave when they feel stuck. People stay when they can see the next step and believe it is achievable.

Hormozi: "Give new hires a promotion within 30 days — something to call mom about." The first promotion is a signal that this company rewards performance and creates growth. It does not need to be a big change — a title, a small comp bump, a privilege. But it must happen.

### Path A: Setter Track

```
Junior Setter (Day 1)
    |
    | Pass 48-hour onboarding + quiz (90%+)
    v
Setter (Week 1-2)
    |
    | 3+ months consistent KPI: 80%+ show rate, 25+ bookings/month
    v
Senior Setter (Month 3-6)
    |
    | 6+ months, high booking-to-close ratio, passes closer training + mock call
    v
Junior Closer (Month 6-9)
    |
    | Transitions to closer track below
    v
```

**Junior Setter to Setter (within 30 days):**
- Trigger: Passes the 48-hour onboarding process, scores 90%+ on the quiz, completes first week of live conversations under supervision
- Comp change: Moves from training rate to full setter comp structure
- Perk: Official team announcement in #wins
- This is the "something to call mom about" moment

**Setter to Senior Setter (3+ months):**
- Trigger: 3 consecutive months of 80%+ show rate, 25+ bookings/month, zero missed EODs
- Comp change: Per-booking bonus increases from $25-$35 to $40-$50
- Perks: Best lead assignments, no weekend shifts (if applicable), input on hiring new setters
- Title: Senior Setter (announce in #wins and on LinkedIn)

**Senior Setter to Junior Closer (6+ months):**
- Trigger: High booking-to-close ratio (their leads close at a higher rate than average), demonstrated interest in closing, passes closer training program + mock call with manager
- Comp change: Transitions to closer comp structure (half calendar initially)
- Risk: The best setter may not be the best closer. Run the full 2-week trial on half calendar before committing.

### Path B: Closer Track

```
Junior Closer (Day 1 OR promoted from Senior Setter)
    |
    | Pass 2-week on-ramp, 30%+ close rate on full calendar for 30+ days
    v
Closer (Month 1-2)
    |
    | 40%+ close rate sustained 3+ months, consistently top 20% of team
    v
Senior Closer (Month 4-6)
    |
    | Demonstrated leadership, willing to mentor, strong culture contributor
    v
Team Lead (Month 6-12)
    |
    | Manages 3-4 reps while maintaining small personal book. Ready to go full management.
    v
Sales Manager (Month 12+)
```

**Junior Closer to Closer (within 30 days):**
- Trigger: Passes the 50-call ramp (at KPI by call 50), passed all 3 milestones
- Comp change: Full calendar, full commission structure
- Perk: Announcement in #wins

**Closer to Senior Closer (3+ months at KPI):**
- Trigger: 40%+ close rate sustained for 3+ consecutive months, consistently in top 20% of team performance, zero PIP history
- Comp change: Commission rate increases by +1-2% (e.g., 10% to 12%)
- Perks: First pick on premium leads, mastermind/event access, input on offer changes
- Title: Senior Closer

**Senior Closer to Team Lead (6+ months as Senior):**
- Trigger: Willing to mentor 2-3 junior reps, maintains 35%+ close rate while mentoring, demonstrates ability to give constructive feedback
- Comp change: 2-3% override on mentees' deals on top of personal commission
- Responsibility: Runs morning drills 2x/week, reviews mentees' calls daily, reports to manager on mentee progress
- Still takes calls (reduced calendar — 3/day instead of 5)

**Team Lead to Sales Manager (12+ months total):**
- Trigger: Proven ability to develop other reps (at least 2 mentees hit KPI), willing to stop taking calls entirely, understands the full sales system from setter through CSM
- Comp change: Manager comp package ($8K-$12K base + 3-5% override + bonus)
- Responsibility: Owns the team's number. Reports to founder.
- This is a fundamentally different role. Not everyone wants it or should take it.

### Path C: CSM Track

```
CSM (Day 1)
    |
    | 6+ months, all KPIs green, 30%+ upsell rate
    v
Senior CSM (Month 6-12)
    |
    | Managing 2+ junior CSMs, owns retention strategy
    v
Head of Client Success (Month 12+)
```

**CSM to Senior CSM (6+ months):**
- Trigger: 95%+ 30-day retention, 85%+ 90-day retention, 5+ testimonials/month, 3+ referrals/month sustained
- Comp change: Base increase to $4,000/mo, referral commission to 12%
- Perk: Input on onboarding process design, attendance at sales team meetings

### Comp Adjustments at Each Level

| Transition | Base Change | Variable Change |
|---|---|---|
| Junior Setter to Setter | Training rate to $2,000-$2,500 | Per-show bonus activates |
| Setter to Senior Setter | +$500/mo | Per-show bonus increases to $40-$50 |
| Senior Setter to Junior Closer | Shifts to $3,000-$3,500 | Commission at 8% |
| Junior Closer to Closer | +$500/mo | Commission at 10% |
| Closer to Senior Closer | +$500/mo | Commission at 12% |
| Senior Closer to Team Lead | +$1,000/mo | +2-3% override on mentees |
| Team Lead to Sales Manager | $8,000-$12,000 base | 3-5% team override + monthly bonus |

## SECTION 9: SCALING PLAYBOOK

Scaling is not "hire more people." Scaling is adding capacity at the rate the system can absorb it without degrading quality. Every premature hire reduces team performance for 2-4 weeks while the new person ramps. If you stack two premature hires on top of each other, you can break the entire machine.

### When and How to Scale: Decision Framework

#### From 1 Closer to 2 Closers

**Trigger:** Closer calendar utilization above 85% for 2 or more consecutive weeks.

**Why 85%?** At 85% utilization, the closer is taking 5-6 calls per day with almost no gaps. They have no breathing room for pre-call prep, follow-ups, or recovery between tough calls. Quality starts to decline. Show rate may drop because the closer cannot send personalized pre-call texts when every minute is booked.

**How:**
1. Run the hiring pipeline (Section 2). Do 20-30 interviews. Do not settle.
2. New closer starts on half calendar (3 calls/day max)
3. Existing closer maintains their calendar
4. Manager splits call review between both closers
5. After 2-week ramp, new closer moves to full calendar
6. Leads are now split evenly (round-robin booking)
7. Monitor both closers' metrics for 30 days to ensure the split did not degrade either

**Warning:** Do not hire a second closer if the first closer's calendar is full because of unqualified leads. That is a setter quality problem, not a capacity problem. Fix qualification first.

#### From 1 Setter to 2 Setters

**Trigger:** Closer has consistent open calendar slots (utilization below 65%) while the setter is maxed on outreach volume.

**How:**
1. New setter handles one offer while existing setter handles the other (Amazon OS / Agency OS split)
2. OR: New setter handles all inbound leads, existing setter focuses on outbound
3. Same onboarding (48-hour SooWei framework)
4. Monitor show rates for both setters — if the new setter's show rate is below 70% after 2 weeks, they need more coaching on qualification

#### When to Hire a Sales Manager

**Trigger:** $300K/mo+ in revenue OR 6+ reps (whichever comes first).

Tim Luong: "$300K+ — founder goes FULL TIME sales manager, not on calls." But this only works until the founder has so many other responsibilities (content, offer, partnerships, strategy) that sales management suffers. At that point, you hire a dedicated manager.

**The hire:**
- This is the hardest and most important hire you will make
- Do NOT hire a "manager." Hire a closer who has managed closers before.
- They must have run a team of at least 4-6 reps at a company doing $200K+/mo
- They must understand your offer, your ICP, and your sales process before they can manage others on it
- Expect a 30-60 day ramp where they learn the business before taking over management
- Pay well. A great sales manager at $20K/mo total comp who lifts team revenue by 30% is worth 10x their cost. A bad sales manager at $10K/mo who tanks morale costs you 6 months of growth.

Tim Luong: "Don't hire a sales manager until you can afford a great one. A mediocre sales manager is worse than the founder managing directly."

#### When to Hire a CSM

**Trigger:** 5+ active clients (for Agency OS) or 15+ active students (for Amazon OS).

Without a CSM, either the founder or the closers handle retention, onboarding, and referral generation. This is a distraction. Every hour a closer spends on client success is an hour they are not on a sales call. At 5+ clients, the overhead becomes significant enough to justify a dedicated hire.

### How to Maintain Culture During Growth

Culture does not scale automatically. It must be actively maintained. Here are the levers:

**Daily standup (never skip):**
The standup is the single most important cultural ritual. It is where energy is set, wins are celebrated, and expectations are reinforced. Pierre: "The day is won or lost the second you wake up. Momentum spirals both ways." The standup IS the wake-up for the team.

When the team was 3 people, the standup felt natural. When the team is 10 people, it requires discipline. The manager must run it the same way every day — same time, same format, same energy. No "let's skip today because we're busy." The day you skip the standup is the day the culture starts to erode.

**Team wins channel:**
Every close gets announced in #wins. The manager makes a big deal of it. Hormozi: "Make a big deal out of wins. Celebrate performance at the end of the month." This is not optional. Celebration is a management tool. It tells the team what behavior is rewarded.

**Energy checks:**
If a rep is consistently low energy (below 6 on the 1-10 scale for 3+ days), the manager schedules a same-day 1-on-1. Low energy is the leading indicator of every performance problem. Catch it early.

**New hire integration:**
When a new person joins, the existing team must welcome them. Assign a "buddy" (the rep closest to their role) for the first 2 weeks. The buddy introduces them to the tools, the rhythm, the inside jokes. The new hire should feel like part of the team by Day 3, not Day 30.

### Common Scaling Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Hiring too fast.**
You hire 3 closers in the same week because "we need to scale." Now you have 3 people in the 2-week ramp simultaneously, your manager is spending 100% of their time on onboarding, your existing closers are not getting coached, and morale drops. Stagger hires by 2-3 weeks minimum.

**Mistake 2: Skipping training because "we need bodies on the phones."**
A closer who skips the Black Marker Method and goes straight to calls will have a 15% close rate instead of 35%. They burn through your leads, demoralize themselves, and then quit. The 2-week on-ramp exists for a reason.

**Mistake 3: Promoting the wrong person to sales manager.**
The best closer is not always the best manager. Closing is a performance skill. Managing is a coaching skill. Some people are great at both. Many are great at one. Before promoting someone, ask: "Do they enjoy developing other people? Do they get energy from someone else's win?" If the answer is no, they belong on the IC track, not the leadership track.

**Mistake 4: Losing the standup as the team grows.**
When the team was 3 people, everyone was in a room together. When the team is 10 people across 3 time zones, the standup becomes a Zoom call. The format must adapt but the discipline must not. Camera on. Full attendance. No exceptions.

**Mistake 5: Scaling reps without scaling lead flow.**
More closers with the same number of leads = lower utilization per closer = lower morale = lower close rates. Hormozi: "Below 60% utilization — morale drops, commission breath." Before hiring another closer, confirm that marketing can deliver enough leads to keep all closers at 70-80% utilization.

**Mistake 6: Not documenting the top performer's process before replicating.**
Pierre's Wipe and Scale: "Before you scale a sales function, identify your top performer, document their framework, then replicate." If you hire 3 new closers without first documenting exactly what your best closer does differently, you are hoping they figure it out on their own. They will not.

### Scaling by Revenue Band (Summary)

| Revenue Band | Team Size | Key Hires | Founder's Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0-$50K/mo | 3 (founder + setter + closer) | First setter, first closer | Founder manages + takes overflow calls |
| $50K-$100K/mo | 4 (+ second setter) | Second setter | Founder manages, 30% on calls |
| $100K-$200K/mo | 6 (+ closer, setter, CSM) | Second closer, CSM | Founder manages full-time, 0% on calls |
| $200K-$300K/mo | 7-8 (+ sales manager) | Sales manager | Founder exits management |
| $300K-$500K/mo | 8-10 (+ third closer, third setter) | Scale reps | Founder focuses on offer, content, strategy |
| $500K+/mo | 10-14 (+ team leads, second CSM) | Team leads, VP Sales | Founder is CEO. Period. |

Pierre: "I'd rather have someone take my money than my mental energy." At every scaling stage, the founder is buying back mental energy by delegating management. The money spent on a sales manager is not a cost — it is the purchase of the founder's attention, which should be deployed on the highest-leverage activities: content creation, offer development, partnerships, and vision.

## QUICK REFERENCE — DECISION FILTERS

**Should I hire another rep?**
Check the trigger metrics in Section 1. If a metric has been sustained for 2+ weeks, hire. If not, wait.

**Should I fire this rep?**
Follow the escalation ladder in Section 5. 3 days red = diagnosis. 5 days red = PIP. Failed PIP = out. Do not skip steps.

**Should I promote this rep?**
Check the specific KPI thresholds in Section 8. Promotion is based on output, not tenure.

**Am I paying enough?**
If your best closer is making less than $12K/mo total comp, you will lose them. Run the example calculations in Section 6 and make sure the math works for top performers.

**Is my team communicating well?**
Audit the #handoffs channel. If handoffs are clean, threaded, and acknowledged within 15 minutes, communication is healthy. If handoffs are messy, unthreaded, or ignored, communication is breaking down.

**When do I stop managing and start delegating?**
When your revenue consistently exceeds $200K/mo and you have a sales manager candidate who has managed teams before. Not before.
