# Agency OS — Full Closer Script (Strategy Call)

> **Version:** 1.0 | **Created:** 2026-03-21
> **Call Type:** B2B Strategy Call (30-45 min)
> **ICP:** $1M+ annual revenue founders with product-market fit, no repeatable growth system
> **Offer:** Done-For-You Growth Operating System | $5K-$25K/mo retainer
> **Promise:** Full-stack growth OS installed in 30 days, operated month over month
> **Differentiator:** Operator model (senior talent embedded), not account manager model

---

## PRE-CALL PREP CHECKLIST

Before every call, the closer reviews and has ready:

- [ ] **Application data** — revenue, team size, industry, marketing channels, stated challenge
- [ ] **Website audit** — 5-minute scan of their site, landing pages, ad library (Meta Ad Library)
- [ ] **LinkedIn** — founder profile, company page, employee count, recent posts
- [ ] **CRM notes** — setter notes, previous touchpoints, lead source, VSL watch time
- [ ] **Quick competitive scan** — who are their obvious competitors? What are they running?
- [ ] **3 tailored questions** — based on their application, not generic discovery
- [ ] **Relevant case study** — pick the one closest to their industry, revenue, or challenge
- [ ] **Pricing range estimate** — based on scope clues from the application ($5K, $10K, $15K, $25K)
- [ ] **Quiet environment** — camera on, professional background, no interruptions for 45 min

**Mental frame before dialing:** You are a growth operator evaluating whether this business is a fit for your team. You are NOT selling. You are diagnosing. If the fit is right, you will offer to help. If not, you will tell them honestly.

## THE SCRIPT

---

### STAGE 1: CONNECTING (2-3 min)
*[Tone: Playful > Curious]*

> "Hey [NAME], appreciate you carving out the time. I'm [CLOSER NAME] — I lead growth operations on Sabbo's team at 247 Growth."

> "I had a chance to review your application before the call, and honestly, it looks like you've built something solid. Before we get into anything, I just want to set the right expectation for how this works."

*(Pause briefly.)*

> "This isn't a sales pitch. Think of this more like a strategy session. The first 20 minutes are about understanding your business — how you're acquiring customers, what's working, what's not, and where the real bottleneck is. If I see a clear way we can help, I'll walk you through what that looks like. If I don't think we're the right fit, I'll tell you that too. Fair enough?"

*(Wait for agreement. This is a micro-commitment — they're agreeing to the frame.)*

> "And just to make sure we get the most out of this — are you in a spot where you can give this your full attention for the next 30-40 minutes? No meetings pulling you away?"

*(If they say they only have 15-20 minutes, address it directly:)*

> "I'd rather reschedule than rush this. The founders we work with are making $5K-$25K monthly investment decisions — that deserves a real conversation, not a speed round. Want to find a better time?"

**TONALITY NOTE:** The opening should feel like a peer-to-peer conversation between two operators. Not formal. Not corporate. Not overly casual. Think: experienced strategist sitting down with another experienced person.

---

### STAGE 2: SITUATION QUESTIONS (5-7 min)
*[Tone: Curious]*

> "So let's start here — give me the 30-second version of the business. What do you sell, who do you sell it to, and roughly what are you doing in annual revenue?"

*(Let them talk. Mirror their language. Take notes.)*

> "Got it. And how did you get to [THEIR REVENUE]? Was that mostly word of mouth, paid ads, outbound — what's driven the growth so far?"

> "How big is the team right now? And who, if anyone, is responsible for marketing and customer acquisition?"

*(This reveals whether they have internal marketing talent, an agency, or the founder is doing it all.)*

> "Walk me through what happens when a lead comes in right now — from the moment someone raises their hand to the moment they become a paying customer. What does that process actually look like?"

*(This is the most important question in Stage 2. It exposes every gap in their funnel without you having to point them out. Let them describe it fully. Do NOT interrupt.)*

> "And do you know roughly what it costs you to acquire a customer right now? Your CAC?"

*(If they don't know:)*

> "That's actually pretty common — and it's one of the things that keeps founders stuck. Hard to scale what you can't measure."

> "What about lifetime value? Once you get a customer, how long do they stay and what's the total value of that relationship?"

> "Last thing on the situation side — what's your marketing budget right now? Monthly spend across everything — ads, tools, people, agencies?"

**TONALITY NOTE:** This entire section should feel like a doctor's intake questions. You are gathering data. You are not reacting, judging, or suggesting yet. Just collecting.

---

### STAGE 3: PROBLEM AWARENESS (5-7 min)
*[Tone: Curious > Concerned]*

Now you diagnose. Use what they told you in Stage 2 to make them articulate the pain out loud.

> "So based on what you're telling me — you're at [REVENUE], you've been growing mostly through [THEIR METHOD], and the acquisition process is [YOUR SUMMARY]. Let me ask you this: what's the biggest thing holding you back from 2x-ing or 3x-ing the business in the next 12 months?"

*(Let them answer. They will usually give a surface-level response. Dig.)*

> "And how long has that been the case?"

> "What have you tried so far to solve it?"

*(This is where you learn about past agencies, freelancers, internal hires that didn't work out.)*

> "How did that go?"

*(Let them tell the story. This is where the real pain surfaces.)*

> "So if I'm hearing you right — you've tried [WHAT THEY TRIED], it didn't deliver the results you needed, and now you're in a position where the business has product-market fit but no reliable way to turn the dial on growth. Is that a fair summary?"

*(Get confirmation. They need to hear their own situation reflected back to them.)*

> "What do you think that's actually costing you? Not just in ad spend — but in opportunity cost. Revenue you're leaving on the table every month because the machine isn't built?"

*(Pause. Let them calculate. This number matters — it becomes the ROI anchor later.)*

> "And how does that sit with you? Knowing that every month this doesn't get fixed, that's another month of [THEIR OPPORTUNITY COST NUMBER] walking out the door?"

**TONALITY NOTE:** When they describe past failures with agencies or hires, lean into concern. Not pity — genuine concern like a colleague who understands the frustration. When they talk about the cost of inaction, shift to slightly more direct. You are making them feel the weight of the status quo.

---

### STAGE 4: PRE-FRAME PSYCHOLOGY (3-5 min)
*[Tone: Curious > Concerned]*

This is adapted from Johnny Mau's revealing question technique. Whatever they say here IS the objection they will present at the close. Listen carefully and neutralize it now.

> "Here's what I want to understand. You're running a [REVENUE] business. You clearly know the growth engine isn't where it needs to be — you said so yourself. You've been dealing with this for [TIME]. So what's actually prevented you from solving this problem until now?"

*(CRITICAL: Shut up and listen. Do not rush past this. Their answer is the key to the entire close.)*

**Common B2B answers and how to pre-frame them NOW:**

---

**If "We've been burned by agencies before":**

> "That's one of the most common things I hear from founders at your level. Can I share why I think that happened? *(wait for yes)* Most agencies operate on an account manager model — you get a junior person managing your account, they're running the same playbook for 15 clients, and no one actually understands your business deeply enough to diagnose the real problem. They run ads and send you a report. That's not what we do. We embed an operator into your business — someone senior who owns the outcome, not just the deliverables. Does that distinction make sense?"

---

**If "We've been trying to do it ourselves / build an internal team":**

> "I respect that — building internal is the instinct every good founder has. Let me ask you this though: how long have you been trying to build that capability internally? *(they answer)* And where are you on it? *(they answer)*. Here's the reality — hiring a full growth team internally means you need a VP of Growth ($150K-$250K), a media buyer ($80K-$120K), an email strategist ($60K-$90K), and a creative person ($60K-$80K). That's $350K-$500K in salary before they produce a single result. And that assumes you find the right people, which takes 6-12 months. Most founders at your stage can't justify that headcount yet. That's exactly the gap we fill."

---

**If "Not sure what we actually need":**

> "That's honest — and frankly, it's a better answer than someone who thinks they know exactly what they need but is wrong. The founders who come to us knowing something is broken but not sure exactly what — those are usually the best engagements. Because the first thing we do is diagnose. We don't prescribe a solution before we understand the actual problem. So the fact that you don't know exactly what you need isn't a blocker — that's literally what this call and our first 30 days together are designed to figure out."

---

**If "Budget / timing isn't right":**

> "I appreciate you being upfront about that. Let me ask you something though — what's the cost of NOT fixing this for another 6 months? You mentioned earlier you're leaving roughly [OPPORTUNITY COST NUMBER] on the table every month. So waiting 6 months doesn't save money — it costs you [6 x OPPORTUNITY COST]. The question isn't whether you can afford to invest in growth. It's whether you can afford to keep operating without a growth engine."

---

*(By reframing their objection NOW, it's dissolved before the pitch. When it resurfaces at the close, you can say: "Remember earlier when we talked about [X] and how [reframe]? That's exactly what's happening right now.")*

**TONALITY NOTE:** This section requires precision. You are not arguing with them or dismissing their concern. You are showing them a different way to see the same situation. Tone should be like a trusted advisor sharing a perspective they haven't considered — not a salesperson handling an objection.

---

### STAGE 5: SOLUTION AWARENESS + FUTURE PACING (5-7 min)
*[Tone: Concerned > Challenging]*

> "So let me make sure I have this right. You're at [REVENUE], growth has been driven by [METHOD], but it's not predictable or scalable. You've tried [WHAT THEY TRIED] and it didn't work because [REASON]. And the main thing that's kept you from fixing this is [STAGE 4 ANSWER]. Does that sound accurate?"

*(Get explicit confirmation. This is the Current State summary.)*

> "Now let me ask you something different. If you had a full acquisition engine installed in the next 30 days — paid media, organic, email, conversion optimization, attribution — all built and operated by one team that owns the outcome and reports directly to you... what does that change for the business?"

*(Let them paint the picture. This is the Future State. They are selling themselves.)*

> "What does that mean in terms of revenue? If the acquisition machine is running and you can predict what comes in the door every month — what number are you targeting?"

> "And for you personally — what changes? I know you're deep in the weeds right now on marketing, on sales, on ops. If you could hand off the entire growth function to a team that runs it like they own it — what does your day look like?"

*(Dig into the personal impact. Founders at this level are burned out from wearing every hat. This is emotional — let them feel it.)*

**Consequence question:**

> "Now flip it. Let's say nothing changes. You don't bring on a growth partner. You keep doing what you've been doing — handling marketing yourself, or hiring another freelancer, or trying another agency that runs the same playbook. Where are you 12 months from now?"

*(Pause. Let them sit with it.)*

> "Is that acceptable to you?"

> "So would you agree that solving this — actually building the machine, not just running another campaign — needs to happen now, not in Q3 or next year?"

**TONALITY NOTE:** The future pacing should be warm and expansive — help them feel the relief of having this solved. The consequence question should be direct and slightly challenging. You are not being aggressive. You are being honest about what inaction costs.

---

### STAGE 6: TRANSITION TO PITCH (1-2 min)
*[Tone: Challenging > Confident]*

> "Look — the reason we run these calls the way we do is because we're selective about who we work with. We take on a limited number of clients at any given time because of the way we operate. We're not an agency that manages 50 accounts with a rotating cast of junior people. We embed into your business. That means we have to make sure the fit is right on both sides."

> "Based on everything you've told me — the business you've built, where you're trying to go, and what's been in the way — I think there's a real fit here. So here's what I want to do: I'm going to walk you through exactly what working with us looks like — the structure, the deliverables, the timeline — and then if it makes sense for both of us, we'll talk about investment and next steps. If it doesn't feel right, no pressure. Cool?"

*(Get their agreement before you pitch. This is permission-based selling. They are inviting you to present, not the other way around.)*

---

### STAGE 7: THE PITCH (5-10 min)
*[Tone: Confident, Steady — NOT excited or salesy]*

> "What we do is fundamentally different from a traditional agency. We don't just run ads and send you a report. We install a complete growth operating system into your business — paid, organic, email, conversion, attribution — and then we operate it for you, month over month, so you can focus on product and team."

> "Here's how it works. Three pillars."

---

**PILLAR 1: GROWTH INFRASTRUCTURE INSTALL (Days 1-30)**

> "The first 30 days are about building the machine. Most agencies skip this — they jump straight into ad spend. We don't. We build the infrastructure first because without it, every dollar you spend on ads is partially wasted."

> "Here's what happens in Month 1:"

> "We start with a full growth audit — your funnel, your messaging, your conversion points, your analytics, your CRM. We map every step of the customer journey and identify where the leaks are."

> "Then we build. Funnel architecture, landing pages, email sequences — lead intake, pre-call nurture, no-show re-engagement, long-term nurture. CRM automation. Tracking and attribution so you know exactly which dollar produced which customer."

> "We build your creative strategy — ad angles, hooks, messaging frameworks tailored to your ICP. Not generic templates. Messaging built from your positioning, your proof, your differentiation."

> "By day 30, the system is live. Campaigns are running. Tracking is verified. Email sequences are firing. And you have a real-time dashboard showing you exactly what's happening."

> "How would having all of that in place change the way you think about growth?"

*(Micro-commitment. Let them verbalize the value.)*

---

**PILLAR 2: FULL-STACK OPERATION (Month 2+)**

> "Pillar two is where most agencies fall apart — and where we're fundamentally different. After the install, we don't hand you a playbook and wish you luck. We operate it."

> "Daily monitoring — ad spend, conversion rates, CPL, CPA. We're watching the numbers every single day, not once a week."

> "Weekly reporting and a strategy call with you — 30 minutes, focused on performance, what we're testing, what's scaling, what's getting killed."

> "Monthly deep-dive reviews — full growth analysis, next 30-day plan, budget reallocation, new creative angles."

> "Creative iteration — new hooks, new angles, new ad formats every 10 days. We don't let creative fatigue kill your campaigns."

> "Email optimization — open rates, click rates, reply rates. We're testing subject lines, send times, segmentation — continuously."

> "And here's the key difference: you get a dedicated growth lead embedded in your business. Not a rotating account manager. One senior operator who knows your ICP, your numbers, your goals, and your competitive landscape. They run this like it's their own company."

> "That's the operator model. We own the outcome — not just the deliverables."

---

**PILLAR 3: DATA + ATTRIBUTION LAYER**

> "Third pillar — and this is what ties it all together — is the data layer."

> "Most founders I talk to can't tell me exactly which channel produced which customer. They know roughly what they're spending, but they can't trace a dollar from ad click to closed deal. That's a massive problem when you're trying to scale."

> "We build full attribution — pixel setup, GA4, UTM architecture, CRM pipeline tracking. Every lead is tagged, every touchpoint is logged, and every conversion is attributed to a specific source."

> "That means when something works, we know it. When something breaks, we catch it immediately. And when you're ready to scale, you know exactly where to put the next dollar."

> "The scorecard we track together includes: cost per lead, cost per qualified lead, call show rate, close rate, CAC, LTV, and client retention. You see these numbers weekly. No guessing."

*(Pause. Let the full picture settle.)*

> "So just to recap — Month 1, we build the entire growth infrastructure. Month 2 forward, we operate it. And the data layer gives you visibility into everything so you can make decisions with confidence, not intuition."

> "Based on what you told me earlier about [THEIR SPECIFIC CHALLENGE], which of those three pillars do you think would have the biggest impact on the business right now?"

*(This forces them to identify with the solution. They are now co-diagnosing, not being sold to.)*

---

### STAGE 8: PRICE + CLOSE (5-10 min)
*[Tone: Confident, Direct — NOT apologetic]*

> "Before I tell you the investment, I want to be upfront about something — this isn't cheap. We're a premium service because we deliver premium results, and we limit the number of clients we take on so we can actually deliver. This isn't a $2K/month SEO retainer or a $1,500/month social media package. This is a full growth team embedded in your business."

*(Pause. Let them absorb the frame.)*

> "The investment ranges from $5,000 to $25,000 per month, depending on scope. Where you'd fall on that spectrum depends on how many channels we're operating, the complexity of your funnel, and how aggressive the growth targets are."

> "Based on what you've told me today — your revenue, your goals, the current gaps — I'd estimate you're looking at roughly $[SPECIFIC NUMBER]/month. That includes the full infrastructure build in Month 1, ongoing operation, the dedicated growth lead, weekly reporting, and everything we just walked through."

*(State the price once. Then stop talking. Let them react. Do NOT fill the silence with justification.)*

**If they're silent or processing:**

> *(Wait 5-7 seconds. Then:)*

> "What's going through your mind?"

**Tie to ROI:**

> "Let me frame it this way. You told me earlier you're leaving roughly [OPPORTUNITY COST] on the table each month. If we close even half that gap — and that's conservative based on what we've done with similar businesses — the retainer pays for itself multiple times over. The real cost isn't the $[X]/month. The real cost is the $[OPPORTUNITY COST]/month you keep losing without a system."

> "This is a 12-month engagement. We're not looking for a 90-day trial — we're building infrastructure that compounds. The results at Month 6 are dramatically different from Month 1, because the system gets smarter over time. Our average client retention is over 90% at the 12-month mark because the numbers speak for themselves."

**The close:**

> "Based on everything we've discussed — the gap in your growth engine, what you're leaving on the table, and what having a full-stack operator team would change — does this feel like the right move?"

*(Let them answer.)*

> "Why do you feel that way?"

*(If they've said yes — they sell themselves here. If the earlier stages were done right, this should be clean.)*

> "Great. The next step is a scoping call — that's a 60-minute deep dive with me and our growth lead where we map out the first 30-day plan specifically for your business. I'll send you a scope document and contract before that call so you can review everything. The scoping call can happen as early as this week."

> "How would you like to proceed?"

### STAGE 9: OBJECTION HANDLING

**PRINCIPLE:** If Stage 4 was done correctly, most objections are already dissolved. When one surfaces:

1. **Acknowledge** — "I hear you. That's a fair concern."
2. **Clarify** — "What specifically about [objection] is the concern?"
3. **Suppose** — "If we could resolve that, would there be anything else holding you back?"
4. **Callback** — "Remember earlier when you told me [Stage 4 answer]? This is exactly that pattern."
5. **Reframe** — Use the appropriate response below.
6. **Check** — "Does that make sense?"
7. **Re-commit** — "So what would you like to do?"

---

**OBJECTION 1: "We've been burned by agencies before"**
*[Tone: Concerned > Challenging]*

> "I hear that a lot from founders at your level — and honestly, you probably should be skeptical. Most agencies deserve that skepticism."

> "Can I share what I think went wrong? *(wait)* Traditional agencies operate on an account manager model. You get a junior person managing your account, they're executing a generic playbook across 20-30 clients, and nobody on their team actually understands your business, your customer, or your numbers deeply enough to make real strategic decisions. They run ads. They send you a PDF. And when results don't come, they blame the market or the creative or the algorithm."

> "That's not what we are. We're an operator model. You get a senior growth lead embedded in your business — someone who learns your ICP, your funnel, your conversion data, your competitive landscape. They run it like they own equity. The reason our retention rate is over 90% is because we don't manage accounts — we build and operate growth engines."

> "Does that feel different from what you've experienced before?"

---

**OBJECTION 2: "I need to think about it"**
*[Tone: Curious — NOT defensive]*

> "I respect that. When you say you need to think about it — what specifically are you wanting to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or whether we're the right team?"

*(This forces them to reveal the REAL objection. Handle that one.)*

**If they stay vague:**

> "Usually when a founder at your level says they need to think about it, there's one specific thing that isn't fully clicking. What part of this are you not 100% on?"

**Pre-frame callback:**

> "Earlier you told me that [STAGE 4 ANSWER] was what prevented you from solving this until now. I want to make sure that same pattern isn't showing up right here. Because the cost of 'thinking about it' for another month is [OPPORTUNITY COST] in revenue you don't capture."

**If genuine timing:**

> "Fair enough. Let me do this — I'll send you a one-page summary of everything we discussed, the scope I'd recommend, and the investment. And let's put 30 minutes on the calendar for [2 DAYS FROM NOW] so you've had time to process but we don't lose momentum. Does [DAY/TIME] work?"

*(Always book a specific follow-up. Never leave it open-ended.)*

---

**OBJECTION 3: "The price is too high"**
*[Tone: Direct > Concerned]*

> "Let me ask you this — is it that the number is more than you expected, or is it that you don't see how the ROI justifies the investment?"

**If more than expected:**

> "I understand. Let me put it in context. To build this capability internally — a VP of Growth, a media buyer, an email strategist, a creative strategist — you're looking at $350K-$500K per year in salary alone. Plus 6-12 months to recruit, hire, and ramp. And that's assuming you find the right people on the first try. What we're offering is a full growth team at a fraction of that cost, producing results in 30 days instead of 12 months."

**If questioning ROI:**

> "That's the right question to ask. Let's do the math together. You told me your average customer is worth [LTV]. Your current CAC is [CAC] — or you don't know it, which means it's probably higher than it should be. If we reduce your CAC by even 30% and increase your lead volume by 50%, what does that do to your bottom line over 12 months?"

*(Let them calculate. The number should be multiples of the retainer.)*

> "So the question isn't whether $[X]/month is a lot of money. It is. The question is whether leaving $[ANNUAL OPPORTUNITY] on the table for another year is acceptable."

---

**OBJECTION 4: "I need to talk to my partner / co-founder / board"**
*[Tone: Understanding > Direct]*

> "Of course — that makes sense. Can I ask: when you bring this to [PARTNER], what do you think their main question or concern will be?"

*(This reveals the real objection — usually money or skepticism.)*

> "And if that concern were resolved, would you personally want to move forward?"

> "Here's what I'd suggest. I'll put together a one-page executive summary — the problem we diagnosed, the solution, the expected ROI, and the investment. Something your partner can review in 5 minutes and have everything they need. Would it also help if we did a brief call with them so they can ask questions directly? I'm happy to do that."

> "Let's set a specific time to reconnect after you've spoken with them. Does [DAY/TIME] work?"

---

**OBJECTION 5: "We're going to try to build this internally first"**
*[Tone: Respectful > Challenging]*

> "I respect that instinct — every founder wants to own the capability long-term. Let me ask you something, though: how long do you think it would take to recruit, hire, and ramp a full internal growth team?"

*(They'll usually say 3-6 months. The real answer is 6-12.)*

> "In my experience, it takes most companies 6-12 months to build a competent internal growth team from scratch. That's 6-12 months of opportunity cost — at the [MONTHLY OPPORTUNITY COST] you mentioned, that's $[TOTAL] in revenue you're not capturing."

> "Here's what a lot of our clients do: they bring us in to build the system and operate it while they're hiring internally. We install the infrastructure, prove the playbook, and then when you have the right people in place, we do a knowledge transfer and hand off a running machine instead of a blank slate. That way you're not choosing between building internal and having results now — you get both."

> "Does that approach make sense?"

## POST-CALL ADMIN CHECKLIST

Complete within 30 minutes of every call:

- [ ] **CRM update** — disposition (closed, follow-up, lost), notes, next action, pipeline stage
- [ ] **Scope estimate** — if moving forward, document recommended retainer tier and scope
- [ ] **Follow-up scheduled** — if not closed, specific date/time in calendar (never "sometime next week")
- [ ] **Follow-up assets sent** — one-page summary, relevant case study, scope outline (within 2 hours)
- [ ] **Setter feedback** — was the lead qualified? Quality of setter notes? Any gaps in pre-call intel?
- [ ] **Debrief notes** — what worked on this call? What could have been better? Which objections came up?
- [ ] **Slack update** — post call result in team channel: "[NAME] — [RESULT] — [REVENUE] company — [NEXT STEP]"

## TONALITY REFERENCE (Quick Guide)

| Tone | When | How It Sounds |
|---|---|---|
| **Curious** | Stages 2-3, all discovery questions | Genuine interest, chin slightly up, "I actually want to understand this" |
| **Concerned** | When they share pain, past failures, frustration | Lean in, lower voice, slow down — like a colleague who gets it |
| **Challenging** | Consequence questions, calling out patterns, Stage 5 | Direct, steady, not aggressive — "I'm going to be honest with you" |
| **Confident** | Pitch, pricing, close | Even pace, no vocal fry, no uptalk — you believe every word |
| **Playful** | Opening rapport, breaking tension after heavy discovery | Smile in your voice, casual — two operators having a real conversation |

**Key rules:**
- Slow down the second half of every question. Give them time to think.
- Silence is your most powerful tool. After asking a question, STOP TALKING.
- Never fill silence with justification, especially after stating the price.
- Match their energy on rapport, lead with your energy on the close.
- If they get emotional about past failures or frustrations, DO NOT rush past it. Sit in it. That pain is what drives the decision.
