## How to Use This Document

9 ad angles organized as 3 core angles x 3 hook variations. Each angle targets a different psychological entry point. Test all 9 in ABO at $50/day each, identify winners after 4 days, then move winners to CBO at $250+/day (per Nik Setting's ABO-to-CBO framework). Kill losers after 3-5 days of data.

**Budget allocation:**
- Cold traffic: 80% of spend across all 3 angles
- Retargeting: 15-20% of total budget
- Lookalike: Rolled into cold traffic campaigns

**KPI targets:**
- CPL (Application): < $150
- CPQL (Qualified Lead): < $300
- Call Show Rate: > 70%
- Close Rate: > 25%

## ANGLE 1: PAIN (What's Broken)

Target the frustration. Make them feel seen. These ads work because founders who are burning money on marketing without results are emotionally charged and actively looking for a way out. Pain ads generate the highest volume of leads but require strong qualification on the back end.

---

### 1A: "Your marketing isn't broken. Your marketing system is."

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
"Your marketing isn't broken. Your marketing system is."

**Body (30-60 seconds):**
You have ads running. You have a website. You might even have an agency. But here is the problem — none of it is connected. Your ads send traffic to a page that does not convert. Your CRM has leads rotting in it because nobody follows up. Your agency sends you a report every month and calls it strategy. That is not marketing. That is expensive chaos. What you are missing is not more tactics. It is a system — a growth operating system that connects acquisition, conversion, and retention into one machine. We build that system. We install it into your business in 30 days. And then we operate it for you, month over month, so you can stop managing marketing and start managing growth.

**CTA:**
"See how we fix it — link in bio / link below."

**Format:** Talking head, founder direct-to-camera. Raw, no fancy graphics. Shot on phone or webcam for authenticity. Subtitles required.

**Target Audience:** Cold — broad targeting, founder/CEO interest stacks. Also works as a Lookalike seed (1% LAL of past clients or applicants).

**Extended Version (60-90 seconds):**
Add after body: "I talked to a founder last week who was spending $15K a month on Meta ads. He had a media buyer, a copywriter, and a VA managing his CRM. Three people. No system connecting them. His cost per qualified lead was over $400 and his close rate was under 15%. Not because his product was bad — his product was great. New landing page, new email sequences, new attribution model, restructured ad account. In 30 days his CPL dropped to $140 and his close rate went to 32%. Same product. Same market. Different system."

## Creative Production Notes

**Talking Head Format (1A, 1C, 2C, 3C):**
- Film direct to camera, eye level, neutral background or office setting
- Natural lighting preferred over studio. Authenticity over production value.
- Phone or webcam is fine for testing. Upgrade to a proper camera for scaled winners only.
- Always add captions/subtitles — 85% of Meta feed video is watched with sound off
- Keep visual clean: no logos, no lower thirds, no branding in the first 3 seconds
- First frame must be compelling enough to stop the scroll without audio

**Text-on-Screen Format (1B, 3B):**
- White or yellow text on dark background. High contrast. Large font.
- One sentence per card. 2-3 seconds per card.
- Background music (subtle, lo-fi or ambient). No voiceover needed for testing.
- Add a voiceover version as a B-variant once the text version proves the hook

**Case Study Walkthrough Format (2A, 3A):**
- Screen share a real dashboard (anonymize client name, keep real numbers)
- Picture-in-picture webcam in corner for face trust
- Walk through the before/after narrative while showing real data
- 60-90 seconds minimum — this format rewards depth over speed

**UGC Style Format (2B):**
- Casual, walking-and-talking, handheld phone footage
- Outdoor setting, coffee shop, or car — anywhere that feels unscripted
- No intro graphics. Start talking immediately.
- This format works best for warm retarget audiences who already recognize you

## Testing Protocol

1. Launch all 9 hooks in ABO format, $50/day each ($450/day total test budget)
2. Run for 4 full days minimum before making decisions
3. Evaluate on CPQL (Cost per Qualified Lead), not CPL — cheap leads that do not qualify are worthless
4. Move top 2-3 performers to CBO at $250+/day with broad targeting
5. Kill bottom performers after 3-5 days of consistent underperformance
6. Rotate new hooks into test slots every 10-14 days to prevent fatigue
7. Always use Post ID method when moving from ABO to CBO (preserves social proof and pixel learning)
8. Never test more than one variable at a time — if testing a new hook, keep the body and CTA the same
9. Track through to CPQL, not just CPL — a $200 CPL that produces $100 CPQL beats a $50 CPL that produces $400 CPQL

## Retargeting Creative Notes

| Audience Segment | Definition | Recommended Angles | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hot | LP visit + 50% VSL watched | 2A or 2B | "Apply Now" (direct) |
| Warm | LP bounce before form | 1C or 3C | "Watch the breakdown" |
| Cold Retarget | Ad engager, no LP visit | 3A or 3B | "See how it works" |
| Email List | Leads who never booked | 3C | "Book your strategy call" |
| Lookalike | 1% LAL of closed clients | 1A or 2C | "Apply to work with us" |

**Frequency caps:** 2-3 impressions per person per day for retargeting. Rotate creative every 7-10 days. If frequency exceeds 4.0 on any ad set, swap creative immediately.

**Platform distribution:**
- Meta (Facebook + Instagram): Primary for all angles. 70% of budget.
- YouTube pre-roll: Use extended versions (60-90s) of winning angles. 20% of budget.
- LinkedIn: Use Angle 3A or 3B only. Professional tone. 10% of budget. Only if targeting B2B/enterprise.

## Iteration Cadence

- **Every 10 days:** Write 2 new hook variations for the winning angle
- **Every 30 days:** Review all 3 angles. If one angle consistently underperforms, replace it with a new angle concept (e.g., Social Proof angle, Founder Story angle, Contrarian angle)
- **Every 90 days:** Full creative refresh. New filming session, new hooks, new formats. Keep the proven body copy structure but update examples, numbers, and case studies.
