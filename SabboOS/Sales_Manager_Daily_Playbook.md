## 1. DAILY RHYTHM (HOUR-BY-HOUR)

This is your day. No deviations. No "I'll skip training today because we're busy." The schedule is the schedule.

```
TIME (EST)    BLOCK                           DURATION    NOTES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10:00 AM      Manager Prep                    60 min      Pull data, prep agenda, review yesterday's EODs
10:30 AM      Coaching Tracker Review          30 min      Check who submitted EODs, tag anyone who didn't
11:00 AM      MORNING TEAM MEETING            30 min      Full team, voice chat, camera on
11:30 AM      Training Block 1                30 min      Daily topic rotation (see Section 3)
12:00 PM      DIAL BLOCK 1 START              150 min     ALL setters in shared voice chat
                                                          Manager monitors, gives live feedback between dials
                                                          Closers take scheduled calls
2:30 PM       BREAK                           60 min      Mandatory. No calls. No Slack. Eat.
3:30 PM       Training Block 2                30 min      Reinforcement drill or objection workshop
4:00 PM       DIAL BLOCK 2 START              120 min     ALL setters back in shared voice chat
                                                          Closers take afternoon calls + follow-ups
6:00 PM       EOD TEAM MEETING                30 min      Numbers check, wins, pipeline, tomorrow prep
6:30 PM       Manager Closeout                30 min      Collect EODs, build daily scorecard, update coaching tracker
7:00 PM       DAY ENDS                        —           Post scorecard to team channel. Done.
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### 10:00 AM — Manager Prep (What You Do Before Anyone Else Is Online)

1. Pull yesterday's numbers from 247growth dashboard or CRM
2. Review all EOD reports submitted last night (closer + setter)
3. Flag any missing EODs — those reps get called out at the morning meeting
4. Check today's calendar: how many calls are booked? Which closers are stacked? Any gaps?
5. Review the coaching tracker: any action items from yesterday's 1-on-1s that need follow-up?
6. Prepare the morning meeting agenda (takes 5 minutes once you have the data)
7. Check lead response times from yesterday — anyone over 60 seconds gets a conversation today

### Voice Chat Rule — DIAL BLOCKS

During Dial Block 1 (12:00-2:30 PM) and Dial Block 2 (4:00-6:00 PM):

- ALL setters are in a shared voice chat (Discord or Zoom). No exceptions.
- The sales manager is in the voice chat the entire time.
- Between dials, the manager gives real-time feedback: "That opening was flat — try this instead."
- Energy stays high. If someone is slumping, the manager calls it out and resets the energy.
- No one dials alone. The voice chat creates accountability and competitive energy.
- If a setter needs to step away (bathroom, water), they say so and come back within 3 minutes.
- Closers may be on separate calls during this time but stay in the voice chat when not on a close.

### Manager's Dial Block Responsibilities

During the dial blocks, the manager is NOT on their own calls. They are:

1. Listening to live setter conversations (when on speaker or dual-audio)
2. Giving real-time coaching between dials ("Great energy on that one" / "You rushed the qualifying question")
3. Tracking live metrics: how many dials, how many connects, how many bookings
4. Keeping the energy up — calling out wins as they happen ("Alex just booked one! Let's go!")
5. Jumping in to model a call if a setter is struggling
6. Being available instantly if a closer needs help mid-call (Slack DM or quick mute/unmute)

## 2. 11 AM TEAM MEETING AGENDA (30 MIN)

Camera on. Voice chat. Full team. No rolling in late. If you're late, you're disrespecting the team.

### MINUTE 0-3: Energy Check

```
SCRIPT:
"Good morning everyone. Before we get into numbers — how's everyone feeling today?
Give me a 1-10 in the chat. 1 is 'barely alive,' 10 is 'ready to run through a wall.'"

[Wait for responses]

If anyone is below a 5:
"[Name], what's going on? What do you need today?"

If everyone is 7+:
"Love it. Let's match that energy on the phones today."
```

Rules:
- This is not small talk. This is how you catch problems before they tank performance.
- If someone is consistently low energy (3 days in a row below 6), schedule a 1-on-1 that day.
- Never skip this. Two minutes of genuine check-in prevents a week of underperformance.

### MINUTE 3-8: Yesterday's Wins

```
SCRIPT:
"Alright, wins from yesterday. I want to hear from everyone.
Doesn't have to be a close — could be a great conversation, a lesson learned,
a new technique that worked. [Name], you're up first."

[Go around the room. Every single rep shares something.]

After each win:
"Nice. [Brief acknowledgment or tactical insight from what they shared.]"

If someone closed:
"[Name] closed yesterday — [deal details]. Ring the bell. Let's go."
```

Rules:
- EVERY rep shares. No spectators.
- If someone says "nothing good happened," push them: "What was the best conversation you had? What did you learn?" There is always something.
- Celebrate closes loudly. Make it a big deal. Hormozi: "Make a big deal out of wins."

### MINUTE 8-15: Numbers Review

```
SCRIPT:
"Numbers. Here's where we stand."

[Share screen or read from dashboard]

"Yesterday's team numbers:
- Total dials: [X]
- Connections: [X]
- Appointments set: [X]
- Calls scheduled today: [X]
- Calls shown yesterday: [X] out of [X] scheduled — [X]% show rate
- Closes: [X]
- Cash collected: $[X]

Week-to-date:
- We're at $[X] against a $[X] target. That's [X]%.
- We need [X] more closes this week to hit target."

Per-rep callouts (brief):
"[Name], you're at [X] dials yesterday — target is [X]. Let's get that up today."
"[Name], 3 bookings yesterday. Strong. Keep that pace."
```

Rules:
- Numbers are facts, not judgments. State them clearly.
- Never shame someone in front of the team. If a rep is way off, say "let's connect after this meeting."
- Always contextualize: where are we vs. target? What do we need today to stay on track?
- Show rate below 70%: address it here. "We need to tighten up our pre-call nurture. Closers, are confirmation texts going out?"

### MINUTE 15-25: Today's Focus

```
SCRIPT:
"Today's focus: [ONE thing]."

Examples:
- "Today's focus is discovery depth. On every call, I want you to go three questions deep.
  Don't accept the first answer. Ask 'why is that important to you?' at least twice."
- "Today's focus is energy on the opener. First 30 seconds set the tone. I want enthusiasm,
  not monotone. We're going to drill this in Training Block 1."
- "Today we have [X] follow-up calls pending. Closers, your priority before new calls
  is clearing the follow-up pipeline. Every follow-up gets a call today."

[Assign specific targets if needed]
"[Name], I want 60 dials from you today — 20 per hour during the dial blocks."
"[Name], you have 5 calls scheduled. Goal is 3 shows minimum. Send confirmations NOW."
```

Rules:
- ONE focus. Not three. Not five. ONE thing the team works on today.
- Connect the focus to yesterday's data. If show rate was low, today's focus is pre-call nurture. If conversations were short, today's focus is discovery depth.
- Be specific. "Do better" is not a focus. "Ask 'why' twice before pitching" is a focus.

### MINUTE 25-30: Pump-Up Close

```
SCRIPT:
"Alright. We've got [X] calls on the calendar today. [X] dials to make.
The opportunity is right in front of us.

[Pick one of these or rotate]:
- 'Every dial is a chance to change someone's life. These people need what we have. Let's go find them.'
- 'We're [X]% to target this week. Today we close the gap. No excuses.'
- 'Remember why you're here. [Reference a recent win]. That's what's possible on every call.'

Let's have a massive day. Training Block 1 starts in 5. See you there."
```

Rules:
- End on energy, not logistics.
- Reference real wins from the team, not generic motivation.
- Keep it under 2 minutes. Don't give a TED talk.

## 3. TRAINING BLOCK TEMPLATES

Two training blocks per day. Block 1 (11:30 AM) is the primary skill session. Block 2 (3:30 PM) is reinforcement or objection practice.

### WEEKLY ROTATION — TRAINING BLOCK 1 (11:30 AM, 30 min)

| Day | Topic | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Intro / Opening | Manager models the ideal first 3 minutes. Then 1-on-1 role play with each rep. Then reps pair up and practice. |
| Tuesday | Discovery Questions | Manager models going 3 layers deep on qualification. Pairs practice with different prospect scenarios. |
| Wednesday | Pitch / Presentation | Manager models the full offer presentation. Pairs practice customizing the pitch to different prospect types. |
| Thursday | Close / Objection Handling | Manager models handling the week's most common objection. Pairs rapid-fire: one throws objections, the other handles. |
| Friday | Game Film | Play ONE real call recording from the week. Full team listens. Discuss: what worked, what to replicate, what to fix. |

### Training Block 1 — Detailed Run-of-Show

**Minutes 0-5: Manager Models**

The manager performs the technique live. Not a lecture. A live demonstration.

```
"Watch how I do this. [Rep name], play the prospect. Give me a realistic scenario."

[Manager runs the technique — opener, discovery sequence, pitch section, or objection handle]

"That's the standard. Here's why I did [specific thing]: [brief explanation]."
```

**Minutes 5-15: 1-on-1 Practice**

Manager pairs with each rep for 2-3 minutes of focused practice.

```
"[Name], you're up. I'm the prospect. Go."

[Rep practices. Manager gives ONE correction.]

"Good. One thing: [specific fix]. Try it again."

[Rep tries again with the fix.]

"Better. Next — [Name], you're up."
```

**Minutes 15-25: Pairs Practice**

Reps pair up and practice with each other. Manager walks the room (or listens in on breakout rooms) and coaches.

```
"Pair up. Person on the left is the rep, person on the right is the prospect.
Run through [today's technique] three times, then switch roles.
I'll be listening. Go."
```

**Minutes 25-30: Debrief**

```
"What was hard about that? [Take 2-3 responses]
Here's the takeaway for today: [one sentence summary].
I want to hear this on your calls during the dial blocks."
```

### Training Block 2 — Reinforcement (3:30 PM, 30 min)

Block 2 is not a repeat of Block 1. It is targeted reinforcement based on what the manager heard during Dial Block 1.

| Option | When to Use | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Objection Rapid Fire | Manager heard weak objection handling in Block 1 | Manager throws objections, each rep handles in 60 seconds |
| Call Opening Drill | Openers sounded flat in the morning | Each rep delivers their opener 3x with increasing energy |
| Discovery Deep Dive | Setters are having short conversations | Role play: rep must ask 5 questions before any pitch |
| Live Call Replay | A rep had a notable call in Block 1 | Play the recording, discuss as team (positive framing) |
| Freestyle Workshop | No specific gap identified | Open the floor: "What's the hardest thing you're dealing with on calls right now?" |

### TRAINING RULES (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Manager models first.** Always. Do not ask a rep to do something you haven't demonstrated. Hormozi: "Manager models first, then 1-on-1, then pairs."
2. **80% doing, 20% explaining.** Training is practice, not lecture. If the manager is talking for more than 5 minutes straight, they are lecturing.
3. **Every rep participates.** No spectators. No "I'll just watch today." If someone tries to opt out, they go first.
4. **Rotate partners.** Don't let reps only practice with their buddy. Different styles sharpen different skills.
5. **ONE technique per session.** Do not try to cover opening + discovery + close in 30 minutes. Pick ONE thing and drill it.
6. **Record it.** If a rep misses training, they watch the recording before their next dial block. No exceptions.
7. **Never skip training.** Even if revenue is up. Especially if revenue is up. Hormozi: "Non-negotiable."

## 4. CALL COACHING FRAMEWORK

This is how you develop closers and setters. Not by telling them what to do — by listening to their calls and giving them ONE thing to fix.

### Step 1: Listen to the Full Call Without Notes

First pass: just listen. Get the feel. Don't write anything down. Experience it the way the prospect experienced it.

Ask yourself:
- Did I feel heard?
- Did I feel rushed?
- Did I trust the rep?
- Was there a clear ask at the end?
- Where did I lose interest or get confused?

### Step 2: Re-Listen and Mark

Second pass: timestamp everything.

| Timestamp | What Happened | Category |
|---|---|---|
| 2:14 | Energy dropped after prospect pushed back | Energy Drop |
| 5:30 | Asked a great discovery question — prospect opened up | Win |
| 8:45 | Prospect said "I'm not sure" and rep moved on instead of digging in | Script Deviation |
| 12:00 | Pitch was too fast — prospect asked "wait, what?" | Confusion Point |
| 15:30 | Objection: "I need to think about it." Rep said "I understand." Dead. | Objection Failure |
| 18:00 | Close attempt was weak — "So, what do you think?" instead of assumptive close | Close Attempt |

Categories to track:
- **Energy Drops** — Where did the rep lose steam?
- **Confusion Points** — Where did the prospect get lost?
- **Script Deviations** — Where did the rep go off-script?
- **Objection Failures** — Where did an objection go unresolved?
- **Wins** — What did the rep do well? (Always include these.)

### Step 3: Give ONE Thing to Fix

Not five. Not three. ONE.

The rep cannot process more than one correction at a time. Pick the single biggest lever — the thing that, if fixed, would have the most impact on the outcome of the call.

```
COACHING DELIVERY:
"I listened to your call with [prospect name]. Good work on [specific win].

The one thing I want you to work on: [specific behavior].

Here's what happened at [timestamp]: [describe the moment].
Here's what I'd do instead: [model the alternative].

This week, focus on this one thing. I'll listen for it on your next calls."
```

### Step 4: Screenshot and Post

Screenshot your feedback. Post it in the team Slack/Discord channel. Every rep should see coaching happening constantly.

Why:
- Reps who aren't being coached see the standard being set.
- Reps who are being coached feel the accountability.
- Everyone learns from everyone else's coaching.

Hormozi: "Be maniacal in posting feedback. Reps should feel you're watching every call so they always perform to the max."

### Step 5: Track in Coaching Tracker

```
COACHING TRACKER ENTRY
────────────────────────────────────
Date:           [YYYY-MM-DD]
Rep:            [Name]
Call:           [Prospect name or ID]
Call Recording:  [Link]
Area Reviewed:  [Opening / Discovery / Pitch / Close / Objection Handling]
Feedback Given: [One-sentence summary]
Action Item:    [Specific behavior change]
Follow-Up Date: [When you'll check if they implemented]
Status:         [Open / Implemented / Still Working]
────────────────────────────────────
```

### Coaching Volume Targets

| Team Size | Calls Reviewed/Day | Coaching Posts/Day |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 reps | 2-3 calls | 2 posts minimum |
| 4-6 reps | 4-6 calls | 3 posts minimum |
| 7+ reps | 6-8 calls | 4 posts minimum |

### Coaching Principles

1. **Celebrate publicly, coach privately.** Never call out a rep's weakness in a team setting. Wins go in the team channel. Corrections go in DMs or 1-on-1s.
2. **Always lead with a win.** Before the correction, acknowledge something they did well. This is not sugarcoating — it reinforces the behaviors you want.
3. **Model, don't lecture.** Show them what "good" sounds like. Play your version of the moment. Then have them try it.
4. **ONE thing.** Repeat until it becomes automatic. Then move to the next thing.
5. **Track everything.** If you don't track it, you can't measure improvement. The coaching tracker is your proof that development is happening.

## 5. 1-ON-1 MEETING TEMPLATE (30-45 MIN PER REP, WEEKLY)

Schedule these at the same time each week. Never cancel. Never reschedule unless the rep asks. This is the most important meeting on your calendar.

Hormozi's 3-Part structure: Personal first. Short-term second. Long-term third.

### Part 1 — Personal (10 min)

```
SCRIPT:
"Before we get into numbers — how are you doing? What's going on in your life?"

[Let them talk. Don't rush this.]

Follow-ups if needed:
- "Are you sleeping okay?"
- "Anything stressing you out outside of work?"
- "How's [thing they mentioned last week]?"
```

Why this matters:
- This is NOT small talk. This is how you catch problems before they tank performance.
- A rep going through a breakup will underperform for 2-3 weeks. If you know, you can adjust expectations and support them. If you don't know, you'll just see bad numbers and wonder why.
- Hormozi: "Personal first — short-term second — long-term third."

Rules:
- Never skip this section, even if you're short on time.
- Never use what they share against them. This is trust-building. One betrayal and you lose the rep.
- If they say "I'm fine" and clearly aren't — push gently. "You sure? You seemed a little off this week."

### Part 2 — Short-Term Performance (20 min)

```
PREPARATION (Before the meeting):
- Rep submits 3 call recordings minimum 24 hours before the 1-on-1
- Manager listens to all 3 and identifies patterns
- Manager picks the ONE skill that would improve the most calls

SCRIPT:
"Let's look at your calls this week. You submitted [X] recordings.

[Play a specific moment from one of the calls]

'Hear that? At [timestamp], [describe what happened].'

Here's what I'd do differently: [model the alternative — actually say it out loud, in character].

This week, I want you to focus on one thing: [specific behavior].
Not two things. Not five. This one thing.

Let's practice it right now."

[Role play for 5-10 minutes. Manager plays prospect.]

"Good. That's what I want to hear on your calls this week. I'll be listening for it."
```

Rules:
- Rep MUST submit 3 recordings before the meeting. If they don't, the meeting is about why they didn't submit recordings. Non-negotiable.
- Always identify ONE skill gap. Resist the urge to give a laundry list.
- Always practice during the 1-on-1. This is not a review meeting — it's a coaching session.
- Track the action item in the coaching tracker. Follow up next week.

### Part 3 — Long-Term Development (10 min)

```
SCRIPT:
"Zooming out — where do you want to be in 6 months? 12 months?"

[Let them answer. Then connect their goals to concrete next steps.]

"Here's what it takes to get there:
- To move from [current role] to [next role], you need to hit [specific metrics] for [timeframe].
- You're currently at [current metrics]. The gap is [gap].
- If you close that gap, we'll have the conversation about [advancement]."

"What can I do to help you get there faster?"
```

Career path to reference:
```
Junior Setter --> Setter --> Senior Setter
                                        \
                                    Junior Closer --> Closer --> Senior Closer --> Sales Manager
```

Advancement triggers:
- Setter to Senior Setter: 80%+ show rate, 4+ bookings/day for 60 days
- Senior Setter to Junior Closer: passes closing role-play assessment, 90-day booking consistency
- Junior Closer to Closer: 25%+ close rate over 50 calls
- Closer to Senior Closer: 35%+ close rate, 90-day consistency, mentoring a junior rep

Hormozi: "Give new hires a promotion within 30 days — something to call mom about." Even a title change (Junior Setter to Setter) creates buy-in.

## 6. WEEKLY TEAM MEETING AGENDA (45-60 MIN)

Same day, same time, every week. This is the anchor meeting of the week.

### MINUTE 0-10: Scoreboard Review

```
SCRIPT:
"Here are this week's numbers."

[Share the weekly scorecard — see Section 10]

"Top performer this week: [Name] — [stats]. [Name], how'd you do it?"

[Let them share. The team learns from the top performer, not just the manager.]

"Team total: $[X] collected against $[X] target. We're at [X]%."

If on target: "We're tracking. Let's keep the foot on the gas."
If behind: "We're [X]% behind. Here's what needs to happen next week: [specific actions]."
```

### MINUTE 10-30: Game Film

```
SCRIPT:
"Game film time. I've got a call from this week I want us to listen to.
[Rep name], thanks for letting us use this — takes guts to put your work out there."

[Play the call — or the relevant 5-10 minute section]

After playing:
"Alright. What did [rep name] do well?"
[Take 3-4 responses from the team]

"What could be improved?"
[Take 3-4 responses — manager guides but lets the team identify the gaps]

"Here's what I'd coach: [manager's one takeaway]. This applies to all of us."
```

Rules:
- Ideally, the rep volunteers their call. If no volunteers, the manager picks (and tells the rep privately beforehand — never ambush someone).
- Always start with what went well. Always.
- The manager gives the final coaching point — not the first one. Let the team learn to analyze calls themselves.
- Pick calls that illustrate the week's most common issue. If everyone is weak on objection handling, pick a call where an objection went unresolved.

### MINUTE 30-45: Seasonal Objection Workshop

```
SCRIPT:
"What's the hardest objection you're hearing right now? The one that's killing your calls?"

[Collect 2-3 objections from the team]

"Let's work on [the most common one]. Here's how I handle it:"

[Manager models the handle — full delivery, in character]

"Now let's drill it. Pair up. One person throws the objection, the other handles it.
Switch after 2 reps. Go."

[5-10 minutes of drilling]

"Alright. Here's the handle one more time: [repeat the framework]. Use this next week."
```

Hormozi: "Focus on seasonal/trending objections." What's trending this week won't be trending next month. Address what's live.

### MINUTE 45-50: Announcements

```
Brief updates:
- Any offer changes (pricing, bonuses, guarantees)
- New case studies or testimonials to reference on calls
- Upcoming events (webinars, launches) that affect the sales process
- Any changes to lead flow, scheduling, or CRM process
```

### MINUTE 50-55: Energy Close

```
SCRIPT:
"This was a [good/tough/great] week. Here's what I'm proud of: [specific example].

Next week's focus: [ONE theme for the week].

Let's have our best week yet. See you [next meeting day]."
```

## 7. GROUP COACHING SESSION TEMPLATE

Use this for larger team coaching sessions, new hire group trainings, or monthly deep-dive skill sessions.

### Step 1: Open Q&A (10 min)

```
SCRIPT:
"Before we start — does anyone have questions? Anything you're stuck on?
Anything from last week's coaching you want to revisit?"

[If they say no, chat casually for a minute. They'll come up with something.]

[If the entire session is answering their questions, that's fine. Answer thoroughly.
Make sure they understand — don't just talk at them.]
```

### Step 2: Weekly Update + Celebrate (5 min)

End-of-week sessions only:

```
"This week's numbers: $[X] cash collected. [X] closes.
Top performer: [Name] — [stats]. Give it up for [Name].

Any company updates: [brief — offer changes, new tools, etc.]

Quick question for the team: what could we do better as an org? I'm listening."
```

### Step 3: Teach ONE Specific Skill (15 min)

```
Pick ONE skill the whole team needs work on based on the week's call reviews.

"This week I noticed [pattern] across multiple calls. Here's how to fix it."

Use examples:
- Walk through a real call moment
- Tell a real story from your own closing experience
- Pull up the relevant script section
- Show a diagram or framework if it helps

Engage the team while teaching:
"[Name], when you hear a prospect say [objection], what do you usually say?"
[Let them answer. Coach on their answer.]

"Everyone still with me? Questions so far?"
```

### Step 4: Drill It Live (15 min)

```
"We've talked about it. Now let's do it. Who wants to be the prospect?"

[Select or accept volunteers]

RUNNING THE MOCK CALL:
- Let the participants go as far as they can. Don't interrupt unless the call is bad and unproductive.
- Stay silent during the simulation. Take notes.
- Let it play out naturally — even if it's messy. That's the learning.

AFTER THE SIMULATION:
1. Ask the prospect: "How did you feel as the prospect? Where did you feel sold to? Where did you feel heard?"
2. Ask the rep: "How did you feel? Where did you get stuck? What would you do differently?"
3. Manager coaches: "Here's what I observed that could be done better: [ONE thing]."

[Run 2-3 simulations if time allows. Rotate participants.]
```

### Step 5: Encouragement Close (5 min)

```
"Good work today. Here's what I want you to take away: [one sentence summary].

This team is getting better every week. I see it in the numbers and I see it on the calls.

Let's go have a massive [rest of the week / next week]. I'm here if you need anything."
```

## 12. HIRING & RAMPING CHECKLIST

### When to Hire

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Calendar utilization > 85% | Closers are maxed out | Hire another closer |
| Show rate dropping despite good nurture | Too many leads, not enough slots | Hire another closer |
| Lead response time > 60 seconds consistently | Setters can't keep up | Hire another setter |
| Revenue plateaued but lead flow is growing | Not enough capacity to convert | Hire the bottleneck role |
| Rep quits or gets fired | Hole in the lineup | Backfill immediately — don't wait |

Hormozi: "I have yet to make less money by hiring more salespeople."

### 4-Round Interview Process

**Round 1: Application Screening**
- Resume review
- 2-minute video pitch: "Sell me on why I should hire you."
- Evaluate: energy, clarity, confidence, effort level in the video
- Pass/fail in 24 hours. Don't let good candidates wait.

**Round 4: Paid Trial (3 Days)**

- 3 days on real leads (or realistic simulated leads if the pipeline can't support waste)
- Manager listens to every call
- Evaluate: coachability, implementation speed, energy, culture fit
- Pass/fail by end of Day 3. Don't drag it out.

### Interview Red Flags (Immediate Disqualification)

- No camera on the interview call
- Late to the interview with no advance notice
- Conducting the interview while driving, walking, or commuting
- Can't clearly articulate their sales experience
- Complains about previous employers without taking responsibility
- "I don't really need training, I just need leads"
- Dishonesty about experience or inflating non-sales roles
- No investment in their own sales education (courses, books, coaching)

Hormozi: "If it's not a 'hell yes,' it's generally a 'no.' Candidates are abundant. Hire for attitude, train for skill."

### 50-Call Ramping Framework

Once hired, every new rep follows this ramp. No shortcuts.

**Day 1-7: Drinking from the Firehose**

Outcome: Get acquainted. Build the foundation.

Checklist:
- [ ] Complete all onboarding materials (company overview, offer deep-dive, ICP training)
- [ ] Set up CRM access, calendar, communication tools
- [ ] Go through ALL training materials provided — before anything else
- [ ] Read through testimonials (at least the last 2 months)
- [ ] Shadow current reps — as much as possible
- [ ] Listen to 5-10 call recordings per day
- [ ] Begin Black Marker Method script memorization
- [ ] Attend all team meetings and trainings (observe, don't participate yet)
- [ ] Daily check-in with manager: "What did you learn today?"

What you're evaluating:
- Coachable and implementing?
- Hungry, engaged, asking questions?
- Culture fit?

**Day 7-14: Limited Live Calls**

Outcome: Start taking calls under direct supervision.

Checklist:
- [ ] Finish all training materials — then go through them AGAIN
- [ ] Set your own appointments (show you're hungry)
- [ ] Take 2-3 calls per day with manager listening live
- [ ] Every call gets reviewed — in team meetings and 1-on-1s
- [ ] Continue Black Marker Method — should be at 50%+ memorization
- [ ] Continue shadowing top performers
- [ ] Daily call review with manager (15 min minimum)

What you're evaluating:
- Sound good on calls?
- Implementing feedback from previous calls?
- Still coachable and asking questions?
- Culture fit holding up under pressure?

Hormozi: "The BEST reps do both [close inbound AND set their own]. Always. Don't be a lazy inbound baby-bird closer."

**Day 15-30: Full Volume**

Outcome: Full call load. Getting toward KPI.

Checklist:
- [ ] Full call volume — same as tenured reps
- [ ] Still getting daily feedback from manager
- [ ] Still going through training (always — this never stops)
- [ ] Submitting EODs perfectly (100% compliance)
- [ ] Setting own appointments in addition to inbound

Milestones:
- Within first 10 calls: 1-2 deals closed minimum
- Within first 20-30 calls: close to or at KPI
- Within first 50 calls: at KPI (statistical significance)

What you're evaluating (quantitative now):
- Close rate trending toward 25%+
- Show rate on self-set appointments 70%+
- EOD compliance 100%
- Script adherence > 80%

**At any point**, if you are NOT seeing coachability, hunger, culture fit, or implementation — you may let the rep go. Don't wait for 50 calls to confirm what you already know.

### Ramping Rules

1. The rep is NOT guaranteed 50 calls. That's the quantitative assessment window. The qualitative assessment runs in parallel and can end the trial at any time.
2. Manager listens to every single call during ramp. Every. Single. One.
3. Daily 15-minute debrief with the new rep during ramp. Non-negotiable.
4. New reps observe training for the first 3 days, then participate.
5. If a rep hasn't closed within 20 calls AND isn't showing improvement call-over-call, have the conversation. Don't let a bad fit linger.
