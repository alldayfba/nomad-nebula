Team Meetings Flowchart

247 Growth — Sales Manager Daily Playbook

Total Meeting Time
30:00
1. Energy Check3 min
"Good morning everyone. Before we get into numbers -- how's everyone feeling today? Give me a 1-10 in the chat. 1 is 'barely alive,' 10 is 'ready to run through a wall.'" If anyone is below a 5: "[Name], what's going on? What do you need today?" If everyone is 7+: "Love it. Let's match that energy on the phones today."
3:00
2. Yesterday's Wins5 min
"Alright, wins from yesterday. I want to hear from everyone. Doesn't have to be a close -- could be a great conversation, a lesson learned, a new technique that worked. [Name], you're up first." After each win: "Nice. [Brief acknowledgment or tactical insight.]" If someone closed: "[Name] closed yesterday -- [deal details]. Ring the bell. Let's go."
Every rep shares. No spectators. There is always something.
5:00

4. Today's Focus10 min
"Today's focus: [ONE thing]." Examples: - "Discovery depth. On every call, go three questions deep." - "Energy on the opener. First 30 seconds set the tone." - "Clearing the follow-up pipeline before new calls." Assign specific targets: "[Name], I want 60 dials from you today." "[Name], you have 5 calls scheduled. Goal is 3 shows minimum."
ONE focus. Not three. Connect it to yesterday's data.

10:00
5. Pump-Up Close5 min
"Alright. We've got [X] calls on the calendar today. [X] dials to make. The opportunity is right in front of us. - 'Every dial is a chance to change someone's life. Let's go find them.' - 'We're [X]% to target this week. Today we close the gap.' - 'Remember why you're here. [Reference a recent win]. That's what's possible.' Let's have a massive day. Training Block 1 starts in 5."
End on energy, not logistics. Reference real wins. Keep it under 2 minutes.

"Here are this week's numbers." "Top performer this week: [Name] -- [stats]. [Name], how'd you do it?" "Team total: $[X] collected against $[X] target. We're at [X]%." On target: "We're tracking. Foot on the gas." Behind: "We're [X]% behind. Here's what needs to happen next week."

[3-4 responses] "What could be improved?" [3-4 responses] "Here's what I'd coach: [one takeaway]. This applies to all of us."

3. Objection Workshop15 min
"What's the hardest objection you're hearing right now? The one that's killing your calls?" [Collect 2-3 from team] "Let's work on [most common one]. Here's how I handle it:" [Manager models the handle -- full delivery, in character] "Pair up. One throws the objection, the other handles it. Switch after 2 reps. Go."
What was the hardest objection this week?

40:00
1. Personal10 min
"Before we get into numbers -- how are you doing? What's going on in your life?" Follow-ups if needed: - "Are you sleeping okay?" - "Anything stressing you out outside of work?" - "How's [thing they mentioned last week]?"
Never skip this. Never use what they share against them. This is trust-building.

2. Short-Term Performance20 min
"Let's look at your calls this week. You submitted [X] recordings." [Play a specific moment] "Hear that? At [timestamp], [describe what happened]." "Here's what I'd do differently: [model the alternative]." "This week, focus on one thing: [specific behavior]. Not two things. Not five. This one thing." [Role play for 5-10 minutes. Manager plays prospect.]
Rep MUST submit 3 recordings before the meeting. Always practice during the 1-on-1. ONE skill gap.

"Zooming out -- where do you want to be in 6 months? 12 months?" "Here's what it takes to get there: - To move from [current role] to [next role], you need to hit [metrics] for [timeframe]. - You're currently at [current]. The gap is [gap]. - If you close that gap, we'll have the conversation about [advancement]." "What can I do to help you get there faster?" Career path: Junior Setter > Setter > Senior Setter > Junior Closer > Closer > Senior Closer > Sales Manager
