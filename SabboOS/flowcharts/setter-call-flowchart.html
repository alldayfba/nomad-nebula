Pattern Interrupt
Get 30 seconds of their time
10 sec
▼
Primary Opener
"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] — I know this is out of the blue, do you have 30 seconds?"
If They Push Back
"Who is this?" / "What's this about?" ▶
"Totally fair — I work with growth systems for founder-led businesses. I was looking at [their company] and had a quick question. Literally 30 seconds."
"I'm busy" ▶
"Totally get it. When's a better 2 minutes? I'll call you back then."
"How'd you get my number?" ▶
"Your business came up when I was researching [their industry] in [their area]. I'm not trying to waste your time — genuinely had a question about how you're handling growth. 30 seconds?"
  • ✕ Do NOT say your company name — it means nothing yet
  • ✕ Do NOT pitch in the opener — you're asking for time
  • ✓ Match their energy — relaxed = relaxed, direct = direct
2
The Hook
Make them curious enough to keep talking
15-20 sec
▼
Primary Hook
"I was looking at [Company] — looks like you've built something solid. Quick question: is your growth right now more systematic, or is it more like... you're figuring it out as you go?"
Alternative Hooks
They're running ads ▶
"I noticed you're running ads — are you happy with what you're getting back from that spend, or does it feel like you're kind of guessing?"
Big following, no clear funnel ▶
"You've got a solid following — are you actually converting that into revenue systematically, or is it more word-of-mouth right now?"
Recently hired/fired an agency ▶
"I saw you were working with [agency/freelancer]. Are you still with them, or are you looking for something different?"
SHUT UP. Let them talk.
3
Dig Into Pain
Diagnose. Qualify. Identify #1 pain.
60-90 sec
▼
Listen 70%, Talk 30%. You are diagnosing, not pitching.
Pick 2-3 Discovery Questions
"What does your customer acquisition look like right now — where do most of your clients come from?"
"Do you have a marketing team or person handling this, or is it on you?"
"What have you tried that hasn't worked?"
"What would it look like if this was actually dialed in? What would change for you?"
"What's the cost of NOT fixing this? Like, how much are you leaving on the table every month?"
Qualification Checklist
  • Revenue $1M+/year
  • Has product-market fit
  • Spending money on marketing
  • Frustrated, wants to fix it
  • Founder is decision maker
  • Can invest $5K-$25K/mo
Qualified: 0 / 6
Recap Their Pain
"So it sounds like the main issue is [X], and that's costing you roughly [Y] a month — is that right?"
"Appreciate the time — honestly based on what you're telling me, I don't think this would be the right fit right now. But if things change, I'd love to reconnect."
  • Do NOT book bad leads. It destroys closer time and YOUR show rate.
  • Close rate on bad leads: 0%. Close rate on good leads: 40-60%.

15-20 sec
▼
Primary Bridge

We work with founder-led businesses in the [their revenue range] and build out the entire growth engine — not just run ads, but install the full system so it's predictable. I think it'd be worth a conversation. Not a pitch — just a strategy session to see if there's a fit."
Alternative Bridges
Case study bridge ▶
"We've helped a few businesses in [their space] go from exactly what you're describing to a predictable pipeline. It might make sense to jump on a quick call with [Closer Name] to see if we can do the same."
Systems problem bridge ▶
"Honestly, what you're describing is a systems problem, not a marketing problem. That's what we solve. Would it make sense to get on a 30-minute call to dig into this properly?"
  • ✕ Do NOT explain the offer in detail — that's the closer's job
  • ✕ Say "strategy session" or "conversation" — NEVER "sales call"
  • ✓ Use a case study if you have one for their industry
5
Book the Call
Lock the time. Get tie-downs. Protect the show.
30-45 sec
▼
Booking Script
"I've got time on [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] — which works better for you?"
After They Pick a Time
"Perfect. I'll send you a calendar link right now. A couple things — the call is with [Closer Name], it's about 30 minutes. Before the call, I'll send you a short video that'll give you context on how we work so you get the most out of it. And [Name] — just make sure you're somewhere quiet with no distractions. Cool?"
Tie-Down #1: Decision Maker (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
"Quick question — if you liked what you heard on the call, is there anyone else who'd need to be involved in the decision? A business partner, COO, anyone?"
Ask: "Can they be on the call too?"
If they can't → Reschedule to when both can attend.
"So just to confirm — if you wanted to move forward, you wouldn't need anyone else's sign-off?"
Tie-Down #2: Financial (Use When Rapport is Strong)
"Just so the call is valuable for everyone — our clients typically invest $5K-$25K a month depending on scope. Is that realistic for you if the fit is right?"
Great. Move on. The call is locked.
"No pressure at all — I just want to make sure the call is worth your time. If budget is tight, maybe we reconnect in a couple months?"

Objection Handling

"I'm not interested."▶
"Totally respect that. Out of curiosity — is that because growth is already dialed in, or is it more of a timing thing?"

If timing → "When's better to revisit?"
If dialed in → "Best of luck — keep me in mind."

"Just send me an email."▶
"Happy to — what's the best email? And just so I send something relevant, what's the biggest marketing challenge you're dealing with right now?"

If they answer the question, you're back in conversation.

"We already have an agency."▶
"Nice — are you happy with the results? I only ask because a lot of founders I talk to have a team but still feel like growth is unpredictable."

If unhappy → "What's not working?"
If happy → "Not trying to fix what's not broken."

"What do you guys do exactly?"▶
"Short version — we install a full growth operating system into founder-led businesses. Acquisition, conversion, retention — and we operate it for you. But honestly, the call is where we'd dig into whether it makes sense for you."

Bridge back to booking. Do NOT get pulled into a 10-min explanation.

"I don't have time for a call."▶
"Totally get it — you're busy, that's exactly why this might be worth it. The call is 30 min and it's specifically about getting your time back from marketing."

Offer a specific time: "What if we did [Day] at [early AM / lunch / end of day]?"

"Is this a sales call?"▶
"It's a strategy session — [Closer] looks at what you're doing now and gives honest feedback on where the gaps are. If there's a fit, great. If not, you walk away with clarity. No pressure either way."

Bridge to booking.

"How much does it cost?"▶
"It depends on scope — that's exactly what the strategy session is for. [Closer Name] will look at your situation, what you need, and give you a straight answer. No surprises."

Do NOT give pricing. That's the closer's job. Bridge to booking.
