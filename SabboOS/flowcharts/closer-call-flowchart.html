Operator Model vs Agency Model

  • Operator: Senior lead embedded, owns the outcome, daily monitoring, your team

Scorecard KPIs

  • Cost per Lead (CPL)
  • Cost per Qualified Lead (CPQL)
  • Call Show Rate
  • Close Rate
  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
  • Lifetime Value (LTV)
  • Client Retention Rate

The 5 Tones

  • Curious -- Discovery, genuine interest
  • Concerned -- Past pain, lean in, slow down
  • Challenging -- Direct, honest, consequence
  • Confident -- Pitch, price, close
  • Playful -- Opening rapport, break tension

Never Do

  • Fill silence after stating price
  • Justify unprompted
  • Bad-mouth competitors by name
  • Promise specific revenue numbers
  • Leave follow-up without a date/time

Objection Handling
As needed Match to objection
▾
Framework (Every Objection)

1. Acknowledge -- "I hear you. That's a fair concern."

2. Clarify -- "What specifically about [objection] is the concern?"

3. Suppose -- "If we could resolve that, would there be anything else holding you back?"

4. Callback -- "Remember earlier when you told me [Stage 4 answer]?"

5. Reframe -- Use the appropriate response below.

6. Check -- "Does that make sense?"

7. Re-commit -- "So what would you like to do?"

Trust / Past Experience
"We already have an agency."
What They Actually Mean

I don't want to go through the pain of switching, and I'm not sure you'd be better.

Exact Response
"I respect that -- and I'm not here to trash whoever you're working with. But can I ask you something? If they were producing the growth you described wanting earlier -- the predictable pipeline, the reduced CAC, the system that runs without you -- would you be on this call right now?"
"Right. So the question isn't whether you have an agency. The question is whether you have the outcome. And there's a reason most agencies don't produce it -- they're built around deliverables, not outcomes. They send you a report. We own the number."
If They Push Back Again
"I get it -- switching feels like a big move. Here's what I'd suggest: let's do the diagnostic anyway. If your current team is actually doing everything right, the audit will show that and I'll tell you to stay. But if there are gaps -- and based on what you've told me, there are -- wouldn't you rather know?"
"We've been burned by agencies before."
What They Actually Mean

I spent a lot of money, got poor results, and I don't trust this category anymore.

Exact Response
"That's fair -- and honestly, I'd be skeptical too. Can I ask what happened? Like, what specifically went wrong with the last agency?"

Listen for: vague reporting, junior team, no accountability, over-promised, disappeared after month 2.

"So what I'm hearing is [diagnose their specific failure]. Here's why that happened -- most agencies are built on the account manager model. You get a senior person on the sales call, then they hand you off to a coordinator who's managing 15 other accounts. Nobody owns your outcome. That's structurally designed to produce what you experienced."
