What Is AI, Really?

Forget the hype. Here's what you actually need to know.

🧠

LLMs (Large Language Models)

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — these are AI models that understand and generate text. Think of them as extremely smart assistants that can read, write, and reason.

💬

Regular Chat vs. AI Agents

Regular chat = you ask a question, it answers. AI agents = you give it a task and it goes and DOES it. Opens websites, scrapes data, writes files, runs code.

THE SECRET SAUCE

What Are Directives?

Directives are SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) written in plain English that tell the AI exactly what to do. Think of them as training manuals for your AI employee.

# Example: amazon-sourcing-sop.md

Goal: Find profitable products to sell on Amazon
Step 1: Scrape the retailer's clearance section
Step 2: For each product, search Amazon for a match
Step 3: Calculate FBA fees, shipping, and net profit
Step 4: Only keep products with ROI > 30%
Step 5: Check BSR — must be under 200,000
Step 6: Output a ranked list with buy links

# The AI reads this and follows it step by step.
# Better SOPs = better AI output. Always.
THE ARCHITECTURE

How The Whole System Works

Three layers working together. This is how the best AI operators in the world run their businesses.

01

Directives (SOPs)

Plain English instructions. "Here's what to do, here's how to do it, here's what good looks like." The AI reads these before every task.

02

AI Brain (Orchestration)

The AI reads the directive, makes decisions, and calls the right tools in the right order. It handles errors and asks questions when stuck.

03

Scripts (Execution)

Python scripts that do the actual work — scraping websites, calculating profits, checking Amazon data. Reliable, fast, repeatable.

KEY CONCEPT

The AI Gets Better Over Time

Unlike a regular tool, AI agents self-improve. Every error becomes a lesson. Every correction makes it smarter.

⚠

Something Breaks

A retailer changes their website layout. The scraper fails on Walmart clearance.

🛠

AI Fixes Itself

The system detects the error, reads the new page structure, updates the scraper code, and tests it.

📚

Updates Its Own SOP

The AI writes "Walmart changed layout on 3/20" into the directive so it never makes the same mistake again.

This is called self-annealing — the system heals and hardens itself automatically.
